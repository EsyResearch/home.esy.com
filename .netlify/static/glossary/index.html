Writing Glossary

Essential terms and concepts for academic writing and essay composition. From thesis statements to citation formats.

30 terms
A

Active Voice

Grammar

A sentence construction where the subject performs the action expressed by the verb.

Analysis

Writing

The process of examining and interpreting evidence to support your argument.

APA Format

Citation

A citation style guide by the American Psychological Association for social sciences papers.

Argumentative Essay

Writing

A type of essay that presents a clear position on a topic and supports it with evidence and reasoning.

B

Body Paragraph

Structure

The main paragraphs of an essay that develop and support the thesis with evidence and analysis.

Brainstorming

Writing

A creative process for generating ideas and topics before beginning to write.

C

Citation

Citation

A reference to a source of information used in academic writing to give credit to original authors.

Comma Splice

Grammar

A grammatical error where two independent clauses are incorrectly joined with only a comma.

Conclusion

Structure

The final paragraph that restates the thesis, summarizes key points, and provides closure.

Counterargument

Writing

An opposing viewpoint that challenges your thesis, addressed to strengthen your argument.

E

Evidence

Research

Facts, statistics, examples, or expert opinions used to support claims in an essay.

H

Hook

Writing

An engaging opening sentence or phrase designed to capture the reader's attention.

I

Introduction

Structure

The opening paragraph that provides background, engages the reader, and presents the thesis.

M

MLA Format

Citation

A citation style guide published by the Modern Language Association for humanities papers.

O

Outline

Structure

A structured plan that organizes your main points and supporting details before writing.

P

Parallel Structure

Grammar

Using the same grammatical form for similar elements in a sentence or series.

Paraphrase

Writing

Restating someone else's ideas in your own words while maintaining the original meaning.

Passive Voice

Grammar

A sentence construction where the subject receives the action expressed by the verb.

Peer Review

Research

The process of having classmates or colleagues evaluate and provide feedback on your writing.

Plagiarism

Citation

The practice of using someone else's work or ideas without proper attribution.

Proofreading

Grammar

The final step of checking for spelling, grammar, and punctuation errors.

Q

Quote

Citation

The exact words from a source, enclosed in quotation marks and properly attributed.

R

Research Question

Research

A focused question that guides your research and helps develop your thesis statement.

Revision

Writing

The process of reviewing and improving content, organization, and clarity of a draft.

Run-on Sentence

Grammar

A sentence that contains multiple independent clauses without proper punctuation or conjunctions.

S

Subject-Verb Agreement

Grammar

The grammatical rule that subjects and verbs must agree in number (singular or plural).

T

Thesis Statement

Structure

A concise summary of the main point or claim of an essay, usually appearing in the introduction.

Topic Sentence

Structure

The first sentence of a paragraph that introduces the main idea and connects to the thesis.

Transition

Writing

Words or phrases that connect ideas and create smooth flow between sentences and paragraphs.

W

Works Cited

Citation

A list of all sources referenced in an essay, formatted according to a specific style guide.
