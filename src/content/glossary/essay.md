An essay is a short piece of writing on a specific topic. It presents a central argument (thesis) supported by evidence and reasoning, following a clear structure: introduction, body, and conclusion.

## Essay Types

- **Argumentative** — presents and defends a position
- **Expository** — explains or informs objectively
- **Narrative** — tells a story or recounts events
- **Descriptive** — creates a vivid picture through details

## Basic Structure

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Introduction | Hook reader, provide context, state thesis |
| Body Paragraphs | Present evidence and analysis |
| Conclusion | Synthesize ideas, reinforce thesis |

## Key Elements

1. **Thesis statement** — your central claim
2. **Topic sentences** — main idea of each paragraph
3. **Evidence** — facts, quotes, examples that support your points
4. **Analysis** — explanation of why evidence matters
5. **Transitions** — connections between ideas

> The word "essay" comes from the French *essayer*, meaning "to try"—an essay is an attempt to explore an idea.
