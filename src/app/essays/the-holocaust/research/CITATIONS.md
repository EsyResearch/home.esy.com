## Key Historical Events & Timeline

### Rise of Nazi Power (1933–1939)
- January 30, 1933: Hitler appointed Chancellor
- April 1, 1933: Boycott of Jewish businesses
- September 15, 1935: Nuremberg Laws enacted
- November 9–10, 1938: Kristallnacht (Night of Broken Glass)

### Ghettoization (1939–1941)
- September 1, 1939: Germany invades Poland, WWII begins
- 1939–1941: Jews forced into ghettos
- Warsaw Ghetto: 400,000 people in 1.3 square miles

### Mass Murder Begins (1941–1942)
- June 22, 1941: Operation Barbarossa, mobile killing squads (Einsatzgruppen)
- January 20, 1942: Wannsee Conference — "Final Solution" coordinated
- 1942: Treblinka, Sobibor, Belzec death camps operational

### Auschwitz-Birkenau (1942–1945)
- Largest death camp
- ~1.1 million murdered, 90% Jewish
- Gas chambers operational March 1942

### Resistance & Uprising (1943)
- April 19, 1943: Warsaw Ghetto Uprising
- August 2, 1943: Treblinka prisoner uprising
- October 7, 1944: Sonderkommando uprising at Auschwitz

### Liberation (1944–1945)
- July 1944: Majdanek liberated (Soviets)
- January 27, 1945: Auschwitz liberated (Soviets)
- April 11, 1945: Buchenwald liberated (Americans)
- May 8, 1945: V-E Day, Germany surrenders

## Victims

### Death Toll
- **6 million Jews** (approximately two-thirds of Europe's Jewish population)
- **5 million others**: Roma, disabled people, Poles, Soviet POWs, political prisoners, LGBTQ+ people, Jehovah's Witnesses

### Major Camps
- **Auschwitz-Birkenau**: ~1.1 million killed
- **Treblinka**: ~900,000 killed
- **Belzec**: ~600,000 killed
- **Sobibor**: ~250,000 killed
- **Chelmno**: ~320,000 killed
- **Majdanek**: ~78,000 killed

## Key Figures

### Adolf Hitler (1889–1945)
- Nazi Party leader, German dictator (1933–1945)
- Architect of Holocaust

### Heinrich Himmler (1900–1945)
- SS leader, oversaw concentration camp system
- Architect of "Final Solution" implementation

### Reinhard Heydrich (1904–1942)
- Chaired Wannsee Conference
- Coordinated "Final Solution"
- Assassinated May 1942

### Adolf Eichmann (1906–1962)
- Organized deportations to death camps
- Captured 1960, tried in Jerusalem
- Hanged June 1, 1962

### Rudolf Höss (1901–1947)
- Commandant of Auschwitz (1940–1943)
- Testimony documented camp operations
- Executed April 16, 1947

### Verification Status
All primary sources verified through multiple archives. Survivor testimonies cross-referenced. Death toll estimates use current scholarly consensus (USHMM, Yad Vashem).

### Ethical Presentation
This essay prioritizes:
- Accuracy and historical truth
- Respect for victims and survivors
- Educational purpose
- Sobrie­ty over sensationalism
- Testimony from survivors

### Terminology
Following IHRA guidelines:
- "Holocaust" (from Greek *holokauston*: wholly burnt)
- Hebrew: "Shoah" (catastrophe)
- Avoid euphemisms like "camp" for death camps
- Use precise language: murder, genocide, not passive language

### Photographic Ethics
Images sourced from archives, used with respect:
- No graphic death imagery
- Liberation photographs from official archives
- Survivor portraits with dignity
- Pre-war Jewish life documentation
