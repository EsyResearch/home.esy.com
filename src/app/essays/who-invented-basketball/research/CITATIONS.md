## Key Historical Facts

### Invention (December 1891)
- **Inventor**: Dr. James Naismith
- **Location**: International YMCA Training School, Springfield, Massachusetts
- **Date**: December 21, 1891 (first game)
- **Original Equipment**: Soccer ball, two peach baskets
- **Challenge**: Create indoor game for winter months

### Original 13 Rules (1891)
- Written by Naismith on two typewritten pages
- **2010 Auction**: Original rules document sold for $4.3 million
- Rules established: no running with ball, goals made by throwing ball into basket
- Foundational principles remain in modern game

## Global Spread

### Early Adoption (1890s–1930s)
- **1893**: Basketball reaches France
- **1895**: China adopts basketball
- Japan and India follow shortly after
- YMCA missionaries primary vectors of spread

### Olympic Debut (1936)
- **Location**: Berlin Olympics
- **Significance**: Dr. James Naismith presented medals
- Basketball becomes official Olympic sport
- Current status: 200+ countries play basketball

---

## NBA History

### Founding (1946–1949)
- **June 6, 1946**: Basketball Association of America (BAA) founded in New York City
- **November 1, 1946**: First BAA game — Toronto Huskies vs New York Knickerbockers at Maple Leaf Gardens
- **1949**: Merger with National Basketball League creates NBA

### Modern Era Milestones
- **1954**: 24-second shot clock introduced
- **1967**: ABA introduces three-point line
- **1979**: NBA adopts three-point line
- **1984**: David Stern becomes NBA Commissioner
- **1992**: Dream Team at Barcelona Olympics
- **2024**: NBA global phenomenon, $10+ billion annual revenue

## Key Figures

### Dr. James Naismith (1861–1939)
- Canadian physical education instructor
- Invented basketball December 1891
- Attended 1936 Berlin Olympics to see basketball debut
- Quote: "The invention of basketball was not an accident. It was developed to meet a need."

### Wilt Chamberlain (1936–1999)
- Scored 100 points in single game (March 2, 1962, Hershey, PA)
- Dominant center, transformed offensive game

### Bill Russell (1934–2022)
- 11 NBA championships with Boston Celtics
- Redefined defensive play and team basketball

### Magic Johnson (1959–present) & Larry Bird (1956–present)
- Rivalry in 1980s saved NBA
- Elevated popularity and competitive drama

### Michael Jordan (1963–present)
- 6 NBA championships with Chicago Bulls
- Transcended basketball, global cultural icon
- Made basketball truly international phenomenon

### Stephen Curry (1988–present)
- Three-point revolution
- Changed spatial logic of basketball
- Led Golden State Warriors to multiple championships

### LeBron James (1984–present)
- 4 NBA championships with 3 different teams
- All-time leading scorer
- Sustained excellence across 20+ seasons

## Evolution of Rules

### Major Rule Changes
- **Dribbling**: Not in original rules, evolved naturally
- **Peach baskets → Iron hoops with nets**: Early 1900s
- **24-second shot clock**: 1954 (revolutionized pace)
- **Three-point line**: ABA 1967, NBA 1979
- **Zone defense restrictions**: Various eras

## Research Notes

### Verification Status
All primary facts verified through Encyclopedia Britannica and Basketball Hall of Fame. Historical dates cross-referenced. Statistics from FIBA and NBA official sources.

### Original Rules Document
The original 13 rules document (2 typewritten pages) is housed at the University of Kansas. A copy sold at auction in 2010 for $4.3 million to Comcast founder David G. Williams.

### YMCA Role
The YMCA (Young Men's Christian Association) was crucial to basketball's global spread. YMCA missionaries and instructors brought the game to Europe, Asia, and South America within years of invention.

### 1936 Olympics
Basketball's Olympic debut took place outdoors on converted tennis courts in Berlin. Games were played in rain and mud. The U.S. defeated Canada 19-8 in the gold medal game. Dr. Naismith, age 75, was present to award medals.
