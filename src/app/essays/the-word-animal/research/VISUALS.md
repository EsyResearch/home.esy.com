## Era Treatment Guide

### Era 1: Ancient (350 BCE - 500 CE)
- **Color Grading**: Warm terracotta, aged bronze, weathered stone
- **Texture**: Marble grain, papyrus surface
- **Filter**: Sepia 30%, desaturate 15%, contrast boost
- **Typography**: Cinzel, Trajan-inspired capitals
- **Imagery Sources**: Roman sculpture, Greek vase painting, museum artifacts

### Era 2: Medieval (500 - 1500 CE)
- **Color Grading**: Vellum gold, manuscript vermillion, deep forest green
- **Texture**: Parchment, illuminated manuscript, tapestry
- **Filter**: Warm gold overlay, vignette heavy
- **Typography**: EB Garamond with initial caps, blackletter accents
- **Imagery Sources**: Bestiary illustrations, cathedral sculpture, manuscripts

### Era 3: Renaissance (1500 - 1700)
- **Color Grading**: Sepia, burnt umber, naturalist green
- **Texture**: Canvas grain, aged paper
- **Filter**: Chiaroscuro contrast, warm shadows
- **Typography**: Cormorant Garamond, humanist elegance
- **Imagery Sources**: Anatomical drawings, natural history plates, portraits

### Era 4: Enlightenment (1700 - 1859)
- **Color Grading**: Cream parchment, ink black, specimen blue
- **Texture**: Scientific paper, engraved plates
- **Filter**: High contrast, minimal color, sharp
- **Typography**: Playfair Display, Didot elegance
- **Imagery Sources**: Taxonomy plates, scientific illustrations, portraits

### Era 5: Modern (1859 - Present)
- **Color Grading**: Full color, clinical whites, laboratory tones
- **Texture**: Photography grain (Victorian to digital)
- **Filter**: Progressive—Victorian sepia to modern color
- **Typography**: Inter, scientific precision
- **Imagery Sources**: Photography, documentary footage, lab imagery

## Visual Parallax Layers

### Depth System
- **Layer 0 (Far Background)**: Solid color or subtle texture
- **Layer 1 (Background)**: Environmental context (speed: 0.2)
- **Layer 2 (Mid)**: Primary subject (speed: 0.5)
- **Layer 3 (Subject)**: Central figure/object (speed: 1.0)
- **Layer 4 (Foreground)**: Overlay elements (speed: 1.3)
- **Layer 5 (Ambient)**: Particles, grain, atmosphere

### Per-Era Parallax Notes
- **Ancient**: Stone layers, dust particles
- **Medieval**: Parchment layers, candlelight flicker
- **Renaissance**: Canvas depth, studio atmosphere
- **Enlightenment**: Paper layers, specimen depth
- **Modern**: Documentary depth, laboratory sterility

- **0-30%**: Darkness, single eye emerges
- **30-60%**: Iris expands, light enters
