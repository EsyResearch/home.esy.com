# Figures Profile: The Silicon Revolution

> 12 key figures profiled with verified data

## Era 1: Invention (1940s-1950s)

### John Bardeen (1908-1991)
**The Quiet Theorist**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era | 1940s |
| Affiliation | Bell Labs |
| Key Achievement | Co-invented point-contact transistor |
| Nobel Prize | Physics 1956 (transistor), Physics 1972 (superconductivity) |
| Notable | Only person to win two Nobel Prizes in Physics |

**Contributions:**
- Provided theoretical framework for semiconductor behavior
- Co-invented the point-contact transistor (1947)
- Later developed BCS theory of superconductivity

**Quote:** "Science is a field which grows continuously with ever expanding frontiers."
*Source: Nobel acceptance speech, 1956*

**Photography:** Available via Bell Labs Archives, Nobel Prize Archives

---

### Walter Brattain (1902-1987)
**The Experimentalist**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era | 1940s |
| Affiliation | Bell Labs |
| Key Achievement | Built first working transistor |
| Nobel Prize | Physics 1956 |

**Contributions:**
- Constructed the first point-contact transistor
- Experimental physicist who made theory practical
- Hands-on approach complemented Bardeen's theory

**Quote:** "I knew the transistor was important, but I had no idea it would lead to all this."
*Source: Bell Labs oral history*

**Photography:** Available via Bell Labs Archives

## Era 2: Silicon Valley Birth (1950s-1960s)

### Robert Noyce (1927-1990)
**The Mayor of Silicon Valley**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era | 1950s-1960s |
| Affiliation | Fairchild → Intel |
| Key Achievement | Planar IC, Intel co-founder |

**Contributions:**
- Co-invented the planar integrated circuit (1959)
- Co-founded Fairchild Semiconductor (Traitorous Eight)
- Co-founded Intel with Gordon Moore (1968)

**Quote:** "Don't be encumbered by history. Go off and do something wonderful."
*Source: Widely attributed*

**Legacy Note:** Charismatic leader who gave Silicon Valley its culture

**Photography:** Available via Computer History Museum, Intel Archives

---

### Gordon Moore (1929-2023)
**The Prophet of Progress**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era | 1960s |
| Affiliation | Fairchild → Intel |
| Key Achievement | Moore's Law |

**Contributions:**
- Articulated Moore's Law (Electronics Magazine, 1965)
- Co-founded Intel with Robert Noyce
- Guided industry roadmap for 50 years

**Quote:** "The complexity for minimum component costs has increased at a rate of roughly a factor of two per year."
*Source: Electronics Magazine, April 19, 1965 — PRIMARY SOURCE*

**Photography:** Available via Intel Archives

---

### Jack Kilby (1923-2005)
**The Other Inventor**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era | 1950s |
| Affiliation | Texas Instruments |
| Key Achievement | Invented integrated circuit |
| Nobel Prize | Physics 2000 |

**Contributions:**
- Demonstrated first integrated circuit (September 12, 1958)
- Parallel invention to Noyce's planar IC
- Received Nobel Prize (Noyce had died by 2000)

**Quote:** "What we didn't realize then was that the integrated circuit would reduce the cost of electronic functions by a factor of a million to one."
*Source: Nobel Lecture, December 2000 — PRIMARY SOURCE*

**Photography:** Available via Texas Instruments Archives, Nobel Prize Archives

## Era 4: Manufacturing Revolution (1980s-2000s)

### Morris Chang (1931-present)
**The Foundry Father**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Era | 1980s-present |
| Affiliation | TI → ITRI → TSMC |
| Key Achievement | Founded TSMC, invented foundry model |

**Contributions:**
- Founded TSMC (1987)
- Invented pure-play foundry business model
- Enabled fabless semiconductor industry

**Quote:** "I didn't start TSMC to compete with Intel. I started it to enable a whole new industry."
*Source: Business interviews*

**Also:** "Geopolitics is real. Technology is not just technology anymore."
*Source: 2022-2023 interviews*

**Legacy Note:** Born in China, MIT/Stanford educated, TI veteran; founded TSMC at 55

**Photography:** Available via TSMC corporate, business press
