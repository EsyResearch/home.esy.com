## Figure 1: Aristotle (Ἀριστοτέλης)
**Epithet**: The Father of Zoology
**Dates**: 384-322 BCE
**Era**: Ancient Greek

### Key Contributions
- First systematic classification of living things
- Coined ζῷον (zōion) for "animal" meaning "living being"
- Divided nature into three kingdoms: plants (grow), animals (feel), humans (reason)
- Wrote *Historia Animalium* cataloging over 500 animal species
- Defined animals by sensation and voluntary movement

### Defining Quotes
- "The soul is the cause and principle of the living body." — *De Anima* (On the Soul)
- "Animals have sensation; and this is what distinguishes them from plants." — *De Anima*, Book II
- "Nature does nothing in vain." — *Politics*, Book I

### Photography/Portrait
- Roman marble copy of Greek bronze, now in Louvre Museum
- Raphael's *School of Athens* (1509-1511) portrait
- Bust from Palazzo Altemps, Rome

## Figure 2: Pliny the Elder (Gaius Plinius Secundus)
**Epithet**: The Roman Encyclopedist
**Dates**: 23-79 CE
**Era**: Roman Imperial

### Key Contributions
- Wrote *Naturalis Historia* (Natural History), 37 volumes
- Cataloged all known animals in systematic fashion
- Used Latin "animal" extensively in scientific context
- First to describe many exotic species for Roman readers
- Died observing Mount Vesuvius eruption

### Defining Quotes
- "In comparing various animals with man, the quadrupeds come nearest to his form." — *Naturalis Historia*, Book VII
- "Nature has given to each animal the means of preservation." — *Naturalis Historia*, Book VIII

### Photography/Portrait
- 19th-century idealized engraving
- Medieval manuscript illuminations
- Como commemorative statue

### Verification Status: ✅ Verified

## Figure 3: Isidore of Seville (Isidorus Hispalensis)
**Epithet**: The Last Scholar of the Ancient World
**Dates**: c. 560-636 CE
**Era**: Visigothic/Early Medieval

### Key Contributions
- Wrote *Etymologiae* (Etymologies), the encyclopedia of the medieval world
- Provided definitive Latin etymology: "Animalia" from "anima" (breath, soul)
- Bridge between Roman knowledge and medieval understanding
- Influential on all subsequent medieval scholars

### Defining Quotes
- "Animals are so called because they are animated by life and moved by spirit (*anima*)." — *Etymologiae*, Book XII

### Photography/Portrait
- Medieval manuscript illuminations
- Cathedral of Seville statue
- 15th-century woodcuts

### Verification Status: ✅ Verified

## Figure 4: Thomas Aquinas
**Epithet**: The Angelic Doctor
**Dates**: 1225-1274 CE
**Era**: High Medieval

### Key Contributions
- Synthesized Aristotelian philosophy with Christian theology
- Developed hierarchy of souls: vegetative (plants), sensitive (animals), rational (humans)
- Argued animals have "sensitive souls" but not immortal souls
- Defined moral relationship between humans and animals

### Defining Quotes
- "The sensitive soul in animals is the substantial form, giving the animal its being." — *Summa Theologica*
- "Brute animals... use external things by natural instinct." — *Summa Theologica*, II-II, Q. 64

### Photography/Portrait
- Carlo Crivelli's *Saint Thomas Aquinas* (1476)
- Fra Angelico fresco
- Manuscript illuminations

### Verification Status: ✅ Verified

## Figure 5: Leonardo da Vinci
**Epithet**: The Universal Genius
**Dates**: 1452-1519 CE
**Era**: Renaissance

### Key Contributions
- Pioneered comparative anatomy between humans and animals
- Drew detailed studies of horses, birds, and anatomy
- Challenged strict human/animal divide through observation
- First systematic studies of animal locomotion and flight

### Defining Quotes
- "The smallest feline is a masterpiece." — attributed
- "Observe the goose's foot: it is a perfect paddle for swimming." — *Codex Leicester*

### Photography/Portrait
- Self-portrait in red chalk (c. 1512)
- Anatomical drawings as context
- Workshop portraits

## Figure 6: Carl Linnaeus (Carl von Linné)
**Epithet**: The Father of Taxonomy
**Dates**: 1707-1778 CE
**Era**: Enlightenment

### Key Contributions
- Created binomial nomenclature (Genus species)
- Established Kingdom Animalia in *Systema Naturae* (1735)
- Placed humans within Animalia as *Homo sapiens*
- Revolutionized biological classification
- Made "animal" a precise scientific term

### Defining Quotes
- "I demand of you, and of the whole world, that you show me a generic character by which to distinguish between Man and Ape." — *Systema Naturae*, 10th edition (1758)
- "Nature does not proceed by leaps." — *Philosophia Botanica*

### Photography/Portrait
- Portrait by Alexander Roslin (1775), Royal Swedish Academy
- Uppsala University portraits
- Commemoration medals and statues

### Verification Status: ✅ Verified

## Figure 7: Charles Darwin
**Epithet**: The Father of Evolution
**Dates**: 1809-1882 CE
**Era**: Victorian

### Key Contributions
- Proposed evolution by natural selection
- United all animals through common ancestry
- Transformed "animal" from category to family tree branch
- Demonstrated humans evolved from earlier animals
- Published *On the Origin of Species* (1859) and *The Descent of Man* (1871)

### Defining Quotes
- "We thus learn that man is descended from a hairy quadruped, furnished with a tail and pointed ears." — *The Descent of Man* (1871)
- "There is no fundamental difference between man and the higher mammals in their mental faculties." — *The Descent of Man*
- "Endless forms most beautiful and most wonderful have been, and are being, evolved." — *On the Origin of Species*

### Photography/Portrait
- Julia Margaret Cameron photograph (1868)
- George Richmond portrait (1840)
- John Collier portrait (1883), National Portrait Gallery

### Verification Status: ✅ Verified

## Figure 8: Thomas Henry Huxley
**Epithet**: Darwin's Bulldog
**Dates**: 1825-1895 CE
**Era**: Victorian

### Key Contributions
- Championed Darwin's theory publicly
- Argued for human-ape connection in famous 1860 debate
- Coined the term "agnostic"
- Promoted science education

### Defining Quotes
- "The question of questions for mankind... is the ascertainment of the place which Man occupies in nature." — *Evidence as to Man's Place in Nature* (1863)
- "I would rather be descended from an ape than from a man who misuses his gifts." — 1860 Oxford debate (paraphrased)

### Photography/Portrait
- Numerous Victorian photographs
- London portrait by John Collier

### Verification Status: ✅ Verified

## Figure 9: Jane Goodall
**Epithet**: The Chimpanzee's Champion
**Dates**: 1934-present
**Era**: Modern

### Key Contributions
- Revolutionized understanding of animal intelligence and emotion
- Named and recognized individual chimpanzees as persons
- Challenged strict human/animal divide through observation
- Advocated for animal rights and conservation

### Defining Quotes
- "The least I can do is speak out for those who cannot speak for themselves." 
- "What makes us human, I think, is an ability to ask questions, a consequence of our sophisticated spoken language."
- "In what terms should we think of these beings, nonhuman yet possessing so very many human-like characteristics?"

### Photography/Portrait
- National Geographic photographs (Michael Nichols)
- Hugo van Lawick documentary stills
- Contemporary portraits

### Verification Status: ✅ Verified

## Figure 10: Richard Dawkins
**Epithet**: The Gene's Voice
**Dates**: 1941-present
**Era**: Modern

### Key Contributions
- Popularized gene-centered view of evolution
- Coined "selfish gene" and "meme" concepts
- Argued against human exceptionalism
- Made evolutionary biology accessible to public

### Defining Quotes
- "We are survival machines – robot vehicles blindly programmed to preserve the selfish molecules known as genes." — *The Selfish Gene* (1976)
- "There is no reason why lineages of genes cannot run unchanged through many species." — *The Ancestor's Tale*

### Photography/Portrait
- Contemporary publicity photographs
- Documentary appearances

### Verification Status: ✅ Verified
