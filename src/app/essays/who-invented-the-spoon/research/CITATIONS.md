## Key Facts Supported

- 30,000 BCE: First spoons carved from seashells and bone (reindeer antler spoon dated to 17,000 BCE)
- 3,000 BCE: Egyptian ivory and silver spoons with hieroglyphic decorations
- 500 BCE: Greek and Roman bronze and silver spoons with cochlear handles for shellfish
- 1500s: English Apostle spoons as christening gifts, origin of "born with a silver spoon" idiom
- 1650s: Development of teaspoons for tea and coffee service
- 1840: Electroplating process makes silver-plated spoons affordable for all classes
- Modern production: 5 billion spoons manufactured annually

## Historical Context

- Medieval travelers carried personal spoons in belt pouches
- Egyptian cosmetic spoons featured decorative handles (swimming maidens, lotus flowers)
- Shakespeare reference: "Come, come, my lord, you'd spare your spoons" (Henry VIII, Archbishop Cranmer)
- Tudor England tradition: wealthy godparents gifted newborns silver Apostle spoons (set of 12 disciples)
- Sheffield and Birmingham factories democratized silverware through electroplating
