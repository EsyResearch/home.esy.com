# Figures — The Bitter History of Chocolate

> Key historical figures for the visual essay, with verified biographical data, quotable statements, and imagery availability.

## F1: Montezuma II (Moctezuma Xocoyotzin)
- **Dates**: c. 1466–1520
- **Role**: Ninth Aztec emperor; embodied the sacred role of cacao in Mesoamerican royal culture
- **Key Facts**:
  - Reportedly consumed 50 cups of chocolate daily from golden goblets (recorded by Cortés and Bernal Díaz)
  - Used cacao beans as currency throughout the Aztec empire
  - Chocolate was reserved for nobility, warriors, and ceremonial use
- **Quotable**: Described by Bernal Díaz del Castillo: "From time to time they brought him some cups of fine gold, with a certain drink made of cacao... he drank about fifty great cups of it" (*The True History of the Conquest of New Spain*, 1568)
- **Imagery**: Codex Mendoza illustrations, colonial-era portraits, Aztec codex depictions — **High availability** (Wikimedia Commons, British Museum)
- **Sources**: S1 (Coe & Coe), S7 (Cortés Letters), S11 (Florentine Codex)

## F2: Hernán Cortés
- **Dates**: 1485–1547
- **Role**: Spanish conquistador who brought cacao to Europe
- **Key Facts**:
  - First European to document cacao at Montezuma's court (1519)
  - Described cacao as "a divine drink which builds up resistance and fights fatigue"
  - Shipped cacao beans to Spain in 1528, sparking European chocolate culture
- **Quotable**: "A cup of this precious drink permits a man to walk for a whole day without food" (attributed, from letters to Charles V)
- **Imagery**: Multiple portraits and engravings — **High availability** (Prado, Wikimedia Commons)
- **Sources**: S7 (Letters from Mexico), S1 (Coe & Coe)

## F3: Conrad van Houten
- **Dates**: 1801–1887
- **Role**: Dutch chemist who invented the cocoa press (1828)
- **Key Facts**:
  - Invented hydraulic cocoa press that separated cocoa butter from cocoa solids
  - Made chocolate cheaper, smoother, and drinkable by the masses
  - His "Dutch process" (alkalizing cocoa) remains standard today
  - Single most important technological innovation in chocolate's democratization
- **Quotable**: His invention has been called "the single most important step in making chocolate available to the masses" (paraphrase from S4, Cadbury)
- **Imagery**: Period portraits — **Medium availability** (Dutch archives, Wikimedia)
- **Sources**: S4 (Cadbury), S1 (Coe & Coe)

## F4: Joseph Fry
- **Dates**: 1795–1879
- **Role**: Created the first modern chocolate bar (1847)
- **Key Facts**:
  - J.S. Fry & Sons of Bristol combined cocoa powder, sugar, and cocoa butter into a moldable paste
  - First "eating chocolate" — previously chocolate was only a drink
  - The Fry family were Quakers, part of the social reformist chocolate tradition
- **Quotable**: Company advertisement: "Fry's Chocolate Cream — the world's first chocolate bar" (1866 marketing)
- **Imagery**: Company historical materials, Victorian-era photographs — **Medium availability**
- **Sources**: S4 (Cadbury), S1 (Coe & Coe)

## F5: Rudolf Lindt
- **Dates**: 1855–1909
- **Role**: Swiss chocolatier who invented conching (1879)
- **Key Facts**:
  - Accidentally left a mixing machine running overnight, discovering the conching process
  - Conching transforms gritty chocolate into smooth, melt-in-the-mouth texture
  - Created the modern sensory experience of chocolate
  - Founded Lindt & Sprüngli, still operating today
- **Quotable**: The accidental overnight conching discovery is chocolate's greatest serendipity — "When he returned, he found that the chocolate had been transformed" (paraphrase from S4)
- **Imagery**: Studio portraits, Lindt company archives — **Medium availability**
- **Sources**: S4 (Cadbury), S1 (Coe & Coe)

## F6: Milton S. Hershey
- **Dates**: 1857–1945
- **Role**: Mass-produced chocolate for America, built a company town
- **Key Facts**:
  - Founded Hershey Chocolate Company (1894), created the Hershey bar
  - Built Hershey, Pennsylvania — a planned company town with streets named Chocolate and Cocoa
  - Established the Milton Hershey School for orphaned children
  - Made chocolate affordable for working-class Americans
- **Quotable**: "Give them quality. That's the best kind of advertising in the world." — Milton Hershey
- **Imagery**: Extensive photographic record — **High availability** (Library of Congress, Hershey Community Archives)
- **Sources**: S4 (Cadbury), S10 (Fraser & Rimas)

## F7: Modern Cocoa Farmers (Composite Figure)
- **Dates**: Contemporary (2000s–present)
- **Role**: The human cost of the $130B industry — smallholder farmers earning below poverty line
- **Key Facts**:
  - Average cocoa farmer in Côte d'Ivoire earns ~$0.78/day (Cocoa Barometer 2022)
  - 1.56 million children engaged in hazardous labor in cocoa in Côte d'Ivoire and Ghana (NORC 2020)
  - Farmers typically earn 6% of the final chocolate bar price
  - Most cocoa farmers have never tasted chocolate
- **Quotable**: "I didn't even know that cocoa was used to make chocolate until I was told." — Ivorian cocoa farmer (documented in S3, Off)
- **Imagery**: Photojournalism from West Africa — **High availability** (Reuters, AP, NGO documentation)
- **Sources**: S3 (Off), S8 (Cocoa Barometer), S9 (NORC)
