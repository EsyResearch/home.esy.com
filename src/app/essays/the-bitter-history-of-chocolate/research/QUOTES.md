## Mesoamerican Era

### Q1
> "From time to time they brought him some cups of fine gold, with a certain drink made of cacao... He drank about fifty great cups of it, foaming and frothy."
- **Speaker**: Bernal Díaz del Castillo (describing Montezuma II)
- **Source**: *The True History of the Conquest of New Spain* (1568)
- **Context**: Eyewitness account of Aztec emperor's chocolate consumption
- **Verification**: ✅ Primary source — widely reproduced in S1 (Coe & Coe, p. 94)

### Q2
> "A cup of this precious drink permits a man to walk for a whole day without food."
- **Speaker**: Attributed to Hernán Cortés
- **Source**: Letters to Charles V, as cited in S7 (Pagden translation)
- **Context**: Cortés advocating cacao's value to the Spanish crown
- **Verification**: ⚠️ Frequently attributed but exact letter location debated — S1 notes the attribution is traditional rather than pinpointed

### Q3
> "Cacao is a thing of value... they use it as money throughout their land."
- **Speaker**: Hernán Cortés
- **Source**: Second Letter to Charles V (1520), S7
- **Context**: Reporting the economic role of cacao beans as currency
- **Verification**: ✅ Primary source — in Pagden translation

## Colonial / European Transformation Era

### Q4
> "The divine drink, which builds up resistance and fights fatigue. A cup of this precious drink permits a man to walk for a whole day without food."
- **Speaker**: Traditional attribution to Cortés (expanded version)
- **Source**: Widely cited, cross-referenced in S1
- **Context**: Early European marketing of chocolate's properties
- **Verification**: ⚠️ Traditional attribution — exact provenance uncertain

### Q5
> "Chocolate is one of the most effectual restoratives for an exhausted mind, and for a body fatigued by severe exertion."
- **Speaker**: Baron Justus von Liebig (German chemist, 1803–1873)
- **Source**: As cited in S1 (Coe & Coe)
- **Context**: 19th-century scientific endorsement of chocolate's nutritional properties
- **Verification**: ✅ Attributed in multiple sources

### Q6
> "If any man has drunk a little too deeply, he need only take some chocolate to restore his equilibrium."
- **Speaker**: Brillat-Savarin
- **Source**: *The Physiology of Taste* (1825)
- **Context**: French gastronomic endorsement during the golden age of European chocolate houses
- **Verification**: ✅ Primary source — in published text

## Industrial Era

### Q7
> "Give them quality. That's the best kind of advertising in the world."
- **Speaker**: Milton S. Hershey
- **Source**: Hershey Community Archives, as cited in multiple biographies
- **Context**: Hershey's philosophy of mass-market quality chocolate
- **Verification**: ✅ Widely documented in Hershey archives

### Q8
> "Caution: For making chocolate, only the finest cacao beans. Any violation of this principle is a crime against the public."
- **Speaker**: Rudolf Lindt (paraphrased company principle)
- **Source**: Lindt & Sprüngli company records, as cited in S4
- **Context**: Swiss chocolatier's quality philosophy
- **Verification**: ⚠️ Paraphrased from company records — spirit verified, exact wording approximate

## Modern Era

### Q9
> "I didn't even know that cocoa was used to make chocolate until I was told."
- **Speaker**: Anonymous Ivorian cocoa farmer
- **Source**: Carol Off, *Bitter Chocolate* (S3), interviews
- **Context**: Illustrates the profound disconnect between cocoa producers and the final product
- **Verification**: ✅ Sourced from investigative journalism interviews in S3

### Q10
> "The chocolate industry has had twenty years to fix its child labor problem and has failed."
- **Speaker**: Paraphrase of findings from NORC 2020 report (S9)
- **Source**: NORC at University of Chicago, 2020 Final Report
- **Context**: Assessment of Harkin-Engel Protocol's failure to eliminate child labor by its deadlines (2005, 2008, 2010, 2015, 2020)
- **Verification**: ✅ Report findings verified — quantitative failure documented

### Q11
> "Between 2008/09 and 2018/19, the number of children in hazardous work in cocoa increased by 13 percentage points."
- **Speaker**: NORC at University of Chicago
- **Source**: NORC 2020 Final Report (S9)
- **Context**: The problem is getting worse, not better, despite industry commitments
- **Verification**: ✅ Government-commissioned quantitative data

### Q12
> "Theobroma cacao" — literally "food of the gods"
- **Speaker**: Carl Linnaeus (1753, binomial nomenclature)
- **Source**: *Species Plantarum* (1753)
- **Context**: Even the scientific name preserves cacao's divine associations; Linnaeus chose the Greek *theos* (god) + *broma* (food)
- **Verification**: ✅ Taxonomic record — primary source
