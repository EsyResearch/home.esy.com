# Prose Quality Audit: Seven Million Years

## Voice Consistency (8.5/10)

The essay maintains a consistent editorial voice throughout: authoritative but accessible, scientific but not clinical. The tone is that of a knowledgeable guide walking you through a museum. Transitions between eras feel natural. The voice never becomes too casual or too academic.

**Strengths:**
- Consistent use of specific dates, specimen names, and measurements throughout
- The narrative maintains emotional engagement without becoming sentimental
- Scientific terminology is introduced naturally without condescension

**Minor issues:**
- None detected that would require revision

## Transition Quality (7.5/10)

**Strengths:**
- Era transitions are handled through the badge system, providing clear temporal orientation
- The narrative thread (from many species to one) is clear and maintained throughout

**Areas for improvement:**
- Some section transitions could be stronger — the shift from "The Coexistence" to "Why We Survived" is slightly abrupt
- The epilogue transition works well emotionally

## Specificity (8.5/10)

The essay scores highly on specificity:
- Named specimens: Toumai, Ardi, Lucy, Turkana Boy, Dmanisi Skull 5
- Specific dates with uncertainty noted: "approximately 7 million years ago," "~315,000 years ago"
- Quantified data: cranial capacities in cc, heights in cm, population percentages
- Named researchers: Brunet, Johanson, White, Berger, Pääbo, Stringer

## Rhythm and Pacing (7.5/10)

**Strengths:**
- The hero section is punchy: "Seven million years. That is how long this story has been running."
- Data sections break up long prose passages effectively
- Quote monuments provide breathing room

**Areas for improvement:**
- Some prose sections could benefit from more sentence length variation
- A few paragraphs in the middle sections run slightly long
