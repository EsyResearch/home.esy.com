## Source 1: Brunet et al. 2002 — Sahelanthropus tchadensis Discovery
- **Citation:** Brunet, M., Guy, F., Pilbeam, D., et al. (2002). "A new hominid from the Upper Miocene of Chad, Central Africa." *Nature*, 418(6894), 145–151.
- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1038/nature00879
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** Sahelanthropus tchadensis description, earliest known hominin candidate, dating (~6–7 MYA), cranial morphology (TM 266-01-060-1, "Toumaï"), debate over hominin vs. ape status
- **Key Data:** Cranial capacity ~320–380 cc; foramen magnum position suggesting upright posture; found in Djurab Desert, Chad

## Source 2: White et al. 2009 — Ardipithecus ramidus
- **Citation:** White, T.D., Asfaw, B., Beyene, Y., et al. (2009). "Ardipithecus ramidus and the Paleobiology of Early Hominids." *Science*, 326(5949), 64–86.
- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1126/science.1175802
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** Ardipithecus ramidus description, 4.4 MYA dating, "Ardi" skeleton (ARA-VP-6/500), facultative bipedalism with arboreal capability, implications for last common ancestor morphology
- **Key Data:** ~50 kg body mass, ~300–350 cc cranial capacity, divergent great toe, woodland habitat

## Source 3: Johanson & White 1979 — Australopithecus afarensis ("Lucy")
- **Citation:** Johanson, D.C. & White, T.D. (1979). "A systematic assessment of early African hominids." *Science*, 203(4378), 321–330.
- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1126/science.104384
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** A. afarensis taxonomy and description, AL 288-1 ("Lucy") specimen, bipedalism evidence, 3.2 MYA dating, East African Rift context
- **Key Data:** ~1.0–1.5 m height, ~375–500 cc cranial capacity, 3.9–2.9 MYA range

## Source 4: Leakey et al. 2001 — Kenyanthropus platyops

- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1038/35068500
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** Evidence of hominin diversity in the Pliocene, flat-faced morphology, 3.5 MYA dating, challenges to linear evolution models

## Source 5: Berger et al. 2015 — Homo naledi
- **Citation:** Berger, L.R., Hawks, J., de Ruiter, D.J., et al. (2015). "Homo naledi, a new species of the genus Homo from the Dinaledi Chamber, South Africa." *eLife*, 4, e09560.
- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.7554/eLife.09560
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** H. naledi description, mosaic morphology (small brain + human-like hands/feet), Rising Star Cave system, possible deliberate body disposal
- **Key Data:** ~465–560 cc cranial capacity, ~1.44–1.47 m height, dated to 236–335 kya (Dirks et al. 2017)

## Source 6: Brown et al. 2004 — Homo floresiensis
- **Citation:** Brown, P., Sutikna, T., Morwood, M.J., et al. (2004). "A new small-bodied hominin from the Late Pleistocene of Flores, Indonesia." *Nature*, 431(7012), 1055–1061.
- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1038/nature02999
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** H. floresiensis ("the Hobbit") description, island dwarfism hypothesis, Liang Bua cave, 60–100 kya dating, stone tool association
- **Key Data:** ~1.0 m height, ~380 cc cranial capacity, ~25 kg body mass

## Source 7: Hublin et al. 2017 — Earliest Homo sapiens (Jebel Irhoud)
- **Citation:** Hublin, J.-J., Ben-Ncer, A., Bailey, S.E., et al. (2017). "New fossils from Jebel Irhoud, Morocco and the pan-African origin of Homo sapiens." *Nature*, 546(7657), 289–292.
- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1038/nature22336
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** Pushing back H. sapiens origins to ~300,000 years ago, pan-African origin model (not just East Africa), Jebel Irhoud fossils with modern face but archaic braincase
- **Key Data:** ~315 ± 34 kya dating, Middle Stone Age tools associated

## Source 8: Green et al. 2010 — Neanderthal Genome
- **Citation:** Green, R.E., Krause, J., Briggs, A.W., et al. (2010). "A Draft Sequence of the Neandertal Genome." *Science*, 328(5979), 710–722.
- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1126/science.1188021
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** Neanderthal-sapiens interbreeding evidence, 1–4% Neanderthal DNA in non-African modern humans, replacement vs. assimilation debate, divergence time ~400–800 kya
- **Key Data:** ~3.2 billion nucleotides sequenced from three Vindija Cave specimens

## Source 9: Reich et al. 2010 — Denisovan Genome
- **Citation:** Reich, D., Green, R.E., Kircher, M., et al. (2010). "Genetic history of an archaic hominin group from Denisova Cave in Siberia." *Nature*, 468(7327), 1053–1060.
- **Type:** Primary research (peer-reviewed journal article)
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1038/nature09710
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** Denisovan discovery and description (from DNA, not morphology), interbreeding with sapiens, ~4–6% Denisovan DNA in Melanesian populations, separate lineage from Neanderthals
- **Key Data:** Genome sequenced from finger bone fragment, Denisova Cave, Altai Mountains, Siberia

## Source 10: Stringer, C. 2012 — Lone Survivors
- **Citation:** Stringer, C. (2012). *Lone Survivors: How We Came to Be the Only Humans on Earth*. New York: Times Books/Henry Holt.
- **Type:** Secondary (scholarly monograph)
- **Tier:** 1
- **ISBN:** 978-0-8050-8891-5

- **Key Themes:** Multiple dispersals out of Africa, mosaic evolution, population size advantages of sapiens

## Source 14: Potts, R. 1996 — Variability Selection
- **Citation:** Potts, R. (1996). "Evolution and Climate Variability." *Science*, 273(5277), 922–923. See also: Potts, R. (1998). "Variability Selection in Hominid Evolution." *Evolutionary Anthropology*, 7(3), 81–96.
- **Type:** Primary research / theoretical framework
- **Tier:** 1
- **DOI:** 10.1002/(SICI)1520-6505(1998)7:3<81::AID-EVAN3>3.0.CO;2-A
- **Verification Status:** ✅ Verified
- **Used For:** Variability selection hypothesis (adaptability to fluctuating environments, not stable ones, drove hominin evolution), alternative to savanna hypothesis, explains generalist strategy of Homo
- **Key Data:** Correlation between climate variability amplitude and major evolutionary transitions

## Source 18: Harmand et al. 2015 — Lomekwian Tools (3.3 MYA)
