**Requirements**:
- [ ] Major events chronologically mapped

**Potential Timeline Scope** (scope-dependent):

#### Technological Evolution
- **3000 BCE** — Bronze Age swords (Mesopotamia, Egypt)
- **1200 BCE** — Iron Age transition
- **500 BCE** — Steel production techniques (India, China)
- **200 BCE** — Pattern welding development (Celtic, Germanic)

- **1000 CE** — European longsword emergence
- **1300 CE** — Damascus steel peak
- **1500 CE** — Rapier development
- **1600 CE** — Japanese katana refinement (Edo period)
- **1700 CE** — Decline as primary weapon (gunpowder)
- **1800 CE** — Ceremonial/symbolic transition
- **1900 CE** — Modern martial arts revival

#### Cultural Milestones
- **Legendary Swords** — Excalibur, Kusanagi, Durandal, Zulfiqar
- **Coronation Swords** — British Crown Jewels, Japanese Imperial Regalia
- **Dueling Culture** — European rapier duels, Japanese samurai code
- **Military Transitions** — Cavalry sabers, infantry swords, naval cutlasses
- **Prohibition Eras** — Sword bans (Japan post-WWII, various European periods)
- **Modern Revival** — Olympic fencing, Japanese sword arts (iaido, kendo)

### 5. Era Treatments (Layer 5)

**Requirements**:
- [ ] Historical periods requiring distinct visual processing
- [ ] Era-appropriate aesthetic defined for each period
- [ ] Visual consistency within eras

**Potential Era Distinctions**:

#### Bronze Age (3000-1200 BCE)
- **Visual Treatment**: Sepia/ochre tones, archaeological aesthetic
- **Characteristics**: Weathered, ancient, excavated artifacts
- **Sources**: Museum photography of bronze swords

#### Iron Age (1200 BCE-500 CE)
- **Visual Treatment**: Darker tones, rougher textures
- **Characteristics**: Transition period, less refined
- **Sources**: Archaeological finds, early historical art

#### Medieval Period (500-1500 CE)
- **Visual Treatment**: Manuscript illumination style, rich colors
- **Characteristics**: Chivalric aesthetic, heraldry
- **Sources**: Illuminated manuscripts, Gothic art

#### Renaissance (1400-1600 CE)
- **Visual Treatment**: Refined, artistic, detailed
- **Characteristics**: Dueling culture, craftsmanship peak
- **Sources**: Renaissance art, technical treatises

#### Early Modern (1600-1800 CE)
- **Visual Treatment**: Formal, ceremonial
- **Characteristics**: Military standardization, decline as weapon
- **Sources**: Military portraits, formal documentation

#### Modern Era (1800-Present)
- **Visual Treatment**: Archival photography, museum documentation
- **Characteristics**: Preservation, revival, ceremonial use
- **Sources**: Black & white photography, modern museum collections

## Research Constraints

### Must Avoid
- **Romanticizing Violence** — Focus on cultural/technical aspects, not glorification
- **Cultural Appropriation** — Respect traditions, avoid superficial treatment
- **Mythology as Fact** — Clearly distinguish legend from verified history
- **Eurocentric Bias** — Give equal weight to non-European traditions
- **Unsourced Claims** — Every factual statement must trace to research package

### Source Quality Standards
- **Tier 1 Priority**: Primary sources (historical texts, museum collections, manuscripts)
- **Tier 2 Acceptable**: Peer-reviewed scholarship, academic publications
- **Tier 3 Use Sparingly**: General reference works, encyclopedias
- **Tier 4 Prohibited**: Unreliable websites, unsourced claims, pop culture references
