## Key Figures Referenced

### J. Robert Oppenheimer (1904–1967)
- Scientific Director of Los Alamos Laboratory
- Led theoretical physics team
- Quote: "Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds" (Bhagavad Gita)
- Security clearance revoked 1954

### General Leslie Groves (1896–1970)
- Military Director of Manhattan Project
- Oversaw all project sites and security
- Selected Oppenheimer as scientific director

### Enrico Fermi (1901–1954)
- Nobel Prize 1938
- Created first controlled nuclear chain reaction (December 2, 1942)
- Key advisor on weapon design

### Leo Szilard (1898–1964)
- Conceived nuclear chain reaction (1933)
- Einstein-Szilard letter to FDR (1939)
- Later opposed use of bomb against Japan

### Niels Bohr (1885–1962)
- Nobel Prize 1922
- Advisor to Manhattan Project
- Advocated for international control of atomic energy

### Richard Feynman (1918–1988)
- Theoretical physicist at Los Alamos
- Worked on calculations for bomb design
- Later Nobel Prize 1965

### Harry S. Truman (1884–1972)
- President who authorized use of atomic bombs
- Became President April 12, 1945 (unaware of Manhattan Project)
- Decision made July 1945

## Key Events & Locations

### Trinity Test (July 16, 1945)
- **Location**: Alamogordo, New Mexico
- **Yield**: ~22 kilotons TNT equivalent
- **Time**: 5:29:45 AM MST
- **Significance**: First nuclear detonation in history

### Hiroshima (August 6, 1945)
- **Bomb**: "Little Boy" (uranium)
- **Time**: 8:15 AM local time
- **Yield**: ~15 kilotons
- **Casualties**: ~140,000 by end of 1945

### Nagasaki (August 9, 1945)
- **Bomb**: "Fat Man" (plutonium)
- **Time**: 11:02 AM local time
- **Yield**: ~21 kilotons
- **Casualties**: ~70,000 by end of 1945

### Project Sites
- **Oak Ridge, Tennessee**: Uranium enrichment
- **Hanford, Washington**: Plutonium production
- **Los Alamos, New Mexico**: Weapon design and assembly
- **University of Chicago**: Fermi's first reactor

## Historical Timeline

- August 2, 1939: Einstein-Szilard letter warns FDR about German atomic research
- December 2, 1942: Fermi achieves first controlled nuclear chain reaction
- 1943: Los Alamos Laboratory established, Oppenheimer appointed director
- July 16, 1945: Trinity test — first nuclear explosion
- August 6, 1945: Hiroshima bombed
- August 9, 1945: Nagasaki bombed
- August 15, 1945: Japan announces surrender
- September 2, 1945: Japan signs formal surrender
