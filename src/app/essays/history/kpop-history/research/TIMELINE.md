## PRE-K-POP ERA (1926-1991)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1926 | Yun Sim-deok records "In Praise of Death" | First Korean popular recording; sold 100,000+ copies |
| 1935 | Lee Nan-young releases "Tears of Mokpo" | Iconic trot song; sold 50,000 copies |
| 1935 | Jeogori Sisters formed | Korea's first girl group |
| 1945 | Korea liberated from Japan | Post-WWII music transformation begins |
| 1950-1953 | Korean War | Devastated music industry |
| 1955 | 8th US Army Show launches | American military entertainment shapes Korean music |
| 1957 | AFKN launches | American Forces Korea Network transforms Korean soundscape |
| 1959 | Kim Sisters arrive in Las Vegas | First Korean act to achieve US success |
| December 27, 1959 | Kim Sisters debut on Ed Sullivan Show | First of 22 appearances |
| October 1, 1961 | KBS 1TV established | Korean television era begins |
| December 2, 1961 | MBC established | Commercial broadcasting begins |
| 1962 | Shin Joong-hyun forms Add4 | Korea's first rock band |
| 1962 | "Charlie Brown" by Kim Sisters reaches #7 Billboard | First Korean Billboard success |
| 1968 | Pearl Sisters release "My Dear" | First million-selling album in Korean pop history |
| 1970 | Kim Min-ki writes "Morning Dew" | Birth of Korean protest music |
| 1972 | Kim Min-ki arrested for protest songs | Yusin Period censorship intensifies |
| 1975 | Emergency Measure Number 9 enacted | "Purification of Popular Music" censorship |
| 1975 | Shin Joong-hyun arrested | Imprisoned for refusing to praise president |
| 1976 | Cho Yong-pil releases "Come Back to Busan Port" | "King of Korean Pop" emerges |
| 1977 | Sanulrim debuts | New wave of Korean rock |
| 1980 | Cho Yong-pil performs at Carnegie Hall | First Korean singer at Carnegie Hall |
| 1980 | Chun Doo-hwan implements "3S Policy" | Cultural control through entertainment |
| 1984 | Lee Sun-hee debuts | Establishes vocal-driven ballad era |
| 1985 | Deulgukhwa releases "March" | Underground rock challenges mainstream |
| 1986 | Kim Wan-sun debuts | "Korean Madonna" pioneers dance-pop |
| June 1987 | June Democratic Uprising | End of authoritarian music control |
| 1987 | Yoo Jae-ha releases "Because I Love You" | "Father of Korean Ballads" |
| 1988 | Seoul Olympics | Korea's cultural coming-out party |
| 1988 | Sobangcha releases "Last Night Story" | One of Korea's earliest idol groups |
