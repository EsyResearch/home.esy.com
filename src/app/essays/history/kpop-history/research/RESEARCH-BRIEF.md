## Primary Research Questions

### Definitional Questions
1. **What is "K-Pop," historically?** When does the term emerge, how does its meaning change, who uses it, and why is it contested?
2. **What came before K-Pop?** What Korean popular music lineages and outside influences shaped modern K-Pop?
3. **How do we periodize K-Pop?** What defines each generation, and what changed between them?

### Industrial Questions
4. **How did the idol training/agency model form and evolve?** What is the trainee system, how does it work, and at what human cost?
5. **What is the economic structure of K-Pop?** Where does revenue come from, how is it distributed, and how has this changed?
6. **How do the major agencies differ?** SM, JYP, YG, HYBE — what are their distinct philosophies and business models?

### Cultural Questions
7. **How do fandom systems operate?** As culture, as labor, as power — what distinguishes K-Pop fandoms?
8. **How does K-Pop construct gender and identity?** How are femininity/masculinity engineered and contested?
9. **What is the role of "concept" in K-Pop?** How does the comeback cycle work, and why does aesthetic matter so much?

### Global Questions
10. **How did K-Pop become global (Hallyu)?** What strategies drove international expansion, and what did K-Pop become in translation?
11. **How did technology transform K-Pop?** From TV broadcasts to TikTok — how did distribution shape the form?
12. **What myths persist about K-Pop?** "Overnight success," "only for teens," "all manufactured" — what does evidence show instead?

### Critical Questions
13. **What are the documented costs?** Mental health, labor conditions, privacy, harassment — handled with evidence and care.
14. **What controversies have shaped the industry?** Contract disputes, scandals, cultural appropriation debates — contextualized.
15. **What is K-Pop becoming?** Current state and evidence-based future trajectories.
