# Scholarly Citations: Key Books on Cambodia and the Khmer Rouge Genocide

## 1. Ben Kiernan - "The Pol Pot Regime"

**Kiernan, Ben.** *The Pol Pot Regime: Race, Power, and Genocide in Cambodia under the Khmer Rouge, 1975-79.* New Haven: Yale University Press, 1996. Third Edition, 2008.

**ISBN**: 978-0-300-14434-5

**Author**: Whitney Griswold Professor Emeritus of History at Yale; founded Cambodian Genocide Program (1994) and Genocide Studies Program (1998); interviewed 500+ survivors

**Key Arguments**:
- Race and ethnic persecution were fundamental to Khmer Rouge ideology
- Documents ~1.7-2 million deaths
- Regional variation in violence across different zones

**Source Tier**: Tier 1 - Yale University Press

---

## 2. David P. Chandler - Two Key Works

### 2a. "Brother Number One"
**Chandler, David P.** *Brother Number One: A Political Biography of Pol Pot.* Westview Press, 1992. Revised 1999.

**ISBN**: 978-0-8133-3510-0

**Key Contribution**: First book-length biography of Pol Pot

### 2b. "Voices from S-21"
**Chandler, David P.** *Voices from S-21: Terror and History in Pol Pot's Secret Prison.* University of California Press, 2000.

**ISBN**: 978-0-520-22247-2

**Key Contribution**: Primary source analysis of S-21 documents; over 14,000 "enemies" processed, fewer than 12 survived

**Author**: Professor Emeritus, Monash University; former US Foreign Service Officer in Cambodia

**Source Tier**: Tier 1 - UC Press / Westview Press

---

## 3. Elizabeth Becker - "When the War Was Over"

**Becker, Elizabeth.** *When the War Was Over: Cambodia and the Khmer Rouge Revolution.* PublicAffairs, 1998.

**ISBN**: 978-1-891620-00-3

**Author**: One of only two Western journalists to interview Pol Pot while in power (December 1978)

**Key Contribution**: Eyewitness observations from inside Democratic Kampuchea; audio recordings of Pol Pot used as ECCC evidence

**Source Tier**: Tier 1 - Authoritative journalism with primary sources

---

## 4. Philip Short - "Pol Pot: Anatomy of a Nightmare"

**Short, Philip.** *Pol Pot: Anatomy of a Nightmare.* Henry Holt, 2005.

**ISBN**: 0-8050-6662-4

**Author**: 25-year BBC foreign correspondent; previously wrote *Mao: A Life*

**Key Contribution**: First-person interviews with Khieu Samphan and Ieng Sary; accessed Chinese/Russian archives

**Source Tier**: Tier 1 - Authoritative biography

---

## 5. Alexander Laban Hinton - Two Key Works

### 5a. "Why Did They Kill?"
**Hinton, Alexander Laban.** *Why Did They Kill?: Cambodia in the Shadow of Genocide.* UC Press, 2005.

**ISBN**: 978-0-520-24179-4

**Key Contribution**: First anthropological analysis of genocide origins; examines perpetrator psychology

### 5b. "Man or Monster?"
**Hinton, Alexander Laban.** *Man or Monster?: The Trial of a Khmer Rouge Torturer.* Duke University Press, 2016.

**ISBN**: 978-0-8223-6273-9

**Key Contribution**: Ethnographic study of Duch trial; adapts "banality of evil" concept

**Author**: Founding Director, Center for the Study of Genocide and Human Rights, Rutgers University

**Source Tier**: Tier 1 - UC Press / Duke University Press

---

## 6. Craig Etcheson - "After the Killing Fields"

**Etcheson, Craig.** *After the Killing Fields: Lessons from the Cambodian Genocide.* Praeger, 2005.

**ISBN**: 978-0-275-98513-4

**Author**: Principal founder of DC-Cam; former Program Manager at Yale Cambodian Genocide Program

**Key Contributions**:
- Revised death toll: ~2.2 million (higher than commonly cited)
- Documents Mass Grave Mapping Project
- Proves senior leadership directed killings

**Source Tier**: Tier 1 - Praeger / Texas Tech University Press
