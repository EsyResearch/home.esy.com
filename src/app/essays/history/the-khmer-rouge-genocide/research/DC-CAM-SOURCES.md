# Documentation Center of Cambodia (DC-Cam) Sources

## Overview

The Documentation Center of Cambodia (DC-Cam) is a Cambodian NGO whose mission is to research and record the era of Democratic Kampuchea (April 17, 1975 - January 7, 1979). Founded in 1995 as an adjunct of Yale University, it became independent in 1997.

**Recognition**: 2018 Ramon Magsaysay Award ("Asia's Nobel Prize")

## Source 2: Genocide Education Program

**URL**: https://www.dccam.org/homepage/education/teacher-training/
**Source Tier**: Tier 1

**Key Statistics**:
- **5,000+ teachers** trained since 2009
- Reaches **1 million students** in grades 9-12
- **7 million+ young Cambodians** have learned DK history since program began
- Training approved by Ministry of Education (MOEYS)

**Curriculum Materials**:
1. **"A History of Democratic Kampuchea (1975-1979)"** - Textbook (2007, 2nd ed 2020)
2. **Teacher's Guidebook** - Methodology guide (2009, 2nd ed 2014)
3. **Student Workbook** - Companion materials

**Mandate**: Since October 2009, all first-year university students required to study DK history

## Source 4: Survivor Testimony

**URL**: https://www.dccam.org/homepage/justice/voices-from-the-khmer-rouge-tribunal/
**Source Tier**: Tier 1

**Key Resources**:
- Interviews with hundreds of victims, survivors, and Khmer Rouge cadres
- **"Walls of S-21"** - Film featuring survivors Bou Meng and Chum Mei
- **"Searching for the Truth"** magazine - Monthly since January 2000
  - 500,000+ copies distributed in Khmer (2000-2005)
  - 7,000 copies/month; 80%+ free distribution in Cambodia

## Source 5: DC-Cam Role in ECCC

**URL**: https://www.dccam.org/homepage/justice/
**Source Tier**: Tier 1

**Evidence Provided**:
- **500,000+ pages** of documents to ECCC
- Supplied to all court sections (prosecutors, judges, defense, civil parties)

**ECCC Outcomes Supported**:
- Duch conviction (2010/2012)
- Nuon Chea and Khieu Samphan convictions (2014/2018)
- **First genocide judgments by an internationalized court in Asia**
