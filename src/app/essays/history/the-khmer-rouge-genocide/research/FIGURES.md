# Khmer Rouge Key Figures: Biographical Research

## 1. POL POT (Saloth Sar)

### Vital Dates
- **Birth:** May 19, 1925, Prek Sbauv village, Kompong Thom Province
- **Death:** April 15, 1998 (aged 72), near Anlong Veng, Cambodia-Thailand border

### Role
- General Secretary of the Communist Party of Kampuchea (1963-1981)
- Prime Minister of Democratic Kampuchea (1976-1979)
- Supreme leader of the Khmer Rouge movement (1963-1997)

### Trial Outcome
- Died before international prosecution; never faced tribunal

### Verified Quotes
1. **"I came to carry out the struggle, not to kill people. Even now, and you can look at me, am I a savage person? My conscience is clear."**
   - Source: Nate Thayer interview, *Far Eastern Economic Review*, October 30, 1997

### Source Citations
- Tier 1: Britannica - https://www.britannica.com/biography/Pol-Pot
- Tier 1: Nate Thayer interview, *Far Eastern Economic Review*, 1997

## 2. NUON CHEA ("Brother Number Two")

### Vital Dates
- **Birth:** July 7, 1926, Battambang Province
- **Death:** August 4, 2019 (aged 93), Phnom Penh

### Role
- Chief Ideologist, Deputy Secretary of the CPK
- Known as "Brother Number Two"

### ECCC Trial Outcome
- **Case 002/01:** Life imprisonment for crimes against humanity
- **Case 002/02:** Convicted of genocide against Vietnamese and Cham populations

### Verified Quotes
1. **"I accept moral responsibility for what happened in Democratic Kampuchea."**
   - Source: ECCC defense statement

### Source Citations
- Tier 1: ECCC Official Profile - https://www.eccc.gov.kh/en/cases/charged-profile/nuon-chea

## 3. KHIEU SAMPHAN

### Vital Dates
- **Birth:** July 27, 1931, Svay Rieng Province
- **Current Status:** Alive, serving life sentence at Kandal Provincial Prison

### Role
- Head of State of Democratic Kampuchea (1976-1979)
- PhD in Economics, University of Paris (1959)

### ECCC Trial Outcome
- **Case 002/01:** Life imprisonment for crimes against humanity
- **Case 002/02:** Convicted of genocide against Vietnamese
- **Appeal (September 22, 2022):** All convictions upheld

### Source Citations
- Tier 1: ECCC Official Profile - https://www.eccc.gov.kh/en/cases/charged-profile/khieu-samphan

## 4. DUCH (Kaing Guek Eav)

### Vital Dates
- **Birth:** November 17, 1942, Kompong Thom Province
- **Death:** September 2, 2020 (aged 77), Phnom Penh

### Role
- Chairman of S-21 (Tuol Sleng) Security Center
- Head of Santebal (secret police)
- Former mathematics teacher

### ECCC Trial Outcome (Case 001)
- **Trial Chamber (2010):** 35 years (reduced for time served)
- **Supreme Court (2012):** Life imprisonment

### Verified Quotes
1. **"I am morally and legally responsible for all crimes inflicted on you at S-21."**
   - Source: ECCC Trial Testimony

### Source Citations
- Tier 1: ECCC Official Profile - https://www.eccc.gov.kh/en/cases/charged-profile/kaing-guek-eav

## 5. NORODOM SIHANOUK

### Vital Dates
- **Birth:** October 31, 1922, Phnom Penh
- **Death:** October 15, 2012 (aged 89), Beijing

### Role
- King of Cambodia (1941-1955, 1993-2004)
- Overthrown in 1970 coup; allied with Khmer Rouge from exile
- Placed under house arrest 1976-1979; lost children to executions

### Verified Quotes
1. **"Yes, I had my part also in the mistake."**
   - Source: *The New York Review of Books*, 1985

### Source Citations
- Tier 1: Britannica - https://www.britannica.com/biography/Norodom-Sihanouk

## 6. LON NOL

### Vital Dates
- **Birth:** November 13, 1913, Prey Veng Province
- **Death:** November 17, 1985 (aged 72), Fullerton, California

### Role
- Led March 18, 1970 coup against Sihanouk
- President of the Khmer Republic (1972-1975)
- Fled Cambodia April 1, 1975; lived in exile in California

### Source Citations
- Tier 1: Britannica - https://www.britannica.com/biography/Lon-Nol
