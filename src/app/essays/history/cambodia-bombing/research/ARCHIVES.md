# Primary Source Archives for Cambodia Bombing Research

A comprehensive guide to archival sources for researching U.S. bombing campaigns in Cambodia (1969-1973).

---

## 1. U.S. National Archives and Records Administration (NARA)

**Location**: National Archives at College Park, MD
**Website**: https://www.archives.gov

### Relevant Record Groups

#### RG 472: United States Forces in Southeast Asia, 1950-1975
- Most heavily used record group for Vietnam conflict research
- Contains operational records, command files, and after-action reports

#### RG 218: Records of the U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff
- Strategic planning documents
- Cambodia operation directives
- **URL**: https://www.archives.gov/findingaid/stat/discovery/218

#### RG 59: General Records of the Department of State
- Subject-Numeric Files, 1963-1973
- Diplomatic cables and memoranda on Cambodia
- **Website**: https://www.archives.gov/research/foreign-policy/state-dept/rg-59-central-files

### Access to Archival Databases (AAD)
- **"Records About Combat Incidents in Cambodia During the Vietnam War"**
- Date range: 6/30/1970 - 6/12/1974
- **URL**: https://aad.archives.gov/aad/series-description.jsp?s=490

## 2. Foreign Relations of the United States (FRUS)

**Website**: https://history.state.gov/historicaldocuments

### Key Volumes

- **Volume VI: Vietnam, January 1969-July 1970** - Critical coverage of secret bombing
- **Volume X: Vietnam, January 1973-July 1975** - Post-Paris Accords period
- **Volume XLII: The Kissinger-Le Duc Tho Negotiations**

**Access**: All FRUS volumes freely available online, full-text searchable

---

## 3. Richard Nixon Presidential Library

**Website**: https://www.nixonlibrary.gov

### National Security Council Files
- Cambodia Operations Series (Boxes 579-590)
- Cambodia files (opened September 12, 2023)
- **Finding Aid**: https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/finding-aids/national-security-council-files

### Kissinger Telephone Conversation Transcripts
- Over 30,000 pages of daily transcripts
- **Finding Aid**: https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/finding-aids/kissinger-telephone-conversation-transcripts

### White House Tapes
- **Resource**: https://nixontapes.org

## 5. Yale Genocide Studies Program (CGP)

**Website**: https://gsp.yale.edu/case-studies/cambodian-genocide-program

### Interactive Geographic Database (CGEO)
**URL**: https://gsp.yale.edu/cambodian-genocide-program-interactive-geographic-database-cgeo

**Contains:**
- **115,273 bombing sites** in **231,467 sorties** (October 1965 - May 1975)
- 13,042 village locations
- 42 aircraft types, 188 munitions types
- Sortie-level data: date, coordinates, ordnance, target description

### U.S. Involvement Page
**URL**: https://gsp.yale.edu/case-studies/cambodian-genocide-program/us-involvement/united-states-bombing-cambodia-1965-1973

## Research Strategies

### Starting Points
1. Begin with FRUS volumes for official documentary record
2. Use Yale CGEO database for bombing data visualization
3. Search National Security Archive for declassified Kissinger materials
4. Check Nixon Library finding aids for specific NSC files

### Physical Research Trips
- **National Archives, College Park**: RG 59, RG 218, RG 472
- **Nixon Library, Yorba Linda**: NSC files, tapes, Kissinger papers
- **AFHRA, Maxwell AFB**: USAF operational records
