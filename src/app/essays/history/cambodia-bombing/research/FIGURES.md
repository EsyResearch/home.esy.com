# Key Figures: Cambodia Bombing (1965-1973)

## U.S. Administration

### Richard Nixon — 37th President of the United States
- Authorized Operation Menu (March 1969)
- Ordered secrecy to avoid Congressional and public reaction
- Announced Cambodia "incursion" April 30, 1970
- Escalated bombing to "anything that flies on anything that moves"
- **Key Quote**: "If, when the chips are down, the world's most powerful nation, the United States of America, acts like a pitiful helpless giant, the forces of totalitarianism and anarchy will threaten free nations and free institutions throughout the world."
- **Photograph**: Official White House portrait; candid photos with Kissinger

### Henry Kissinger — National Security Adviser (1969-1975)
- Designed dual reporting system with Colonel Sitton
- Personally selected bombing targets: "Strike here, strike there"
- Ordered FBI wiretaps on staffers after Beecher leak
- **Key Quote**: "Anything that flies on anything that moves." (December 9, 1970)
- **Key Quote**: "We will destroy whoever did this." (to FBI Director Hoover)
- **Photograph**: Official portrait; with Nixon in Oval Office

### Melvin Laird — Secretary of Defense (1969-1973)
- Opposed secrecy (not the bombing itself)
- Warned Nixon that secrecy was unsustainable
- **Key Quote**: "I was all for hitting those targets in Cambodia, but I wanted it public."
- **Photograph**: Pentagon official portrait

### William P. Rogers — Secretary of State (1969-1973)
- Initially kept in the dark about Operation Menu
- Feared bombings would derail Paris peace talks
- **Photograph**: State Department portrait

## Military Leadership

### General Earle Wheeler — Chairman, Joint Chiefs of Staff
- First proposed bombing Cambodian sanctuaries (January 30, 1969)
- One of few with full knowledge of dual reporting system
- **Photograph**: Military portrait

### General Creighton Abrams — MACV Commander
- Recommended B-52 strikes against COSVN (February 9, 1969)
- Claimed targeted areas were "underpopulated"
- Later testified about "special furnace" for destroying records
- **Photograph**: Military portrait in Vietnam

### Colonel Ray Sitton — "Mr. B-52"
- JCS B-52 expert who designed dual reporting system
- Would bring target lists to White House for Kissinger
- Backchanneled actual coordinates to field
- Promoted to Lieutenant General for his role
- **Photograph**: If available - military portrait

### Colonel Alexander Haig — Kissinger's Military Aide
- Key architect of dual reporting system
- Met with Sitton in February 1969 to design secrecy scheme
- Laughed on phone when Kissinger relayed "anything that moves" order
- **Photograph**: Military portrait (pre-White House Chief of Staff)

## Whistleblowers

### Major Hal Knight — USAF Whistleblower
- Supervised radar crews at Bien Hoa
- Received secret coordinates; ordered to burn all records
- Delivered code "The ball game is over" after strikes
- Resigned from Air Force over moral concerns
- Wrote to Senator Proxmire (December 1972)
- Testified before Senate Armed Services Committee (July 16, 1973)
- **Key Quote**: "It is my firm conviction that the American people, through their elected representatives, have the right to know how the war has been conducted."
- **Photograph**: If available from congressional testimony

### William Beecher — New York Times Military Correspondent
- Broke story of secret bombing (May 9, 1969)
- Article headline: "Raids in Cambodia by U.S. Unprotested"
- Triggered Kissinger's wiretap program
- **Photograph**: Press photo or byline image

## Congressional Figures

### Senator J. William Fulbright (D-AR) — Chairman, Foreign Relations Committee
- Longest-serving chair of Foreign Relations Committee (1959-1974)
- Dual reporting system specifically designed to deceive his committee
- Led televised Vietnam War hearings
- **Key Quote**: "Does the President assert - as kings of old - that as Commander in Chief he can order American forces anywhere for any purpose that suits him?"
- **Photograph**: Senate portrait

### Senator Frank Church (D-ID)
- Co-author of Cooper-Church (1970) and Case-Church (1973) amendments
- Later chaired Church Committee on intelligence abuses
- **Photograph**: Senate portrait

### Senator John Sherman Cooper (R-KY)
- Republican co-sponsor of Cooper-Church Amendment
- Former Ambassador to India and East Germany
- **Photograph**: Senate portrait

### Senator Clifford Case (R-NJ)
- Republican co-sponsor of Case-Church Amendment
- **Key Quote** (joint letter with Church): "[The bill] is neither pro-war nor antiwar, hawkish nor dovish. It is our sole purpose to make sure that this question is passed upon by Congress."
- **Photograph**: Senate portrait

### Senator Thomas Eagleton (D-MO)
- Sponsored Eagleton Amendment to cut bombing funds (1973)
- Forced August 15, 1973 deadline
- **Photograph**: Senate portrait

### Rep. Robert Drinan (D-MA)
- First to introduce impeachment resolution against Nixon (July 31, 1973)
- Cited "the totally secret air war in Cambodia for 14 months"
- **Key Quote**: "How can we impeach the President for concealing a burglary but not for concealing a massive bombing?"
- **Photograph**: Congressional portrait

### Rep. Elizabeth Holtzman (D-NY)
- Sued to stop Cambodia bombing in federal court
- Won initial injunction from Judge Judd (July 25, 1973)
- Case reached Supreme Court
- **Photograph**: Congressional portrait

## Cambodian Leaders

### Norodom Sihanouk — Prince and Head of State (until March 1970)
- Tacitly allowed Vietnamese communist sanctuaries
- Permitted Sihanouk Trail supply route through Cambodia
- Overthrown by Lon Nol coup (March 18, 1970)
- Later allied with Khmer Rouge in exile
- **Photograph**: Royal portrait; political photos

### Lon Nol — General, later President (1970-1975)
- Led coup against Sihanouk (March 18, 1970)
- Aligned with United States
- Received U.S. air support against Khmer Rouge
- Fled Cambodia April 1, 1975
- **Photograph**: Military or presidential portrait

### Pol Pot (Saloth Sar) — Khmer Rouge Leader
- Led communist insurgency during bombing
- Exploited bombing devastation for recruitment
- Seized power April 17, 1975
- Oversaw Cambodian genocide (1975-1979)
- **Photograph**: Rare photographs from before or during Democratic Kampuchea

## Scholars and Researchers

### Ben Kiernan — Yale Historian
- Founded Cambodian Genocide Program
- Co-authored "Bombs Over Cambodia" (with Taylor Owen)
- Leading scholar on Khmer Rouge origins
- **Key Work**: *How Pol Pot Came to Power* (1985, 2004)
- **Photograph**: Academic portrait

### Taylor Owen — Oxford/McGill Scholar
- Co-authored "Bombs Over Cambodia"
- Obtained and analyzed declassified bombing data
- Later publicly corrected tonnage estimate

### Holly High — University of Sydney
- Identified database errors in tonnage calculations
- Led re-analysis of SEADAB and CACTA databases
