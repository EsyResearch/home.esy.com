## CHAPTER-BY-CHAPTER RESEARCH MAPPING

### Chapter 1: Deep Time
**Core Content:**
- Earth's 4.54 Ga history as context for human evolution
- Hominin evolution represents only ~0.15% of Earth's history
- Scale visualization: if Earth's history were 24 hours, humans appear in last 4 seconds

**Key Sources:**
- USGS Geologic Time Scale
- NASA Earth imagery (Public Domain)
- Radiometric dating standards

**Quotes Available:** Darwin (lowly origin), Sagan (pale blue dot), Smail (long view)

- Genetic analysis techniques
- Comparative anatomy

**Key Sources:**
- Smithsonian methodology resources

- 3D fossil scans (Smithsonian CC0)

### Chapter 3: Tree of Life
**Core Content:**
- Phylogenetic position of humans among primates
- Great ape relationships (humans share ~98.8% DNA with chimps)
- Tree-not-ladder evolution model
- Multiple coexisting hominin species

**Key Sources:**
- Molecular clock calibrations
- Phylogenetic tree SVGs (Wikimedia Public Domain)
- Published cladograms

**Visual Assets:** Tree diagrams, divergence timelines

### Chapter 4: African Origins
**Core Content:**
- East African Rift Valley as "cradle of humanity"
- Pan-African origins model (Scerri et al.)
- Earliest hominins: Sahelanthropus, Orrorin, Ardipithecus
- Australopithecines diversity

**Key Specimens:**
- Toumaï (TM 266-01-060-1)
- Ardi (ARA-VP-6/500)
- Lucy (AL 288-1)
- Laetoli footprints

**Key Sources:**
- Brunet et al. 2002 (Sahelanthropus)
- White et al. 2009 (Ardipithecus)
- Johanson et al. 1978 (A. afarensis)

**Quotes Available:** Hublin (pan-African), Johanson (Lucy), Haile-Selassie (Ardi)

### Chapter 5: Walking Upright

### Chapter 6: Genus Homo
**Core Content:**
- Emergence of Homo genus (~2.5 Ma)
- H. habilis and first stone tools
- H. ergaster/erectus and modern body plan
- First dispersals from Africa

**Key Specimens:**

- Dmanisi crania

**Key Sources:**

- Harmand et al. 2015 (Lomekwian)

### Chapter 7: Other Humans
**Core Content:**
- Neanderthals: anatomy, culture, genome
- Denisovans: genetic discovery, limited morphology
- H. floresiensis: island evolution
- H. naledi: unexpected survival
- H. luzonensis: Philippine isolation

**Key Data:**
- Neanderthal brain size: 1200-1700 cc
- Admixture: 1-2% Neanderthal in non-Africans
- Denisovan admixture: 1.9-6% in Melanesians
- H. floresiensis brain: ~400 cc (contested interpretation)

**Key Sources:**
- Green et al. 2010 (Neanderthal genome)
- Reich et al. 2010 (Denisovan genome)
- Berger et al. 2015 (H. naledi)
- Brown et al. 2004 (H. floresiensis)

**Quotes Available:** Pääbo (always mixed), Slon (Denisova 11), Berger (unexpected)

---

### Chapter 8: Homo Sapiens
**Core Content:**
- Emergence ~315 ka (Jebel Irhoud)
- African origins with population structure
- Symbolic behavior: ochre, beads, cave art
- Anatomically modern but behaviorally complex development

**Key Sites:**
- Jebel Irhoud, Morocco (315 ka)
- Omo Kibish, Ethiopia (233 ka)
- Blombos Cave, South Africa (100-70 ka)
- Pinnacle Point (164 ka)

**Key Sources:**
- Hublin et al. 2017 (Jebel Irhoud)
- Henshilwood et al. (Blombos)
- Marean et al. (Pinnacle Point)

**Cautions:**
- Avoid claiming "first" symbolic behavior at specific dates
- Ochre use may be functional, not symbolic
- "Behavioral modernity" concept debated

---

### Chapter 9: Migrations
**Core Content:**
- Out of Africa dispersals (multiple waves)
- Routes: Southern vs Northern
- Timing: 70-50 ka main wave
- Admixture events during dispersal

**Key Events:**
- First OoA (H. erectus): 1.9-1.8 Ma
- Early H. sapiens dispersals: 120-80 ka (Skhul/Qafzeh)
- Main OoA: 70-50 ka
- Australia arrival: >50 ka
- Americas: 24-15 ka (debated)

**Key Sources:**
- Reich 2018 (Who We Are and How We Got Here)
- Natural Earth maps (Public Domain)
- Migration route reconstructions

**Quotes Available:** Reich (mixtures all the way down), Stringer (complex origin)

## UNCERTAINTY HANDLING PROTOCOL

### Categories of Uncertainty

1. **Taxonomic** - Species definitions, genus boundaries
2. **Chronological** - Dating precision, range overlaps
3. **Behavioral** - Inference from material culture
4. **Genetic** - Admixture percentages, phenotype reconstruction
5. **Migration** - Routes, timing, population sizes

### Language Guidelines

| Confidence | Phrasing |
|------------|----------|
| Very High | "Evidence shows..." |
| High | "Current evidence indicates..." |
| Moderate | "Most researchers interpret..." |
| Low | "This remains debated..." |
| Contested | "Different studies suggest..." |

### Topics Requiring Extra Care
- Neanderthal extinction causes
- Language origins
- Bipedalism drivers
- Racial categorization
- Linear progression narratives
