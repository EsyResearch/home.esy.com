## EARLIEST HOMININS (7-4 Ma)

### Sahelanthropus tchadensis
- **Date Range:** ~7-6 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** TM 266-01-060-1 ("Toumaï")
- **Location:** Chad, Central Africa
- **Key Features:** Small brain (~350 cc), flat face, forward foramen magnum
- **Significance:** Possibly oldest known hominin; bipedalism debated
- **Confidence:** Moderate (single site, limited postcranial evidence)
- **Citation:** Brunet et al. 2002, Nature

### Orrorin tugenensis
- **Date Range:** ~6 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** BAR 1002'00 (femur fragment)
- **Location:** Tugen Hills, Kenya
- **Key Features:** Femoral morphology suggesting bipedalism
- **Significance:** Evidence of bipedalism by 6 Ma
- **Confidence:** Moderate
- **Citation:** Senut et al. 2001, Comptes Rendus

### Ardipithecus kadabba
- **Date Range:** 5.8-5.2 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** ALA-VP-2/10
- **Location:** Middle Awash, Ethiopia
- **Key Features:** Teeth intermediate between apes and later hominins
- **Confidence:** Moderate
- **Citation:** Haile-Selassie 2001, Nature

### Ardipithecus ramidus ("Ardi")
- **Date Range:** 4.4 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** ARA-VP-6/500
- **Location:** Middle Awash, Ethiopia
- **Key Features:** 45% complete skeleton; divergent big toe; pelvis showing bipedal adaptations
- **Significance:** Demonstrates bipedalism in woodland environment (not savanna)
- **Brain Size:** ~300-350 cc
- **Confidence:** High (extensive skeleton)
- **Citation:** White et al. 2009, Science

## AUSTRALOPITHECINES (4-2 Ma)

### Australopithecus anamensis
- **Date Range:** 4.2-3.9 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** KNM-KP 29281 (tibia)
- **Location:** Kenya (Kanapoi, Allia Bay)
- **Key Features:** Bipedal; primitive dentition
- **Significance:** Oldest definitive australopithecine
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Leakey et al. 1995, Nature

### Australopithecus afarensis
- **Date Range:** 3.9-2.9 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** LH 4 (mandible, Laetoli)
- **Famous Specimens:**
  - AL 288-1 "Lucy" (40% complete, Hadar, 3.2 Ma)
  - AL 333 "First Family" (13+ individuals)
- **Location:** Ethiopia (Hadar), Tanzania (Laetoli)
- **Key Features:** Small brain (~430 cc), bipedal, sexually dimorphic
- **Associated Evidence:** Laetoli footprints (3.66 Ma)
- **Confidence:** High (extensive fossil record)
- **Citation:** Johanson et al. 1978, Kirtlandia

### Australopithecus africanus
- **Date Range:** 3.3-2.1 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** Taung child (1924)
- **Famous Specimens:**
  - Sts 5 "Mrs. Ples" (Sterkfontein)
  - StW 573 "Little Foot" (Sterkfontein, 3.67 Ma)
- **Location:** South Africa
- **Key Features:** Gracile build, rounded cranium
- **Brain Size:** 400-500 cc
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Dart 1925, Nature

### Australopithecus garhi
- **Date Range:** 2.5 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** BOU-VP-12/130
- **Location:** Bouri, Middle Awash, Ethiopia
- **Key Features:** Large teeth; possibly associated with earliest stone tools
- **Significance:** Potential tool-maker candidate
- **Confidence:** Moderate
- **Citation:** Asfaw et al. 1999, Science

### Australopithecus sediba
- **Date Range:** 1.98-1.95 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** MH1 (juvenile male)
- **Location:** Malapa, South Africa
- **Key Features:** Mosaic of Australopithecus and Homo features
- **Significance:** Possible ancestor of Homo genus (debated)
- **Confidence:** High (well-preserved specimens)
- **Citation:** Berger et al. 2010, Science

### Kenyanthropus platyops
- **Date Range:** 3.5-3.2 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** KNM-WT 40000
- **Location:** Lomekwi, Kenya
- **Key Features:** Flat face, small molars
- **Significance:** Demonstrates early hominin diversity
- **Confidence:** Moderate (crushed cranium)
- **Citation:** Leakey et al. 2001, Nature

## PARANTHROPUS (ROBUST AUSTRALOPITHECINES)

### Paranthropus aethiopicus
- **Date Range:** 2.7-2.3 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** WT 17000 "Black Skull"
- **Location:** Lake Turkana, Kenya
- **Key Features:** Large sagittal crest, massive jaws
- **Confidence:** Moderate
- **Citation:** Walker et al. 1986, Nature

### Paranthropus boisei
- **Date Range:** 2.3-1.2 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** OH 5 "Zinj" / "Nutcracker Man"
- **Location:** East Africa (Olduvai, Koobi Fora)
- **Key Features:** Massive molars, pronounced sagittal crest
- **Significance:** Specialized diet (hard foods)
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Leakey 1959, Nature

### Paranthropus robustus
- **Date Range:** 2.0-1.0 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** TM 1517
- **Location:** South Africa (Kromdraai, Swartkrans)
- **Key Features:** Robust cranium, large cheek teeth
- **Significance:** Coexisted with early Homo
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Broom 1938

## EARLY HOMO (2.5-1.5 Ma)

### Homo habilis
- **Date Range:** 2.3-1.4 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** OH 7 (Olduvai Gorge)
- **Key Features:** Larger brain (~510-600 cc), smaller teeth
- **Significance:** First species assigned to Homo; associated with Oldowan tools
- **Debate:** Species validity questioned by some
- **Confidence:** Moderate (heterogeneous sample)
- **Citation:** Leakey et al. 1964, Nature

### Homo rudolfensis
- **Date Range:** 1.9-1.8 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** KNM-ER 1470
- **Location:** Lake Turkana, Kenya
- **Key Features:** Large brain (~750 cc), flat face
- **Debate:** May belong to Homo or separate genus
- **Confidence:** Moderate
- **Citation:** Leakey 1973, Nature

### Homo ergaster/erectus (Early African)
- **Date Range:** 1.9-1.5 Ma (Africa)
- **Key Specimens:**
  - KNM-ER 3733 (cranium)
  - KNM-WT 15000 "Turkana Boy" (nearly complete skeleton, 1.6 Ma)
- **Location:** East Africa
- **Key Features:** Tall, modern body proportions, brain ~850 cc
- **Significance:** First hominin with modern-like body plan
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Brown et al. 1985, Nature

## HOMO ERECTUS (sensu lato)

### Asian Homo erectus
- **Date Range:** 1.8 Ma - 117 ka (possibly later)
- **Key Specimens:**
  - Trinil 2 "Java Man" (type specimen)
  - Sangiran 17 (most complete Asian cranium)
  - Zhoukoudian "Peking Man" (multiple individuals)
  - Ngandong specimens (possibly ~117-108 ka)
- **Location:** Indonesia, China
- **Key Features:** Thick cranial vault, brain 900-1100 cc
- **Significance:** Long-surviving lineage; first to leave Africa at scale
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Dubois 1894

### Dmanisi Hominins
- **Date Range:** 1.85-1.77 Ma
- **Key Specimens:**
  - D2280, D2282, D2700, D3444 (crania)
  - D4500 "Skull 5"
- **Location:** Dmanisi, Georgia
- **Key Features:** Small brains (546-730 cc), primitive features, Oldowan tools
- **Significance:** Earliest well-dated hominins outside Africa
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Lordkipanidze et al. 2013, Science

## MIDDLE PLEISTOCENE HOMO

### Homo antecessor
- **Date Range:** 1.2-0.78 Ma
- **Type Specimen:** ATD6-5
- **Location:** Gran Dolina, Atapuerca, Spain
- **Key Features:** Modern-like face, large brain
- **Significance:** Possible common ancestor of Neanderthals and H. sapiens
- **Confidence:** Moderate (limited specimens)
- **Citation:** Bermúdez de Castro et al. 1997, Science

### Homo heidelbergensis
- **Date Range:** ~700-200 ka
- **Type Specimen:** Mauer 1 (mandible, 1908)
- **Key Specimens:** Sima de los Huesos (~28 individuals, ~430 ka)
- **Location:** Europe, Africa
- **Key Features:** Large brain (1100-1400 cc), robust build
- **Debate:** Taxon validity disputed; may include ancestral populations of both Neanderthals and H. sapiens
- **Confidence:** Moderate (taxonomic controversy)
- **Citation:** Schoetensack 1908

## NEANDERTHALS & DENISOVANS

### Homo neanderthalensis
- **Date Range:** ~400-40 ka
- **Type Specimen:** Neanderthal 1 (Feldhofer, 1856)
- **Key Sites:** La Chapelle, La Ferrassie, Shanidar, Vindija
- **Geographic Range:** Europe, Western Asia
- **Key Features:** Large brain (1200-1700 cc), robust skeleton, cold adaptations
- **Genome:** Fully sequenced (Vindija, Altai)
- **Admixture:** 1-2% in non-Africans
- **Confidence:** Very High
- **Citation:** King 1864; Green et al. 2010

### Denisovans
- **Date Range:** >300-30 ka
- **Key Specimens:**
  - Denisova 3 (finger bone, first identified)
  - Denisova 11 "Denny" (Neanderthal-Denisovan hybrid)
- **Location:** Denisova Cave, Altai Mountains, Russia; possibly Tibetan Plateau
- **Key Features:** Known primarily from DNA; limited morphological data
- **Genome:** High-coverage sequence available
- **Admixture:** 1.9-6% in Melanesians; 0.1-0.2% in East Asians
- **Confidence:** High (genetic data strong; morphology limited)
- **Citation:** Reich et al. 2010, Nature; Meyer et al. 2012, Science

## LATE PLEISTOCENE ISLAND HOMININS

### Homo floresiensis
- **Date Range:** 190-50 ka
- **Type Specimen:** LB1 (partial skeleton)
- **Location:** Liang Bua Cave, Flores, Indonesia
- **Key Features:** Very small body (~1.06 m), small brain (~400 cc)
- **Significance:** Survived alongside H. sapiens; island dwarfism or primitive ancestry debated
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Brown et al. 2004, Nature

### Homo luzonensis
- **Date Range:** >67 ka (possibly >130 ka)
- **Type Specimen:** CCH1 (metatarsal)
- **Location:** Callao Cave, Luzon, Philippines
- **Key Features:** Small body, mix of primitive (Australopithecus-like) and derived features
- **Significance:** Another island-endemic hominin lineage
- **Confidence:** Moderate (limited fossils)
- **Citation:** Detroit et al. 2019, Nature

---

## HOMO NALEDI

### Homo naledi
- **Date Range:** 335-236 ka
- **Type Specimen:** DH1 (holotype cranium)
- **Key Sites:** Dinaledi Chamber, Lesedi Chamber (Rising Star Cave)
- **Location:** Cradle of Humankind, South Africa
- **Key Features:** Small brain (~500 cc), human-like hands/feet, primitive torso
- **Significance:** Primitive body plan contemporary with early H. sapiens; possible deliberate body disposal
- **Sample Size:** >1,800 specimens from 18+ individuals
- **Confidence:** High
- **Citation:** Berger et al. 2015, eLife

## EARLY HOMO SAPIENS

### Jebel Irhoud Specimens
- **Date:** 315,000 ± 34,000 years
- **Specimens:** 5+ individuals
- **Location:** Morocco
- **Key Features:** Modern face, elongated braincase
- **Significance:** Oldest known H. sapiens
- **Citation:** Hublin et al. 2017, Nature

### Omo Kibish (Omo I & II)
- **Date:** 233,000 ± 22,000 years (revised 2022)
- **Location:** Ethiopia
- **Key Features:** Omo I more modern; Omo II more archaic
- **Significance:** Demonstrates morphological variation in early H. sapiens
- **Citation:** Vidal et al. 2022, Nature

### Herto (BOU-VP-16/1)
- **Date:** 160-154 ka
- **Location:** Middle Awash, Ethiopia
- **Key Features:** H. sapiens idaltu; cut marks suggesting mortuary practices
- **Citation:** White et al. 2003, Nature

### Skhul/Qafzeh
- **Date:** 120-80 ka
- **Location:** Israel
- **Significance:** Earliest H. sapiens outside Africa; evidence of early OoA dispersal
- **Citation:** Stringer et al. 1989, Nature
