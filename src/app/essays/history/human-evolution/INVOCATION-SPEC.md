## LAYER 2: CONTENT SPECIFICATION

### Chapter 1: Deep Time

**Opening Hook:**
> "If Earth's 4.5 billion year history were compressed into a single year, humans would appear in the final 4 seconds before midnight on December 31st."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Scale visualization - interactive clock/calendar analogy
2. Geological context - where hominins fit in Earth history
3. What 7 million years means - nested timeline

**Key Data Points:**
- Earth: 4.54 billion years
- Life: ~3.8 billion years
- Hominins: ~7 million years
- Homo sapiens: ~315,000 years
- Recorded history: ~5,000 years

**Quotes to Include:**
- Darwin: "the indelible stamp of his lowly origin"
- Smail: "the long view lowers the blood pressure"

**Evidence Cards:** None (conceptual chapter)

**Misconception Checkpoint:**
- MYTH: "Evolution is a ladder from primitive to advanced"
- REALITY: "Evolution is a branching bush with many contemporary lineages"

---

### Chapter 2: How We Know

**Opening Hook:**
> "Every fossil tells a story - but reading that story requires multiple scientific disciplines working in concert."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Fossil formation - taphonomy basics
2. Dating methods - radiometric, paleomagnetism, stratigraphy
3. Genetic analysis - ancient DNA revolution
4. Comparative anatomy - skeletal evidence

**Evidence Cards:**
| Type | Title | Content |
|------|-------|---------|
| Method | Radiometric Dating | Uranium-series, argon-argon, radiocarbon |
| Method | Ancient DNA | Extraction from bones, degradation patterns |
| Method | Stratigraphy | Layer-by-layer context |

**Quote:**
- Wood: "I'm trying to reduce our ignorance about human evolution"

**Uncertainty Framing:**
- Emphasize that evidence is fragmentary
- Dating has error ranges
- New discoveries can change understanding

---

### Chapter 3: Tree of Life

**Opening Hook:**
> "We share 98.8% of our DNA with chimpanzees. That 1.2% difference - accumulated over 6-7 million years - is what makes us human."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Primate phylogeny - where humans fit
2. Great ape relationships - genetic distances
3. Tree-not-ladder - multiple contemporary lineages
4. Why the "bushy" tree matters

**Interactive Module:**
- Zoomable phylogenetic tree (OneZoom-inspired)
- Highlight hominin branch
- Click species for details

**Key Data Points:**
- Human-chimp divergence: 6-7 Ma
- Human-gorilla divergence: ~9 Ma
- Lowest genetic diversity among great apes: humans

**Misconception Checkpoint:**
- MYTH: "Humans evolved from chimpanzees"
- REALITY: "Humans and chimps share a common ancestor"

---

### Chapter 4: African Origins

**Opening Hook:**
> "The East African Rift Valley - a geological scar running through the continent - preserved the evidence of our earliest ancestors in its sediments."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Rift Valley geology - why fossils preserved here
2. Earliest candidates - Sahelanthropus, Orrorin, Ardipithecus
3. Australopithecines - diversity and adaptation
4. Pan-African origins model

**Evidence Cards:**
| Specimen | Species | Date | Significance |
|----------|---------|------|--------------|
| Toumai | S. tchadensis | ~7 Ma | Oldest candidate hominin |
| Ardi | A. ramidus | 4.4 Ma | Woodland bipedalism |
| Lucy | A. afarensis | 3.2 Ma | Most famous australopith |
| Little Foot | A. africanus | 3.67 Ma | Most complete skeleton |

**Interactive Module:**
- Map of major African fossil sites
- Click site for key finds

**Quotes:**
- Hublin: "a pan-African process and more complex scenario"
- Johanson: "I knew from the beginning that she would be important"

---

### Chapter 5: Walking Upright

**Opening Hook:**
> "The footprints at Laetoli, frozen in volcanic ash 3.66 million years ago, show creatures walking upright - before brain expansion, before stone tools."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Laetoli footprints - evidence and interpretation
2. Anatomical changes - pelvis, spine, foramen magnum
3. Why bipedalism? - hypotheses and evidence
4. Costs and benefits - energy, carrying, thermoregulation

**Evidence Cards:**
| Evidence | Type | Date | Significance |
|----------|------|------|--------------|
| Laetoli footprints | Trace fossil | 3.66 Ma | Oldest bipedal evidence |
| Foramen magnum | Anatomy | Multiple | Position indicates posture |
| Pelvis shape | Anatomy | Multiple | Restructured for walking |

**Comparison Module:**
- Side-by-side: ape vs. human skeleton
- Highlight key bipedal adaptations

**Uncertainty Framing:**
- Multiple hypotheses for bipedalism origins
- Avoid single-cause explanations
- Climate correlation ≠ causation

---

### Chapter 6: Genus Homo

**Opening Hook:**
> "When the first stone tools were struck 3.3 million years ago, something unprecedented had begun - technology as a survival strategy."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Defining Homo - brain, body, tools
2. Homo habilis - "handy man" and Oldowan tools
3. Homo ergaster/erectus - modern body plan
4. First dispersal - Dmanisi and beyond

**Evidence Cards:**
| Specimen | Species | Date | Key Features |
|----------|---------|------|--------------|
| OH 7 | H. habilis | 1.8 Ma | Type specimen |
| Turkana Boy | H. ergaster | 1.6 Ma | Near-complete skeleton |
| Dmanisi skulls | Early Homo | 1.8 Ma | First outside Africa |

**Interactive Module:**
- Tool industries timeline
- Click industry for artifacts and techniques

**Tool Industries Data:**
| Industry | Date | Maker | Key Artifacts |
|----------|------|-------|---------------|
| Lomekwian | 3.3 Ma | Unknown | Large cores, anvils |
| Oldowan | 2.9 Ma | H. habilis | Choppers, simple flakes |
| Acheulean | 1.76 Ma | H. erectus | Handaxes, cleavers |

### Chapter 7: Other Humans

**Opening Hook:**
> "For most of our evolutionary history, we were not alone. Multiple human species walked the Earth simultaneously."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Neanderthals - anatomy, culture, genome
2. Denisovans - the genetic ghost
3. Homo floresiensis - the "hobbit"
4. Homo naledi - unexpected survivor
5. Admixture and interbreeding

**Evidence Cards:**
| Species | Date Range | Brain Size | Key Trait |
|---------|------------|------------|-----------|
| H. neanderthalensis | 400-40 ka | 1200-1700 cc | Cold adaptation |
| Denisovans | 300-30 ka | Unknown | Known from DNA |
| H. floresiensis | 190-50 ka | ~400 cc | Island dwarfism |
| H. naledi | 335-236 ka | ~500 cc | Primitive + derived |

**Interactive Module:**
- Species coexistence timeline
- Horizontal bars showing temporal overlap

**Admixture Data:**
| Source | Recipient | Percentage | Timing |
|--------|-----------|------------|--------|
| Neanderthal | Non-Africans | 1-2% | ~47 ka |
| Denisovan | Melanesians | 1.9-6% | ~45 ka |
| Denisovan | Tibetans | EPAS1 gene | High-altitude adaptation |

**Quotes:**
- Pääbo: "We have always mixed... wherever we met them"
- Berger: "Expect the unexpected"

### Chapter 8: Homo Sapiens

**Opening Hook:**
> "In a cave in Morocco, 315,000 years ago, people who looked remarkably like us were shaping stone tools and burying their dead."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Jebel Irhoud - oldest H. sapiens
2. African origins and population structure
3. Symbolic behavior - ochre, beads, engravings
4. Blombos Cave and the MSA

**Evidence Cards:**
| Site | Date | Evidence |
|------|------|----------|
| Jebel Irhoud | 315 ka | Oldest H. sapiens fossils |
| Blombos Cave | 100-70 ka | Ochre, beads, engravings |
| Pinnacle Point | 164 ka | Coastal adaptation |
| Bizmoune | 142 ka | Oldest shell beads |

**Interactive Module:**
- Blombos artifacts gallery
- Click artifact for significance

**Uncertainty Framing:**
- "Behavioral modernity" concept debated
- Ochre may be functional, not symbolic
- Evidence sampling bias toward certain regions

### Chapter 9: Migrations

**Opening Hook:**
> "Between 70,000 and 50,000 years ago, a small group of humans left Africa. Their descendants would eventually populate every continent."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Early dispersals - Skhul/Qafzeh
2. Main Out of Africa wave
3. Routes and timing
4. Australia arrival
5. Americas colonization

**Evidence Cards:**
| Event | Date | Evidence |
|-------|------|----------|
| Skhul/Qafzeh | 120-80 ka | Early failed dispersal |
| Main OoA | 70-50 ka | Genetic bottleneck |
| Australia | >50 ka | Maritime crossing |
| Americas | 24-15 ka | Beringia route |

**Interactive Module:**
- Animated migration map
- Timeline scrubber for different periods
- Climate overlay option

**Climate Correlation:**
| Event | Climate | Significance |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| MIS 5e | Interglacial | Window for early dispersals |
| LGM | Glacial maximum | Beringia exposed, sea levels low |
| Green Sahara | Humid period | Movement across Sahara possible |

**Quotes:**
- Reich: "mixtures all the way down"

### Chapter 10: Culture & Cognition

**Opening Hook:**
> "The same 32 geometric symbols appear in caves across Europe over 30,000 years. This was not the start-up phase of a new invention."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Fire control - Wonderwerk Cave
2. Cooking hypothesis
3. Language evolution - no direct evidence
4. Art and symbolism - cave paintings, figurines
5. Neolithic transition

**Evidence Cards:**
| Innovation | Date | Site | Evidence |
|------------|------|------|----------|
| Controlled fire | 1 Ma | Wonderwerk | Burned bone, ash |
| Cooking | 780 ka | Gesher Benot Ya'aqov | Burned seeds, wood |
| Cave art | 40 ka+ | Chauvet | Figurative paintings |
| Architecture | 11.5 ka | Gobekli Tepe | Monumental pillars |

**Interactive Module:**
- Technology timeline
- Click era for key innovations

**Quotes:**
- von Petzinger: "not the start-up phase of a brand-new invention"
- Wrangham: "Cooking is what makes the human diet human"

**Uncertainty Framing:**
- Language evolution timing unknown
- Cognitive interpretations from artifacts speculative
- Symbolic vs. functional use of ochre debated

---

### Chapter 11: Present & Future

**Opening Hook:**
> "You carry within your DNA fragments of Neanderthals, perhaps Denisovans, and countless ancestors stretching back millions of years."

**Content Blocks:**
1. Genetic unity of humanity
2. Recent evolution (last 10,000 years)
3. What makes us human - ongoing questions
4. Future research directions

**Key Data Points:**
- All humans share common ancestors within last 100,000 years
- Human genetic diversity lower than chimps
- No biological basis for race categories
- Ongoing selection on lactase, malaria resistance, etc.

**Quotes:**
- Tishkoff: "as a species, humans are very similar"
- Cavalli-Sforza: "differences between individuals are much greater than between races"

**Closing:**
Return to deep time perspective. We are one species, recently emerged, with a shared origin and shared future.

### Image Requirements

**Specimen Photography:**
- Neutral background (bone or gray)
- Consistent lighting direction
- Scale indicator where applicable
- Caption with specimen ID, species, date

**Reconstruction Images:**
- ALWAYS labeled "Artist's reconstruction" or "Scientific reconstruction"
- Attribution to artist required
- Note if AI-assisted

**Maps:**
- Natural Earth base (Public Domain)
- Consistent projection per chapter
- Migration routes use curved lines, not straight

**Diagrams:**
- SVG format for scalability
- Accessible colors with patterns
- Text labels, not legends where possible

## LAYER 5: ACCESSIBILITY SPECIFICATION

### WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

| Criterion | Requirement | Implementation |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| 1.1.1 | Alt text for images | All images have descriptive alt |
| 1.3.1 | Info and relationships | Semantic HTML, ARIA where needed |
| 1.4.1 | Use of color | Never color alone; patterns + labels |
| 1.4.3 | Contrast | 4.5:1 text, 3:1 graphics |
| 2.1.1 | Keyboard | Full keyboard navigation |
| 2.4.3 | Focus order | Logical tab sequence |
| 2.4.7 | Focus visible | High-contrast focus indicators |
| 2.5.5 | Target size | 44x44px minimum touch targets |
| 3.1.5 | Reading level | Flesch-Kincaid Grade 8-9 |
| 4.1.2 | Name, role, value | ARIA labels on interactive elements |

### Screen Reader Support

**SVG Charts:**
```html
<svg role="img" aria-labelledby="chartTitle chartDesc">
  <title id="chartTitle">Species Timeline</title>
  <desc id="chartDesc">Horizontal bar chart showing temporal ranges...</desc>
</svg>
```

**Interactive Components:**
- Use `aria-expanded` for accordions
- Use `aria-describedby` for glossary terms
- Announce dynamic updates with `aria-live`
