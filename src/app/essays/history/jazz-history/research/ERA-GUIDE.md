# Jazz Visual Essay: Era Guide

> Visual treatment specifications by era for design research

---

## Era Definitions and Visual Treatments

| Era | Date Range | Visual Treatment | Color Palette | Typography Character |
|-----|------------|------------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Pre-Jazz** | Pre-1900 | Sepia/aged paper | Warm browns, cream | Woodtype, Victorian |
| **New Orleans** | 1890s-1917 | High contrast B&W | Deep blacks, bright whites | Hand-painted, brass band posters |
| **Jazz Age** | 1920s | Warm B&W, vignettes | Gold accents, Art Deco | Geometric, glamour |
| **Swing** | 1930s-1945 | Sharp B&W | Ballroom elegance | Studio polish, clean sans-serif |
| **Bebop** | 1945-1955 | Noir B&W | Dramatic shadows | Club intimacy, angular |
| **Cool/Hard Bop** | 1955-1965 | Cool tones, early color | Blue Note aesthetic | Modernist, Reid Miles influence |
| **Free/Fusion** | 1965-1980 | Saturated color | Psychedelic, experimental | Hand-drawn, organic |
| **Modern** | 1980-present | Contemporary | Full color, digital | Clean, institutional |

## Era 1: Pre-Jazz Foundations (Pre-1900)

### Visual Characteristics
- **Source Material**: Engravings, early photography (daguerreotypes)
- **Processing**: Sepia tones, paper texture overlays
- **Imagery Scarcity**: Sparse visual documentation

### Available Archives
- Library of Congress (sheet music covers)
- Smithsonian (early instruments)
- Schomburg Center (African American musical heritage)

### Typography
- Victorian serif typefaces
- Woodtype display fonts
- Hand-lettered sheet music covers

### Color Palette
```
Primary:    #8B7355 (sepia brown)
Secondary:  #F5F5DC (cream/beige)
Accent:     #2F1810 (dark brown)
```

## Cross-Era Design Principles

### Consistent Elements
1. **Typography hierarchy**: Era-appropriate display + neutral body text
2. **Photo treatments**: Respect source era; don't over-process
3. **Transitions**: Visual bridges between eras (e.g., recording technology imagery)

### Visual Metaphors
- **Migration routes**: Maps, train lines, river paths
- **Recording technology**: Phonograph → tape → digital
- **Integration timeline**: Segregated → integrated venues

### Accessibility
- Ensure sufficient contrast in B&W eras
- Provide alt text for all historical photographs
- Consider reduced motion alternatives
