## Musicians on Their Craft and Philosophy

### Duke Ellington
> "Music is my mistress, and she plays second fiddle to no one."
- **Source**: *Music Is My Mistress* (autobiography), 1973
- **Context**: Opening statement of his autobiography

### Jelly Roll Morton
> "Jazz music is to be played sweet, soft, plenty rhythm."
- **Source**: Library of Congress interviews with Alan Lomax, May-June 1938
- **Verification**: LOC Jelly Roll Morton Collection

> "I invented jazz."
- **Source**: Frequent self-introduction, verified by multiple contemporaries
- **Context**: Exaggerated but reflects pioneering role in codifying jazz composition

### Charlie Parker
> "Music is your own experience, your own thoughts, your wisdom. If you don't live it, it won't come out of your horn."
- **Source**: Multiple documented interviews
- **Verification**: NPR, Down Beat archives

### Thelonious Monk
> "The piano ain't got no wrong notes."
- **Source**: Multiple documented sources
- **Verification**: Down Beat, NEA Jazz Masters

### Bill Evans
> "I've never approached the Piano like a thing in itself, but as a gateway to Music."
- **Source**: Documented interviews
- **Verification**: NEA Jazz Masters biography

### John Coltrane
> "I would like to be a saint."
- **Source**: Documented interviews, multiple sources
- **Verification**: NPR, Coltrane biographies

### Charles Mingus
> "Making the simple complicated is commonplace; making the complicated simple, awesomely simple, that's creativity."
- **Source**: Documented in multiple sources
- **Verification**: NEA Jazz Masters

### Sun Ra
> "Space is the place."
- **Source**: Album title (1972), documentary film title (1974)
- **Verification**: Discography, filmography; catchphrase used throughout career
- **Context**: Central to Sun Ra's Afrofuturist philosophy

## Musicians on Improvisation and Expression

### Benny Goodman
> "The most important element is still improvisation, the liberty a soloist has to stand up and play a chorus in the way he feels."
- **Source**: 1939 interview
- **Verification**: NPR archives

### Lester Young
> "I believe if you're paying a man to play, and that man is on the bandstand and can play, he should get a chance to tell his story."
- **Source**: Documented interviews
- **Context**: Origin of "tell your story" as jazz metaphor

### Count Basie
> "I think the band can swing just as much whether you play two notes or fifty notes."
- **Source**: Multiple documented interviews
- **Verification**: NPR, Smithsonian

### Ella Fitzgerald
> "I stole everything that I heard, but mostly I stole from the horns."
- **Source**: Multiple documented interviews
- **Verification**: Smithsonian, NEA Jazz Masters

### Sarah Vaughan
> "I always wanted to imitate the horns."
- **Source**: Documented interviews
- **Verification**: NEA Jazz Masters

## Musicians on Race, Society, and Struggle

### Sidney Bechet
> "The blues, and the spirituals and the remembering, and the waiting and the suffering, and the looking at the sky watching the dark come down — that's all inside the music. The music, it's my whole story."
- **Source**: *Treat It Gentle* (autobiography), 1960
- **Verification**: Multiple editions

### Nina Simone
> "To most white people, jazz means black and jazz means dirt, and that's not what I play. I play black classical music."
- **Source**: Documented interviews
- **Verification**: Multiple sources, Rock and Roll Hall of Fame

### Abdullah Ibrahim (Dollar Brand)
> "It was almost impossible for a black man to earn a living there as a musician."
- **Source**: 1979 interview (on South Africa under apartheid)
- **Verification**: South African History Online

### Mary Lou Williams
> "Jazz arises from a spirit of love, it comes from the mind and heart and goes through the fingertips."
- **Source**: *Down Beat* interview, 1964
- **Verification**: Rutgers IJS archives

> "Anything you are shows up in your music — jazz is whatever you are playing yourself, being yourself."
- **Source**: *Down Beat* interview, 1964

## On Specific Moments and Recordings

### Billie Holiday (on "Strange Fruit")
> "There wasn't even a patter of applause when I finished. Then a lone person began to clap nervously. Then suddenly everyone was clapping."
- **Source**: *Lady Sings the Blues* (autobiography), 1956
- **Context**: First performance of "Strange Fruit" at Cafe Society

### Dizzy Gillespie (on 52nd Street)
> "52nd Street was a mother" who nurtured jazz.
- **Source**: Multiple documented interviews
- **Verification**: JazzFuel, NPR

### Louis Armstrong (on King Oliver)
> "Joe Oliver is still King."
- **Source**: Stated throughout his life
- **Verification**: Louis Armstrong House Museum, multiple sources

## Critics and Historians on Jazz

### Ernest Ansermet (on Sidney Bechet, 1919)
> "I wish to set down the name of this artist of genius; as for myself, I shall never forget it, it is Sidney Bechet."
- **Source**: Swiss conductor's 1919 review
- **Verification**: Britannica, first classical praise of jazz
- **Significance**: First major classical music figure to recognize jazz artistry

### Duke Ellington (on Sidney Bechet)
> "The very epitome of jazz... everything he played in his entire life was completely original. I honestly think he was the most unique man ever to be in this music."
- **Source**: Documented tribute
- **Verification**: Multiple sources

### Bing Crosby (on Ella Fitzgerald)
> "Man, woman or child, Ella is the greatest of them all."
- **Source**: Public statements
- **Verification**: Smithsonian

### Benny Goodman (on Johnny Dodds)
> "No one ever surpassed Dodds in achieving a finer tone with the clarinet."
- **Source**: Documented interviews
- **Verification**: Down Beat archives

### Bill Evans (on Bud Powell)
> "If I had to choose one single musician for his artistic integrity, for the absolute purity of his music, it would be Bud Powell."
- **Source**: Documented interviews
- **Verification**: Multiple sources

### Jelly Roll Morton (on Freddie Keppard)
> "[He was] the first modern trumpeter" who pioneered "the Dixieland band format."
- **Source**: Library of Congress interviews, 1938
- **Verification**: LOC collection

### Sonny Rollins (on Clifford Brown)
> "Clifford was a profound influence on my personal life."
- **Source**: Documented tribute
- **Verification**: NEA Jazz Masters

## On Integration and Civil Rights

### Lionel Hampton (on Benny Goodman)
> "What [Goodman] did in those days — and they were hard days in 1937 — made it possible for Negroes to have their chance in baseball and other fields."
- **Source**: Documented interviews
- **Context**: On joining Goodman's integrated quartet

## On Jazz Diplomacy and Global Impact

### (On Jazz Diplomacy paradox)
> "The very musicians sent abroad to embody freedom often returned to segregation and daily racism."
- **Source**: USC Center on Public Diplomacy analysis
- **Context**: Cold War Jazz Ambassadors program

### (On Berlin Wall)
> "Jazz could not physically dismantle the Berlin Wall, but it inspired a spirit of individualism and resistance."
- **Source**: Cold War jazz scholarship
- **Context**: Jazz behind the Iron Curtain

## Controversial/Debunked Quotes

### Nick LaRocca
> "Our music is strictly white man's music... My contention is that the Negroes learned to play this rhythm and music from the whites."
- **Source**: Letters and interviews (widely documented)
- **Context**: LaRocca's claims rejected by historians; ODJB made first RECORDING, not first jazz
- **Usage**: Historical context for debunking origin myths
