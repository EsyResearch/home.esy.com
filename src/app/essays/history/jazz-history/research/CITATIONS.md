# Jazz Visual Essay: Citations

## Source Tier Classification

| Tier | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Tier 1** | Primary archives, peer-reviewed scholarship, institutional records | LOC, Smithsonian, academic journals |
| **Tier 2** | Authoritative secondary sources, reputable journalism | Oxford Press books, NPR, Britannica |
| **Tier 3** | General reference, specialized websites | AllMusic, jazz blogs, fan sites |

---

## Tier 1: Primary and Archival Sources

### Archives and Collections

1. **Library of Congress - William P. Gottlieb Collection**
   - 1,600 photographs (1938-1948), public domain since 2010
   - URL: https://www.loc.gov/collections/william-p-gottlieb-photographs/
   - Coverage: 250+ musicians including Holiday, Fitzgerald, Armstrong, Parker, Ellington

2. **Library of Congress - National Recording Registry**
   - "Body and Soul" - Coleman Hawkins (1939), Added 2004
   - "Strange Fruit" - Billie Holiday (1939), Added 2002
   - "Carnegie Hall Jazz Concert" - Benny Goodman (1938)
   - "Minnie the Moocher" - Cab Calloway (1931), Added 2019
   - URL: https://www.loc.gov/programs/national-recording-preservation-board/

3. **Library of Congress - Jelly Roll Morton/Alan Lomax Interviews (1938)**
   - May-June and December 1938 recordings
   - URL: https://www.loc.gov/collections/jelly-roll-morton/

4. **Hogan Jazz Archive, Tulane University**
   - 11,000 photographs, 65,000 sound recordings, 2,000 reels oral history
   - Largest collection of New Orleans jazz oral histories
   - URL: https://library.tulane.edu/locations/tusc/hogan-archive

5. **New Orleans Jazz Museum / Louisiana State Museum**
   - 25,000+ items including 12,000 photographs
   - Louis Armstrong's first cornet, first jazz recording disc (1917)
   - URL: https://nolajazzmuseum.org/

6. **Institute of Jazz Studies, Rutgers University**
   - 200,000+ recordings
   - Mary Lou Williams Collection, Women In Jazz Collection
   - URL: https://www.libraries.rutgers.edu/jazz

7. **Smithsonian National Museum of American History**
   - Duke Ellington Collection, Ella Fitzgerald Collection
   - Jazz Oral History Program (150+ interviews)
   - URL: https://americanhistory.si.edu/collections

8. **Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture (NYPL)**
   - Louis Armstrong Jazz Oral History Project
   - Don Redman, Billy Taylor, Ron Carter papers
   - URL: https://www.nypl.org/locations/schomburg

9. **National Endowment for the Arts - Jazz Masters Program**
   - Official biographies of 170+ honorees
   - URL: https://www.arts.gov/honors/jazz

### Academic Scholarship

10. **Donald M. Marquis, *In Search of Buddy Bolden: First Man of Jazz***
    - Louisiana State University Press, 1978; revised 2005
    - Definitive scholarly biography based on decades of archival research

11. **Gene H. Anderson, "The Genesis of King Oliver's Creole Jazz Band"**
    - *American Music* 12, no. 3 (1994), pp. 283-303
    - Peer-reviewed; collates oral histories, newspapers, census records

12. **Emily Landau, *Spectacular Wickedness: Sex, Race, and Memory in Storyville, New Orleans***
    - Louisiana State University Press, 2013
    - Scholarly examination debunking Storyville myths

## Tier 2: Authoritative Secondary Sources

### Books

13. **Ted Gioia, *The History of Jazz***
    - Oxford University Press, 1997; 2nd ed. 2011; 3rd ed. 2021
    - Gary Giddins: "the best book of its kind"

14. **Gary Giddins and Scott DeVeaux, *Jazz***
    - W.W. Norton, 2009
    - Leading academic textbook

15. **Alan Lomax, *Mister Jelly Roll***
    - 1950; based on Library of Congress interviews
    - Classic of jazz literature

16. **Duke Ellington, *Music Is My Mistress***
    - Autobiography, 1973

17. **Billie Holiday, *Lady Sings the Blues***
    - Autobiography, 1956

### Journalism and Documentary

18. **NPR Jazz Coverage**
    - West Coast Cool, Kind of Blue, George Wein profiles
    - "The Jazz Ambassadors" documentary coverage
    - URL: https://www.npr.org/sections/music/

19. **PBS American Masters**
    - Jazz documentaries and musician profiles
    - URL: https://www.pbs.org/americanmasters/

20. **Down Beat Magazine**
    - Founded 1934; digital archive 2008-2023
    - Internet Archive: 585 issues 1937-1961
    - Coltrane/Dolphy interviews, historical reviews

21. **Carnegie Hall Archives**
    - 1938 Goodman concert documentation
    - Hard Bop Timeline
    - URL: https://www.carnegiehall.org/Explore/Archives

### Encyclopedias and Reference

22. **Britannica - Jazz entries**
    - Multiple artist and era profiles
    - URL: https://www.britannica.com/art/jazz

23. **New Georgia Encyclopedia - Fletcher Henderson**
    - Scholarly entry on "Architect of Swing"

24. **AllMusic**
    - Artist discographies and biographies
    - URL: https://www.allmusic.com/

25. **JazzFuel**
    - 52nd Street history, venue documentation
    - URL: https://jazzfuel.com/

### Institutional Sources

26. **Blue Note Records**
    - Van Gelder Studio documentation
    - Francis Wolff photography archive (20,000+ images)

27. **USC Center on Public Diplomacy**
    - Jazz Ambassadors analysis
    - Cold War cultural diplomacy research

28. **South African History Online**
    - Abdullah Ibrahim documentation
    - URL: https://www.sahistory.org.za/

29. **Pulitzer Prize Archive**
    - *Blood on the Fields* (Wynton Marsalis, 1997)
    - First jazz Pulitzer Prize
