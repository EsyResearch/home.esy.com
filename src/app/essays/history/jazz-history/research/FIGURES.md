## Era 1: New Orleans Origins (Pre-1920)

### Buddy Bolden (1877-1931)
**Role**: Cornetist — regarded as the first jazz musician

| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| Born | September 6, 1877, New Orleans |
| Died | November 4, 1931, Jackson, Louisiana |
| Active | c. 1895-1907 |

**Key Contributions**:
- Rearranged typical New Orleans dance band to accommodate blues
- Known for powerful, loud, "wide open" playing style
- Directly inspired King Oliver, Freddie Keppard, Bunk Johnson
- Led band by 1895; by 1902 listed in city directory as musician

**Verified Quote**: None — Bolden left no recordings and no interviews

**Photo Archive**: Only known photograph (c. 1905 band photo) at Hogan Jazz Archive, Tulane University

**Source**: Donald M. Marquis, *In Search of Buddy Bolden*

---

### Jelly Roll Morton (1890-1941)
**Role**: Pianist, composer — first true jazz composer

| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| Born | October 20, 1890, New Orleans (Ferdinand Joseph La Menthe) |
| Died | July 10, 1941, Los Angeles |
| Active | c. 1902-1941 |

**Key Contributions**:
- First to write down jazz arrangements
- Composed "King Porter Stomp," "Original Jelly Roll Blues," "Black Bottom Stomp"
- Red Hot Peppers recordings (1926-1930) are landmark jazz compositions

**Verified Quotes**:
- "Jazz music is to be played sweet, soft, plenty rhythm." — to Alan Lomax, Library of Congress, 1938
- "I invented jazz" — frequent self-introduction

**Photo Archive**: Hogan Jazz Archive, Library of Congress, New Orleans Jazz Museum

**Source**: Alan Lomax, *Mister Jelly Roll*; LOC Jelly Roll Morton Collection

### King Oliver (1881-1938)
**Role**: Cornetist, bandleader — mentor to Louis Armstrong

| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| Born | December 19, 1881, Aben, Louisiana |
| Died | c. April 10, 1938, Savannah, Georgia |
| Active | c. 1907-1937 |

**Key Contributions**:
- Pioneered use of mutes in jazz
- First extensive recordings by African American jazz band (April 5, 1923, Gennett)
- Invited Louis Armstrong to Chicago (July 8, 1922)
- Developed two-cornet breaks with Armstrong

**Photo Archive**: Hogan Jazz Archive, New Orleans Jazz Museum (1921 sidewalk photo), Library of Congress

**Source**: Gene H. Anderson, "The Genesis of King Oliver's Creole Jazz Band"

### Sidney Bechet (1897-1959)
**Role**: Clarinetist, soprano saxophonist — first major jazz soloist

| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| Born | May 14, 1897, New Orleans |
| Died | May 14, 1959, Garches, France |
| Active | 1914-1959 |

**Key Contributions**:
- First influential soprano saxophonist
- First jazz musician praised by classical authority (Ernest Ansermet, 1919)
- First to tour Europe with jazz (1919)

**Verified Quotes**:
- "The blues, and the spirituals and the remembering... that's all inside the music."
- Ernest Ansermet on Bechet (1919): "I wish to set down the name of this artist of genius"
- Duke Ellington: "The very epitome of jazz"

**Photo Archive**: Hogan Jazz Archive, New Orleans Jazz Museum, Library of Congress
