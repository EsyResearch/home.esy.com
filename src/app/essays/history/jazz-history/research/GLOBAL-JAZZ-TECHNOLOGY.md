## GLOBAL JAZZ SCENES

### Paris, France
**Timeline**: James Reese Europe tours (1918) → Sidney Bechet arrives (1925) → First Festival International de Jazz (1948) → Expat community flourishes

**Key Figures**:
- **Sidney Bechet** (settled permanently 1951) - mentored first French jazzmen
- **Bud Powell** (1959-1963) - bebop piano genius
- **Kenny Clarke** (settled 1956) - bebop drummer
- **Django Reinhardt** - French gypsy jazz pioneer

**Context**: Paris became hub for African-American artists escaping segregation

---

### Tokyo, Japan
**Timeline**: American GI presence (post-WWII) → Blue Note Tokyo opens (1988)

**Key Venues**:
- **Blue Note Tokyo** (1988) - 300-seat venue, Japan's premier jazz club
- **Cotton Club Tokyo**
- **Shinjuku Pit Inn**

**Notable**: Japanese jazz fans considered among most knowledgeable globally; Tokyo scene now rivals New York

---

### Copenhagen, Denmark
**Timeline**: Jazzhus Montmartre opens (1959) → Dexter Gordon arrives (1962) → Golden era (1962-1976)

**Key Figures**:
- **Dexter Gordon** (1962-1976) - most associated artist
- **Ben Webster** (1964-1973)
- **Kenny Drew** (settled 1964)
- **Niels-Henning Orsted Pedersen** - Danish bass virtuoso

**Key Venue**: Jazzhus Montmartre - "the cozy atmosphere was the reason 10-12 of the biggest international jazz stars settled in Copenhagen"

---

### London, United Kingdom
**Timeline**: Club Eleven (1948) → Ronnie Scott's opens (1959) → First American Modern Jazz artists (1961)

**Key Venue**: **Ronnie Scott's Jazz Club** (1959-present) - "spiritual home of British jazz"

**Key Figure**: **Ronnie Scott** (1927-1996) - inspired by NYC's 52nd Street

---

### São Paulo/Rio de Janeiro, Brazil (Bossa Nova)
**Timeline**: Guitar school in Copacabana (1956) → "Chega de Saudade" (1958) → Carnegie Hall concert (1962) → Getz/Gilberto (1964)

**Key Figures**:
- **Tom Jobim** (1927-1994) - "Father of bossa nova"
- **João Gilberto** (1931-2019) - created syncopated guitar style
- **Stan Getz** - American collaborator

**Impact**: "The Girl from Ipanema" became one of the most recorded songs of all time

---

### Cape Town, South Africa
**Timeline**: Jazz Epistles (1959) → Apartheid restrictions → "Mannenberg" (1974) → Ibrahim returns (early 1990s) → Mandela inauguration (1994)

**Key Figure**: **Abdullah Ibrahim/Dollar Brand** (b. 1934) - "leading figure in Cape jazz"

**Anthem**: "Mannenberg" (1974) - "unofficial national anthem of South Africa, theme tune of the anti-apartheid movement"

**Quote**: "It was almost impossible for a black man to earn a living there as a musician." - Abdullah Ibrahim, 1979

---

### Havana, Cuba (Afro-Cuban Jazz)
**Timeline**: Valdes family musical laboratory (1940s-50s) → Irakere founded (1973) → Dizzy discovers Irakere (1977) → Grammy Award (1980)

**Key Figures**:
- **Chucho Valdés** (b. 1941) - 7 Grammy Awards, 2025 NEA Jazz Master
- **Arturo Sandoval** - Irakere trumpeter
- **Paquito D'Rivera** - Irakere saxophonist

**Innovation**: Irakere - first use of batá, abakuá, ararà drums in modern Cuban jazz

---

### Lagos, Nigeria (Afrobeat Intersection)
**Timeline**: Fela studies in London (1950s) → Returns to Lagos (1960s) → Labels music "Afrobeat" (1968) → Afrika Shrine residency (1970-75)

**Key Figure**: **Fela Anikulapo Kuti** (1938-1997) - "principal innovator of Afrobeat"

**Jazz Connections**: Roy Ayers, Randy Weston, Branford Marsalis collaborations

---

### Berlin, Germany (Cold War)
**Context**: West Berlin had Jazz at the Philharmonic, American GI clubs; East Berlin/GDR had state censorship, underground "rib records" on X-ray plates

**Quote**: "Jazz could not physically dismantle the Berlin Wall, but it inspired a spirit of individualism and resistance"

## TECHNOLOGY TIMELINE

| Era | Date | Technology | Jazz Impact |
|-----|------|------------|-------------|
| Acoustic | Pre-1925 | Horn collection, direct cutting | 3-min limit; poor frequency range |
| Electrical | 1925+ | Microphones, vacuum tubes | Fuller, more natural sound |
| 78 RPM | 1925+ | Standardized speed | ~3 minutes per side maximum |
| LP Revolution | June 21, 1948 | 33 1/3 rpm, 22.5 min/side | Extended improvisations possible |
| Multitrack | 1953+ | Les Paul's 8-track | Studio becomes instrument |
| Stereo | Late 1950s | Two-channel recording | Spatial separation |
| FM Radio | WBGO (1979) | 24-hour jazz broadcasting | Wider audience reach |
| Digital | 1970s+ | CD, Pro Tools | RVG remastering editions |

### The LP Revolution (June 21, 1948)
- Columbia introduces 12-inch LP at Waldorf Astoria
- Up to 22.5 minutes per side (vs. 3 minutes on 78)
- Blue Note, Prestige shift to album format mid-1950s
- "Revolutionized the jazz industry's adoption of the long-playing record"

### Rudy Van Gelder (1924-2016)
- "Several thousand sessions" recorded
- Blue Note, Prestige, Verve, CTI labels
- From 1999: Remastered 250+ Blue Note classics to 24-bit digital
- Studio entered National Register of Historic Places (2022)

## FUSION AND CONTEMPORARY FIGURES

### Herbie Hancock (b. 1940)
- Miles Davis Second Great Quintet (1963)
- *Head Hunters* (1973) - first jazz album to win RIAA Platinum
- 14 Grammy Awards including Album of the Year (2007)

### Wayne Shorter (1933-2023)
- Art Blakey's Jazz Messengers, Miles Davis Quintet
- Co-founded Weather Report (1970)
- 12 Grammy Awards

### Chick Corea (1941-2021)
- Return to Forever (1972-1977)
- 28 Grammy Awards - most for any jazz artist

### Weather Report (1970-1986)
- Joe Zawinul, Wayne Shorter, Jaco Pastorius
- "Indisputably the premier fusion band"
- "Birdland" (1977)

### Pat Metheny (b. 1954)
- 20 Grammy Awards in 10 categories
- ECM Records period (1975-1984)
- NEA Jazz Master (2018)

### Wynton Marsalis (b. 1961)
- Artistic Director, Jazz at Lincoln Center
- *Blood on the Fields* - first jazz Pulitzer Prize (1997)
- 9 Grammy Awards

### Esperanza Spalding (b. 1984)
- First jazz artist to win Grammy Best New Artist (2011)
- Harvard professor (2017-2022)
- Librettist for Wayne Shorter's opera *Iphigenia*

### Contemporary (2020s)
- **Kamasi Washington** - *The Epic* (2015), *Fearless Movement* (2024)
- **Robert Glasper** - jazz/R&B/hip-hop fusion
- **Terrace Martin**, **Snarky Puppy**, **Christian Scott**

## INSTITUTIONS

### Jazz at Lincoln Center (1987-present)
- 1991: Wynton Marsalis becomes Artistic Director
- 2004: Frederick P. Rose Hall opens - first venue designed for jazz

### Berklee College of Music (1945-present)
- Founded as Schillinger House
- 1975: Students create The Real Book
- 2004: Hal Leonard legitimizes Real Book

### Jazz Diplomacy / Jazz Ambassadors (1955-present)
- Willis Conover's VOA "Music USA" (1955-1996)
- Millions of listeners behind Iron Curtain
- Benny Goodman Soviet tour (1962)
- Duke Ellington Soviet tour (1971)

**The Paradox**: "The very musicians sent abroad to embody freedom often returned to segregation and daily racism"

### Newport Jazz Festival (1954-present)
- Founded by George Wein, Elaine and Louis Lorillard
- "Established the template for major jazz festivals worldwide"

### Montreux Jazz Festival (1967-present)
- Founded by Claude Nobs with Atlantic Records' Ertegun brothers
- Second-largest annual jazz festival in the world
- UNESCO Memory of the World archive
