## WOMEN FIGURES

### Early Era (1900s-1920s)

#### Bessie Smith (1894-1937)
**"Empress of the Blues"**
- 160 recordings; "Down Hearted Blues" (1923) sold 2M copies
- Recorded with Fletcher Henderson, Benny Goodman, Louis Armstrong
- **Quote**: "It's a long old road, but I know I'm gonna find the end."
- **Photo Archives**: LOC Gottlieb Collection, Carl Van Vechten Collection

#### Ma Rainey (1886-1939)
**"Mother of the Blues"**
- 92 songs for Paramount (1923-1928)
- "See See Rider," "Prove It on Me," "Bo-Weevil Blues"
- Grammy Lifetime Achievement (2023)
- Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1990)

#### Lil Hardin Armstrong (1898-1971)
**Pianist, Composer, Arranger**
- Played piano for all Hot Fives and Hot Sevens recordings
- Drove Louis Armstrong's solo career; convinced him to leave King Oliver
- Compositions: "Struttin' with Some Barbecue," "Just for a Thrill"
- Collapsed and died at Louis Armstrong memorial concert

---

### Swing Era (1930s-1940s)

#### Billie Holiday (1915-1959)
**"Lady Day"**
- Composer: "Don't Explain," "Fine and Mellow," "God Bless the Child"
- "Strange Fruit" (1939) - "most anguished expression of protest against man's inhumanity to man"
- **Quote**: "There wasn't even a patter of applause when I finished. Then a lone person began to clap nervously."
- **Photo Archives**: LOC Gottlieb Collection (public domain)

#### Ella Fitzgerald (1917-1996)
**"First Lady of Song"**
- First African American woman to win Grammy (1958)
- 14 Grammy Awards; 40M+ albums sold
- 19-volume "Songbook" series (1956-1964)
- **Quote about her**: "Man, woman or child, Ella is the greatest of them all." - Bing Crosby
- **Photo Archives**: Smithsonian, Herman Leonard Collection

#### Mary Lou Williams (1910-1981)
**"The First Lady of Jazz" - Pianist, Composer, Arranger, Educator**
- Chief arranger for Andy Kirk's Twelve Clouds of Joy (1929-1942)
- Arrangements for Ellington, Goodman, Armstrong, Dorsey
- Mentor to Monk, Parker, Powell, Gillespie
- Founded Mary Records - first such company by a woman in jazz
- **Quote**: "Jazz arises from a spirit of love, it comes from the mind and heart and goes through the fingertips."
- **Archives**: Rutgers IJS holds Mary Lou Williams Collection

#### Hazel Scott (1920-1981)
**Virtuoso Pianist, Civil Rights Activist**
- First African American woman to host own TV show (1950)
- Auditioned for Juilliard at age 8
- "Darling of Cafe Society" - first integrated nightclub
- Refused to perform for segregated audiences
- By 1945: earning $75,000/year (over $1M today)

### Modern Era (1950s-Present)

#### Sarah Vaughan (1924-1990)
**"Sassy," "The Divine One"**
- Won Apollo Theater contest (1942)
- With Ella, elevated jazz vocalist as equal to instrumentalists
- **Quote**: "I always wanted to imitate the horns."
- Grammy Lifetime Achievement (1989), NEA Jazz Master (1989)

#### Melba Liston (1926-1999)
**Trombonist, Arranger - Major Behind-the-Scenes Figure**
- First woman trombonist in major big bands (outside all-female bands)
- Personally recruited by Dizzy Gillespie (1948)
- 40-year collaboration with Randy Weston
- Arrangements for Ellington, Basie, Quincy Jones
- Often worked as ghostwriter
- NEA Jazz Masters (1987)

#### Betty Carter (1930-1998)
**"Betty Bebop"**
- Founded Bet-Car Records (1971) - early independent label
- Legendary mentor (with Art Blakey) for young musicians
- NEA Jazz Masters (1992), National Medal of Arts (1997)

#### Abbey Lincoln (1930-2010)
**Singer, Activist**
- *We Insist! Freedom Now Suite* (1960) - seminal civil rights recording
- Participated in UN protest after Lumumba assassination
- Produced "Naturally 62" fashion show - opened conversation about Black hair
- NEA Jazz Masters (2003)

#### Shirley Horn (1934-2005)
**"D.C.'s Own" - Pianist-Vocalist**
- Invited by Miles Davis to play Village Vanguard intermission sets
- "Here's to Life" (1992) - signature song
- Kennedy Center Honor (2004), NEA Jazz Masters (2004)

#### Nina Simone (1933-2003)
**"High Priestess of Soul"**
- Trained for classical concert career at Juilliard
- **Quote**: "To most white people, jazz means black and jazz means dirt, and that's not what I play. I play black classical music."
- Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (2018)

#### Alice Coltrane (1937-2007)
**Pianist, Harpist, Spiritual Leader**
- Replaced McCoy Tyner in John Coltrane's group (1966)
- One of few harpists in jazz history
- Major proponent of spiritual jazz
- Founded Vedantic Center (1975)

#### Carla Bley (1936-2023)
**Composer, Arranger, Record Label Pioneer**
- Co-founded Jazz Composers Guild (1964)
- Founded JCOA Records, WATT Records, New Music Distribution Service
- NEA Jazz Masters (2015)

## ALL-WOMEN BANDS

### International Sweethearts of Rhythm (1937-1949)
- First racially-integrated all-female band in US
- Named "America's #1 All-Girl Orchestra" by Down Beat (1944)
- Diverse membership: Chinese, Mexican, Indian, Hawaiian members
- Defied Jim Crow laws touring the South
- Once performed for 11,000 in Kansas City
- National Recording Registry (2011)

## BARRIERS AND ACHIEVEMENTS

### Union Membership
- 800 women in 1930s New York Musicians Union
- Unions were segregated; Black musicians often denied full membership
- Seattle's Local 458: Women consistently ~10% of "colored" union

### Recording Discrimination
- 1940s Down Beat stated women could be "good girls" or "play like men"
- 1958 "Great Day in Harlem" photo: Only 3 women of 57 jazz greats

### Behind-the-Scenes Pioneers
- **Mary Lou Williams**: Arrangements for Ellington, Goodman, etc.
- **Lil Hardin Armstrong**: Managed Louis Armstrong's career
- **Melba Liston**: Ghostwriter for Gillespie, Jones, Weston

## ARCHIVES

### Library of Congress - William P. Gottlieb Collection
- 1,600 negatives (1938-1948)
- Public domain since 2010
- 250+ musicians including Holiday, Fitzgerald, Armstrong

### Institute of Jazz Studies (Rutgers)
- 200,000+ recordings
- Mary Lou Williams Collection
- Women In Jazz Collection
- Billie Holiday's jewelry, Ella Fitzgerald's gown

### Hogan Jazz Archive (Tulane)
- 2,000+ reels oral history (largest collection)
- 40,000+ recordings
- 6,000+ photographs
- Specialty: New Orleans jazz

### Schomburg Center (NYPL)
- 11+ million items on African American experience
- Louis Armstrong Jazz Oral History Project
- Don Redman, Billy Taylor, Ron Carter papers

### Smithsonian NMAH
- Duke Ellington, Ella Fitzgerald collections
- Jazz Oral History Program (150+ interviews)
- Herman Leonard, Francis Wolff, Chuck Stewart photos

### New Orleans Jazz Museum
- 25,000+ items
- Louis Armstrong's first cornet
- First jazz recording disc (1917)
- 12,000 photographs
