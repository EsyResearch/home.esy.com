# Word Family — The Descendants of φωνή (phonē)

> Complete catalogue of English words derived from Greek φωνή, with morphological analysis, first attestation dates, and source references.

## The PIE Root

**\*bʰeh₂-** — "to speak, say, tell"

This Proto-Indo-European root produced two major Greek branches:
1. **φημί** (phēmi) — "I say, I speak" → φήμη (phēmē, "fame, report") → Latin *fāma* → English *fame*, *defame*, *infamous*
2. **φωνή** (phonē) — "voice, sound, utterance" → the entire -phone/-phon- family

The root also produced Latin **for** ("to speak") → English *confess*, *profess*, *fate* (< Latin *fātum*, "thing spoken/decreed")

**Sources**: S1 (Beekes), S4 (Chantraine), S16 (Wiktionary PIE reconstruction)

## Morphological Analysis

### How -phone/-phon- Functions in English

| Position | Form | Function | Examples |
|----------|------|----------|----------|
| Suffix | -phone | "sound-making/receiving instrument" | telephone, microphone, saxophone |
| Suffix | -phony | "quality of sound" | cacophony, euphony, symphony |
| Prefix | phono- | "relating to sound/voice" | phonograph, phonology, phoneme |
| Standalone | phone | "telephone device" (clipped) | phone book, smartphone, phone call |

### The Clipping Process (telephone → phone)

| Stage | Form | Period | Status | Source |
|-------|------|--------|--------|--------|
| 1 | telephone | 1835–1878 | Full form, only form | S3 |
| 2 | 'phone | 1878–~1920s | Clipped with apostrophe (marking omission) | S3, S14 |
| 3 | phone | ~1920s–present | Standalone word, no apostrophe, fully independent | S3, S10 |
| 4 | phone + X | 1901–present | Generates own compounds (phone booth, smartphone) | S14 |

**Key linguistic insight** (S6, Marchand): Once a clipped form begins generating its own compounds, it is no longer a shortened version of the parent word — it is a new lexical item. *Phone* achieved this by 1901.

## Cross-Linguistic Comparison

How other languages name the telephone/phone:

| Language | Word | Literal Meaning | Etymology |
|----------|------|----------------|-----------|
| French | téléphone | far-sound | Greek tele + phonē (coined ~1828) |
| German | Telefon | far-sound | Greek tele + phonē (via Reis, 1861) |
| Spanish | teléfono | far-sound | Greek tele + phonē |
| Italian | telefono | far-sound | Greek tele + phonē |
| Japanese | 電話 (denwa) | electric-speech | Chinese characters: 電 (electric) + 話 (speech) |
| Mandarin | 电话 (diànhuà) | electric-speech | Same characters as Japanese |
| Korean | 전화 (jeonhwa) | electric-speech | Sino-Korean reading of same characters |
| Arabic | هاتف (hātif) | caller/whisperer | From Arabic root h-t-f (to call out) |
| Hebrew | טלפון (telefon) | far-sound | Borrowed from European languages |
| Finnish | puhelin | speaking-instrument | From Finnish *puhe* (speech) + -lin (instrument) |
| Swahili | simu | — | From English "seem" (early telephone operators' word) |

**Observation**: European languages universally borrowed the Greek compound. East Asian languages calqued the concept ("electric speech"). Arabic and Finnish coined native-root words. This makes *telephone/phone* one of the most successful Greek-origin internationalisms.
