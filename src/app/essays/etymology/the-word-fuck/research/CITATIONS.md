## Verification Notes

1. **Proto-Germanic reconstruction**: The form *fukkōną is reconstructed based on comparative evidence from Middle Dutch *fokken*, Middle Low German *fukken*, and dialectal Norwegian *fukka*. Not directly attested but follows standard historical linguistic methodology.

2. **Roger Fuckebythenavele**: Discovery announced by Dr. Paul Booth in 2015. The name appears in Chester County Court Rolls (1310-1311) as a byname, suggesting the word was already established in vernacular speech with sexual connotations.

3. **Acronym debunking**: All acronym etymologies (FUCK, SHIT, GOLF, etc.) are modern folk etymologies. Acronymic word formation of this type did not exist in English until the 20th century. The OED and all major etymological authorities reject these origins.

4. **Dictionary gap (1795-1965)**: The 170-year absence from general dictionaries is documented in Sheidlower (2009) and reflects Victorian-era obscenity laws (Comstock Act, 1873) and publishing standards rather than actual disuse of the word.
