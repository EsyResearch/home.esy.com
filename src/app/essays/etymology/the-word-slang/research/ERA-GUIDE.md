# ERA-GUIDE.md — The Word "Slang"

## Era Treatment Guide

### Era 1: Criminal Underground (1720s–1780s)

**Visual Mood**: Dark, claustrophobic, secretive

**Color Palette**:
- Primary: Sepia, charcoal
- Accent: Candlelight gold (#c9a227)
- Background: Aged vellum (#f5f0e6)

**Typography**:
- Display: Blackletter (danger, secrecy)
- Body: Caslon (period authenticity)
- Hierarchy: Dense, irregular

**Layout**:
- Dense columns
- Heavy shadows
- Narrow margins
- Claustrophobic feel

**Cultural Context**:
- Gin Lane London
- Thieves' dens
- Tyburn executions
- Hogarth's world

**Reference Materials**:
- Hogarth prints
- Broadside ballads
- Tavern scenes

---

### Era 2: Lexicographic Dawn (1785–1860)

**Visual Mood**: Scholarly subversion, encyclopedic parody

**Color Palette**:
- Primary: Cream paper, black ink
- Accent: Sealing wax red (#8b2332)
- Background: Off-white

**Typography**:
- Display: Didone (Bodoni, Didot)
- Body: Baskerville
- Hierarchy: Classical, symmetrical

**Layout**:
- Dictionary-style columns
- Neoclassical elegance
- White space as status
- Formal rules

**Cultural Context**:
- Johnson's shadow
- Grose's rebellion
- Subversive scholarship

**Reference Materials**:
- Dictionary title pages
- Engraved portraits
- Period bookplates

---

### Era 3: Victorian Gatekeeping (1860–1911)

**Visual Mood**: Institutional, authoritative, slightly oppressive

**Color Palette**:
- Primary: High-contrast B&W
- Accent: Pure black (#000000)
- Background: Clean white

**Typography**:
- Display: Slab-serif (Clarendon, Rockwell)
- Body: Century Schoolbook
- Hierarchy: Industrial, ruled

**Layout**:
- Dense columns
- Hairline rules
- Formal page architecture
- Encyclopedia format

**Cultural Context**:
- Empire and propriety
- Class anxiety
- Linguistic gatekeeping
- Bradley's Britannica

**Reference Materials**:
- Encyclopedias
- Victorian scholarly works
- Period photographs

---

### Era 4: American Expansion (1919–1950s)

**Visual Mood**: Democratic, journalistic, energetic

**Color Palette**:
- Primary: Newsprint cream (#faf8f0)
- Accent: Printer's red (#cc3333)
- Text: Black

**Typography**:
- Display: American Typewriter, Courier
- Body: News Gothic, Franklin Gothic
- Hierarchy: Open, horizontal

**Layout**:
- More open than British
- Horizontal emphasis
- Space to breathe
- Journalistic grid

**Cultural Context**:
- Mencken's Baltimore
- Jazz age vocabulary
- American cultural independence
- Anti-British snobbery

**Reference Materials**:
- Mencken photographs
- American newspapers
- Urban street scenes

---

### Era 5: Academic Legitimation (1960s–1990s)

**Visual Mood**: Clean, systematic, campus-oriented

**Color Palette**:
- Primary: Neutral grays
- Accent: Muted teal (#2d6a6a)
- Background: White/off-white

**Typography**:
- Display: Humanist sans (Meta, Gill Sans)
- Body: Charter, Utopia
- Hierarchy: Systematic, accessible

**Layout**:
- Generous white space
- Clean hierarchy
- Sidebars for definitions
- Academic conventions

**Cultural Context**:

- Eble's research
- Labov's tension

**Reference Materials**:
- University campuses
- Academic textbooks
- Lecture halls

---

### Era 6: Digital Present (1999–present)

**Visual Mood**: Interface-driven, democratic, chaotic

**Color Palette**:
- Primary: Neutral backgrounds
- Accent: Interface blue (#2563eb)
- Highlights: Bright, saturated

**Typography**:
- Display: Variable fonts (Inter, Space Grotesk)
- Body: System fonts
- Hierarchy: UI patterns

**Layout**:

- Interface elements
- Upvote indicators
- Dense information

**Cultural Context**:

**Reference Materials**:

- Text messages

## Transition Guidelines

| From Era | To Era | Transition |
|----------|--------|------------|
| 1 → 2 | Underground → Lexicographic | Paper brightens, type formalizes |
| 2 → 3 | Lexicographic → Victorian | Rules appear, contrast increases |
| 3 → 4 | Victorian → American | Layout opens, red appears |
| 4 → 5 | American → Academic | Palette neutralizes, cleaner lines |
| 5 → 6 | Academic → Digital | Interface elements emerge |
