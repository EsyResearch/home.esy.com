# TIMELINE.md — The Word "Slang"

## Chronological Milestones

| # | Year | Locale | Source | Sense/Event | Significance |
|---|------|--------|--------|-------------|--------------|
| 1 | c.1520s | England | Early attestations | *Pedlar's French* coined | Earliest English term for criminal/vagabond language |
| 2 | 1566 | England | Harman, *Caveat* | First cant glossary published | Systematic documentation of underworld vocabulary begins |
| 3 | 1699 | London | B.E. Gentleman | First slang dictionary | *Canting Crew* dictionary published |
| 4 | 1724 | London | *London Journal* | *Slango* appears | "Trade and Slango of poetry" — earliest trace of word form |
| 5 | 1730s | London | Stage plays | Character names Slang/Slango | Theatrical association with deception |
| 6 | 1741 | London | Mary Young (Jenny Diver) account | Verb *to slang* | "slanging the gentry mort" — first verb usage |
| 7 | **1756** | London | Toldervy, *Two Orphans* | **Noun *slang*** | **FIRST ATTESTATION**: "knew the slang well" |
| 8 | 1785 | London | Grose, *Vulgar Tongue* | Major slang dictionary | 9,000 words; fieldwork methodology |
| 9 | 1801 | England | OED attestation | Meaning expansion | "Jargon of any particular profession" |
| 10 | 1818 | England | OED attestation | Meaning expansion | "Informal language characterized by vividness" |
| 11 | 1859 | London | Hotten, *Dictionary* | Etymological histories | First scholarly treatment of slang varieties |
| 12 | 1860s | England | Isaac Taylor | Etymology proposed | "Narrow strip of land" theory advanced |
| 13 | 1911 | Britain | Bradley, *Britannica* | Canonical definition | "Conscious offence against propriety" |
| 14 | 1919 | USA | Mencken, *American Language* | American rehabilitation | Slang defended as creative, respectable |
| 15 | 1937 | London | Partridge, *Dictionary* | Standard reference | 7 editions through 1970s |
| 16 | 1996 | USA | Eble, *Slang and Sociability* | Academic legitimization | Sociolinguistic functional definition |
| 17 | 1999 | USA | Urban Dictionary founded | Democratization | User-generated slang lexicography begins |
| 18 | 2016 | USA | Liberman, OUPblog | Etymology claim | "The origin of slang is known!" |
