## Key Figures Referenced

### Liu Bang (劉邦, 256–195 BCE)
- Founder of Han Dynasty (206 BCE)
- Title: King of Han (漢王)
- Quote attribution: Traditional (likely apocryphal)

### Emperor Wu of Han (漢武帝, 156–87 BCE)
- Expanded Han territory to Korea, Vietnam, Central Asia
- Established Confucianism as state ideology

### Sima Qian (司馬遷, c. 145–86 BCE)
- Father of Chinese History
- Author of *Shiji*
- Quote source: "Letter to Ren An"

### King Sejong the Great (世宗大王, 1397–1450)
- Creator of Hangul (1443)
- Quote source: *Hunminjeongeum* Preface (1446)

### King Gojong (고종, 1852–1919)
- Emperor of Korean Empire
- Renamed Korea "Daehan Jeguk" (大韓帝國) in 1897
- Used 韓 (Korean Han), not 漢 (Chinese Han)

### Prince Shōtoku (聖徳太子, 574–622 CE)
- Promoted Chinese characters (Kanji 漢字) in Japan
- Quote source: 607 CE Letter to Sui Emperor

### Nguyễn Trãi (阮廌, 1380–1442)
- Vietnamese independence literature
- Quote source: *Bình Ngô đại cáo* (1428)

### Liang Qichao (梁啟超, 1873–1929)
- Coined "Zhonghua minzu" (中華民族) in 1902
- Quote source: "Observations on Chinese History" (1905)

### Sun Yat-sen (孫中山, 1866–1925)
- Founder of Republic of China
- Quote source: Post-1911 Revolution statements on Han Chinese identity

## Script Evolution

### Oracle Bone Script (甲骨文, c. 1250–1046 BCE)
Carved on animal bones and turtle shells

### Bronze Script (金文, c. 1046–256 BCE)
Cast on bronze ritual vessels

### Seal Script (篆書, c. 221–206 BCE)
Standardized under Qin Dynasty

### Clerical Script (隸書, c. 206 BCE–220 CE)
Developed during Han Dynasty

### Regular Script (楷書, c. 3rd century CE–present)
Standard modern script

## Historical Timeline

- c. 400 BCE: Han River named in central China
- 206 BCE: Han Dynasty founded by Liu Bang
- 141 BCE: Emperor Wu begins expansion
- 108 BCE: Han commanderies established in Korea
- 220 CE: Han Dynasty falls, "Han" persists as identity
- c. 400 CE: Kanji systematized in Japan
- 607 CE: Shōtoku's embassy to Sui China
- 939 CE: Vietnam independent, continues using Chữ Hán
- 1443: Hangul created by King Sejong
- 1897: Korean Empire adopts 大韓帝國 (Daehan Jeguk)
- 1902: Liang Qichao coins "Zhonghua minzu"
- 1949: PRC codifies "Han Chinese" (漢族)
- 2014: Source Han fonts released

## Research Notes

### Character Distinction
- **Chinese Han (漢)**: Water radical (氵), from Han River → Han Dynasty → Han Chinese → Han characters
- **Korean Han (韓)**: Different character, from Samhan confederacies → meaning "great/leader" → Korean identity
