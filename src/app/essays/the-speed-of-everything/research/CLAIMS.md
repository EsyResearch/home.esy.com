# Claims & Source Tracking: The Speed of Everything

## Verified Speed Claims

### 1. Continental Drift — Tectonic Plate Motion
- **Claim**: Tectonic plates move at 1–10 cm/year (approximately 1–3 nanometers per second)
- **Specific value**: The North American plate moves at ~2.5 cm/year relative to the Mid-Atlantic Ridge
- **Source**: USGS, "Understanding plate motions" — https://pubs.usgs.gov/gip/dynamic/understanding.html
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — USGS reports 1–10 cm/year range; GPS measurements confirm
- **Tier**: 1

### 2. Human Hair Growth
- **Claim**: Human hair grows at approximately 15 cm/year (~5 nm/s)
- **Source**: Journal of Investigative Dermatology, Saitoh et al. (1970) — standard reference for hair growth rates
- **Additional source**: Healthline medical review — https://www.healthline.com/health/how-fast-does-hair-grow
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — 0.35–0.45 mm/day is the accepted clinical range
- **Tier**: 1

### 3. Human Walking Speed
- **Claim**: Average human walking speed is 1.4 m/s (5 km/h or 3.1 mph)
- **Source**: Bohannon, R.W. (1997). "Comfortable and maximum walking speed of adults aged 20–79 years." Age and Ageing, 26(1), 15–19
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — meta-analysis of gait studies
- **Tier**: 1

### 4. Speed of Sound in Air
- **Claim**: The speed of sound in dry air at 20°C is 343 m/s (1,235 km/h, Mach 1)
- **Source**: NIST / CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics
- **Additional**: Kinsler, L.E. et al. "Fundamentals of Acoustics" (4th ed.), Wiley
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — standard physics reference value
- **Tier**: 1

### 5. Speed of Sound in Water
- **Claim**: Sound travels at approximately 1,480 m/s in water at 20°C — about 4.3× faster than in air
- **Source**: NOAA Ocean Acoustics — https://oceanexplorer.noaa.gov/explorations/sound01/background/acoustics/acoustics.html
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — NOAA standard reference
- **Tier**: 1

### 6. Bullet Speed — Typical Rifle
- **Claim**: A typical rifle bullet travels at 900–1,500 m/s (Mach 2.6–4.4)
- **Specific**: 5.56mm NATO — ~940 m/s muzzle velocity
- **Source**: Carlucci, D.E. & Jacobson, S.S. "Ballistics: Theory and Design of Guns and Ammunition" (2nd ed.), CRC Press
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — within standard ballistic references
- **Tier**: 1

### 7. Earth's Orbital Velocity
- **Claim**: Earth orbits the Sun at approximately 29,800 m/s (107,000 km/h)
- **Source**: NASA Solar System Exploration — https://solarsystem.nasa.gov/planets/earth/overview/
- **Additional**: Williams, D.R. "Earth Fact Sheet" — NASA GSFC
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — derived from orbital radius and period
- **Tier**: 1

### 8. Earth's Rotational Speed at Equator
- **Claim**: A point on the equator moves at ~465 m/s (1,674 km/h) due to Earth's rotation
- **Source**: Calculated from Earth's circumference (40,075 km) / 24 hours; confirmed by NASA Earth Fact Sheet
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — straightforward geometric calculation
- **Tier**: 1

### 9. Speed of Light in Vacuum
- **Claim**: The speed of light in vacuum is exactly 299,792,458 m/s (by definition since 1983)
- **Source**: Bureau International des Poids et Mesures (BIPM), definition of the metre
- **Additional**: NIST fundamental constants — https://physics.nist.gov/cgi-bin/cuu/Value?c
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — this is a defined constant, not measured
- **Tier**: 1

### 10. Speed of Electrical Signals in Copper Wire
- **Claim**: Electrical signals propagate at ~2/3 the speed of light in copper wire (~2×10⁸ m/s)
- **Source**: Griffiths, D.J. "Introduction to Electrodynamics" (4th ed.), Cambridge University Press
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — signal propagation velocity depends on medium; ~0.67c is standard for copper
- **Tier**: 1

### 11. Nerve Impulse Speed
- **Claim**: Nerve impulses travel at 1–120 m/s depending on fiber type; typical motor neurons ~50–120 m/s
- **Source**: Purves, D. et al. "Neuroscience" (6th ed.), Sinauer Associates
- **Additional**: Kandel, E.R. et al. "Principles of Neural Science" (5th ed.)
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — myelinated motor neurons: 70–120 m/s; unmyelinated pain fibers: 0.5–2 m/s
- **Tier**: 1

### 12. Solar System's Galactic Orbital Speed
- **Claim**: The solar system orbits the Milky Way center at ~220 km/s (792,000 km/h)
- **Source**: Reid, M.J. et al. (2014). "Trigonometric Parallaxes of High Mass Star Forming Regions." ApJ, 783(2), 130
- **Additional**: IAU recommended value ~220 km/s; some recent measurements suggest ~230 km/s
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — well-established within ±10 km/s
- **Tier**: 1

### 13. Glacier Flow Speed
- **Claim**: Most glaciers flow at 10–200 m/year; fast glaciers like Jakobshavn reach 40+ m/day
- **Source**: Cuffey, K.M. & Paterson, W.S.B. "The Physics of Glaciers" (4th ed.), Academic Press
- **Additional**: Joughin, I. et al. (2014), Science — Jakobshavn velocity measurements
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — wide range depending on glacier type and conditions
- **Tier**: 1

### 14. Blood Flow Speed
- **Claim**: Blood in the aorta flows at ~1 m/s; in capillaries, it slows to ~0.001 m/s (1 mm/s)
- **Source**: Guyton, A.C. & Hall, J.E. "Textbook of Medical Physiology" (13th ed.)
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — standard cardiovascular physiology
- **Tier**: 1

### 15. International Space Station Orbital Speed
- **Claim**: The ISS orbits at approximately 7,660 m/s (27,600 km/h)
- **Source**: NASA ISS fact sheet — https://www.nasa.gov/feature/facts-and-figures
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — consistent with low Earth orbit calculations
- **Tier**: 1

### 16. Fastest Human (Usain Bolt)
- **Claim**: Peak speed of Usain Bolt during the 100m: 12.27 m/s (44.17 km/h), reached at ~65m mark
- **Source**: Graubner, R. & Nixdorf, E. (2011). "Biomechanical Analysis of the Sprint and Hurdle Events at the 2009 IAAF World Championships in Athletics." IAAF New Studies in Athletics
- **Verification**: ✅ Confirmed — biomechanical analysis of the 2009 Berlin World Championship race
- **Tier**: 1
