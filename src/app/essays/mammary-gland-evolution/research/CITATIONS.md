## Key Scientific Facts

### Evolutionary Origins
- **310 million years ago**: Synapsid ancestors (proto-mammals)
- **Original function**: Apocrine gland secretion onto parchment-shelled eggs
- **Evolution pathway**: Moisture → antimicrobial → nutritive → milk
- **Key insight**: Lactation predates live birth

### Human Breast Anatomy
- **Lobes**: 15-20 per breast
- **Alveoli**: Up to 100,000 per breast (milk-producing units)
- **Structure**: Alveoli → ducts → lactiferous sinuses → nipple
- **Unique feature**: Permanent breast development (unique among primates)

## Species Comparisons

### Platypus (Monotreme)
- **No nipples**: Most primitive mammalian form
- **Milk delivery**: Seeps through specialized skin patches
- **Nursing method**: Babies lap milk from fur
- **Significance**: Represents ancestral lactation state

### Kangaroo (Marsupial)
- **4 nipples, 2 milk types**: Simultaneous different compositions
- **Adaptation**: Different milk for different-aged joeys
- **Pouch nursing**: Extended development outside womb

### Cow (Ungulate)
- **4 teats, 1 udder**: Concentrated mammary system
- **Production**: 6-7 gallons/day
- **Domestication**: ~10,000 years ago
- **Status**: World's primary commercial milk source

### Human (Primate)
- **2 breasts**: Pectoral position (unique among mammals)
- **Permanent development**: Breasts enlarged outside lactation
- **Milk complexity**: 200+ unique oligosaccharides
- **Breastfeeding**: Recommended for first 6 months minimum

### Blue Whale (Cetacean)
- **Hidden mammary slits**: Nipples retract for streamlining
- **Fat content**: 35-50% (vs 4% human)
- **Production**: ~200 liters/day
- **Calf growth**: ~200 lbs/day

### Seal (Pinniped)
- **50% fat milk**: Richest of all mammals
- **Pup growth**: 4-5 lbs/day
- **Maternal sacrifice**: Mothers fast during nursing, losing 40% body weight

## Anatomical Terminology

### Alveolus (pl. Alveoli)
- Milk-producing unit
- Hollow sphere lined with epithelial cells
- Extracts nutrients from bloodstream
- Surrounded by myoepithelial cells

### Myoepithelial Cells
- Muscle-like cells surrounding alveoli
- Contract during let-down reflex
- Triggered by oxytocin hormone

### Lactiferous Ducts
- Transport milk from alveoli to nipple
- Converge at lactiferous sinuses
- 15-20 ducts per breast

### Cooper's Ligaments
- Suspensory ligaments
- Support breast tissue structure
- Connect breast to chest wall

## Evolutionary Timeline

### 310 Million Years Ago
- Synapsid ancestors develop apocrine skin glands
- Function: Egg moisture maintenance

### 280-250 Million Years Ago
- Secretions gain antimicrobial properties
- Protect eggs from infection

### 200-150 Million Years Ago
- Secretions become nutritive
- Early lactation evolves
- Egg dependency decreases

### 125 Million Years Ago
- Live birth evolves (placental mammals)
- Lactation already established

### Present Day
- 6,400+ mammalian species
- All defined by mammary glands
- Diverse adaptations across environments
