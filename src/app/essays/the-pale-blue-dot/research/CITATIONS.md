## Key Facts Verification

### The Photograph
- **Date taken**: February 14, 1990 (Valentine's Day)
- **Distance**: 6.06 billion kilometers from Earth
- **Spacecraft**: Voyager 1
- **Earth's size in image**: 0.12 pixels
- **Signal travel time**: 5.5 hours at light speed to reach Earth

### Mission Details
- **Voyager 1 launch**: September 5, 1977
- **Primary mission**: Study Jupiter and Saturn
- **Frames taken**: 60 frames of the solar system
- **Camera turned off**: Shortly after this final portrait session

### Carl Sagan's Role
- **Requested photograph**: Sagan persistently advocated for the image
- **NASA resistance**: Engineers worried about camera damage pointing toward Sun
- **Scientific value**: Admitted to have "no scientific value"
- **Philosophical value**: Became one of the most important space photographs

### Famous Quote
Sagan's famous reflection begins: "Look again at that dot. That's here. That's home. That's us." Full text published in his 1994 book "Pale Blue Dot: A Vision of the Human Future in Space."
