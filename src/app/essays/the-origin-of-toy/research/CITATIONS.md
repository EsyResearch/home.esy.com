## Key Figures Referenced

### Geoffrey Chaucer (c. 1343–1400)
- Among earliest recorded users of "toy" in Middle English
- *The Canterbury Tales* established many English words

### William Shakespeare (1564–1616)
- Used "toy" 30+ times with meanings: fancy, whim, ornament, trifle
- Never used to mean "child's plaything"

### Robert Cawdrey (c. 1538–1604)
- Compiled *A Table Alphabeticall* (1604), first English dictionary
- Captured early definitions showing "toy" as trifle and ornament

### John Locke (1632–1704)
- *Some Thoughts Concerning Education* (1693)
- Emphasized learning through play

### Samuel Johnson (1709–1784)
- *A Dictionary of the English Language* (1755)
- Definition: "TOY: A petty commodity; a trifle; a thing of no value; a plaything; a bauble"

### Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1712–1778)
- *Émile* (1762) revolutionized thinking about childhood
- Advocated for natural play as essential to development

### Johan Huizinga (1872–1945)
- *Homo Ludens* (1938)
- Argued play is older than culture, foundational to human civilization

### A.C. Gilbert (1884–1961)
- Invented Erector Set (1913)
- "The Man Who Saved Christmas"

### Ole Kirk Christiansen (1891–1958)
- Founded LEGO (from Danish "leg godt" = "play well")

### Ruth Handler (1916–2002)
- Co-founded Mattel, created Barbie (1959)
