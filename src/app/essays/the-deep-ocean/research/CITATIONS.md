## Key Facts Verification

### Depth & Pressure Statistics
- **Challenger Deep depth**: 10,935m (verified by NOAA, MBARI)
- **Pressure at depth**: ~1,086 atmospheres (calculated from depth)
- **Temperature**: Near-freezing 4°C in abyssal zones (WHOI)

### Ocean Zones (Verified by NOAA/WHOI)
- Epipelagic: 0-200m
- Mesopelagic: 200-1,000m
- Bathypelagic: 1,000-4,000m
- Abyssopelagic: 4,000-6,000m
- Hadalpelagic: 6,000-11,000m

### Exploration Timeline
- **1872-1876**: HMS Challenger Expedition (documented)
- **1960**: Trieste descent (Piccard & Walsh) - 10,916m
- **1977**: Hydrothermal vents discovered (documented)
- **2012**: Cameron's solo descent - 10,908m
- **2019**: Vescovo's record - 10,928m (TIME, Nat Geo verification)

### Environmental Impact
- **90% heat absorption**: Climate data from NOAA
- **30% CO₂ absorption**: Oceanographic research
- **Plastic pollution**: Vescovo discovery 2019 (TIME verification)

## Research Methodology

All statistics and historical facts were cross-referenced across multiple Tier 1 sources. Ocean zone classifications follow standard oceanographic definitions from NOAA and WHOI. Depth measurements are from official expedition reports and verified by multiple institutions.
