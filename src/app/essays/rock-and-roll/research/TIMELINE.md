# Timeline: Rock & Roll Research Package

## Pre-Rock Foundations (1920s-1949)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source |
|------|-------|--------------|--------|
| **1920** | Mamie Smith records "Crazy Blues" for Okeh Records | First blues recording by African American artist; sold 75,000 copies first month | Library of Congress |
| **1922** | Trixie Smith records "My Baby Rocks Me With One Steady Roll" | Earliest documented use of "rock" and "roll" together in music | Carnegie Hall Timeline |
| **December 29, 1928** | Pinetop Smith records "Pinetop's Boogie Woogie" | First hit boogie-woogie recording; Blues Hall of Fame | Blues Foundation |
| **December 23, 1938** | "From Spirituals to Swing" concert at Carnegie Hall | First major integrated concert at Carnegie Hall; featured boogie-woogie trio | Vanguard Records |
| **1941-1942** | Alan Lomax records Muddy Waters for Library of Congress | Documented pre-electric Delta blues; primary source archive | Library of Congress |
| **1943** | Muddy Waters moves to Chicago | Beginning of Chicago electric blues transformation | Multiple sources |
| **1944** | Sister Rosetta Tharpe records "Strange Things Happening Every Day" | First gospel to reach Billboard R&B Top 10; some credit as first rock record | Smithsonian |
| **1947** | Roy Brown records "Good Rockin' Tonight" | Used "rock" musically rather than sexually; covered by Wynonie Harris, Elvis | Multiple sources |
| **1947** | Ahmet Ertegun and Herb Abramson found Atlantic Records | Major independent label that would define R&B and soul | PBS American Masters |
| **June 21, 1948** | Columbia Records unveils the LP (33 1/3 RPM) | Revolutionized album format | Library of Congress |
| **December 7, 1948** | First 45 RPM record pressed at RCA Victor | Created the format that would define rock singles | Rolling Stone |
| **1949** | Fats Domino records "The Fat Man" | First rock record to sell 1 million copies | PBS American Masters |
| **June 17, 1949** | Billboard renames "Race Records" chart to "Rhythm & Blues" | Jerry Wexler coins the term; ends explicit racial designation | Britannica |

## Rock & Roll Explosion (1950-1959)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source |
|------|-------|--------------|--------|
| **January 3, 1950** | Sun Studio opens in Memphis | "Memphis Recording Service" would become ground zero for rock | NEH |
| **June 3, 1950** | Chess Records founded in Chicago | Would record Chuck Berry, Muddy Waters, Howlin' Wolf, Bo Diddley | Britannica |
| **March 1951** | "Rocket 88" recorded at Sun Studio | Often cited as first rock and roll record; first recorded distortion | Library of Congress |
| **July 11, 1951** | Alan Freed debuts "Moondog Rock and Roll Party" on WJW Cleveland | Popularized term "rock and roll" to white audiences | Encyclopedia of Cleveland History |
| **February 1, 1952** | Sun Records label officially launched | Sam Phillips' independent label | Tennessee Encyclopedia |
| **March 21, 1952** | Moondog Coronation Ball, Cleveland Arena | First major rock and roll concert; 20,000+ attempted entry | Cleveland Historical, Guinness |
| **1952** | Big Mama Thornton records "Hound Dog" | Topped R&B chart 7 weeks; she was paid $500, no royalties | Britannica |
| **July 7, 1954** | Dewey Phillips plays Elvis on WHBQ Memphis | "That's All Right" first airplay | Memphis Music Hall of Fame |
| **October 18, 1954** | First transistor radio (Regency TR-1) released | Enabled private listening; teenage revolution | Smithsonian |
| **1955** | Chuck Berry records "Maybellene" at Chess | #1 R&B, #5 Pop; sold 1 million copies by year's end | Rock Hall |
| **September 1955** | Little Richard records "Tutti Frutti" at J&M Studio | Breakthrough rock single; immediately covered by Pat Boone | Multiple sources |
| **April 10, 1956** | Nat King Cole attacked on stage in Birmingham | KKK assault during all-white concert; part of anti-rock campaign | Equal Justice Initiative |
| **1956** | Elvis Presley's "Hound Dog" released | Sold 10 million copies; defined rock's crossover moment | Multiple sources |
| **1957** | Jerry Lee Lewis records "Whole Lotta Shakin' Going On" | Sun Records' post-Elvis peak | Rock Hall |
| **1957** | Seth Lover's humbucker pickups first appear on Gibson guitars | Defined rock guitar tone for decades | Gibson |
| **October 1958** | Muddy Waters tours UK | Electric blues "shocks" British audiences; seeds British blues boom | uDiscover Music |
| **November 1959** | Payola scandal breaks | Congressional investigation begins | History.com |
| **May 2, 1960** | Dick Clark testifies before Congress | Survives payola scandal; Alan Freed destroyed | History.com |
| **January 20, 1965** | Alan Freed dies | Age 43, broke, blackballed; pioneering DJ forgotten | Multiple sources |

## British Invasion & Global Spread (1960s)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source |
|------|-------|--------------|--------|
| **March 17, 1962** | Blues Incorporated debuts at Ealing Club | "Britain's First Rhythm & Blues Band" — Korner and Davies | uDiscover Music |
| **October 1962** | First American Folk Blues Festival in UK | Muddy Waters, Howlin' Wolf seen by future rock stars | Wikipedia |
| **April 13, 1962** | Star-Club opens in Hamburg | Beatles play opening seven weeks; Europe's premier rock venue | Beatles Bible |
| **February 9, 1964** | Beatles on Ed Sullivan Show | 73 million viewers; British Invasion begins | Multiple sources |
| **September 1964** | Animals' "House of the Rising Sun" hits #1 US | First non-Beatles British Invasion chart-topper | Wikipedia |
| **March 1965** | Clapton leaves Yardbirds | "Too commercial" — purist blues stance | Wikipedia |
| **June 30-July 2, 1966** | Beatles perform at Budokan, Tokyo | Triggers Japanese "Group Sounds" explosion | Group Sounds sources |
| **July 1966** | *Blues Breakers with Eric Clapton* released | "The Beano Album" redefines electric blues guitar | Multiple sources |
| **1967** | Tropicalia movement emerges in Brazil | Veloso, Gil, Os Mutantes fuse rock with Brazilian traditions | NPR, Wikipedia |
| **1968** | Zodiak Free Arts Lab opens in Berlin | Seeds of Krautrock; Tangerine Dream, Cluster origins | Springer |
| **1968-1969** | Yardbirds dissolve; Led Zeppelin forms | "Atomic blues" for the nuclear age | Multiple sources |
| **1969** | Veloso and Gil arrested by Brazilian military | Tropicalia suppressed; artists later exiled | NPR |

## Genre Evolution & Legacy (1970s-1990s)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source |
|------|-------|--------------|--------|
| **1970** | Kraftwerk formed in Dusseldorf | Electronic rock pioneers | Wikipedia |
| **1971** | Neu! formed | Motorik beat influences punk, post-punk, electronic | Wikipedia |
| **1975** | Punk emerges (Ramones, Sex Pistols) | Rock stripped to essentials; DIY ethos | Multiple sources |
| **1977** | Bowie's *Low* released | Fusion of British art rock and Krautrock | Multiple sources |
| **August 1, 1981** | MTV launches | "Video Killed the Radio Star" — visual rock era begins | Multiple sources |
| **1986** | Chuck Berry regains "Maybellene" writing credit | 31 years after release; court battle won | NPR |
| **1986** | First Rock and Roll Hall of Fame induction ceremony | 60% of inaugural class African American | Rock Hall |
| **1987** | Ruth Brown wins royalties lawsuit | Atlantic pays 20 years back royalties; Rhythm and Blues Foundation created | Wikipedia |
| **1991** | Nirvana's *Nevermind* released | Grunge/alternative breaks mainstream | Multiple sources |
| **1995** | Rock and Roll Hall of Fame opens in Cleveland | Acknowledges city's role in popularizing genre | Rock Hall |
| **2017** | Sister Rosetta Tharpe inducted into Rock Hall | "Godmother of Rock and Roll" finally recognized | Rock Hall |
