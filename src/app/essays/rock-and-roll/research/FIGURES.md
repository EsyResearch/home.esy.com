# Key Figures: Rock & Roll Research Package

## Pre-Rock Pioneers (1920s-1940s)

### Boogie-Woogie & Piano

**Clarence "Pinetop" Smith** (1904-1929) — First Boogie-Woogie Recording Star
- Recorded "Pinetop's Boogie Woogie" (December 29, 1928), first recording with "boogie" in title
- Died from gunshot wound in Chicago dance-hall fight at age 24
- Quote on record: Directed "the girl with the red dress on" to "not move a peg" until told to "shake that thing"
- Photograph: Young Black man at piano, late 1920s Chicago studio setting

**Jimmy Yancey** (c. 1895-1951) — "Father of Boogie-Woogie"
- Chicago groundskeeper at Comiskey Park who influenced generations of pianists
- Created the "Yancey bass" left-hand figure
- Photograph: Older Black man, dignified, at upright piano

**Meade "Lux" Lewis** (1905-1964) — Carnegie Hall Boogie Master
- Composed "Honky Tonk Train Blues"
- Performed at 1938 Carnegie Hall "Spirituals to Swing" concert
- Photograph: At piano, 1938 concert attire

### Jump Blues

**Louis Jordan** (1908-1975) — "Father of Rhythm & Blues," "Grandfather of Rock 'n' Roll"
- Alto saxophonist and bandleader; 57 R&B chart hits; 113 weeks at #1 (1943-1950)
- Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Early Influence inductee (1987)
- Quote: "Rock and roll would have never happened without him." — Doc Pomus
- Photograph: In zoot suit with saxophone, performing energetically

**Big Joe Turner** (1911-1985) — "Boss of the Blues"
- Kansas City shouter; Carnegie Hall debut with Pete Johnson (1938)
- Later recorded "Shake, Rattle and Roll" (1954)
- Photograph: Large man at microphone, commanding presence

### Gospel & Electric Blues

**Sister Rosetta Tharpe** (1915-1973) — "Godmother of Rock and Roll"
- First gospel recording star; pioneered electric guitar distortion
- "Strange Things Happening Every Day" (1944) first gospel to reach R&B Top 10
- Rock and Roll Hall of Fame inductee (2017)
- Quote: "All this new stuff they call Rock and Roll, why I've been playing that for years now." (1957)
- Photograph: In sequined dress with Gibson SG, performing with fire

**T-Bone Walker** (1910-1975) — Electric Blues Guitar Pioneer
- First blues guitarist to make electric guitar a solo centerpiece (1935)
- Invented vocabulary for electric blues: single-string phrases, double-string slurs
- Quote about him: "I thought 'Jesus Himself had returned to earth playing electric guitar.'" — B.B. King
- Photograph: In suit, playing guitar behind his head (signature move)

**Muddy Waters** (1913-1983) — "Father of Modern Chicago Blues"
- Recorded by Alan Lomax for Library of Congress (1941-1942)
- Quote: "When I went into the clubs, the first thing I wanted was an amplifier."
- Photograph: At Chess Records with electric guitar, Chicago club setting

**Thomas A. Dorsey** (1899-1993) — "Father of Gospel Music"
- Formerly "Georgia Tom" (blues pianist with Ma Rainey)
- Fused blues technique with sacred music; composed over 1,000 gospel songs
- Quote about him: "Combined the good news of gospel with the bad news of blues" — Anthony Heilbut
- Photograph: At church piano, dignified elder

## Rock & Roll Explosion (1950s)

### The Creators

**Chuck Berry** (1926-2017) — Rock's Supreme Poet
- "Maybellene" (1955) moved Chess from R&B into mainstream rock
- Quote about him: "Rock & roll guitar starts here." — Rolling Stone
- Lost royalties for 31 years when Alan Freed was added to "Maybellene" credits
- Photograph: Duck walk pose with Gibson ES-350T

**Little Richard** (1932-2020) — "The Architect of Rock and Roll"
- Recorded "Tutti Frutti," "Long Tall Sally," "Good Golly Miss Molly"
- Quote: "I am the innovator. I am the originator. I am the emancipator. I am the architect of rock 'n' roll."
- Quote on covers: "When Pat Boone covered my record, I was mad, I wanted to get him... he was stoppin' my progress."
- Photograph: At piano, pompadour, sequined shirt, mouth wide open

**Fats Domino** (1928-2017) — New Orleans' Gentle Giant
- Record sales in 1950s-60s rivaled only by Elvis Presley
- Quote: "Rock 'n roll is nothing but rhythm and blues, and we've been playing it for years down in New Orleans."
- Quote: Elvis pointed to Fats and said, "There's the real king of rock 'n' roll." (1969)
- Photograph: At piano, smiling, in New Orleans club setting

**Bo Diddley** (1928-2008) — Rhythm Innovator
- Self-titled 1955 debut introduced the "Bo Diddley beat"
- Quote: "I just came up with the Bo Diddley beat, and it seems everybody around the fucking world is copying it. I'm not getting a dime for it."
- Quote: "Don't trust nobody but your mama, and even then, look at her real good."
- Photograph: With rectangular "cigar box" guitar

**Elvis Presley** (1935-1977) — The Crossover
- Bridged Black and white music for mass audience
- Quote: "A lot of people seem to think I started this business. But rock 'n' roll was here a long time before I came along. Nobody can sing it like colored people." (1957)
- Photograph: Sun Studio session, 1954, young and hungry

### The Enablers

**Sam Phillips** (1923-2003) — Sun Records Founder
- Produced "Rocket 88," discovered Elvis, invented slapback echo
- First non-performer inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1986)
- Quote: "I knew that for black music to come to its rightful place in this country, we had to have some white singers come over and do black music—not copy it, not change, not sweeten it."
- Photograph: At Sun Studio mixing board, Memphis

**Alan Freed** (1921-1965) — "Moondog," Rock's First DJ Champion
- Popularized "rock and roll" on radio starting July 11, 1951
- Organized Moondog Coronation Ball (March 21, 1952)
- Quote: "When the dance was stopped, I went off and cried. I'm not ashamed to admit it."
- Destroyed by payola scandal; died penniless at 43
- Photograph: At WJW microphone, Cleveland, 1951

**Leo Mintz** (1910-1976) — Record Store Visionary
- Observed white teenagers buying R&B at Record Rendezvous
- Sponsored Alan Freed's radio show and concerts
- Quote: Freed later admitted "It was more Leo's idea than mine" about naming rock and roll
- Photograph: Behind counter at Record Rendezvous, Cleveland

**Leonard Chess** (1917-1969) & **Phil Chess** (1921-2016) — Chess Records Founders
- Polish immigrant brothers who recorded Chuck Berry, Muddy Waters, Howlin' Wolf
- 2120 South Michigan Avenue became pilgrimage site for British bands
- Photograph: In Chess Records office, Chicago

**Ahmet Ertegun** (1923-2006) — Atlantic Records Co-Founder
- Quote: "We did it for one main reason. We wanted to make the kind of records we wanted to buy."
- Later became chairman of Rock and Roll Hall of Fame
- Photograph: In recording studio with Ray Charles

**Cosimo Matassa** (1926-2014) — New Orleans Recording Engineer
- Captured virtually every New Orleans R&B hit from late 1940s through early 1970s
- Rock and Roll Hall of Fame inductee (2012)
- Photograph: At J&M Studio mixing board

### The Exploited

**Big Mama Thornton** (1926-1984) — "Hound Dog" Original
- Her 1953 version topped R&B chart for seven weeks, sold 500,000+ copies
- Paid flat fee of $500, no royalties
- Quote: "Didn't get no money from them at all. Everybody livin' in a house but me. I'm just livin." (1984)
- Died penniless in Los Angeles boarding house
- Photograph: Powerful woman with harmonica, commanding stage presence

**Ruth Brown** (1928-2006) — "Miss Rhythm"
- Her hits made Atlantic "The House That Ruth Built"
- Left Atlantic in 1960s with no savings; worked as bus driver, floor scrubber
- Won 20-year legal battle; received $20,000 in back royalties
- Photograph: Elegant performer in 1950s evening gown

**Jackie Brenston** (1930-1979) — "Rocket 88" Vocalist
- Credited on "Rocket 88" though Ike Turner led the band and wrote it
- Never achieved sustained success
- Photograph: Young man at microphone, 1951 session

## British Invasion & Beyond (1960s)

### British Blues Pioneers

**Alexis Korner** (1928-1984) — "Founding Father of British Blues"
- Co-founded Blues Incorporated 1961, ran the Ealing Club
- Rock and Roll Hall of Fame inductee (2024)
- Photograph: With acoustic guitar, London club setting

**Eric Clapton** (b. 1945) — Blues Purist Turned Guitar God
- Left Yardbirds for being "too commercial"; joined Mayall, then Cream
- Photograph: With Gibson Les Paul, Bluesbreakers era

**John Mayall** (1933-2024) — "Godfather of British Blues"
- His Bluesbreakers was finishing school for Clapton, Peter Green, Mick Taylor
- Photograph: At microphone with harmonica, 1960s club

### The Invasion

**The Beatles** — Liverpool's Gift to the World
- Hamburg crucible transformed raw talent into world-beaters
- Quote: "I was born in Liverpool, but I grew up in Hamburg." — John Lennon
- Photograph: Cavern Club, Hamburg Star-Club, Ed Sullivan Show

**Mick Jagger** (b. 1943) & **Keith Richards** (b. 1943) — Rolling Stones Core
- Reconnected because of blues albums Keith was carrying
- Quote about them: They brought American bluesmen back to American audiences
- Photograph: Early Stones at Chess Records, 2120 South Michigan

### Global Nodes

**Caetano Veloso** (b. 1942) & **Gilberto Gil** (b. 1942) — Tropicalia Founders
- Fused psychedelia with Brazilian traditions; arrested by military regime 1969
- Quote: "The violent aspect of rock 'n' roll interested us more because we were seeing violence."
- Photograph: Performing together, late 1960s Brazil

**Os Mutantes** — Brazil's Psychedelic Pioneers
- "House band" of Tropicalia; influenced Beck, David Byrne
- Photograph: Trio in colorful 1960s attire with instruments

## Technology Innovators

**Leo Fender** (1909-1991) — Solid-Body Guitar Creator
- Created first mass-produced solid-body electric guitar
- Rock and Roll Hall of Fame inductee (1992)
- Never learned to play guitar
- Photograph: At Fender factory workbench

**Les Paul** (1915-2009) — Inventor and Guitarist
- Created "The Log" solid-body prototype; invented multitrack recording
- National Inventors Hall of Fame (2005), Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1988)
- Commissioned first 8-track recorder
- Photograph: With Gibson Les Paul guitar, in home studio

**Seth Lover** (1910-1997) — Humbucker Pickup Inventor
- Gibson engineer who invented the PAF humbucker pickup (1955-1957)
- Patent No. 2,896,491 granted 1959
- Photograph: Gibson engineer at workbench

**Phil Spector** (1939-2021) — "Wall of Sound" Creator
- Created dense, reverberant production technique at Gold Star Studios
- Produced Ronettes, Crystals, Righteous Brothers
- Photograph: At Gold Star mixing board, 1960s
