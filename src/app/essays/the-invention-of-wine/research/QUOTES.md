### Quote 2: Latin Proverb

> (In wine, there is truth)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Speaker | Traditional Latin proverb |
| Attribution | Often attributed to Pliny the Elder |
| Source | Ancient Roman/Greek wisdom |
| Verification | ✅ **VERIFIED** — Traditional proverb |
| Usage | Footer closing |

**Note**: The proverb predates any single attribution. Greek equivalent: "Ἐν οἴνῳ ἀλήθεια" (*En oino aletheia*). Pliny references the concept but likely did not originate the phrase.

## Quote Selection Guidelines

### For Serious/Educational Tone
- Hemingway (current) — sophistication + accessibility
- Pasteur (verified) — scientific authority
- Jefferson — historical gravitas

### For Literary/Poetic Tone
- Stevenson — "bottled poetry"
- Omar Khayyám — Persian poetry
- Ovid — classical elegance

### For Philosophy/Wisdom
- "In vino veritas" (current) — timeless
- Pliny the Elder — classical wisdom

### AVOID
- Franklin "beer" quote misattributed to wine
- Unverified Galileo quote without qualifier
- Dom Pérignon "drinking stars" (likely apocryphal)

## Verification Process

1. **Primary source check**: Locate original text
2. **Publication verification**: Confirm book/letter/document
3. **Context review**: Ensure quote isn't taken out of context
4. **Attribution accuracy**: Confirm speaker is correct

### Verification Status Key
- ✅ **VERIFIED**: Primary source confirmed
- ⚠️ **UNVERIFIED**: Cannot confirm, use with qualifier
- ❌ **INCORRECT**: Known misattribution, do not use
