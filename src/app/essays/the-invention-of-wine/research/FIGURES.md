## Ancient Figures

### King Scorpion I
**Role in Narrative**: Archaeological evidence of wine's importance

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Era | Predynastic Egypt (~3150 BCE) |
| Significance | Buried with 700 wine jars at Abydos |
| Current Coverage | Brief mention in Split Screen section |
| Expansion Potential | Low — archaeological figure, not biographical |

**Key Facts**:
- One of the earliest Egyptian rulers
- His tomb contained the largest ancient wine collection ever found
- Jars were sealed with clay stoppers stamped with origin marks
- Wine was considered necessary for the afterlife

**Source**: Archaeological excavations at Abydos; Egyptological records

### Dionysus (Greek) / Bacchus (Roman)
**Role in Narrative**: Religious/mythological significance of wine

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Era | Greek mythology (~1500 BCE onward) |
| Significance | God of wine, fertility, ritual madness, theater |
| Current Coverage | Single sentence in Era 3 |
| Expansion Potential | Medium — could explore cult practices |

**Key Facts**:
- Son of Zeus and Semele (mortal woman)
- Worship involved ecstatic rituals (Dionysian mysteries)
- Associated with transformation (grape → wine, mortal → divine)
- Romans adopted him as Bacchus
- Symposia (drinking gatherings) were philosophical rituals in his honor

**Quote Potential**: Ancient Greek sources on Dionysian worship

### Pliny the Elder (23-79 CE)
**Role in Narrative**: Classical documentation of wine

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Era | Roman Empire (1st century CE) |
| Significance | Wrote extensively about wine in *Natural History* |
| Current Coverage | Attribution for "In vino veritas" (footer) |
| Expansion Potential | Medium — could add more quotes |

**Key Facts**:
- Roman naturalist and author
- *Natural History* dedicated significant space to viticulture
- Classified grape varieties and wine regions
- Died observing Vesuvius eruption (79 CE)

**Verified Quote**: "In vino veritas" (In wine, truth) — traditional Latin proverb, often attributed to Pliny

## Medieval Figures

### Dom Pérignon (1638-1715)
**Role in Narrative**: Monastic advancement of winemaking

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Era | 17th century France |
| Significance | Benedictine monk who advanced sparkling wine |
| Current Coverage | Single mention in Era 5 |
| Expansion Potential | Medium — but must debunk myths |

**Key Facts**:
- Cellar master at Abbey of Hautvillers
- **IMPORTANT**: Did NOT invent champagne (common myth)
- Did improve blending techniques
- Did improve bottling and corking methods
- Famous quote "I am drinking stars!" is likely apocryphal

**Note**: Exercise caution with champagne myths. Dom Pérignon's contributions were real but often exaggerated.

## Scientific Figures

### Louis Pasteur (1822-1895)
**Role in Narrative**: Scientific understanding of fermentation

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Era | 19th century France |
| Significance | Discovered yeast's role in fermentation |
| Current Coverage | Not mentioned (gap) |
| Expansion Potential | HIGH — explains fermentation science |

**Key Facts**:
- French chemist and microbiologist
- 1857-1858: Proved fermentation caused by living organisms (yeast)
- 1860s: Studied wine problems commissioned by Napoleon III
- Published *Études sur le vin* (Studies on Wine, 1866)
- Developed pasteurization (named after him)
- Showed wine spoilage caused by unwanted microorganisms

**Verified Quote**: "A bottle of wine contains more philosophy than all the books in the world." — Attributed, verification needed

**Research Value**: Pasteur's work explains WHY fermentation works, bridging ancient practice and modern science.

### Dr. Patrick McGovern (1944-Present)
**Role in Narrative**: Discovery of earliest wine evidence

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Era | Contemporary (research ongoing) |
| Significance | Lead author of 2017 PNAS study confirming 6000 BCE wine |
| Current Coverage | Not mentioned by name (gap) |
| Expansion Potential | HIGH — humanizes the discovery |

**Key Facts**:
- Scientific Director of Biomolecular Archaeology Project, Penn Museum
- Pioneer of "molecular archaeology" for ancient beverages
- Authored *Ancient Wine* (2003) and *Uncorking the Past* (2009)
- Identified wine residue at Hajji Firuz Tepe (Iran)
- Led Georgia study published in PNAS (2017)
- Nicknamed "the Indiana Jones of Ancient Ales, Wines, and Extreme Beverages"

**Verified Affiliation**: University of Pennsylvania Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology

**Research Value**: McGovern connects the essay's opening claim (6000 BCE) to real scientific work.

## Phylloxera Era Figures

### Jules-Émile Planchon (1823-1888)
**Role in Narrative**: Identified cause of phylloxera epidemic

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Era | 19th century France |
| Significance | Botanist who identified phylloxera aphid |
| Current Coverage | Not mentioned (gap) |
| Expansion Potential | HIGH — hero of phylloxera section |

**Key Facts**:
- French botanist at University of Montpellier
- 1868: Identified *Phylloxera vastatrix* (now *Daktulosphaira vitifoliae*)
- Traveled to Texas (1873) to study American resistant vines
- Advocated for grafting solution
- Saved European wine through scientific collaboration

**Research Value**: Essential figure for phylloxera expansion section.
