## Key Research Findings

### Archaeological Consensus
- **Earliest confirmed wine**: Georgia, ~6000 BCE (PNAS 2017)
- **Detection method**: Tartaric acid residue in Neolithic pottery
- **Lead researcher**: Dr. Patrick McGovern (Penn Museum)

### Fermentation Science
- **Natural process**: Wild yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae) on grape skins
- **Alcohol limit**: ~14-15% (yeast dies at higher concentrations)
- **Key discovery**: Louis Pasteur's 1860s fermentation studies

### Economic Scale (Modern)
- **Global production**: ~260 million hectoliters annually
- **Market value**: $300-340 billion
- **Vineyard area**: 7.3 million hectares
- **Grape varieties**: 10,000+ cultivated worldwide

### Major Historical Crisis
- **Phylloxera epidemic**: 1860s-1890s
- **Impact**: Destroyed 70% of European vineyards
- **Solution**: Grafting European vines onto American rootstock
- **Legacy**: Fundamentally transformed global viticulture
