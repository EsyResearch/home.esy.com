# Timeline: The Invention of Wine

> Chronological reference for essay narrative

---

## Prehistoric Era (Before 3000 BCE)

| Date | Event | Location | Significance |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|
| ~8000 BCE | Wild grape (*Vitis vinifera sylvestris*) grows across Eurasia | Caucasus to Mediterranean | Precursor to domesticated wine grapes |
| **~6000 BCE** | **Earliest confirmed winemaking** | Georgia (Gadachrili Gora) | PNAS 2017 study; tartaric acid in pottery |
| ~5400-5000 BCE | Wine production at Hajji Firuz Tepe | Iran | Previously thought to be earliest; now secondary |
| ~4000 BCE | Wine spreads to Mesopotamia | Iraq/Syria | Trade routes establish wine culture |
| ~3500 BCE | First written references to wine | Sumer | Cuneiform tablets mention wine |

---

## Ancient Civilizations (3000 BCE - 500 CE)

| Date | Event | Location | Significance |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|
| **~3150 BCE** | **King Scorpion I buried with 700 wine jars** | Abydos, Egypt | Largest ancient wine burial ever found |
| ~3000 BCE | Egyptian hieroglyphics document viticulture | Egypt | Earliest visual records of winemaking |
| ~2500 BCE | Wine trade established across Eastern Mediterranean | Levant | Phoenician traders spread viticulture |
| **~1500 BCE** | **Dionysian cult emerges** | Greece | Wine becomes central to Greek religion |
| ~1200 BCE | Phoenicians establish vineyards in North Africa | Carthage | Wine spreads beyond Mediterranean basin |
| ~800 BCE | Homer references wine extensively | Greece | *Iliad* and *Odyssey* document wine culture |
| ~600 BCE | Greeks bring wine to southern France | Massalia (Marseille) | Foundation of French viticulture |
| ~500 BCE | Symposia (drinking parties) formalized | Athens | Wine linked to philosophy and democracy |
| **~200 BCE** | **Rome begins industrial-scale production** | Italy | Wooden barrels introduced; vintages classified |
| ~100 BCE | Romans plant vineyards in Gaul | France | Burgundy, Bordeaux regions established |
| 79 CE | Vesuvius erupts, preserving Pompeii wine bars | Italy | Archaeological evidence of Roman wine culture |
| **~325 CE** | **Speyer wine bottle sealed** | Germany | Oldest unopened wine bottle still extant |
| 380 CE | Christianity becomes Rome's state religion | Roman Empire | Eucharist ensures continued wine production |

---

## Medieval Period (500 - 1500 CE)

| Date | Event | Location | Significance |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|
| ~500 CE | Monasteries begin preserving viticultural knowledge | Europe | Benedictine monks maintain vineyards |
| ~800 CE | Charlemagne promotes vineyard cultivation | Holy Roman Empire | Wine production expands across Europe |
| ~1100 CE | Cistercian monks classify Burgundy terroirs | France | First systematic vineyard classification |
| ~1200 CE | Bordeaux wine trade with England flourishes | France/England | Economic importance of wine established |
| ~1300 CE | Glass bottles begin replacing barrels for storage | Europe | Better preservation; aging becomes possible |
| 1492 | Columbus brings *Vitis vinifera* to Americas | Caribbean | Beginning of New World viticulture |

---

## Early Modern Period (1500 - 1850)

| Date | Event | Location | Significance |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|
| ~1530s | Spanish plant vineyards in Mexico, Peru | Americas | First New World wine production |
| **~1668** | **Dom Pérignon improves sparkling wine** | Champagne, France | Advances (though myths exaggerated) |
| 1659 | First South African vineyard planted | Cape Town | Wine reaches Southern Hemisphere |
| 1769 | Spanish missions establish California vineyards | California | Foundation of American wine industry |
| 1788 | First Australian vines planted | Sydney | Wine reaches Oceania |
| 1830s | Bordeaux classification system develops | France | Quality hierarchy formalized |

---

## The Phylloxera Era (1850 - 1900)

| Date | Event | Location | Significance |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|
| 1854 | American vines sent to European botanical gardens | France/UK | Unknowingly carrying phylloxera |
| **1863** | **Mysterious vine disease appears** | Southern France | First phylloxera outbreak |
| 1868 | Jules-Émile Planchon identifies phylloxera aphid | France | Cause of epidemic identified |
| 1870s | Epidemic spreads across France | France | Bordeaux, Burgundy devastated |
| 1878 | Phylloxera reaches Spain and Portugal | Iberia | Pan-European crisis |
| 1880s | **70% of European vineyards destroyed** | Europe | Worst agricultural disaster in history |
| 1881 | International Phylloxera Congress | Bordeaux | Scientific cooperation to find solution |
| **1880s-90s** | **American rootstock solution adopted** | Europe | Grafting saves European wine |
| 1890s | European vineyards replanted on American roots | Europe | Recovery begins; transformation complete |

---

## Modern Era (1900 - Present)

| Date | Event | Location | Significance |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|
| 1920-1933 | Prohibition | United States | American wine industry devastated |
| 1935 | AOC (Appellation) system established | France | Terroir legally protected |
| 1936 | First Italian DOC regulations | Italy | Quality control formalized |
| **1976** | **Judgment of Paris** | France | California wines beat French in blind tasting |
| 1980s | Australian wine industry expands globally | Australia | New World gains market share |
| 1990s | Chile, Argentina emerge as major producers | South America | Globalization of wine |
| 2000s | Natural wine movement gains momentum | Global | Return to minimal intervention |
| 2010s | Climate change begins shifting wine regions | Global | Warming affects traditional areas |
| **2017** | **PNAS study confirms 6000 BCE Georgian wine** | Georgia | Oldest evidence verified |
| 2020s | Global wine market exceeds $300 billion | Global | Industry reaches unprecedented scale |
