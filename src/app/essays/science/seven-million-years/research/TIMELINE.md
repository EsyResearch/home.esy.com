## Key Transitions

1. **Bipedalism** (~7–4 MYA): The first defining hominin trait. Debated drivers: savanna expansion, woodland foraging, thermoregulation, carrying hypothesis.

2. **Tool Use** (~3.3–2.6 MYA): Lomekwian and Oldowan technologies. Initially not exclusive to Homo — Australopithecus or Kenyanthropus may have been the first tool makers.

3. **Leaving Africa** (~1.8 MYA): Homo erectus reaches Dmanisi, Georgia. Eventually spreads to Java, China, and possibly southern Europe.

4. **Fire Mastery** (~1.0–0.4 MYA): Gradual progression from opportunistic use to controlled hearths. Transforms diet, social behavior, and cognitive development.

5. **Neanderthal-Sapiens Divergence** (~550–765 kya): Molecular clock estimate. H. heidelbergensis or similar form is the last common ancestor.

6. **Sapiens Origin** (~315 kya): Jebel Irhoud fossils push back the origin by 100,000 years. Pan-African emergence, not single origin point.

7. **Symbolic Revolution** (~100–40 kya): Gradual accumulation of evidence for art, personal ornaments, burial, long-distance exchange networks. Timing debated — African Middle Stone Age vs. European Upper Paleolithic.

8. **Out of Africa** (~65–50 kya): The main dispersal event. Sapiens replace or absorb all other hominin populations within 30,000 years.

9. **Neanderthal Extinction** (~40 kya): Multiple causes debated — climate, competition, disease, demographic swamping through interbreeding.

10. **Global Colonization** (~50–15 kya): Sapiens reach Australia (~65 kya), Europe (~45 kya), Americas (~15 kya or earlier). Only species to inhabit every continent.
