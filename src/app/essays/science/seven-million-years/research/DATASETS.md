## Dataset 6: Climate Data Reference

Used for: Climate Overlay visualization

**Primary data source:** Lisiecki, L.E. & Raymo, M.E. (2005). "A Pliocene-Pleistocene stack of 57 globally distributed benthic δ18O records." *Paleoceanography*, 20(1), PA1003.

The LR04 benthic δ18O stack provides a continuous climate record over the past 5.3 million years. Higher δ18O values indicate colder global temperatures and larger ice sheets.

### Key Climate Events Relevant to Hominin Evolution

| Event | Date (kya) | δ18O Trend | Impact on Hominins |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Onset of Northern Hemisphere glaciation | ~2,700 | Sharp increase | Drove habitat fragmentation; may have triggered Paranthropus/Homo divergence |
| Mid-Pleistocene Transition | ~1,200–700 | Shift from 41ky to 100ky cycles | More extreme glacial-interglacial swings; drove brain expansion? |
| Marine Isotope Stage 6 (Penultimate Glacial) | ~191–130 | Major glacial | Africa's green periods → sapiens dispersal within Africa |
| Eemian Interglacial (MIS 5e) | ~130–115 | Warm peak | First sapiens movements into Levant |
| Marine Isotope Stage 4 | ~71–57 | Cold snap | Possible Toba bottleneck (~74 kya); population contraction |
| Marine Isotope Stage 3 | ~57–29 | Variable | Main Out of Africa dispersal; Neanderthal-sapiens coexistence |
| Last Glacial Maximum | ~26–19 | Maximum cold | Population refugia; America colonization via Beringia |
| Younger Dryas | ~12.9–11.7 | Brief cold snap | Last gasp of Ice Age; triggers sedentism → agriculture |

**Note:** The full LR04 dataset is publicly available and contains δ18O values at ~1,000-year intervals for the past 5.3 million years. For the visualization, we'll downsample to ~5,000-year intervals and overlay species range bars.
