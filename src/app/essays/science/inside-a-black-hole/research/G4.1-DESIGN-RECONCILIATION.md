# G4.1 Design Research Reconciliation: Inside a Black Hole

The design research for "Inside a Black Hole" is **thematically authentic, cross-essay novel, implementation-complete, and not generic**. The visual system is derived from the physics of black holes — accretion disk gold, gravitational lensing blue, quantum violet — and every design decision traces to a physical phenomenon. 1. **Grid distortion implementation** — The spacetime grid motif is described conceptually but lacks SVG path data or a mathematical model for the distortion curves. The producer will need to implement this from the conceptual description.
   - **Severity:** Low — the grid is a decorative motif at 8% opacity, not a core visualization
   - **Recommendation:** Accept as-is; production agent can implement reasonable distortion

2. **Star field compression (Section 3)** — The post-crossing "stars compress into a circle above" effect is described but not specified in terms of particle count, size distribution, or animation timing.
   - **Severity:** Low — this is a secondary effect within the scroll-crossing visualization
   - **Recommendation:** Accept as-is; production agent has sufficient guidance

3. **Mobile Penrose interaction model** — The document says "simplified (pre-set light cones, larger buttons)" but doesn't specify which points are pre-set or button layout.
   - **Severity:** Low — mobile-specific interaction details are typically resolved in production
   - **Recommendation:** Accept as-is

**Verdict:** ✅ PASS — All core visualizations have implementation-ready design tokens. Minor gaps are at the decorative/secondary level and won't block production.
