# Homo naledi — Timeline

Two temporal scales: **Deep Time** (geological/evolutionary context) and **Discovery & Science** (modern research narrative). Each entry is marked as either `[ESSAY]` (directly relevant to the visual essay narrative) or `[CONTEXT]` (background framing).

## Key Narrative Tensions (for essay structure)

1. **The Dating Shock** — Morphology said ~2 Ma; radiometric dating said ~300 ka. This single result reframed every interpretation.
2. **Small Brain, Complex Behavior?** — 560 cc brain but potentially deliberate body disposal, fire use, symbolic markings. Where is the threshold for "behavioral modernity"?
3. **Mosaic Anatomy** — Not a transitional form on a line; a unique combination of ancient and derived traits that survived alongside sapiens.
4. **The Cave as Argument** — The inaccessibility of the Dinaledi Chamber is itself evidence: if naledi bodies weren't deliberately placed, how did 15+ individuals end up 80 m underground with no other fauna?
5. **Science in Public** — Berger's open-access, social-media-first, junior-scientist-led approach broke paleoanthropology's gatekeeping norms — and drew backlash for moving too fast.
6. **The 2023 Controversy** — Fire, burial, and rock art claims push the envelope far beyond what most researchers accept from the current evidence. The essay must hold this tension honestly.
