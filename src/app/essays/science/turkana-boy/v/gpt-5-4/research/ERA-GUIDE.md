# ERA GUIDE — The World of Turkana Boy

## Chronological Placement

Turkana Boy belongs to the African Early Pleistocene, around 1.53–1.6 million years ago. This is long after the first appearance of bipedal australopiths but still far before the later cognitive and symbolic worlds usually associated with recent humans.

## What Makes This Era Distinct

### 1. Body-size expansion in early *Homo*
By this interval, some African *Homo* populations had moved beyond the shorter-bodied, more mixed locomotor pattern seen in earlier australopiths. Larger body size and longer lower limbs become central evolutionary facts.

### 2. Open-country ecological pressure
This is a world in which heat, water economy, and ranging efficiency matter. Thermoregulation is not a side note; it is one of the best explanations for why body form itself becomes a major evolutionary story.

### 3. Technology is no longer incidental
Acheulean large cutting tools already exist in the broader Turkana Basin by 1.76 Ma. The essay should treat technology as established context rather than as a brand-new invention introduced by Turkana Boy personally.

### 4. Dispersal is underway
By the time of KNM-WT 15000, early *Homo* has already appeared outside Africa at Dmanisi. The question is no longer whether dispersal happened; it is what kind of body made that dispersal possible.

## Environmental Mood

- Bright, exposed, wind-cut terrain
- Sparse shade
- Large daily heat load
- Basin-and-lake geography rather than dense forest
- A landscape where endurance and movement matter more than climbing efficiency

## What Readers Should Feel

This is not Lucy's world. Lucy belongs to a more transitional body and earlier ecological puzzle. Turkana Boy belongs to a harsher, more exposed, more mobile world. The essay should make the reader feel that the environment itself is selecting for legs.

## Period Vocabulary

- **Early Pleistocene**: geological epoch interval containing major early *Homo* expansion
- **Acheulean**: stone-tool tradition marked by large cutting tools such as handaxes and cleavers
- **Dispersal**: expansion of populations into new regions
- **Thermoregulation**: the management of body heat and water balance
- **Linear body form**: relatively tall, narrow, long-limbed physique often associated with heat adaptation

## Design Implication

Downstream design should treat this era as **sunlit anatomy**, not cave mystery and not warm Pliocene amber. The visual world is lake basin, exposed sediment, bright bone, and physiological clarity.
