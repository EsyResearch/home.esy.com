## [S01] Brown et al. 1985

**Citation**: Brown, F., Harris, J., Leakey, R. & Walker, A. (1985). Early *Homo erectus* skeleton from west Lake Turkana, Kenya. *Nature*, 316, 788–792.
**DOI**: 10.1038/316788a0
**Tier**: 1
**Relevance**: Sections 2, 10, 11, 12 — Original scientific announcement of KNM-WT 15000 and its significance.
**Key Data Points**:
- Discovery at Nariokotome, west of Lake Turkana, by Kamoya Kimeu in 1984
- Exceptionally complete early *Homo* skeleton
- Age around 1.5–1.6 Ma
- Established the specimen as central evidence for early *Homo erectus/ergaster* anatomy
**Verification**: Published in *Nature*; foundational discovery paper.

## [S03] Smith 2004

**Citation**: Smith, S.L. (2004). Skeletal age, dental age, and the maturation of KNM-WT 15000. *American Journal of Physical Anthropology*, 125(2), 105–120.
**DOI**: 10.1002/ajpa.10376
**Tier**: 1
**Relevance**: Sections 2, 3 — The key paper on the dental-age vs. skeletal-age discrepancy.
**Key Data Points**:
- Modern human children can show meaningful divergence between dental and skeletal maturation
- KNM-WT 15000 cannot be assigned a simple age from one developmental marker alone
- Supports caution in claiming either fully human-like or fully ape-like growth
**Verification**: AJPA paper directly focused on maturation of the specimen.

**Verification**: Scholarly synthesis chapter by leading dental development researchers.

## Research Notes

- The abscess opening in the mandible is usable as a narrative entry point, but the pathology section should be written cautiously and anchored to specimen-based description rather than overconfident medical reconstruction.
- The speech section must be framed as a debate, not a resolved conclusion: older literature uses KNM-WT 15000 to argue for limited breath control, while later reassessment complicates that claim.
- The body-form section should avoid overstating "fully modern" in every respect. The essay claim is strongest when focused on long legs, linear proportions, heat adaptation, and distance walking rather than claiming identity with recent *Homo sapiens* anatomy in every detail.
