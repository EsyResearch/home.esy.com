## Cluster Context

This essay is a cluster essay within the paleoanthropology series. It sits alongside `lucy` and `homo-naledi` as a focused deep dive derived from the broader `seven-million-years` parent essay.

Where Lucy explores early bipedalism and *Homo naledi* explores the paradox of complex behavior with a small brain, Turkana Boy explores the emergence of the first recognizably modern human body: long legs, narrow trunk, heat-adapted proportions, and the locomotor design that made long-distance movement possible.

## Topic & Core Thesis

KNM-WT 15000 is the most complete early *Homo* skeleton ever discovered and one of the clearest windows into when our body plan truly emerged. This essay begins with a child's death and works outward from a single pathology to a species-level transformation: how an infected molar leads to questions of growth, thermoregulation, locomotion, migration, cognition, and inheritance.

The core thesis is that Turkana Boy represents the first draft of the modern human body. His skeleton marks a transition from earlier hominin compromises to a body built for distance, heat, and expansion across landscapes.

## Scope Definition

### What's In Scope

1. The dental abscess and leading hypotheses about cause of death
2. The age-at-death problem: dental age vs. skeletal age discrepancy
3. Growth and development compared with modern humans and chimpanzees
4. Body proportions, thermoregulation, and Allen's Rule
5. Locomotion and long-distance walking biomechanics
6. Brain size in evolutionary context
7. The speech question and thoracic vertebral canal evidence
8. Acheulean tools and forward-planning technology
9. Out-of-Africa dispersal in *Homo ergaster/erectus*
10. Kamoya Kimeu, the discovery story, and excavation history
11. Stratigraphic and dating context around the Nariokotome find
12. The evolutionary inheritance of this body plan into living humans

### What's Out of Scope

- A full retelling of all hominin evolution already covered in `seven-million-years`
- A general essay on Acheulean technology beyond what is needed for Turkana Boy's context
- Broader *Homo erectus* debates not directly relevant to KNM-WT 15000
- Speculative narrative scenes not grounded in evidence

## Narrative Direction

The approved narrative structure is "The Forensic Unspooling."

The essay should begin clinically with death and then reverse-engineer a life. Each section is driven by a forensic question that the bones answer, and each answer should naturally raise the next question. The reading experience should feel cumulative, investigative, and precise.
