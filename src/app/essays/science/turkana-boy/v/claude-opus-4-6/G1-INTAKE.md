## Confirmed Topic & Scope

**Title**: Turkana Boy — The First Modern Body  
**Category**: Science / Paleoanthropology

**Read Time Target**: 28–35 minutes  
**Source Target**: 16–20+ sources at Tier-1/2  

**Thesis**: KNM-WT 15000 is the first hominin in the fossil record whose body is built for range — long legs, narrow trunk, heat-adapted proportions. The essay reverse-engineers a life from a death, starting with the dental abscess and expanding outward through anatomy, locomotion, cognition, tools, and migration.

## Key Research Questions

1. What does the dental abscess pathology reveal about cause of death?
2. Why do teeth and bones give different ages (8 vs 11)?
3. How does the body plan compare to earlier hominins (Lucy) and later species (naledi)?
4. What does the narrow thoracic vertebral canal imply about speech capability?
5. What is the relationship between thermoregulation and the transition to open-country life?
6. How does Turkana Boy connect to the first Out-of-Africa dispersal?
