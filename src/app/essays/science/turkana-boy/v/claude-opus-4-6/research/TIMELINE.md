# Timeline — Turkana Boy: The First Modern Body

**Build**: opus-4-6 (Claude Opus 4.6)

---

## Deep Time Context

| Date (Ma) | Event | Significance |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| ~6–7 Ma | Sahelanthropus tchadensis | Earliest possible hominin; foramen magnum position debated |
| ~4.4 Ma | Ardipithecus ramidus | Facultative biped; woodland habitat |
| ~3.9 Ma | Australopithecus anamensis | Early obligate biped at Lake Turkana |
| **~3.2 Ma** | **Lucy (AL 288-1)** | **Bipedal but short-legged, long-armed; compromise body** |
| ~2.8 Ma | Earliest Homo (LD 350-1) | Jaw fragment from Ledi-Geraru, Ethiopia |
| ~2.6 Ma | Earliest stone tools (Lomekwi 3) | Predates genus Homo; found west of Lake Turkana |
| ~2.0 Ma | Homo habilis sensu lato | Small-bodied, still australopith-like postcranially |
| **~1.9 Ma** | **Homo ergaster appears** | **First tall, narrow-bodied hominin in Africa** |
| ~1.85 Ma | KNM-ER 3733 cranium | Early African Homo erectus/ergaster skull from East Turkana |
| **~1.77 Ma** | **Dmanisi hominins, Georgia** | **First Out-of-Africa dispersal; small-brained but upright** |
| ~1.6 Ma | Acheulean technology appears | Handaxes requiring planning and bilateral symmetry |
| **~1.53 Ma** | **KNM-WT 15000 (Turkana Boy)** | **The specimen: dies at 8–11 years, already 5'3"** |
| ~1.0 Ma | Homo erectus in Java (Trinil) | Dispersal to Southeast Asia established |
| ~0.78 Ma | Homo antecessor, Atapuerca | Earliest European hominin with modern facial features |
| **~0.335–0.236 Ma** | **Homo naledi, Rising Star Cave** | **Small-brained but complex behavior** |
| ~0.3 Ma | Homo sapiens emerges (Jebel Irhoud) | Modern humans in North Africa |

## Discovery & Excavation Timeline

| Date | Event | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| August 22, 1984 | Kamoya Kimeu finds fragment | A single cranial fragment on the eroded surface at Nariokotome III, west shore of Lake Turkana |
| August–September 1984 | First excavation season | Richard Leakey's team begins systematic excavation. Multiple postcranial elements recovered |
| 1985 | Second season | Additional elements recovered; Brown et al. publish the first formal description in *Nature* |
| 1985–1988 | Seasons 3–5 | Continued excavation. Total of 108 bone fragments recovered from an area of ~28 m² |
| 1993 | Walker & Leakey monograph published | *The Nariokotome Homo erectus Skeleton* — 16 specialist chapters, the definitive technical analysis |
| 2001 | Latimer & Ohman reanalysis | Identify skeletal dysplasia (congenital vertebral anomaly), reinterpreting the narrow spinal canal as pathological |
| 2015 | Meyer & Haeusler revision | Confirm dysplasia interpretation; argue the specimen cannot be used to infer speech incapacity for the species |

## Key Measurements (KNM-WT 15000)

| Measurement | Value | Significance |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| Age at death | 7.6–8.8 years (dental) or ~11 years (skeletal, modern standards) | Central developmental debate |
| Stature at death | ~160 cm (5'3") | Taller than many adult modern human populations |
| Projected adult stature | ~185 cm (6'1") | Among the tallest hominins known at this date |
| Endocranial volume | ~880 cc | 63% of modern human mean; well within Homo erectus range |
| Completeness | 108 fragments, ~40% of skeleton | Most complete early Homo skeleton ever found |
| Crural index | High (long tibia relative to femur) | Tropical adaptation; matches modern East Africans |
| Bi-iliac breadth | Narrow relative to stature | Consistent with linear tropical body form, contrasts with Lucy's wide pelvis |
