## Body Proportion Comparisons

| Lucy (A. afarensis, AL 288-1) | ~107 | Adult female, ~3.2 Ma |

## Endocranial Volume Comparison

| Species / Specimen | Volume (cc) | Date (Ma) |
|--------------------|------------|-----------|
| Sahelanthropus tchadensis | ~360 | ~7.0 |
| Australopithecus afarensis (Lucy) | ~400–450 | ~3.2 |
| Homo habilis (range) | 510–687 | ~2.4–1.6 |
| **KNM-WT 15000 (Turkana Boy)** | **~880** | **1.53** |
| Homo erectus (full range) | 600–1,100 | 1.9–0.1 |
| Homo naledi | ~465–560 | ~0.335–0.236 |
| Homo heidelbergensis | 1,100–1,400 | ~0.7–0.3 |
| Homo sapiens (modern) | ~1,350 (mean) | ~0.3–present |

**Source**: Antón 2003; Holloway et al. 2004

**Note**: Turkana Boy's 880 cc is a juvenile value. Adult Homo ergaster would likely have reached 900–1,000 cc, based on the ~15% postnatal brain growth expected after age 8–9.

**Sources**: WHO growth standards (modern human); Leigh & Shea 1996 (chimpanzee); Ruff & Walker 1993 (Turkana Boy projections)

**Key insight**: At age ~8, Turkana Boy was already the height of an average modern 14-year-old. Whether this reflects ape-like rapid growth or an intermediate pattern is the core of the age debate.
