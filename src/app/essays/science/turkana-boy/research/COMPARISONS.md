## Synthesis: Where Turkana Boy Sits

### Proportional Modernity Index (qualitative)

A non-quantitative summary of how each species' overall body plan compares to the modern human condition across the metrics above.

| Species | Body Plan Summary | Distance from Modern |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| *A. afarensis* | Committed biped but short-legged, wide-pelved, long-armed. Woodland-adapted compromise. | Distant |
| *A. africanus* | Similar to *A. afarensis* with slightly more derived features. | Distant |
| *H. habilis* | Postcranially still australopith-like; brain expanding but body not yet modern. | Moderate-distant |
| ***H. ergaster* (Turkana Boy)** | **First recognizably modern body plan: long legs, narrow trunk, high crural index, low humerofemoral index, heat-adapted linear form. Ecological logic of the modern body is present.** | **Close** |
| *H. erectus* (later) | Similar to *H. ergaster* with regional variation. Brain continues to expand. Body proportions maintained. | Close |
| Dmanisi | Postcranially similar to Turkana Boy but with smaller brain and some primitive features. | Moderate-close |
| *H. naledi* | Mosaic: modern-looking legs and feet but curved fingers, small brain, primitive shoulders. Not a straight line. | Mixed |
| *H. sapiens* | Reference standard. Extended childhood, large brain, fully modern proportions. | — |

### Key Comparative Insight for the Essay

Turkana Boy is the inflection point. Before him, hominin bodies are compromises — bipedal but not ranging, upright but not linear, heat-tolerant but not heat-optimized. After him, the basic body plan is set: what changes is the brain, the technology, and the culture. The body itself — the long-legged, narrow-trunked, heat-adapted architecture — is Turkana Boy's draft, and we're still running it.

**Key insight**: The two models diverge dramatically in their implications. The fast maturation model (ape-like tempo) means Turkana Boy was nearly full-grown at death and would have been a relatively short adult (~175–180 cm). The extended growth model (modern-human-like tempo) means he had years of growth remaining and would have exceeded 180 cm. The age-at-death question is not academic — it determines the entire reconstruction of his adult form.

**Key insight**: Turkana Boy's proportions fall squarely with modern tropical Africans — specifically resembling the Dinka and other Nilotic populations. This is not coincidence: it reflects the same thermoregulatory solution to the same environmental problem (open equatorial heat stress). Wheeler's modelling (1991, 1993) demonstrated that a tall, narrow body dissipates heat up to 60% more efficiently than a short, wide one in direct sun, and reduces water requirements by approximately 30%.
