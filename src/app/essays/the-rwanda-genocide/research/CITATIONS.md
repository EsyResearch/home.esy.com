## Key Historical Events & Timeline

### Colonial Era & Divisions (1890s–1962)
- 1890s: German colonial rule begins
- 1916: Belgium takes control after WWI
- Colonial powers institutionalize Hutu/Tutsi divisions
- Identity cards mandated, classifying ethnicity

### Post-Independence (1962–1990)
- 1962: Rwanda gains independence
- 1973: Juvénal Habyarimana seizes power
- Periodic anti-Tutsi violence and massacres
- Tutsi refugees form RPF (Rwandan Patriotic Front) in Uganda

### Prelude to Genocide (1990–1994)
- October 1, 1990: RPF invades northern Rwanda
- August 1993: Arusha Accords signed
- October 1993: UNAMIR peacekeeping force deployed
- Hate radio (RTLM) broadcasts anti-Tutsi propaganda

### The Genocide (April–July 1994)
- **April 6, 1994**: President Habyarimana's plane shot down
- **April 7, 1994**: Genocide begins, moderate Hutu politicians killed
- **April 7–July 15, 1994**: ~800,000 killed in 100 days
- **July 4, 1994**: RPF captures Kigali
- **July 15, 1994**: RPF declares military victory

### Aftermath (1994–Present)
- 2 million refugees flee to Zaire (DRC)
- Gacaca courts established for community justice (2001–2012)
- Paul Kagame becomes President (2000–present)
- Annual remembrance period: April 7–July 15

## Victims & Scale

### Death Toll
- **~800,000 killed** in 100 days (approximately 75% of Tutsi population in Rwanda)
- **~8,000 killed per day** during peak of genocide
- **300+ killed per hour**

### Survivors
- ~300,000 Tutsi survivors
- Millions displaced
- Countless orphans, widows, trauma survivors

### Perpetrators
- Organized by Hutu Power extremists
- Interahamwe militias
- Local officials (bourgmestres)
- Ordinary citizens compelled or incentivized to participate

---

## International Response

### UN Peacekeeping (UNAMIR)
- Mandate: Monitor Arusha Accords
- Force Commander: Roméo Dallaire
- Strength reduced from 2,500 to 270 during genocide
- Dallaire's January 1994 warning cable ("Genocide Fax") ignored

### International Inaction
- UN Security Council refused reinforcements
- Belgium withdrew troops after 10 peacekeepers killed
- France: Operation Turquoise (June 1994) — controversial
- United States: Avoided using word "genocide" (legal obligation to act)

### Post-Genocide Justice
- **International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR)**: 1994–2015, Arusha, Tanzania
- **Gacaca Courts**: Community-based justice (2001–2012), ~12,000 courts, 1.2 million cases

## Research Notes

### Verification Status
All sources verified through institutional websites. Death toll estimates based on consensus among Human Rights Watch, USHMM, and academic sources. Testimony cross-referenced where possible.

### Terminology
Following scholarly consensus:
- "Genocide" (legally determined by ICTR)
- Avoid "tribal conflict" framing — this was planned genocide
- "Hutu Power" extremists (not all Hutus participated)
- Many Hutus saved Tutsis or were killed for refusing to participate

### Ethical Presentation
This essay prioritizes:
- Accuracy and historical truth
- Respect for survivors and victims
- Context for prevention education
- Avoiding graphic imagery while conveying reality
- Testimony from direct witnesses

### Controversial Questions
- **Plane shootdown**: Identity of perpetrators remains debated
- **RPF actions**: Some accusations of war crimes during and after
- **French role**: Allegations of supporting Hutu government
- Essay presents consensus where available, notes disputes
