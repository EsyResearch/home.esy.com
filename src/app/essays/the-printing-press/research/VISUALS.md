## Tier 1: Primary Archive Sources

### British Library
**Collection**: Diamond Sutra and Dunhuang manuscripts
**Key Asset**: Diamond Sutra (Or.8210/P.2) — oldest dated printed book (868 CE)
- 16-foot scroll, 10.5 inches wide
- Seven strips of yellow-stained paper
- Woodcut illustration of Buddha expounding to Subhuti (oldest dated book illustration)
- High-resolution images available for educational use

**URL**: https://www.bl.uk/collection-items/the-diamond-sutra
**Access**: Digital images available; contact for high-resolution

---

### Gutenberg Museum, Mainz
**Collection**: World's most significant Gutenberg collection
**Key Assets**:
- Two original Gutenberg Bibles (including Shuckburgh Bible, acquired 1978)
- Reconstructed Gutenberg workshop with working press replica
- Medieval manuscripts and incunabula
- Historic printing presses and typesetting equipment
- Copper engraving of Gutenberg after André Thevet (1584)

**Press Area Downloads**:
- Hand mold photographs
- Type cases in printing workshop
- Interior views of Print Shop
- 42-line Gutenberg Bible pages

**URL**: https://www.mainz.de/microsite/gutenberg-museum-en/
**Access**: Press images available; contact museum for high-resolution

**Note**: Building under renovation; exhibitions temporarily at Natural History Museum

### Bibliothèque nationale de France
**Collection**: Jikji and Asian printing materials
**Key Asset**: Jikji (1377) — oldest surviving metal movable type book
- Only second volume survives (39 leaves)
- 24.6 cm × 17 cm
- Five saddle stitches binding

**URL**: https://www.bnf.fr/
**Access**: Contact Manuscrits Orientaux division

## Era-Specific Visual Treatments

### Era 1: Asian Origins (700s-1400)
**Visual Language**:
- Warm earth tones (clay, paper, wood)
- Brush stroke aesthetics
- Chinese/Korean calligraphic elements
- Aged paper textures

**Reference**: Tang/Song Dynasty art, Korean Goryeo ceramics

### Era 2: Gutenberg's Workshop (1440-1460)
**Visual Language**:
- Medieval European tones (deep reds, golds, dark wood)
- Gothic typography aesthetics
- Candlelit workshop atmosphere
- Metal and wood textures

**Reference**: Northern European medieval art, illuminated manuscripts

### Era 3: European Spread (1460-1500)
**Visual Language**:
- Renaissance Italian influence
- Brighter, more varied palette
- Maps and geographic elements
- Growing commercial energy

**Reference**: Early Renaissance art, maritime maps

### Era 4: The Cascade (1500-1550)
**Visual Language**:
- Reformation-era aesthetics
- Black/white contrast (printed pamphlets)
- Growing chaos/energy
- Information explosion visualization

**Reference**: Reformation woodcuts, Luther-era graphics

## Photography Style Guidelines

- **Artifact shots**: High contrast, dramatic lighting, detail focus
- **Museum interiors**: Environmental context, human scale
- **Document close-ups**: Readable text, texture visible
- **Reconstructions**: Action shots demonstrating process
- **Maps/diagrams**: Clean, modern, animated for scroll-lock
