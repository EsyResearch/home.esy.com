## Key Facts Verification

### Early Pioneers
- **1885**: Karl Benz creates the Benz Patent-Motorwagen (often cited as first automobile)
- **1886**: Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach develop motorized carriage
- **1889**: Daimler founds what becomes Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft (DMG)
- **Internal combustion engine**: Key technology enabling automobiles

### The Model T Revolution
- **1908**: Ford Model T introduced
- **Original price**: $850
- **1913**: Moving assembly line implemented at Highland Park plant
- **1914**: Production time reduced from 12 hours to 93 minutes
- **1920s price**: Dropped to $260 (due to mass production efficiencies)
- **Total produced**: 15 million+ Model Ts (1908-1927)
- **Market share**: ~50% of all cars sold in United States at peak

### Assembly Line Innovation
- **Moving assembly line**: 1913, Highland Park, Michigan
- **Productivity increase**: 8x improvement in production speed
- **Worker wages**: $5/day (1914) - doubled typical manufacturing wages
- **Working hours**: Reduced to 8-hour days
- **Impact**: Made cars affordable for middle class

### Global Expansion
- **Early 20th century**: Automotive industry spreads to Europe, Asia
- **Post-WWII boom**: Rapid expansion in production and ownership
- **1950s-1960s**: Peak of American automotive dominance
- **Japanese quality revolution**: 1970s-1980s challenges American manufacturers
- **Globalization**: Modern supply chains span continents

### Electric Vehicle Resurgence (IEA 2024 Data)
- **2023**: 14 million electric vehicles sold globally
- **Market share**: 18% of new car sales worldwide (2023)
- **China leading**: 60% of global EV sales
- **Europe**: 25% of new car sales are EVs
- **United States**: 10% of new car sales are EVs
- **2030 projection**: 30-35% of global new car sales could be EVs

### Automotive Statistics
- **1 billion+**: Cars on roads worldwide (2024 estimate)
- **80 million+**: New cars sold annually (pre-pandemic levels)
- **3,000+ parts**: Average modern car contains
- **Emissions**: Transportation accounts for ~27% of CO₂ emissions (U.S.)

### Modern Innovations
- **Anti-lock braking** (ABS): 1970s widespread adoption
- **Airbags**: Required in U.S. from 1998
- **GPS navigation**: Consumer availability 1990s-2000s
- **Autonomous driving**: Level 2-3 systems available 2020s
- **Electric powertrains**: Rapid advancement 2010s-2020s

## Research Methodology

Historical timeline verified through Daimler AG corporate archives (1885-1886 Benz and Daimler innovations) and The Henry Ford Museum documentation (Model T and assembly line). Production statistics from Ford Motor Company official records. Early photographic evidence from Library of Congress archives. Encyclopedia Britannica and Smithsonian provide historical context and cross-referencing.

Electric vehicle statistics from International Energy Agency's Global EV Outlook 2024 report, representing most current market data. Industry developments verified through Autocar historical journalism archives. All dates and specifications cross-referenced across multiple primary sources.
