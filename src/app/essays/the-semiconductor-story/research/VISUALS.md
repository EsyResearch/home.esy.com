## Era-Based Visual Treatment

### Era 1: Foundation (1947-1956)
**Treatment**: Black & white, high grain, documentary feel

**Visual Sources**:
- Bell Labs archives (AT&T)
- Nobel Prize archives
- Computer History Museum

**Processing Notes**:
- Maintain authentic B&W aesthetic
- Don't colorize period photography
- Use period-appropriate grain levels

---

### Era 2: Silicon Valley Genesis (1957-1968)
**Treatment**: Faded color, warm tones, Kodachrome aesthetic

**Visual Sources**:
- Computer History Museum (Fairchild founding)
- Intel Museum (founding materials)
- Corporate archives

**Processing Notes**:
- Warm color grading
- Slight desaturation
- Period-appropriate color science

---

### Era 3: Microprocessor Revolution (1968-1987)
**Treatment**: Saturated color, corporate photography aesthetic

**Visual Sources**:
- Intel Museum (product launches, corporate events)
- TIME Magazine (Andy Grove 1997)
- Trade publication archives

**Processing Notes**:
- Higher saturation than modern
- Magazine photography aesthetic
- Clean corporate environments

---

### Era 4: Modern Era (1987-Present)
**Treatment**: High contrast, clean, contemporary editorial

**Visual Sources**:
- TSMC corporate photography
- ASML press materials
- Bloomberg/Reuters photography
- Contemporary cleanroom imagery

**Processing Notes**:
- Modern color science
- High contrast
- Clean, clinical feel for fabs
