## 2. Process Node Evolution

### Complete Timeline

| Year | Node | Key Achievement |
|------|------|-----------------|

| 1974 | 6 μm | Intel 8080 |
| 1978 | 3 μm | Intel 8086 |

| 2003 | 90 nm | - |
| 2005 | 65 nm | - |
| 2007 | 45 nm | - |
| 2009 | 32 nm | - |
| 2012 | 22 nm | Intel FinFET |
| 2013 | 16 nm | TSMC FinFET |
| 2015 | 14 nm | Intel Broadwell |
| 2018 | 7 nm | TSMC, Samsung |
| 2019 | 5 nm | TSMC production |
| 2022 | 3 nm | Samsung GAA, TSMC |
| 2025 | 2 nm | In development |

**Key Transitions**:
- **1997**: Last year gate length matched node name (Intel 0.25 μm)
- **2012**: FinFET transistors introduced (Intel 22nm)
- **2022**: GAA (Gate-All-Around) transistors (Samsung 3nm)

## 3. Global Manufacturing Market Share

### Fabrication Capacity by Region

| Region | 1990 | 2000 | 2020 | 2024 |
|--------|------|------|------|------|
| United States | 37% | ~20% | 12% | 10% |
| Japan | 19% | ~25% | ~15% | 6% |
| Europe | 44% | ~15% | ~8% | - |
| Taiwan | <5% | ~15% | 21% | 22% |
| South Korea | <5% | ~15% | 20% | 12% |
| China | 0% | <5% | 16% | 28% |

### Key Statistics
- **1988**: Japan peaked at 51% of global chip sales
- **2024**: East Asia (Taiwan, Korea, China) holds ~70% of total manufacturing
- **Taiwan**: 60% all semiconductors, 90% advanced semiconductors
