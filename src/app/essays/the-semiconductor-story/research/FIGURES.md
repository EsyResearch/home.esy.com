## 1. William Shockley

**Full Name**: William Bradford Shockley
**Dates**: February 13, 1910 – August 12, 1989
**Nationality**: American

### Key Contributions
- Team leader of Bell Labs solid-state physics group that invented the transistor (1947)
- Developed bipolar junction transistor (1948), more commercially viable than point-contact
- Published "Electrons and Holes in Semiconductors" (1950), foundational textbook
- Founded Shockley Semiconductor Laboratory (1956), spawning Silicon Valley
- Nobel Prize in Physics (1956), shared with Bardeen and Brattain

### Verified Quotes

1. > "It has today occurred to me that an amplifier using semiconductors rather than vacuum is in principle possible."
   - **Source**: Laboratory notebook entry, December 29, 1939
   - **Reference**: [Today in Sci](https://todayinsci.com/S/Shockley_William/ShockleyWilliam-Quotations.htm)

2. > "If you take a bale of hay and tie it to the tail of a mule and then strike a match and set the bale of hay on fire, and if you then compare the energy expended shortly thereafter by the mule with the energy expended by yourself in the striking of the match, you will understand the concept of amplification."
   - **Source**: PBS Transistor documentary materials
   - **Reference**: [PBS Transistor](https://www.pbs.org/transistor/album1/shockley/shockley3.html)

3. > "A magnificent Christmas present"
   - **Context**: Describing the December 23, 1947 transistor demonstration
   - **Reference**: [Computer History Museum](https://www.computerhistory.org/siliconengine/invention-of-the-point-contact-transistor/)

## 2. John Bardeen

**Full Name**: John Bardeen
**Dates**: May 23, 1908 – January 30, 1991
**Nationality**: American

### Key Contributions
- Only person to win Nobel Prize in Physics twice (1956 transistor, 1972 BCS superconductivity)
- Developed surface states theory enabling transistor breakthrough (1947)
- Co-invented point-contact transistor with Brattain (December 16, 1947)
- Pioneer in semiconductor theory

### Verified Quotes

1. > "Science is a field which grows continuously with ever expanding frontiers. Further, it is truly international in scope."
   - **Source**: Nobel Prize Banquet Speech, December 10, 1972
   - **Reference**: [Nobel Prize](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/1972/bardeen/speech/)

2. > "Science is a collaborative effort. The combined results of several people working together is often much more effective than could be that of an individual scientist working alone."
   - **Source**: Nobel Prize Banquet Speech, December 10, 1972
   - **Reference**: [Today in Sci](https://todayinsci.com/B/Bardeen_John/BardeenJohn-Quotations.htm)

3. > "I had no idea of working in solid state physics; in fact, I had no idea what it was when I came to Princeton."
   - **Source**: Lillian Hoddeson, "No Boundaries: University of Illinois Vignettes" (2004)
   - **Reference**: [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/author/quotes/7801606.John_Bardeen)

## 3. Walter Brattain

**Full Name**: Walter Houser Brattain
**Dates**: February 10, 1902 – October 13, 1987
**Nationality**: American (born in China to American parents)

### Key Contributions
- Experimental physicist who physically constructed first transistor
- Co-invented point-contact transistor with Bardeen (December 1947)
- Expert in surface physics and semiconductor materials
- Nobel Prize in Physics (1956)

### Verified Quotes

1. > "I would like to start by emphasizing the importance of surfaces. It is at a surface where many of our most interesting and useful phenomena occur. We live for example on the surface of a planet."
   - **Source**: Nobel Lecture, December 11, 1956
   - **Reference**: [Nobel Prize](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/1956/brattain/biographical/)

2. > "It is at a surface where the catalysis of chemical reactions occur. It is essentially at a surface of a plant that sunlight is converted to a sugar. In electronics, most if not all active circuit elements involve non-equilibrium phenomena occurring at surfaces."
   - **Source**: Nobel Lecture, December 11, 1956
   - **Reference**: [Citatis](https://citatis.com/a32692/)

3. > "Bardeen and I produced an amplifier using the field effects at very low frequencies... But the next night, I swore them all to secrecy."
   - **Context**: Account during "Miracle Month" of 1947
   - **Reference**: [Computer History Museum](https://computerhistory.org/blog/the-surface-state-job/)

## 4. Robert Noyce

**Full Name**: Robert Norton Noyce
**Dates**: December 12, 1927 – June 3, 1990
**Nationality**: American
**Epithet**: "The Mayor of Silicon Valley"

### Key Contributions
- Co-invented monolithic integrated circuit using silicon and planar process (1959)
- Co-founded Fairchild Semiconductor (1957) as one of the "Traitorous Eight"
- Co-founded Intel Corporation (1968) with Gordon Moore
- First CEO of Intel (1968-1975)
- Pioneered practical IC that enabled mass production

### Verified Quotes

1. > "Innovation is everything. When you're on the forefront, you can see what the next innovation needs to be."
   - **Source**: Various interviews and writings
   - **Reference**: [Toolshero](https://www.toolshero.com/toolsheroes/robert-noyce/)

2. > "Knowledge is power. Knowledge shared is power multiplied."
   - **Source**: Documented in multiple biographies
   - **Reference**: [Stanford](https://web.stanford.edu/class/e145/2007_fall/materials/noyce.html)

3. > "I don't have any recollection of a 'Boom! There it is!' light bulb going off... [It was] a logical sequence. If I hit a wall, I'd back up and then find a path, conceptually, all the way through to the end."
   - **Context**: Interview about the invention of the IC
   - **Reference**: [Stanford](https://web.stanford.edu/class/e145/2007_fall/materials/noyce.html)

4. > Predicted in 1965: "portable telephones, personal paging systems, and palm-sized TVs"
   - **Source**: Leslie Berlin's biography
   - **Reference**: [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Robert-Noyce)

## 5. Gordon Moore

**Full Name**: Gordon Earle Moore
**Dates**: January 3, 1929 – March 24, 2023
**Nationality**: American

### Key Contributions
- Formulated "Moore's Law" (1965) — transistor density doubles every ~2 years
- Co-founded Fairchild Semiconductor (1957) as one of "Traitorous Eight"
- Co-founded Intel Corporation (1968) with Robert Noyce
- Intel CEO (1975-1987), Chairman (1979-1997), Chairman Emeritus (1997-2023)

### Verified Quotes

1. > "The complexity for minimum component costs has increased at a rate of roughly a factor of two per year. Certainly over the short term this rate can be expected to continue, if not to increase."
   - **Source**: "Cramming more components onto integrated circuits," Electronics Magazine, April 19, 1965
   - **Reference**: [Computer History Museum](https://www.computerhistory.org/siliconengine/moores-law-predicts-the-future-of-integrated-circuits/)

2. > "I just did a wild extrapolation saying it's going to continue to double every year for the next 10 years."
   - **Source**: 2015 interview reflecting on 1965 article
   - **Reference**: [Intel](https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/history/virtual-vault/articles/moores-law.html)

## 6. Andy Grove

**Full Name**: Andrew Stephen Grove (born Grof Andras Istvan)
**Dates**: September 2, 1936 – March 21, 2016
**Nationality**: Hungarian-American

### Key Contributions
- Third employee and third CEO of Intel (1987-1998)
- Transformed Intel into world's largest semiconductor company
- Led "Intel Inside" marketing campaign
- Author of "Only the Paranoid Survive" (1996)
- TIME Magazine "Man of the Year" (1997)
- Championed strategic pivot from memory chips to microprocessors

### Verified Quotes

1. > "Success breeds complacency. Complacency breeds failure. Only the paranoid survive."
   - **Source**: "Only the Paranoid Survive" (1996)
   - **Reference**: [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/work/quotes/664484-only-the-paranoid-survive)

2. > "A strategic inflection point is a time in the life of business when its fundamentals are about to change. That change can mean an opportunity to rise to new heights. But it may just as likely signal the beginning of the end."
   - **Source**: "Only the Paranoid Survive" (1996)
   - **Reference**: [Inc.](https://www.inc.com/jeremy-goldman/13-insightful-quotes-from-intel-visionary-andy-grove.html)

3. > "Bad companies are destroyed by crisis, Good companies survive them, Great companies are improved by them."
   - **Source**: Various Grove writings and speeches
   - **Reference**: [BrainyQuote](https://www.brainyquote.com/authors/andy-grove-quotes)

4. > "Your career is literally your business. You own it as a sole proprietor."
   - **Source**: "Only the Paranoid Survive" (1996)
   - **Reference**: [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/author/quotes/37708.Andrew_S_Grove)

## 7. Jack Kilby

**Full Name**: Jack St. Clair Kilby
**Dates**: November 8, 1923 – June 20, 2005
**Nationality**: American

### Key Contributions
- Invented first integrated circuit at Texas Instruments (September 12, 1958)
- Co-inventor of handheld calculator
- Co-inventor of thermal printer
- Held over 60 patents
- Nobel Prize in Physics (2000)
- National Medal of Science (1970)

### Verified Quotes

1. > "While Robert and I followed our own paths, we worked hard together to achieve commercial acceptance for integrated circuits. If he were still living, I have no doubt we would have shared this prize."
   - **Context**: Nobel Prize acceptance, referencing Robert Noyce (died 1990)
   - **Reference**: [Nobel Prize](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2000/kilby/biographical/)

2. > "People often ask me what I'm proud of, and, of course, the integrated circuit is at the top of the list. I'm also proud of my wonderful family. I have two daughters and five granddaughters, so you could say that the Kilbys specialized in girls."
   - **Source**: Nobel Prize biographical notes
   - **Reference**: [Nobel Prize](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2000/kilby/biographical/)

3. > "My dad's goal was to do whatever it took to run his business and to help people, but I thought that amateur radio was a fascinating subject. It sparked my interest in electronics, and that's when I decided that this field was something I wanted to pursue."
   - **Context**: On the ice storm that sparked his interest
   - **Reference**: [Texas Instruments](https://www.ti.com/about-ti/newsroom/company-blog/from-idea-to-invention-the-origin-story-of-the-tiny-chip-that-changed-the-world.html)

## 8. Morris Chang

**Full Name**: Morris Chang Chung-mou (Chinese: 張忠謀)
**Dates**: Born July 10, 1931 (age 93)
**Nationality**: Taiwanese-American

### Key Contributions
- Founded TSMC (1987), world's first dedicated semiconductor foundry
- Pioneered "pure-play foundry" business model enabling fabless semiconductor industry
- TSMC CEO (1987-2005, 2009-2018) and Chairman (until 2018)
- Previously Group VP at Texas Instruments (1958-1983)
- Transformed Taiwan into global semiconductor manufacturing hub

### Verified Quotes

1. > "Without strategy, execution is aimless. Without execution, strategy is useless."
   - **Source**: Multiple interviews and business school case studies
   - **Reference**: [Antoine Buteau](https://www.antoinebuteau.com/lessons-from-morris-chang-of-tsmc/)

2. > "Our strategy is simple: We are everybody's foundry."
   - **Source**: TSMC corporate philosophy
   - **Reference**: [Quartr](https://quartr.com/insights/edge/the-silicon-empire-tsmcs-revolution-and-morris-changs-legacy)

3. > "A lot of people have dreams. I have a vision. A dream is something you can do by yourself. A vision is something that you need a team for."
   - **Source**: Various interviews and profiles
   - **Reference**: [CommonWealth Magazine](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=3114)

4. > "I believe technology is of the utmost importance, and I believe that in a tech company, nothing is more important than having a CEO with a tech background."
   - **Source**: Leadership philosophy discussions
   - **Reference**: [CommonWealth Magazine](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=3849)

### Quote About Morris Chang

> "The world is full of successful people, but heroes are rare. There is a difference between success and impact. I think Morris - his career, his philosophies, TSMC, its strategy, its core values - is absolutely a study in industrial revolution."
> — **Jensen Huang**, NVIDIA CEO, Computer History Museum event, October 2007
