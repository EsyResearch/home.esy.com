**Date Compiled**: December 19, 2025

## Research Overview

This research package provides the cognitive science, rhetorical theory, and visual metaphor foundations for a visual essay that treats hooks as **cognitive mechanisms** rather than tips or formulas.

## Core Finding

**A hook is a cognitive threshold** - a transition point where the reader crosses from their world into the essay's world. Like a doorway, it creates an "event boundary" in the brain, signaling that a new mental episode is beginning.

- **Secondary Metaphor**: The Lens (for orientation/focus function)

## Key Insights for Visual Essay

1. **Mechanism over Tactics**: Hooks work because humans are prediction machines with limited processing capacity, driven by information gaps, wired to orient toward novelty
2. **The Inverted U-Curve**: Too little information = overwhelming; too much = boring; moderate gap = maximum curiosity
3. **Three Classical Goals**: Make the audience attentive, receptive, and well-disposed (not just "grab attention")
4. **Visible Artifice Problem**: Quintilian warned that technique calling attention to itself undermines trust - applicable to "gimmicky" hooks
5. **Doorway as Transformation**: Unlike bridges (same on both sides) or lenses (focus as destination), doorways capture the transformational crossing moment
