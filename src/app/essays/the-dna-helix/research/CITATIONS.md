## Key Facts Verification

### Discovery Timeline
- **1869**: Friedrich Miescher discovers "nuclein" (documented)
- **1950**: Erwin Chargaff discovers base pairing rules (A=T, G=C)
- **1952**: Rosalind Franklin captures Photo 51 (May 1952)
- **1953**: Watson & Crick publish double helix structure (April 25, 1953, Nature)
- **2003**: Human Genome Project completed (13 years, $2.7 billion)
- **2012**: Jennifer Doudna & Emmanuelle Charpentier develop CRISPR-Cas9

### Base Pairing
- **A-T pair**: 2 hydrogen bonds
- **G-C pair**: 3 hydrogen bonds
- **Chargaff's Rules**: Amount of adenine equals thymine; guanine equals cytosine

### Human Genome Statistics
- **3.2 billion base pairs**: Total human DNA
- **23 chromosome pairs**: Human genetic material
- **99.9% identical**: Human DNA similarity between individuals
- **0.1% difference**: What makes each person unique
