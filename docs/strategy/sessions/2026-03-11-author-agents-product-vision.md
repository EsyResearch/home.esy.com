**Esy is where you go to hire author agents or write in your own writing style.**

Two offerings, one platform:
1. **Hire a pre-built author agent** — curated agents with distinct voices and specialties
2. **Create your own agent** — fine-tuned to your personal writing style from your own samples

## The Problem

Every AI writing tool produces the same voice. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Jasper, Copy.ai — they all generate text that reads like it came from the same machine. The tells are obvious: em dashes everywhere, "delve," three-part lists with colons, relentless hedging, performative positivity. Readers have developed instincts for it. The homogenization is so complete that AI-written content is becoming a liability rather than an asset.

This is a universal problem that affects:
- **Students** who need essays that sound like they wrote them
- **Professionals** who need resumes, cover letters, and LinkedIn content in their voice
- **Writers** (Substack, blogs, books) who want AI assistance without losing their identity
- **Executives** building personal brands through thought leadership
- **Businesses** that need brand-voice-consistent content at scale

Nobody has solved this. Current tools offer "style" or "tone" dropdowns (formal, casual, professional) — those are costumes, not voices. A real voice is sentence rhythm, vocabulary instincts, how someone handles uncertainty, their relationship with punctuation, how they open arguments. That's what Esy captures.

## The Product

### How It Works

1. **User submits writing samples** — essays, emails, messages, articles, anything that sounds like them at their best
2. **Esy builds their author agent** — extracts voice fingerprints: sentence patterns, vocabulary, argument structure, tone, personality
3. **User requests content through an intake form** — topic, length, citation format, type (essay, resume, cover letter, article)
4. **Agent produces content in their voice with real citations** — the output reads like the user wrote it, not like AI generated it
5. **Agent improves over time** — more usage and feedback makes the voice model better

### Two Product Tiers

**Pre-Built Author Agents (Hire an Agent)**
- Curated agents with distinct voices and specialties
- Available immediately — no writing samples needed
- Examples: "The Science Writer," "The Economics Analyst," "The Storyteller," "The Technical Writer"
- Lower price point, lower friction, immediate value
- Serves: people who just need something written well and distinctly, fast

**Custom Author Agents (Write as You)**
- Fine-tuned to the user's personal writing style
- Requires writing sample upload (essays, emails, articles)
- The agent becomes their persistent writing identity
- Higher price point, higher value, higher lock-in
- Serves: professionals, writers, students who care about authenticity

### The Intake Form (Core UX Pattern)

The intake form remains the central UX — users don't write prompts, they fill out structured fields:

- **Select or create agent** (pre-built or custom)
- **Content type** (essay, research paper, cover letter, resume, article, blog post)
- **Topic / job description / subject**
- **Length**
- **Citation format** (APA, MLA, Chicago — for academic content)
- **Number of sources** (for research content)
- **Additional context** (specific requirements, professor's rubric, job posting)
- **Generate**

No prompt engineering. The template absorbs the complexity. The agent carries the voice.

## Why This Wins

### The PhotoAI Parallel

Pieter Levels built PhotoAI — upload selfies, the AI learns your face, generates photos of you in any context. $100K+/month revenue. One core mechanic: fine-tuning on personal identity.

Esy is the same model applied to writing voice instead of visual identity. Upload your writing, the AI learns your voice, generates content that sounds like you in any context. Same mechanic, higher stakes (career, grades, reputation), deeper lock-in.

### Moat: Compounding Stickiness

The longer someone uses their author agent, the more writing it ingests, the better it gets at sounding like them. Switching to a competitor means starting over and losing everything the model learned. This is genuine lock-in that deepens with usage — not artificial lock-in through data hostage.

### Moat: Portability as Trust

Users can export their voice profile. Esy doesn't trap their identity — it makes it portable. Paradoxically, this creates stronger retention because users trust you. You're the only platform treating their voice as THEIR asset, not your data.

### Defense Against Frontier Labs

If OpenAI or Anthropic ships native voice matching:
- That validates the concept and educates the market (good for Esy)
- Esy becomes the **portable personality layer** that works across ALL models
- "Your voice shouldn't be trapped inside ChatGPT. It should work everywhere."
- Esy sits between the frontier models and the user — like Stripe sits between banks and products

### The API Play: Personality as a Service

The long-term infrastructure play: an API that carries someone's voice to any application.
- Other writing tools license Esy's voice infrastructure
- Businesses use the API to maintain brand voice across all communications
- The author agent becomes a persistent identity layer across the entire writing surface of the internet

## Go-To-Market

### Phase 1: Build & Demonstrate (Now — Month 3)

**Build your own author agent first.**
- Collect your best writing samples (essays, emails, messages)
- Build the voice capture pipeline
- Test: can the agent produce content that sounds like you?
- This IS the product research — every friction point becomes a product insight

**Build 5-10 pre-built agents from friends/family.**
- Each person contributes writing samples
- Each agent has a distinct voice and specialty
- Publish at `esy.com/agents` — the public demonstration page
- Side-by-side proof that voice differentiation is real

**Offer free credits to students.**
- Students upload their writing, build their voice, test the agent
- You get volume, feedback, and real conversations with potential customers
- Students are ideal early users: write constantly, give honest feedback, have source material

### Phase 2: Launch & Charge (Month 3-6)

**Pricing structure:**
- Free tier: 1-2 essays/month with a pre-built agent
- $12-19/month: Unlimited essays with pre-built agents
- $29-39/month: Custom author agent (your voice) + all content types
- Per-artifact pricing option for occasional users

**Landing pages targeting proven demand:**
- `esy.com/essay-writer` — "Essays that sound like you wrote them"
- `esy.com/research-paper-writer` — "Research papers with real citations, in your voice"
- `esy.com/resume-writer` — "Resumes in your voice, not AI voice"
- `esy.com/cover-letter-writer` — "Cover letters that sound like you, not a template"

**SEO content on `/school/`:**
- "How to make AI writing sound like you"
- "Why AI essays all sound the same (and how to fix it)"
- "Best AI essay writer that doesn't sound like AI"
- Question-format H1s, concise first-paragraph answers, video + transcript

### Phase 3: Expand & Scale (Month 6-12)

- Add content types: LinkedIn posts, blog articles, book chapters, video scripts
- Launch comparison pages: `esy.com/compare/esy-vs-chatgpt`, `esy.com/compare/esy-vs-jenni-ai`
- Build free tools for SEO: citation formatter, APA generator, word counter, reading time calculator
- Dense footer internal linking (the Levels playbook)
- Explore fantasy sports, stock research, and other verticals as separate agent types
- Begin API development for Personality as a Service

### Phase 4: Platform (Month 12+)

- API launch — other apps can use Esy voice profiles
- Marketplace — users share/sell their agent templates
- Enterprise — businesses create brand voice agents for their teams
- Cross-platform portability — export voice profiles, use anywhere

## Technical Direction

### Voice Capture Pipeline

- User uploads 5-20 writing samples (minimum word count TBD through testing)
- Pipeline extracts: sentence length distribution, vocabulary frequency, punctuation patterns, argument structure, opening/closing tendencies, rhetorical habits, formality level
- Voice profile stored as structured data associated with the user's agent
- Applied as system-level context when generating any content

### Model Strategy

- Not dependent on one model — voice profiles work as context for any LLM
- Use the best model for each subtask (research vs. writing vs. voice matching)
- Voice profile is model-agnostic — the personality layer sits above the model layer

### Quality Bar

- "Could have come from me" — not perfect, just demonstrably better than generic AI
- 70% voice accuracy is a perceptual leap given how flat current AI writing is
- System improves with usage — more writing samples + feedback = better voice match

3. **One avatar, one workflow first** — students/writers who want essays in their own voice with real citations
4. **Multi-vertical expansion later** — fantasy sports, stock research, video scripts all use the same agent architecture
5. **Landing page strategy is the growth engine** — hundreds of pages targeting different keywords, same product behind all of them
6. **Levels' SEO playbook** — use case pages + comparison pages + free tools + dense internal linking
7. **esy.com/school stays** — restructure articles as question-format H1s for featured snippets and LLM citations
8. **FAQ schema still valuable for LLMs** — Google won't show rich results, but ChatGPT/Perplexity still parse it (confirmed: users already coming from ChatGPT)
9. **EssayPro is dying** — organic traffic down 51K in one month, structural decline. The demand persists, the supply is collapsing. Esy fills the gap.
10. **The domain works** — esy.com reads as "easy," brand-neutral enough for any vertical, memorable and short
