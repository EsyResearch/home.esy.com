# Channel & Funnel Architecture

**Date:** March 24, 2026
**Type:** Strategy Session — Distribution Channels, Audience Segmentation & Monetization Funnel
**Context:** Defines how Skool, LinkedIn, two newsletters, courses, and the Esy app connect into a unified growth system with clear audience routing.

---

## Two Audiences, Two Funnels

Esy serves two distinct audiences. Each has its own entry point, newsletter, and conversion path. Mixing them kills relevance.

### Technical Builders

People who want to build AI agent workflows, understand architecture, write code.

```
skool.com/ai-agents (free community)
    → synthesize.esy.com (Substack newsletter — technical breakdowns)
        → Esy app (platform/tooling)
```

### Knowledge Workers (the revenue engine)

Researchers, students, corporate workers learning to use AI tools for their job.

```
LinkedIn (daily content)
    → esy.com/school (Beehiiv newsletter — AI skills for work)
        → esy.com/courses (structured learning)
            → Esy app (workflow product)
```

## Channel Roles

| Channel | Audience | Purpose | Effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | Knowledge workers, corporate, researchers | Primary discovery for revenue audience | 70% of growth energy |
| **skool.com/ai-agents** | Technical builders, developers | Community discovery + credibility | 30% of growth energy |
| **synthesize.esy.com** | Technical audience | Deep workflow breakdowns, build trust with builders | Secondary |
| **esy.com/school** | Knowledge workers | AI skills education, primary email capture | Primary |
| **esy.com/courses** | Both (skewed knowledge workers) | Structured learning, onboarding into Esy | Conversion layer |
| **Esy app** | Both | The product. Recurring revenue. | End of funnel |

## Why LinkedIn → School Is the Money Path

| Audience | How they pay | LTV | Sales cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Technical builders | Hard to monetize unless Esy is infra they depend on | High if sticky | Short |
| Researchers/students | Low individual, institutional budgets possible | Low–medium | Long |
| **Corporate workers** | Company budget, expense it, team seats | **Highest — recurring, multi-seat** | Medium |

Corporate workers are the primary revenue target. They have budgets, need to justify AI adoption, and pay monthly for tools that make them productive. LinkedIn is where they live.

## Skool Strategy

### What skool.com/ai-agents is for

- Free community — no paywall
- Top-of-funnel for the technical audience
- Funnels members into synthesize.esy.com (not School)
- Builds credibility, ecosystem, word-of-mouth

### What it is not

- Not a revenue source (no paid membership)
- Not a course platform (courses live on esy.com)
- Not for the School audience (different intent — "AI agents" signals builder mindset)

### Skool's DR 91 does not transfer to esy.com

Skool community pages sit on skool.com's domain. Any ranking power benefits Skool, not Esy. The community is valuable for audience building, not SEO. The page may rank for long-tail terms like "AI agents community" but won't crack competitive head terms like "AI agents" due to thin content and intent mismatch.

## Newsletter Architecture

### synthesize.esy.com (Substack)

- **Audience:** Technical builders, developers
- **Content:** How workflows are built for Esy, architecture decisions, prompt engineering patterns, technical deep-dives
- **Custom domain:** synthesize.esy.com — brand stays with Esy, backend swappable later
- **Subscriber list is exportable** — Substack dependency is manageable

### esy.com/school (Beehiiv)

- **Audience:** Researchers, students, corporate workers
- **Content:** How to use AI tools at work — Claude, NotebookLM, Notion, practical tutorials
- **Role:** Primary email capture mechanism, revenue funnel

### Content routing filter

> "Would a marketing manager understand and benefit from this?"

- **Yes** → esy.com/school
- **No, requires code** → synthesize.esy.com

## Courses Strategy

### esy.com/courses is an AI skills platform, not an Esy tutorial hub

Courses cover the full AI tool landscape:
- How to use Claude Code
- How to use Claude Cowork
- Notion + Claude workflows
- NotebookLM tutorials
- AI research fundamentals
- Esy-specific workflow courses

This is the bigger play. "Learn how to use Esy" is a product tutorial. "Learn how to use AI tools for work" is a destination.

### Three tiers

| Tier | Content | Pricing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1: Individual tools** | Claude Code, Claude Cowork, NotebookLM, Notion AI | Free | Volume drivers, SEO, top of funnel |
| **Tier 2: Workflows** | Multi-tool pipelines, research workflows, end-to-end processes | Free now, paid later | Esy naturally appears as the glue |
| **Tier 3: Esy-native** | Build workflows in Esy, advanced automations, Esy for teams | Free now, paid later | Product adoption |

### First 5–10 courses: all free

No distribution yet. Charging now optimizes for revenue that doesn't exist. Free courses get:
- Proof of demand
- Completion data
- Testimonials
- Users inside the Esy app

### When to introduce paid courses

- 500+ completions across free courses
- Users asking for advanced content
- Clear data on which courses drive app signups
- Social proof established

### Long-term model

Free courses stay free forever (permanent top-of-funnel). Paid courses become the next level. Eventually bundle into a membership: all courses + Esy app for one monthly price. Courses become a retention feature, not a separate revenue line.

## The Complete Funnel

```
LinkedIn (free daily content — "how to use AI at work")
    ↓
esy.com/school (free newsletter — nurture + trust)
    ↓
esy.com/courses (free → paid — structured learning using Esy)
    ↓
Esy app (subscription — the product)

skool.com/ai-agents (free community — technical discovery)
    ↓
synthesize.esy.com (newsletter — technical depth + trust)
    ↓
Esy app (platform/tooling for builders)
```

### Path variations

- **Knowledge worker who needs hand-holding:** School → Courses → App
- **Knowledge worker who's ready:** School → App directly
- **Technical builder:** Skool → Synthesize → App
- **Search-driven discovery:** Google → Course page → App

All paths end at recurring revenue from the Esy app. Courses aren't an alternative to the app — they're onboarding disguised as education.
