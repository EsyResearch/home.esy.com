# Era Treatment Guide: The Scramble for Africa

> **Visual Treatment**: Archival documentary photography + cartographic storytelling  
> **Primary Eras**: Pre-Scramble, Scramble Peak, Consolidation, Legacy

---

## Era 1: Pre-Scramble Context (Before 1880)

### Historical Character
- European knowledge of Africa limited to coasts
- Age of exploration and mapping
- Missionary and commercial activity
- Pre-colonial African states at their zenith

### Visual Treatment

**Photography Style**:
- Sepia tones or warm black-and-white
- Early photographic techniques (daguerreotype aesthetics)
- More reliance on engravings and illustrations from period publications
- Vignette effects suggesting older documentation

**Color Palette**:
- Parchment and aged paper tones: `#F4E4C1`, `#E8D5B7`
- Faded sepia: `#8B7355`, `#6B5344`
- Ink blacks softened: `#2C2416`

**Processing Effects**:
- Subtle grain
- Slightly faded contrast
- Warm color cast
- Soft vignetting at edges

**Map Style**:
- Hand-drawn cartographic aesthetics
- Period typography
- Incomplete coastlines suggesting limited knowledge
- Terra incognita styling for unexplored regions

**Typography Association**:
- Serif fonts suggesting Victorian-era publishing
- Ornate chapter markers
- Period newspaper styling

---

## Era 2: The Scramble Peak (1880–1900)

### Historical Character
- Intense European competition
- Berlin Conference reshapes continent
- Major battles and resistance
- Colonial infrastructure beginnings
- Height of "civilizing mission" ideology

### Visual Treatment

**Photography Style**:
- Sharp black-and-white
- High contrast for dramatic effect
- Documentary photography emerging
- Formal posed portraits for leaders
- Battlefield photography beginning

**Color Palette**:
- Stark contrasts: pure black `#000000` and cream `#F5F5DC`
- Deep burgundy for violence/conflict: `#722F37`
- Colonial administrative green: `#355E3B`
- Brass/gold for European military: `#B5A642`
- Iron gray for industrialization: `#434B4D`

**Processing Effects**:
- Higher contrast than Era 1
- Crisp grain
- Some dust/scratch effects for archival feel
- More defined shadows

**Map Style**:
- Clear territorial boundaries
- Color-coded colonial powers
- Railroad and port additions
- Before/after comparisons

**Typography Association**:
- Strong serif headings
- Official document styling
- Treaty and proclamation aesthetics

---

## Era 3: Consolidation (1900–1914)

### Historical Character
- Colonial systems entrenched
- Reform movements emerge
- Atrocity documentation
- Final territorial adjustments
- Pre-WWI tensions building

### Visual Treatment

**Photography Style**:
- Improved photography quality
- Documentary journalism emerging
- Atrocity photography (Congo, Herero)
- Administrative and infrastructure documentation
- Some early color tinting possible

**Color Palette**:
- Cleaner blacks and whites
- Document cream: `#FFFDD0`
- Reform movement colors: justice blue `#4169E1`
- Blood red for atrocities: `#8B0000`
- Institutional gray: `#696969`

**Processing Effects**:
- Cleaner, sharper images
- Less grain than earlier eras
- More professional framing
- Occasional tinting for emphasis

**Map Style**:
- Detailed colonial boundaries
- Infrastructure networks prominent
- Statistical overlays possible
- Modern cartographic styling emerging

**Typography Association**:
- Cleaner typefaces
- Reform pamphlet aesthetics
- Newspaper headline styling

---

## Era 4: Legacy and Modern Parallels (Post-1914)

### Historical Character
- WWI reshuffles colonies
- Seeds of independence movements
- Continuing impact of colonial borders
- Modern recognition and reckoning

### Visual Treatment

**Photography Style**:
- Transition to modern photography
- Color photography available for recent materials
- Documentary style fully developed
- Contrast with earlier eras

**Color Palette**:
- Modern neutrals
- Independence movement colors (Pan-African: red, gold, green)
- Contemporary design sensibilities
- High contrast for emphasis

**Processing Effects**:
- Modern processing
- Clear, sharp presentation
- Color for contemporary context
- Black-and-white for historical continuity

**Map Style**:
- Modern nation-state boundaries
- Colonial origin overlays
- Data visualization integration
- Interactive potential

---

## Thematic Visual Treatments

### Resistance and Victory

**Applied to**: Adwa, Isandlwana, Samori Ture, Mahdist Sudan

**Treatment**:
- Elevated contrast suggesting drama
- Warm tones suggesting African perspective
- Motion blur effects for battle energy
- Hero lighting on African leaders

**Color Accent**: Gold/amber `#FFB347` for triumph

---

### Colonial Brutality

**Applied to**: Congo atrocities, Herero genocide, forced labor

**Treatment**:
- High contrast black-and-white
- Documentary starkness
- No romanticization
- Clinical, evidence-based presentation

**Color Accent**: Blood red `#8B0000` used sparingly

---

### European Machination

**Applied to**: Berlin Conference, treaty deception, diplomatic maneuvering

**Treatment**:
- Formal, almost sterile presentation
- Document and map focus
- Cold color temperatures
- Bureaucratic aesthetics

**Color Accent**: Cold gray-blue `#6B7B8C`

---

### African Societies Pre-Colonization

**Applied to**: Context setting, what was lost

**Treatment**:
- Warm, rich tones
- Emphasis on sophistication and organization
- Cultural artifact focus
- Respectful, non-exoticizing presentation

**Color Accent**: Earth tones `#8B4513`, `#CD853F`

---

## Transition Styling

### Era-to-Era Transitions

**Pre-Scramble → Scramble Peak**:
- Gradual contrast increase
- Warm to neutral color shift
- Map complexity increasing
- Typography sharpening

**Scramble Peak → Consolidation**:
- Photography quality improvement
- Documentary aesthetics strengthening
- Color palette expanding
- Reform imagery emerging

**Consolidation → Legacy**:
- Historical to contemporary bridge
- Black-and-white to color transition
- Archival to modern sources
- Analytical overlay integration

## Animation Principles

### Era-Appropriate Motion

**Pre-Scramble**:
- Slow, deliberate reveals
- Map unfurling effects
- Page-turn aesthetics
- Contemplative pacing

**Scramble Peak**:
- Faster, more urgent motion
- Territory claiming animations
- Battle energy effects
- Competitive tension in timing

**Consolidation**:
- Documentary steadiness
- Evidence presentation pacing
- Reform urgency building
- Accumulating impact effects

**Legacy**:
- Reflective, measured pace
- Historical perspective zooms
- Connection-drawing animations
- Cyclical/echoing motions
