## Research Summary

### Topic Scope
The Scramble for Africa encompasses the period from approximately 1870 to 1914, during which European powers partitioned and colonized nearly the entire African continent. This research covers:

- **Causes**: Economic drivers, nationalist competition, technological enablers, ideological justifications
- **Key Events**: Berlin Conference, major battles and treaties, colonial administrations
- **Key Figures**: European architects (Leopold II, Bismarck, Rhodes) and African leaders (Menelik II, Samori Touré, Cetshwayo)
- **Resistance**: Military opposition, diplomatic strategies, adaptation
- **Impact**: Border creation, economic extraction, cultural suppression, lasting legacies
