## Layer 1: Strategic Foundation

### Project Title
**The Silicon Revolution**
*How a Tiny Switch Changed Everything*

### Executive Brief

**Central Question**: How did a laboratory curiosity invented in 1947 become the foundation of modern civilization—and why does its future now shape the fate of nations?

**Emotional Throughline**: Discovery → Industrialization → Globalization → Reckoning

The reader begins with curiosity about how technology works, passes through the entrepreneurial drama of Silicon Valley's formation, watches manufacturing capability migrate across the Pacific, and arrives at the present moment where chips have become as strategic as oil. They leave understanding not just *what* semiconductors are, but *why* they matter and *who* made them matter.

**Stakes**: 
- Without semiconductors, no phones, no internet, no modern medicine, no electric vehicles, no AI
- A single earthquake in Taiwan could halt global automobile production
- The race for chip supremacy now defines US-China competition

**Transformation Promise**: The reader will stop seeing "chips" as abstract black boxes and start seeing the human decisions, manufacturing miracles, and geopolitical chess moves that make their world run.

### Visual Treatment Philosophy

**Primary Mode**: Photorealistic
- Archival photography from Bell Labs, Fairchild, Intel eras
- Corporate/press photography for modern figures
- Facility photography (fabs, cleanrooms, machines)
- Product photography (transistors, chips, wafers)

**Secondary Mode**: Technical Illustration
- Transistor operation diagrams (clear, not busy)
- Fabrication process visualization
- Process node comparison graphics
- EUV lithography explanation

**Data Visualization Mode**:
- Moore's Law progression (animated chart)
- Global manufacturing distribution (map visualization)
- Company market share (over time)
- Process node evolution timeline

**Hybrid Rules**:
- Clear separation between modes
- Photos for people and places
- Diagrams for concepts and processes
- Data viz for metrics and trends
- Never composite photos with illustrations

**Tone Guidance**: EDUCATOR
- Clarity over drama
- One metaphor per chapter maximum
- Avoid: purple prose, forced profundity, metaphor soup
- Model after: Asianometry, Bloomberg Businessweek, NYT tech explainers

### Scroll-Lock Specification

**Philosophy**: Scroll-lock sequences reveal complexity—used for technical explanations and pivotal moments, not for drama alone.

**Lock Zones**:

5. **EUV Lithography** (Chapter 6) — 700px lock for technical reveal
6. **Global Manufacturing Map** (Chapter 7) — 600px lock for geographic animation

**Scroll-as-Input Behavior**:
- Scroll drives animation progress (not time)
- Smooth easing on lock/unlock (400ms cubic-bezier)
- Skip affordance always visible ("↓ Scroll to continue" or "Skip →")
- Reduced motion: static key frames with manual advance

### Themed Progress Indicator

**Concept**: Circuit Trace Progress Bar

A stylized circuit trace that builds across the screen as the reader progresses. The trace:
- Starts as a simple line (transistor era)
- Gains branches and complexity (integrated circuit era)
- Becomes dense and layered (modern era)
- Reaches a chip icon at 100%
