### Animation Philosophy

**"The Written Word"** â€” This essay's animation should feel like watching language being written, transmitted, and transformed. Motion evokes inscription, transmission across wire, and the quiet accumulation of meaning over centuries.

- **Preferred easing**: `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` â€” Material Design standard: purposeful, not showy
- **Greek letter appearance**: Stroke animation (stroke-dasharray/dashoffset), 800ms per character â€” like watching a scribe
- **Compound assembly**: Elements slide and dock with `ease-out`, 400ms â€” mechanical, like a printing press
- **Tree growth**: Branches extend with `ease-in-out`, 600ms â€” organic, like a living word growing
- **Truncation**: Letters fade with `ease-out`, 500ms â€” gradual loss, like erosion
- **Era transitions**: Background color crossfade, 800ms â€” the word moving through time
- **Scroll-driven**: All major animations tied to scroll position, not time
- **`prefers-reduced-motion`**: All animations collapse to simple opacity fades, 200ms
