## Research Questions

### Primary Questions

1. **Who invented the fork?**
   - Answer: No single inventor; evolved from cooking tools in multiple cultures
   - Evidence: Archaeological finds in China (2400 BCE), Egypt, Rome

2. **Why was the fork considered scandalous?**
   - Answer: Religious association with devil's pitchfork; rejection of "God's gift" of fingers
   - Evidence: Peter Damian's condemnation, clergy attributing deaths to fork use

3. **How did the fork spread through Europe?**
   - Answer: Through royal marriages (Byzantine → Venice/Germany → Italy → France → England)
   - Evidence: Theophanu (972), Maria Argyropoulina (1004), Catherine de' Medici (1533)

4. **When did the fork become standard?**
   - Answer: Italy by 1600; France by late 17th century; England by 18th century; universal by 19th century
   - Evidence: Historical accounts, etiquette books, industrial production records

5. **Why four tines?**
   - Answer: Optimal balance of piercing and scooping capabilities
   - Evidence: Design evolution from 2→3→4 tines, stabilization in early 19th century

### Secondary Questions

6. **Which fork is the salad fork?**
   - Answer: Smaller fork, placed to the left of the dinner fork
   - Evidence: Emily Post's Etiquette (1922), consistent etiquette sources

7. **What side does the fork go on?**
   - Answer: Left side of the plate in Western table settings
   - Evidence: Codified etiquette from Victorian era onward

8. **Why did England resist the fork?**
   - Answer: National pride, association with Italian/foreign customs, masculinity concerns
   - Evidence: Thomas Coryat's mockery as "Furcifer"

9. **What role did pasta play?**
   - Answer: Practical necessity drove Italian adoption; forks ideal for twirling noodles
   - Evidence: Correlation between pasta popularity and fork adoption in Italy

10. **Why do some cultures not use forks?**
    - Answer: Cultural choice, not technological limitation; different relationships to food
    - Evidence: Chopsticks (5,000+ years), hand dining traditions with specific protocols
