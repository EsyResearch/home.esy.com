# Era Guide: The Word "Han"

> Visual treatment guidelines for each historical era
> Research Date: December 14, 2025

---

## Era Overview

This essay spans over 2,000 years across four distinct civilizations. Each era requires distinct visual treatment to communicate its historical moment while maintaining coherent narrative flow.

---

## Era 1: Ancient Origins (Pre-206 BCE)

### Period: Oracle Bone & Bronze Age

**Time Span**: c. 1250–206 BCE
**Primary Locations**: Shang/Zhou China, Yellow River region

### Visual Character

| Attribute | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Color Palette** | Bronze tones, oxidized greens, warm ivory |
| **Texture** | Patina, ancient metal, oracle bone grain |
| **Typography** | Oracle bone script, bronze inscription forms |
| **Lighting** | Warm, fire-lit, ceremonial glow |
| **Atmosphere** | Ritualistic, sacred, primordial |

### Visual References
- Oracle bone inscriptions (crack patterns, burnt colors)
- Bronze ritual vessels (patinated surfaces, relief inscriptions)
- Archaeological photography (museum lighting)
- Ancient map aesthetics (simplified geography)

### Script Treatment
- Oracle bone forms of proto-漢 characters
- Bronze inscription examples
- Large seal script specimens

### Mood Keywords
Ancient, sacred, foundational, mysterious, ritualistic

## Era 2: Han Dynasty Glory (206 BCE–220 CE)

### Period: Imperial Unification

**Time Span**: 206 BCE–220 CE
**Primary Locations**: Han Empire (Chang'an, Luoyang, across China)

### Visual Character

| Attribute | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Color Palette** | Imperial red, gold, jade green, indigo |
| **Texture** | Silk, lacquer, jade, paper |
| **Typography** | Clerical script (隶书), transitioning to regular |
| **Lighting** | Court illumination, paper lantern warmth |
| **Atmosphere** | Imperial grandeur, scholarly refinement |

### Visual References
- Han Dynasty tomb paintings and murals
- Jade burial suits and artifacts
- Imperial seals and documents
- Silk paintings and calligraphy

### Script Treatment
- Small seal script → Clerical script evolution
- Stone stele inscriptions
- Brush calligraphy on silk

### Mood Keywords
Imperial, expansive, foundational, cultured, powerful

## Era 3: Transmission Period (3rd–10th Century CE)

### Period: Spread to Korea, Japan, Vietnam

**Time Span**: 3rd–10th century CE
**Primary Locations**: Korean Peninsula, Japanese Islands, Vietnamese territories

### Visual Character

| Attribute | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Color Palette** | Ink black, paper cream, regional accent colors |
| **Texture** | Manuscript paper, temple wood, ceramic |
| **Typography** | Regular script with regional adaptations |
| **Lighting** | Temple candlelight, scholarly study atmosphere |
| **Atmosphere** | Scholarly transmission, religious devotion, cultural borrowing |

### Visual References
- Buddhist sutra manuscripts
- Early Korean, Japanese, Vietnamese manuscripts
- Temple architecture and artifacts
- Map of cultural transmission routes

### Script Treatment
- Chinese regular script as "parent"
- Early Hanja specimens
- Early Kanji specimens
- Early Chữ Hán specimens

### Mood Keywords
Transmission, adaptation, devotion, scholarship, journey

## Era Transition Guidelines

### Visual Transitions Between Eras

| From | To | Transition Style |
|------|-----|------------------|
| Ancient | Han Dynasty | Bronze → silk morphing |
| Han Dynasty | Transmission | Scroll unrolling → map animation |
| Transmission | Korean Renaissance | Focus zoom on Korea |
| Any historical | Modern | Color saturation shift, grain reduction |
| Modern | Contemporary | Clean fade, typography evolution |

### Color Transition Palette

```
Ancient Origins:      #8B7355 (bronze) → #4A6741 (patina)
Han Dynasty:         #B8202D (imperial red) → #C9A83E (gold)
Transmission:        #2C3E50 (ink) → #FDF5E6 (paper)
Korean Renaissance:  #1E3F66 (royal blue) → #FFFFFF (white)
Japanese:            #4A225D (indigo) → #FFB7C5 (sakura)
Vietnamese:          #D4AF37 (amber) → #228B22 (palm)
Modern:             Sepia tones → gradual color introduction
Contemporary:       Full color, clean design
```
