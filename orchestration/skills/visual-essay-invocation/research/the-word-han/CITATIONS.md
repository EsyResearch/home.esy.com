## Tier 1: Primary & Academic Sources

### Historical Primary Sources

1. **Sima Qian. *Shiji* (Records of the Grand Historian). c. 94 BCE.**
   - Type: Primary historical source
   - Content: Foundational history of Han Dynasty, Liu Bang's rise
   - Used for: Han Dynasty founding narrative, early Chinese historiography
   - Access: Chinese Text Project (ctext.org)

2. **King Sejong. *Hunminjeongeum* (The Correct Sounds for the Instruction of the People). 1446.**
   - Type: Primary source, UNESCO Memory of the World
   - Content: Original promulgation of Hangul alphabet
   - Used for: King Sejong quotes, Hangul creation narrative
   - Access: National Museum of Korea digital archives

3. **Nguyễn Trãi. *Bình Ngô đại cáo* (Great Proclamation on the Pacification of the Wu). 1428.**
   - Type: Primary source, UNESCO Memory of the World
   - Content: Vietnamese declaration of independence from Ming China
   - Used for: Nguyễn Trãi quotes, Vietnamese identity narrative
   - Access: Vietnamese historical archives

4. **Ban Gu. *Han Shu* (Book of Han). c. 111 CE.**
   - Type: Primary historical source
   - Content: Official history of Western Han Dynasty
   - Used for: Han Dynasty historical details
   - Access: Chinese Text Project (ctext.org)

---

### Academic Journals & Publications

5. **Rawski, Evelyn S. "Reenvisioning the Qing: The Significance of the Qing Period in Chinese History." *Journal of Asian Studies* 55, no. 4 (1996): 829-850.**
   - Type: Peer-reviewed academic journal
   - Content: Discussion of Zhonghua minzu concept development
   - Used for: Liang Qichao and modern Chinese nationalism

6. **Leibold, James. "Competing Narratives of Racial Unity in Republican China." *Modern China* 32, no. 2 (2006): 181-220.**
   - Type: Peer-reviewed academic journal
   - Content: Analysis of Han Chinese ethnic identity formation
   - Used for: Sun Yat-sen's nationalism, Han ethnic identity

7. **Sohn, Ho-Min. *The Korean Language*. Cambridge University Press, 1999.**
   - Type: Academic linguistics, university press
   - Content: Comprehensive study of Korean language history
   - Used for: Korean Han (韓) etymology, Hangul development

8. **DeFrancis, John. *The Chinese Language: Fact and Fantasy*. University of Hawaii Press, 1984.**
   - Type: Academic linguistics, university press
   - Content: Analysis of Chinese writing system spread
   - Used for: Han character diffusion scholarship

## Notes on Source Quality

### Strongest Sources (Tier 1)
- *Shiji* and *Han Shu* provide definitive Chinese historical foundation
- *Hunminjeongeum* is the primary source for Hangul creation
- Peer-reviewed journals ensure academic rigor

### Cross-Verification
- All key claims verified across at least two independent sources
- Historical dates confirmed against multiple academic sources
- Quotes verified against primary sources where available

### Gaps Documented
- Some traditional attributions (Liu Bang, Emperor Wu quotes) are legendary
- Early Wani narrative has legendary elements
- Exact mechanisms of character spread have scholarly debate
