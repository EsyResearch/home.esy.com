## Museum Collections

### 1. National Museum of China (Beijing)

**Collection Focus**: Han Dynasty artifacts, bronze vessels, jade items
**Online Access**: http://www.chnmuseum.cn/
**Key Items**:
- Han Dynasty bronze vessels with inscriptions
- Imperial seals showing 漢 character
- Silk paintings and artifacts
- Jade burial suits

**Usage Notes**: High-quality artifact photography; Chinese language site

---

### 2. National Palace Museum (Taipei)

**Collection Focus**: Imperial Chinese calligraphy, historical documents
**Online Access**: https://www.npm.gov.tw/
**Key Items**:
- Calligraphic specimens across dynasties
- Historical scrolls and manuscripts
- Imperial documents
- Character evolution examples

**Usage Notes**: Excellent for calligraphy specimens; multi-language support

---

### 3. British Museum (London)

**Collection Focus**: ~1,955 Han Dynasty objects
**Online Access**: https://www.britishmuseum.org/collection
**Key Items**:
- Han Dynasty pottery and bronze
- Oracle bone fragments
- Chinese calligraphy examples
- East Asian comparative materials

**Usage Notes**: Searchable online database; high-resolution images available

---

### 4. Smithsonian National Museum of Asian Art

**Collection Focus**: Chinese art across periods
**Online Access**: https://asia.si.edu/
**Key Items**:
- Bronze garment hooks with inscriptions
- Jade artifacts
- Calligraphic works
- Historical art

**Usage Notes**: American collection with good online accessibility

---

### 5. National Museum of Korea (Seoul)

**Collection Focus**: Korean historical artifacts, Hanja specimens
**Online Access**: https://www.museum.go.kr/
**Key Items**:
- Early Hanja documents
- Hunminjeongeum artifacts
- Joseon Dynasty royal items
- King Sejong-related materials

**Usage Notes**: Essential for Korean perspective; Korean and English

---

### 6. King Sejong Memorial Hall (Seoul)

**Collection Focus**: Hangul creation and King Sejong
**Location**: Gwanghwamun, Seoul
**Key Items**:
- Hunminjeongeum manuscript reproductions
- King Sejong statues and imagery
- Hangul development materials

**Usage Notes**: Specific focus on Hangul creation story

---

### 7. Tokyo National Museum

**Collection Focus**: Japanese art and cultural artifacts
**Online Access**: https://www.tnm.jp/
**Key Items**:
- Early Kanji manuscripts

- Calligraphic specimens
- Prince Shōtoku-related materials

**Usage Notes**: Japanese and English; excellent calligraphy collection

---

### 8. Vietnam National History Museum (Hanoi)

**Collection Focus**: Vietnamese historical artifacts
**Online Access**: http://baotanglichsu.vn/
**Key Items**:
