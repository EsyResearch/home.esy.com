## Orwell's Life (1903–1950)

### Early Life (1903–1922)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **June 25, 1903** | Eric Arthur Blair born in Motihari, Bengal Presidency, British India | Father was a civil servant in the Opium Department |
| **1904** | Family returns to England | Mother raises Eric and sisters in Henley-on-Thames |
| **1911** | Attends St Cyprian's preparatory school | Traumatic experiences later documented in "Such, Such Were the Joys" |
| **1917** | Wins King's Scholarship to Eton | Prestigious achievement, attended 1917-1921 |
| **1921** | Graduates from Eton | Opts not to attend university |
| **1922** | Joins Indian Imperial Police | Travels to Burma; begins five years as colonial enforcer |

### Burma Period (1922–1927)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **1922-1927** | Serves as Assistant Superintendent of Police in Burma | Stations include Mandalay, Moulmein, Katha, Insein |
| **1926** | Witnesses and participates in colonial violence | Later documented in "A Hanging" and "Shooting an Elephant" |
| **1927** | Returns to England on leave | Never returns; resigns from police to become a writer |

**What he learned**: How power operates at ground level; the psychology of the enforcer; guilt that fueled his politics

### Down and Out Years (1928–1936)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **1928-1929** | Lives in Paris, works as dishwasher (*plongeur*) | Voluntary poverty to understand the oppressed |
| **1930-1931** | Tramping in England | Lives among the destitute |
| **1933** | Publishes *Down and Out in Paris and London* | First book, adopts pen name "George Orwell" |
| **1934** | Publishes *Burmese Days* | Fictional treatment of colonial Burma |
| **1935** | Publishes *A Clergyman's Daughter* | Novel |
| **June 1936** | Marries Eileen O'Shaughnessy | Partnership until her death in 1945 |
| **1936** | Publishes *Keep the Aspidistra Flying* | Novel about money and poverty |
| **1937** | Publishes *The Road to Wigan Pier* | Commissioned study of working-class conditions in Northern England |

### Spanish Civil War (1936–1937)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **July 17, 1936** | Spanish Civil War begins | Military uprising against Republican government |
| **December 1936** | Orwell arrives in Barcelona | Plans to report, decides to fight |
| **January 1937** | Joins POUM militia | Workers' Party of Marxist Unification, not Stalinist International Brigades |
| **January-May 1937** | Fights on Aragon Front outside Huesca | Trench warfare through harsh winter |
| **May 3-8, 1937** | Barcelona May Days | Communist forces (backed by USSR) attack POUM and anarchists — Orwell witnesses left fighting left |
| **May 20, 1937** | Shot through the throat by fascist sniper | Bullet narrowly misses carotid artery; voice permanently damaged |
| **May-June 1937** | POUM declared illegal by Republic under Soviet pressure | Orwell's comrades arrested, some "disappeared" |
| **June 23, 1937** | Flees Spain | Barely escapes NKVD-influenced police seeking POUM members |
| **1938** | Publishes *Homage to Catalonia* | Account of his war experience; sells poorly initially |

**What he learned**: How totalitarians on the left (Stalinists) could be as dangerous as those on the right; how truth gets falsified in service of ideology; the NKVD's methods

### World War II and BBC Years (1939–1945)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **September 3, 1939** | Britain declares war on Germany | WWII begins for Britain |
| **1939-1941** | Various journalism, rejected for military service | Deemed medically unfit (lungs) |
| **August 1941** | Joins BBC Eastern Service | Talks Producer for broadcasts to India |
| **1941-1943** | Produces over 200 radio programs | Countering Nazi/Japanese propaganda; works in Senate House (inspires Ministry of Truth) |
| **November 1943** | Resigns from BBC | "Wasted two years doing hack work" |
| **November 1943** | Becomes literary editor of *Tribune* | Socialist newspaper |
| **1944** | Completes *Animal Farm* | Repeatedly rejected by publishers afraid of offending Soviet ally |
| **March 29, 1945** | Wife Eileen dies during surgery | Orwell devastated; left with adopted son Richard |
| **August 17, 1945** | *Animal Farm* published by Secker & Warburg | Immediate success; financial security at last |

**What he learned**: How propaganda works from the inside; how even democratic governments manipulate truth; the machinery of official information

### Writing *Nineteen Eighty-Four* (1946–1949)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **May 1946** | Moves to Barnhill farmhouse, Isle of Jura, Scotland | Remote location; no electricity, difficult access |
| **1946-1947** | Writes first draft of *Nineteen Eighty-Four* | Working title "The Last Man in Europe" |
| **August 1947** | Nearly drowns in Gulf of Corryvreckan | Boat capsizes; survives with son Richard |
| **October 1947** | Completes first draft | Immediately collapses; tuberculosis worsens |
| **December 1947** | Enters Hairmyres Hospital, Glasgow | TB treatment for seven months |
| **July 1948** | Returns to Jura | Against medical advice |
| **November 1948** | Completes final draft | Types it himself while bedridden |
| **December 1948** | Final manuscript sent to publisher | Weighs less than 100 pounds; near death |
| **January 1949** | Enters sanatorium in Gloucestershire | Then University College Hospital, London |
| **June 8, 1949** | *Nineteen Eighty-Four* published | Immediate critical and commercial success |
| **October 13, 1949** | Marries Sonia Brownell | From hospital bed |
| **October 21, 1949** | Aldous Huxley writes letter to Orwell | Famous letter comparing their dystopian visions |
| **January 21, 1950** | George Orwell dies | Age 46; buried as Eric Arthur Blair in Sutton Courtenay, Oxfordshire |

## Historical Context: The Rise of Totalitarianism

### Soviet Union Under Stalin

| Date | Event | Connection to *1984* |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| **1924** | Lenin dies; Stalin begins consolidation of power | Rise of Big Brother |
| **1929** | Stalin achieves total control; collectivization begins | The Party's absolute power |
| **1934** | Kirov assassination triggers purges | Beginning of terror as governance |
| **1936-1938** | Moscow Show Trials (Great Purge) | Public confessions to impossible crimes; "2+2=5" logic |
| **1937** | "Yezhovshchina" — peak of terror | 750,000+ executed; millions to gulags |
| **1937-1940** | Yezhov falls from power, is erased from photographs | Unpersoning, memory holes |
| **1939** | Nazi-Soviet Pact | Alliances shift overnight; Eurasia becomes ally, then enemy |
| **1940** | Yezhov executed after torture and confession | The purger is purged |

### Nazi Germany

| Date | Event | Connection to *1984* |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| **1933** | Hitler becomes Chancellor | Totalitarian power seizes democracy |
| **1934** | Night of the Long Knives | Party purges its own |
| **1934-1938** | Nuremberg Rallies | Two Minutes Hate; mass psychology |
| **1933-1945** | Goebbels runs Propaganda Ministry | Ministry of Truth |
| **1933** | Gestapo established | Thought Police |
| **1938** | Kristallnacht | Terror as policy |
| **1939** | WWII begins | Permanent war |

### Other Totalitarian Events

| Date | Event | Connection to *1984* |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| **1922** | Mussolini's March on Rome | Original fascism |
| **1936-1939** | Spanish Civil War | Orwell's direct experience |
| **February 1945** | Yalta Conference | Division into "three superstates" — Oceania, Eurasia, Eastasia |
| **1946** | Iron Curtain descends | Cold War begins |
| **1947** | Truman Doctrine | Permanent global confrontation |
| **1948** | Berlin Blockade | Cold War heats up as Orwell finishes novel |

## *1984* in the Novel's Timeline

### The Novel's Internal Chronology

| "Date" in Novel | Event | Notes |
|-----------------|-------|-------|
| **April 4, 1984** | Winston begins his diary | "It was a bright cold day in April, and the clocks were striking thirteen" |
| **Week 1** | Winston writes "DOWN WITH BIG BROTHER" | First thoughtcrime |
| **Weeks 2-4** | Winston notices Julia | Fears she is Thought Police |
| **c. May 1984** | Julia passes note: "I love you" | Beginning of affair |
| **May-August 1984** | Winston and Julia's secret meetings | Golden Country, room above Mr. Charrington's shop |
| **August 1984** | Winston and Julia visit O'Brien | Believe they are joining the Brotherhood |
| **c. September 1984** | Winston reads "The Book" | Goldstein's *Theory and Practice of Oligarchical Collectivism* |
| **c. September 1984** | Arrested by Thought Police | Mr. Charrington revealed as agent |
| **September-? 1984** | Torture in Ministry of Love | "How many fingers am I holding up?" |
| **Room 101** | Winston betrays Julia | "Do it to Julia!" |
| **After release** | Winston and Julia meet once | "I betrayed you" / "I betrayed you" |
| **Final scene** | Chestnut Tree Café | "He loved Big Brother" |
