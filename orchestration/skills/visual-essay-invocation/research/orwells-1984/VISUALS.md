#### The Yezhov Erasure — ESSENTIAL

**This is THE visual demonstration of unpersoning**

| Version | Description | Significance |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Original (1937) | Stalin walking with Yezhov and others at Moscow-Volga Canal | Shows history as it happened |
| Doctored (c. 1940) | Yezhov removed, water/background painted in | Shows historical erasure in action |

**Source**: Wikimedia Commons, multiple archives  
**Rights**: ✅ Public Domain  
**Usage**: Side-by-side comparison is powerful visual for Ministry of Truth / memory holes

## Visual Treatment Guide

### Color Palettes by Era

| Era | Palette | Rationale |
|-----|---------|-----------|
| **Burma (1922-1927)** | Sepia, colonial warmth | Imperial nostalgia, guilt |
| **Poverty (1928-1936)** | Desaturated, grey | Bleak conditions |
| **Spain (1936-1937)** | Harsh contrast, red/black | Revolution, blood, anarchist colors |
| **BBC (1941-1943)** | Official grey-green | Institutional, wartime Britain |
| **Soviet 1930s** | Red-dominated, harsh | Propaganda aesthetic |
| **Nazi Germany** | Black/red/white | Party colors |
| **Jura (1946-1948)** | Cold blues, grey | Isolation, illness |
| **1984 (the novel)** | Grey, fluorescent, stark | Ministry of Truth aesthetic |

### Typography Suggestions

| Context | Style | Rationale |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Chapter titles | Sans-serif, stark | Industrial, totalitarian |
| Quotes from novel | Typewriter font | Winston's diary |
| Historical context | Serif, traditional | Documentary authority |
| Party slogans | Bold sans-serif, propaganda | "WAR IS PEACE" aesthetic |
