# Timeline: The History of Pizza

> Research Output | December 12, 2025

## Verified Chronological Events

All dates verified against Tier 1-2 sources. Approximate dates marked with "c." (circa).

---

## Prehistoric & Ancient Era (10,000 BCE - 500 CE)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source Tier |
|------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| c. 10,000 BCE | **Neolithic Grain Cultivation Begins** | Wheat domestication enables bread-based cuisine; foundation for all flatbreads | Tier 1 |
| c. 8,000 BCE | **First Leavened Breads** | Egyptians discover fermentation; flat and leavened breads coexist | Tier 1 |
| c. 6th Century BCE | **Persian Shield-Baked Flatbreads** | Soldiers under Darius the Great bake flatbreads with cheese and dates on battle shields | Tier 2 |
| c. 5th Century BCE | **Greek Plakous** | Greeks prepare "plakous" (flat cakes) topped with olive oil, herbs, onions, garlic | Tier 2 |
| c. 1st Century CE | **Roman Focaccia Developed** | "Panis focacius" (hearth bread) topped with herbs and olive oil becomes Roman staple | Tier 2 |
| c. 79 CE | **Pompeii's Evidence** | Preserved bakeries and flatbread-making equipment discovered in volcanic ash | Tier 1 |

---

## Medieval & Renaissance (500 - 1700 CE)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source Tier |
|------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **997 CE** | **First Documented Use of "Pizza"** | Gaeta codex records tenant owing "duodecim pizze" (12 pizzas) annually to bishop—first written instance of the word | Tier 1 (Primary) |
| c. 1000-1200 | **Pizza Terms Spread in Italy** | Variations of "pizza/pitta" appear in Italian regional documents | Tier 2 |
| **1522** | **Tomato Arrives in Europe** | Spanish bring tomatoes from Americas; initially considered poisonous, grown as ornamental | Tier 1 |
| c. 1600s | **Neapolitans Adopt Tomato** | Working-class Neapolitans first add tomato to flatbreads; elite still avoid it | Tier 2 |

---

## Birth of Modern Pizza: Naples (1700 - 1900)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source Tier |
|------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| c. 1700s | **Pizza Emerges as Street Food** | Vendors (pizzaioli) sell pizza from open-air stands in Naples to working poor ("lazzaroni") | Tier 2 |
| **1738** or **1830** | **Antica Pizzeria Port'Alba Opens** | Disputed as world's first pizzeria; originally served vendors, later opened dining room | Tier 2 (disputed dates) |
| c. 1750-1800 | **Pizza Marinara Codified** | Tomato, garlic, oregano, oil—named for fishermen (marinai) who ate it | Tier 2 |
| **1807** | **54 Pizzerias Documented in Naples** | Municipal records show thriving pizza industry; pizza established as urban food culture | Tier 1 |
| **1835** | **Alexandre Dumas Describes Pizza** | French novelist documents pizza in Naples in "Le Corricolo"—one of first outside descriptions | Tier 2 |
| **1889** | **Pizza Margherita Created** | Raffaele Esposito allegedly creates patriotic pizza for Queen Margherita; tomato, mozzarella, basil = Italian flag colors | Tier 2 (legendary) |
| **1889** | **Royal Endorsement Letter** | Letter from royal household thanking Esposito (authenticity debated) | Tier 3 (unverified) |

---

## American Journey (1900 - 1945)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source Tier |
|------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **1880-1920** | **Great Italian Immigration** | 4 million Italians immigrate to US; bring pizza tradition to New York, Boston, Philadelphia | Tier 1 |
| **1905** | **Lombardi's Opens in NYC** | Gennaro Lombardi opens first licensed American pizzeria at 53½ Spring Street, Little Italy | Tier 1 |
| c. 1905-1920 | **Adaptation to America** | Coal ovens replace wood; cow's milk mozzarella replaces buffalo; American pizza begins diverging | Tier 2 |
| **1924** | **Totonno's Opens in Brooklyn** | Antonio Pero (trained at Lombardi's) opens iconic Coney Island pizzeria | Tier 2 |
| **1933** | **Patsy's Opens in Harlem** | Pasquale Lancieri expands pizza uptown; celebrity clientele follows | Tier 2 |
| **1943** | **Deep-Dish Pizza Invented** | Ike Sewell and Ric Riccardo open Pizzeria Uno in Chicago; create thick-crust deep-dish style | Tier 2 |
| **1943-1945** | **WWII Soldiers Discover Pizza** | American GIs stationed in Italy develop taste for pizza; return home craving it | Tier 2 |

---

## National & Global Expansion (1945 - 1990)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source Tier |
|------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **1945-1950** | **Post-War Pizza Boom** | Returning soldiers drive explosive demand; pizzerias multiply across US | Tier 2 |
| **1957** | **First Frozen Pizza** | Celentano Brothers introduce frozen pizza; pizza enters American homes | Tier 2 |
| **1958** | **Pizza Hut Founded** | Dan and Frank Carney open first Pizza Hut in Wichita, Kansas; franchise era begins | Tier 1 |
| **1960** | **Domino's Founded** | Tom Monaghan buys small Ypsilanti, Michigan pizzeria; pioneers delivery model | Tier 1 |
| **1962** | **Hawaiian Pizza Invented** | Sam Panopoulos adds pineapple to pizza in Canada; creates controversial classic | Tier 2 |
| **1964** | **John's of Bleecker Street Opens** | NYC institution opens; represents maturing American pizza culture | Tier 3 |
| **1966** | **Domino's 30-Minute Guarantee** | Delivery innovation changes American dining habits | Tier 2 |
| **1970s** | **Pizza Becomes #1 Fast Food** | Pizza overtakes hamburgers as America's favorite fast food | Tier 2 |
| **1984** | **Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana Founded** | AVPN established to protect authentic Neapolitan pizza standards | Tier 1 |
| **1984** | **October = National Pizza Month** | US designation celebrates pizza's cultural impact | Tier 3 |

---

## Artisanal Revival & Global Phenomenon (1990 - Present)

| Date | Event | Significance | Source Tier |
|------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **1988** | **Pizzeria Bianco Opens** | Chris Bianco opens in Phoenix; pioneers American artisanal pizza movement | Tier 2 |
| **1996** | **Una Pizza Napoletana Opens** | Anthony Mangieri's traditionalist approach influences craft pizza culture | Tier 2 |
| **1997** | **Traditional Specialty Guaranteed (TSG)** | EU grants Neapolitan pizza legal protection | Tier 1 |
| **2000s** | **Artisanal Pizza Renaissance** | Focus on ingredients, fermentation, wood-fired ovens; pizza elevated to "serious" cuisine | Tier 2 |
| **2003** | **Bianco Wins James Beard Award** | First pizzaiolo to win major culinary award; legitimizes pizza as craft | Tier 1 |
| **2010** | **Neapolitan Pizza Seeks UNESCO Status** | Italy nominates pizza-making art for cultural heritage recognition | Tier 2 |
| **2017** | **UNESCO Inscribes Neapolitan Pizzaiuolo** | Art of Neapolitan pizza-making added to Intangible Cultural Heritage list | Tier 1 |
| **2020s** | **Pizza Global Dominance** | $150+ billion global pizza industry; consumed in virtually every country | Tier 2 |

## Key Turning Points Summary

| Year | Turning Point | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| 997 | First "pizza" documentation | Proves the word and concept predate Naples by centuries |
| 1522 | Tomato arrives in Europe | Enables the defining ingredient of modern pizza |
| 1889 | Margherita legend | Elevates pizza from peasant food to national symbol |
| 1905 | Lombardi's opens | Transplants pizza to America, begins divergent evolution |
| 1943 | Deep-dish invented | Pizza becomes canvas for American innovation |
| 1958 | Pizza Hut founded | Begins franchising era; pizza becomes accessible nationwide |
| 2017 | UNESCO recognition | Pizza-making acknowledged as intangible cultural heritage |
