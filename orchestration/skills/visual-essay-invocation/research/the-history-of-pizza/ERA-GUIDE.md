# Era Guide: Visual Treatment for Pizza History

> Research Output | December 12, 2025

## Overview

This guide defines the visual treatment, color grading, and aesthetic approach for each historical era of the pizza visual essay. These treatments create visual coherence and temporal orientation for readers.

---

## Era 1: Ancient Hearths (Prehistory - 500 CE)

### Temporal Span
10,000 BCE (grain cultivation) → 79 CE (Pompeii) → 500 CE

### Visual Aesthetic
**"Archaeological Memory"**

The distant past rendered through the lens of what survives: stone, pottery, preserved fragments. Warm earth tones dominate, suggesting hearth fires, baked clay, and sun-warmed grain.

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Terracotta | #C47853 | Primary accent, pottery |
| Sand | #E8D5B7 | Background, grain |
| Charcoal | #3D3D3D | Text, shadows |
| Ochre | #CC7722 | Warmth, flame |
| Faded Gold | #B8A56A | Highlights, wheat |

### Processing Treatment
- **Texture**: Subtle stone/pottery texture overlay
- **Grain**: Light analog grain (film-like)
- **Contrast**: Medium, slightly faded
- **Edges**: Soft vignetting, archaeological fragment feel
- **Typography**: Carved/inscribed aesthetic

### Transition Marker
*Fade to parchment tones as we approach medieval era*

---

## Era 2: The Word Emerges (500 - 1700 CE)

### Temporal Span
997 CE (Gaeta document) → 1522 (tomato arrives) → 1700 (Naples street food)

### Visual Aesthetic
**"Illuminated Document"**

Medieval manuscripts meet Renaissance exploration. Rich, jewel-toned colors emerge as we move through this era—the visual vocabulary of scribes, monks, and early printed books.

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Parchment | #F2E6D9 | Background |
| Monastery Blue | #1C4B82 | Accent, illumination |
| Vermillion | #D14836 | Accent, capitals |
| Sepia Ink | #5C4033 | Text, illustrations |
| Gold Leaf | #D4AF37 | Highlights, initials |
| Tomato Introduction | #C24D37 | Foreshadowing, New World |

### Processing Treatment
- **Texture**: Parchment/vellum overlay
- **Style**: Illuminated manuscript borders for key sections
- **Contrast**: Higher in document reproductions
- **Typography**: Calligraphic, blackletter suggestions
- **Special**: When tomato appears, warm red enters palette for first time

### Transition Marker
*The red of the tomato spreads across the frame, warming into Naples street life*

## Era 3: Streets of Naples (1700 - 1889)

### Temporal Span
18th century street food → 1807 (54 pizzerias) → 1889 (Margherita)

### Visual Aesthetic
**"Neapolitan Light"**

The golden warmth of Southern Italy. Oil painting luminosity for early period, transitioning to early photography sepia for later decades. Mediterranean light: warm, directional, life-giving.

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Naples Yellow | #FADA5E | Primary warmth |
| Mediterranean Blue | #4F86C6 | Bay of Naples, sky |
| Terracotta | #C04000 | Architecture, ovens |
| Tomato Red | #C24D37 | Pizza sauce, accents |
| Mozzarella White | #FFFEF2 | Fresh cheese, highlights |
| Basil Green | #4F7942 | Fresh herbs (arrives with Margherita) |

### Processing Treatment
- **Early (1700s)**: Oil painting aesthetic, soft edges, warm vignette
- **Mid (1800s)**: Daguerreotype/early photo processing
- **Late (1880s)**: Hand-tinted photography, deliberate artifice
- **Texture**: Canvas grain for paintings; photographic grain for later
- **Light**: Always warm, directional, Mediterranean noon/golden hour

### Key Moment: The Margherita
When the flag-colored pizza appears, process shifts to:
- High saturation on red/white/green
- Patriotic brightness
- Slight halo/glow on the pizza itself

### Transition Marker
*Ship silhouette on Naples bay fades to Ellis Island*

## Era 4: The Great Migration (1900 - 1945)

### Temporal Span
1880s immigration wave → 1905 (Lombardi's) → 1943 (deep-dish) → 1945 (GIs return)

### Visual Aesthetic
**"Immigrant Noir"**

Black-and-white documentary photography. The high-contrast world of newspaper photography, Ellis Island documentation, and coal-oven shadows. Occasional sepia warmth for nostalgic moments.

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Pure Black | #000000 | Shadows, coal ovens |
| Paper White | #F5F5F5 | Highlights, flour |
| Newspaper Gray | #808080 | Mid-tones |
| Sepia Memory | #704214 | Nostalgic overlays |
| Coal Ember | #8B0000 | Oven glow, warmth |

### Processing Treatment
- **Primary**: High-contrast black-and-white
- **Grain**: Heavy photographic grain
- **Texture**: Newspaper halftone for some images
- **Light**: Harsh, directional (flash photography, coal-oven glow)
- **Exception**: Sepia tinting for family/nostalgic moments

### Key Moments
- **Ellis Island**: High contrast, documentary
- **Little Italy**: Street photography, crowded, warm
- **WWII**: Military photography, historical grain

### Transition Marker
*B&W slowly saturates as color film arrives in the 1950s*

---

## Era 5: American Empire (1945 - 1990)

### Temporal Span
Post-war boom → 1958 (Pizza Hut) → 1970s (franchise era) → 1980s (pop culture)

### Visual Aesthetic
**"Americana Bright"**

The transition from documentary B&W to Kodachrome saturation. Neon signs, checkered tablecloths, drive-in colors. The visual vocabulary of American abundance and suburban expansion.

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Pizza Hut Red | #ED2227 | Franchise era |
| Neon Glow | #FF5F1F | Signs, nightlife |
| Checkered Red | #B22222 | Tablecloth pattern |
| Formica Cream | #F5DEB3 | Countertops |
| Chrome Silver | #C0C0C0 | Diners, cars |
| 1980s Teal | #008B8B | Late era accent |

### Processing Treatment
- **1950s**: Kodachrome saturation, slightly faded edges
- **1960s-70s**: Vibrant, commercial photography
- **1980s**: MTV-era brightness, pop color
- **Texture**: Clean, commercial; occasional neon glow effect
- **Light**: Artificial—fluorescent, neon, flash

### Key Moments
- **Suburban Pizzeria**: Warm family lighting
- **Franchise Launch**: Corporate clean, bright
- **Pop Culture**: High saturation, entertainment gloss

### Transition Marker
*Commercial brightness softens into artisanal craft warmth*

## Era 6: The Craft Renaissance (1990 - Present)

### Temporal Span
1988 (Bianco opens) → 2000s (artisanal movement) → 2017 (UNESCO) → Today

### Visual Aesthetic
**"Modern Warmth"**

Contemporary food photography meets documentary realism. Natural light, shallow depth of field, honest textures. The visual language of craft, authenticity, and elevated cuisine.

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Wood-Fire Amber | #FF8C00 | Oven flames, warmth |
| Craft Black | #1A1A1A | Backgrounds, contrast |
| Natural Cream | #FFF8E7 | Dough, fresh ingredients |
| Char Leopard | #2D2D2D | Crust spots |
| San Marzano Red | #CF1020 | Authentic tomato |
| Heritage Green | #556B2F | Basil, natural |

### Processing Treatment
- **Primary**: High-resolution, clean processing
- **Depth**: Shallow depth of field for portraits and details
- **Light**: Natural light emphasis; warm wood-fire glow
- **Texture**: Honest—flour, char, wood grain visible
- **Color**: Rich but natural; no over-saturation

### Key Moments
- **Artisan Portrait**: Shallow depth, natural light, authentic environment
- **Pizza Close-up**: Macro detail, steam, char
- **UNESCO Recognition**: Documentary realism, celebration

### Special: The Perfect Pizza Shot
- Overhead or 45-degree angle
- Visible char/leopard spotting
- Steam rising
- Natural light with rim lighting from oven
- Shallow depth of field

## Transition Choreography

The visual essay should flow through eras with intentional transitions:

| From | To | Transition Technique |
|------|-----|---------------------|
| Ancient | Medieval | Stone texture dissolves to parchment |
| Medieval | Naples | Red of tomato spreads across frame |
| Naples | Immigration | Ship silhouette; bay to ocean |
| Immigration | American | B&W slowly gains saturation |
| American | Craft | Commercial brightness warms to natural |

## Global Interlude Treatment

For sections covering pizza's global spread:

### Visual Approach
Distinct color stories per region, maintaining pizza's unifying warmth:
- **Japan**: Clean, minimal, slight blue undertone
- **India**: Warm spice tones, golden light
- **Brazil**: Vibrant tropical saturation
- **Australia**: Outdoor, natural light, casual

Each maintains the essay's visual system while acknowledging cultural context.

## Implementation Notes

1. **Era changes should be gradual**, not abrupt—scroll-driven color grade shifts
2. **Key moments** (Margherita creation, Lombardi's opening, UNESCO) deserve special treatment
3. **The pizza itself** should always look appetizing regardless of era treatment
4. **Portraits** in all eras should honor subjects with flattering, dignified light
5. **Documentary moments** (crowds, streets, process) can be grittier than portraits
