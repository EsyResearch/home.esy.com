# Research Gaps: Deep Antiquity (Archaeological Record)

> **Module**: 01-antiquity
> **Last Updated**: December 23, 2024

---

## Overview

Archaeological interpretation involves scholarly debate, but the existence of sites and material culture is well-established. The key gaps relate to interpretive claims, ethnic attribution, and connections to later narratives.

---

## Tier 1 Gaps: Do Not Claim

### Gap 1.1: Ethnic Identity from Material Culture

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | That pottery or architecture definitively identifies a specific ethnic group |
| **Why Insufficient** | Material culture can be adopted, traded, or shared across groups |
| **What We Know** | Distinctive material cultures existed (Canaanite, Philistine Bichrome, etc.) |
| **What We Don't Know** | Whether all users of a material culture shared ethnic identity |
| **Recommendation** | Describe material cultures without assuming ethnic homogeneity |

### Gap 1.2: Biblical Narratives as Historical Fact

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | That archaeological evidence "proves" biblical conquest narratives |
| **Why Insufficient** | Archaeological evidence often contradicts or doesn't support conquest models |
| **What We Know** | Settlements emerged in highlands; destruction layers exist at various sites |
| **What We Don't Know** | Whether these map to specific biblical events |
| **Scholarly Consensus** | "Archaeological data indicates a gradual emergence of Israelites in the southern hill country, not a sudden conquest" |
| **Recommendation** | Present archaeological evidence independently of religious narratives |

## Tier 2 Gaps: Scholarly Debate

### Gap 2.1: Causes of the Late Bronze Age Collapse

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | What caused the ~1200 BCE collapse |
| **Position A** | Sea Peoples invasions were primary cause |
| **Position B** | Climate change (mega-drought) was primary cause |
| **Position C** | Systems collapse — interconnected trade networks failed together |
| **Position D** | Internal factors (class conflict, disease) |
| **Current View** | Multi-causal; "complex, multi-faceted factors rather than a single event" |
| **Esy Approach** | Present multiple factors; avoid single-cause explanation |

### Gap 2.2: Sea Peoples Origins

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | Where exactly the Sea Peoples (including Philistines) originated |
| **Positions** | Aegean, Mycenae, Crete, Cyprus, Anatolia — all proposed |
| **Evidence** | Genetic and ceramic evidence points to Aegean/Mediterranean, but specifics debated |
| **Esy Approach** | Use "likely Aegean or Mediterranean origin"; avoid claiming specific homeland |

### Gap 2.3: Destruction Layer Attribution

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | What caused specific destruction layers at Bronze Age sites |
| **Issue** | Of 148 sites with claimed LBA destructions, "61% have either been misdated, assumed based on little evidence, or simply never happened" |
| **Esy Approach** | Note that destruction evidence exists but attribution is debated |

## Tier 3 Gaps: Politically Contested

### Gap 3.1: Who Were the "First" Inhabitants

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Issue** | Archaeological evidence used to claim "original" inhabitants |
| **Historical Evidence** | Region has been continuously inhabited for 1.5 million years; no single "first" people after initial colonization |
| **Political Use** | Both Israeli and Palestinian claims sometimes invoke ancient presence |
| **Esy Approach** | Emphasize deep continuity and population change over millennia; no single "original" people relevant to modern claims |

### Gap 3.2: Merneptah Stele and Israelite Origins

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Issue** | The stele (~1207 BCE) mentions "Israel" — what does this mean? |
| **Historical Evidence** | A group called "Israel" existed in Canaan by ~1207 BCE |
| **Political Use** | Used to establish "ancient" Israelite presence |
| **Scholarly View** | Merneptah Stele describes Israel as a people, not a state; emergence was gradual |
| **Esy Approach** | Present the stele as evidence of an entity called "Israel" without extrapolating to modern claims |

## Narrative Framing Risks

### Risk 1: "Proving" Religious Texts

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Risk** | Presenting archaeology as confirming or denying biblical narratives |
| **Mitigation** | Present archaeological evidence on its own terms |
| **Recommended Language** | "Archaeological evidence shows..." not "Archaeology proves/disproves the Bible..." |

### Risk 2: Ancient = Modern Continuity

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Risk** | Implying that ancient peoples map directly onto modern national identities |
| **Mitigation** | Note that populations mixed, migrated, and transformed over millennia |
| **Recommended Language** | "Ancient Canaanites are not identical to any modern population" |

### Risk 3: Jericho as Palestinian Symbol

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Risk** | Jericho's UNESCO status was celebrated as Palestinian heritage; acknowledging this is important but shouldn't imply modern ownership claims from ancient presence |
| **Mitigation** | Note UNESCO designation neutrally; focus on archaeological significance |

## Terminology Guidance

| Term | Issue | Recommendation |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Israelites | Ancient vs. modern | Specify "ancient Israelites" or "Iron Age Israelites" |
| Canaanites | Ancient peoples | Use for Bronze Age material culture |
| Palestinians | Modern national identity | Do not use for ancient peoples |
| Jews | Religious/ethnic | "Judahites" or "Judeans" for ancient kingdom inhabitants |
