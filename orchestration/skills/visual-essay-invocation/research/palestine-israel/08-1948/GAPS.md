# Research Gaps: WWII, Holocaust, Partition, Nakba (1945-1949)

> **Module**: 08-1948
> **Last Updated**: December 23, 2024

---

## Overview

This is the most contested period in the entire narrative. The events of 1948 are viewed through completely different lenses:
- **Israeli lens**: War of Independence; survival after Holocaust; defensive war
- **Palestinian lens**: Nakba (Catastrophe); ethnic cleansing; colonial dispossession

Both perspectives contain truth. The task is to document what happened accurately while acknowledging the divergent meanings.

## Tier 1 Gaps: Do Not Claim

### Gap 1.1: Precise Casualty Figures

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | Exact death tolls for 1948 war |
| **Why Insufficient** | Records incomplete; estimates vary |
| **What We Know** | Approximately 6,000 Israelis; 10,000-15,000 Arabs (including civilians) |
| **What We Don't Know** | Exact breakdown by combatant/civilian; specific incidents |
| **Recommendation** | Use "approximately"; cite ranges |

### Gap 1.2: Precise Refugee Numbers

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | Exact number of Palestinian refugees |
| **Why Insufficient** | No comprehensive census during flight |
| **What We Know** | 700,000-750,000 is widely accepted range |
| **What We Don't Know** | Precise count; breakdown by cause of flight |
| **Recommendation** | Use range "700,000-750,000" or "over 700,000" |

### Gap 1.3: Arab Leadership Orders to Flee

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | That Arab leaders broadly ordered people to flee |
| **Why Insufficient** | Traditional Israeli claim not supported by research |
| **What We Know** | Happened occasionally and locally; was not general policy |
| **What We Don't Know** | Complete documentation of all local decisions |
| **Scholarly Consensus** | "The idea that the Palestinian exodus was predominantly the result of orders from the Arab leadership is no longer taken seriously" (Morris) |
| **Recommendation** | Note that this claim is disputed; research does not support it |

### Gap 1.4: Deir Yassin Death Toll

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | Exact number killed at Deir Yassin |
| **Why Insufficient** | Estimates vary from 107 to 254; propaganda inflated early estimates |
| **What We Know** | Mass killing of civilians occurred |
| **What We Don't Know** | Precise count |
| **Recommendation** | Use "100+" or "over 100"; note it was a massacre regardless of exact number |

## Tier 2 Gaps: Scholarly Debate

### Gap 2.1: Was the Nakba Planned?

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | Whether Palestinian displacement was deliberate policy |
| **Position A (Ethnic Cleansing)** | Systematic plan; Plan Dalet was blueprint |
| **Proponents** | Ilan Pappé, Palestinian scholars |
| **Position B (War Result)** | Displacement was result of war, not initial intent |
| **Proponents** | Benny Morris (partial), Israeli mainstream |
| **Position C (Mixed)** | Some planned, some not; varied by time and place |
| **Proponents** | Morris (revised view) |
| **Current Evidence** | Plan Dalet enabled but may not have required expulsion; local commanders had discretion; some expulsions clearly ordered |
| **Esy Approach** | Present the debate; note that both flight and expulsion occurred; avoid claiming single explanation |

### Gap 2.2: Transfer Thinking in Zionism

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | How central "transfer" thinking was to Zionism |
| **Position A** | Transfer was always implicit in Zionist goal |
| **Position B** | Transfer was discussed but not policy; war created opportunity |
| **Position C** | Some leaders advocated transfer; others did not |
| **Evidence** | Documented discussions of transfer (Ben-Gurion, Weizmann); but also discussions of coexistence |
| **Esy Approach** | Note that transfer was discussed by Zionist leaders; note variety of views |

### Gap 2.3: Arab Armies' Intentions

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | What Arab states intended when they invaded |
| **Position A** | Genocide; "drive Jews into the sea" |
| **Position B** | Protect Arab population; prevent partition |
| **Position C** | Varied; some sought territorial gain (Abdullah) |
| **Evidence** | Rhetoric was extreme; actions were limited; coordination was poor |
| **Esy Approach** | Note stated goals and actual conduct; avoid attributing genocidal intent without evidence |

### Gap 2.4: Jordanian-Israeli Collusion

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | Extent of secret Israel-Jordan understanding |
| **Position A** | Full collusion; Abdullah agreed to partition |
| **Position B** | Informal understanding; not formal agreement |
| **Evidence** | Shlaim documented meetings; extent of agreement debated |
| **Esy Approach** | Note meetings and informal understanding; avoid claiming formal treaty |

### Gap 2.5: Resolution 181 Legality

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Claim** | Whether UN partition was legally binding |
| **Position A** | General Assembly resolutions are recommendations, not law |
| **Position B** | Created legal basis for Israel |
| **Position C** | International acceptance made it customary law |
| **Esy Approach** | Note it was UNGA recommendation; note acceptance by some, rejection by others |

## Tier 3 Gaps: Politically Contested

### Gap 3.1: Ethnic Cleansing Terminology

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Issue** | Whether "ethnic cleansing" is appropriate for 1948 |
| **Israeli Position** | Inappropriate; population transfer in war; not comparable to Holocaust |
| **Palestinian Position** | Appropriate; systematic removal of population |
| **Scholarly Position** | Some scholars (Pappé) use it; others (Morris) find it too deterministic |
| **Esy Approach** | Use descriptive language ("expulsion," "flight," "displacement"); note the debate over characterization |

### Gap 3.2: Who Started the War

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Issue** | Responsibility for initiating violence |
| **Israeli Position** | Arabs rejected partition and attacked; Israel defended |
| **Palestinian Position** | Partition was unjust imposition; resistance was legitimate |
| **Scholarly Position** | Both sides prepared for war; both engaged in violence; structural conflict made war likely |
| **Esy Approach** | Note that violence followed partition vote; note both sides engaged; avoid assigning sole blame |

### Gap 3.3: Refugee Right of Return

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Issue** | Whether Palestinians have right to return |
| **Israeli Position** | No right; return would destroy Jewish state; compensation instead |
| **Palestinian Position** | International law supports return; Resolution 194 |
| **Legal Position** | Resolution 194 was recommendation; customary international law on refugees is complex |
| **Esy Approach** | Present Resolution 194; note ongoing dispute; note that issue remains unresolved |

### Gap 3.4: Nakba vs. Holocaust Comparison

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Issue** | Whether comparison is appropriate |
| **Israeli Position** | Comparison is antisemitic; Holocaust was unique genocide |
| **Palestinian Position** | Both are catastrophes; not claiming equivalence |
| **Scholarly Position** | They are different in scale and nature; both shaped their peoples |
| **Esy Approach** | Present both as catastrophes; note they are different; avoid direct comparison that implies equivalence |

### Gap 3.5: Israeli "War of Independence" Framing

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Issue** | Whether "War of Independence" is appropriate framing |
| **Israeli Position** | Accurate; Israel fought for independence against would-be destroyers |
| **Palestinian Position** | Obscures conquest and dispossession |
| **Scholarly Position** | Both framings capture aspects; events were both |
| **Esy Approach** | Use neutral "1948 War" or explain both terms; "For Israel, the War of Independence; for Palestinians, the Nakba" |

## Narrative Framing Risks

### Risk 1: Presenting Nakba as Solely Voluntary

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Risk** | Suggesting Palestinians left voluntarily or on Arab orders |
| **Mitigation** | Research clearly shows expulsions occurred alongside flight |
| **Recommended Language** | "Palestinians fled and were expelled..." |

### Risk 2: Presenting Nakba as Solely Genocidal

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Risk** | Comparing to Holocaust in way that minimizes or instrumentalizes |
| **Mitigation** | Note displacement, not genocide; 700,000 refugees, not 6 million murdered |
| **Recommended Language** | "The Nakba resulted in 700,000+ refugees and the destruction of Palestinian society" |

### Risk 3: Ignoring Israeli Casualties and Fear

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Risk** | Presenting only Palestinian suffering |
| **Mitigation** | Note Israeli casualties (~6,000) and genuine fear after Holocaust |
| **Recommended Language** | "Both peoples experienced 1948 as existential..." |

### Risk 4: Ignoring Palestinian Suffering

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Risk** | Presenting only as Israeli independence story |
| **Mitigation** | Document Nakba fully; note displacement and destruction |
| **Recommended Language** | "The same events that created Israel destroyed Palestinian society" |

### Risk 5: Simple Moral Judgment

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Risk** | Assigning clear right/wrong to either side |
| **Mitigation** | Present complexity; note both peoples' perspectives |
| **Recommended Language** | "Understanding 1948 requires holding multiple truths simultaneously" |

## Key Principle for This Module

**Two truths, one war.**

The 1948 War was both the Israeli War of Independence and the Palestinian Nakba. One people achieved statehood after the Holocaust; another lost their homeland. These are not competing claims about what happened — they are different meanings of the same events. Understanding 1948 requires acknowledging both:

- Israel was created
- 700,000+ Palestinians became refugees
- Israeli Jews fought and died for their state
- Palestinians fled and were expelled from their homes
- The same war created Israel and destroyed Palestinian society

Neither people's experience delegitimizes the other's. Both are real.
