# Research Synthesis: Common Myths / Common Truths

> **Module**: sidebars/myths-truths
> **Last Updated**: December 23, 2024

---

## Executive Summary

Advocacy for both Israelis and Palestinians relies on mythologized histories that serve political arguments but obscure reality. This sidebar doesn't equate the two sides or pretend they're equally valid — it simply holds both accountable to evidence. The goal is not "both sides are wrong" nihilism but "the truth is more complex than slogans" understanding.

---

## Part 1: Pro-Israel Myths

### Myth 1: "A land without a people for a people without a land"

**The Claim**: Palestine was empty or sparsely populated before Zionist immigration.

**The Reality**:
- 1878 Ottoman census: ~450,000 people in Palestine (85-90% Arab)
- Thriving villages, towns, agriculture, commerce
- The phrase was used by early Zionists who knew the land wasn't empty — it meant Arabs weren't a "people" (nation) in the modern sense
- This semantic trick doesn't change the demographic reality

**Sources**: Ottoman census records; Rashid Khalidi, *Palestinian Identity*

---

### Myth 2: "Arabs rejected every peace offer"

**The Claim**: Palestinians have rejected every opportunity for peace, proving they don't want it.

**The Reality**:
- Palestinians accepted 1988 PNC declaration recognizing Israel
- PLO accepted Oslo Accords (1993)
- Arab Peace Initiative (2002) offered full normalization
- Some offers (Camp David 2000) were presented as "take it or leave it" with disputed terms
- Israel has also rejected offers (Saudi peace plan, various negotiating positions)

**Key Point**: Both sides have rejected offers. Framing Palestinians as uniquely rejectionist is selective history.

**Sources**: Clayton Swisher, *The Truth About Camp David*; Arab Peace Initiative text

---

### Myth 3: "Palestinians fled voluntarily in 1948"

**The Claim**: Palestinians left during the war because Arab leaders told them to, expecting to return after victory.

**The Reality**:
- Israeli "New Historians" (Morris, Pappé, Shlaim) documented forced expulsions
- Declassified Israeli archives show Plan Dalet included population transfer
- Some fled fear of violence (Deir Yassin massacre spread terror)
- Some were directly expelled (Lydda, Ramle)
- Arab radio broadcasts telling people to leave: largely debunked myth

**Sources**: Benny Morris, *The Birth of the Palestinian Refugee Problem Revisited*; Walid Khalidi, *All That Remains*

---

### Myth 4: "There was no such thing as Palestine"

**The Claim**: "Palestine" is a modern invention; the land was just southern Syria.

**The Reality**:
- "Palestine" (from Philistia) used by Greeks, Romans, Byzantines, Arabs, Ottomans
- British Mandate was officially "Palestine"
- Palestinians identified with the land long before 1948
- National consciousness crystallized in early 20th century (like many nationalisms)
- The name is 2,000+ years old

**Sources**: Module 03-roman-byzantine; naming-etymology sidebar

---

### Myth 5: "Jordan is Palestine"

**The Claim**: Palestinians already have a state — Jordan — so they don't need another one.

**The Reality**:
- Transjordan was separated from Mandate Palestine in 1921
- Jordan is a distinct nation with its own identity and monarchy
- Palestinians in Jordan have faced discrimination (Black September 1970)
- The vast majority of Palestinians don't identify as Jordanian
- This claim denies Palestinian self-determination

**Sources**: Avi Shlaim, *Collusion Across the Jordan*

## Part 2: Pro-Palestinian Myths

### Myth 6: "Jews are just European colonizers"

**The Claim**: Zionism is purely a European colonial project with no connection to the land.

**The Reality**:
- ~50% of Israeli Jews are Mizrahi (Middle Eastern/North African origin)
- Jewish connection to the land spans 3,000+ years (archaeological, textual evidence)
- Zionism emerged partly from European colonialism but also from Jewish persecution
- Many "returning" Jews were refugees, not imperial settlers
- Holocaust survivors had nowhere else to go

**Key Point**: The settler colonial framework has explanatory power but doesn't capture the full picture.

**Sources**: Derek Penslar, *Zionism and Technocracy*; Module 06-nationalism

---

### Myth 7: "There was no Jewish connection to the land"

**The Claim**: Jews are foreigners with no historical tie to Palestine.

**The Reality**:
- Iron Age Israelite kingdoms are archaeologically attested
- Jerusalem was Jewish capital for centuries
- Jewish presence (small) continued throughout history
- Daily Jewish prayers reference Jerusalem for 2,000 years
- Diaspora resulted from Roman conquest, not voluntary departure

**Sources**: Israel Finkelstein, *The Bible Unearthed*; Module 01-antiquity, 02-ancient-peoples

---

### Myth 8: "Israel is uniquely evil among nations"

**The Claim**: Israel is the worst human rights violator in the world; singular evil.

**The Reality**:
- Israel's occupation involves documented human rights violations
- But comparable or worse situations exist globally (Uyghurs, Yemen, Sudan, etc.)
- Disproportionate focus on Israel at UN noted by multiple observers
- This doesn't excuse Israeli violations — but "uniquely evil" is not supportable
- Criticism of Israel is legitimate; singling out Israel above all others raises questions

**Sources**: Human Rights Watch comparative reports; UN voting patterns analysis

---

### Myth 9: "The conflict started in 1948"

**The Claim**: Everything began with Israel's creation; before that, peaceful coexistence.

**The Reality**:
- Tensions began in late Ottoman period with Zionist immigration
- Violence occurred in 1920, 1921, 1929 (Hebron massacre), 1936-39 Arab Revolt
- Both communities had armed groups before 1948
- 1948 was culmination, not beginning, of decades of conflict
- Pre-1948 coexistence was real but so was pre-1948 violence

**Sources**: Module 06-nationalism, 07-mandate

---

### Myth 10: "Palestinians never used violence"

**The Claim**: Palestinian resistance has been entirely nonviolent; all violence is Israeli.

**The Reality**:
- Palestinians have used both nonviolent and violent resistance
- Suicide bombings during Second Intifada killed ~1,000 Israeli civilians
- October 7, 2023: ~1,200 killed including civilians, hostages taken
- Acknowledging Palestinian violence doesn't justify occupation
- Both peoples have blood on their hands

**Sources**: Module 11-oslo-split, 12-present

## Part 3: Common Truths

These are facts that people on both sides often know but rarely state publicly:

### Truth 1: The Nakba Happened

- 700,000+ Palestinians were displaced in 1948
- Many were forcibly expelled; villages destroyed
- This is documented by Israeli and Palestinian historians
- Acknowledging it doesn't delegitimize Israel's existence
- Denying it denies Palestinian experience

### Truth 2: October 7 Was a Massacre

- ~1,200 people killed, mostly civilians
- Hostages taken including children
- This was a war crime regardless of context
- Context explains but doesn't justify
- Minimizing it denies Israeli/Jewish pain

### Truth 3: Occupation Is Real

- West Bank has been under military occupation since 1967 (57 years)
- Settlements expand continuously
- Palestinians live under different legal system than settlers
- This isn't just "disputed territory"
- International law is clear on this

### Truth 4: Both Peoples Exist and Aren't Going Away

- ~7 million Jews live in Israel
- ~7 million Palestinians live in historic Palestine + diaspora
- Neither is going to disappear
- Any solution requires acknowledging both
- "From the river to the sea" (either version) ignores reality

### Truth 5: There Are No Clean Hands

- Both national movements have committed violence against civilians
- Both have leaders who chose war over compromise
- Both have genuine grievances
- Moral clarity about specific acts ≠ declaring one side wholly good/evil
- Justice requires honesty about one's own side
