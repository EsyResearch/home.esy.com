# R&B History: Visual Archives and Image Sources

A comprehensive guide to visual archives, photography collections, and image licensing sources for R&B history research.

---

## 1. Major Archives and Museums

### Rock & Roll Hall of Fame Library & Archives (Cleveland, OH)

**Collections:**
- Over 200 archival collections including personal papers of performers, radio DJs, photographers, poster artists, and collectors
- Music-business records from record executives, artist managers, labels, historic venues, recording studios
- Stage design and lighting specialist materials
- Long-running concert tour documentation

**Notable Holdings:**
- Don Cornelius collection materials
- Extensive photography collections from music photographers

**Access:**
- Location: 2809 Woodland Avenue, Cleveland, Ohio
- Open Monday-Wednesday, 10am-4pm by advanced appointment only
- Requires 3 weeks' notice (4 weeks for audio/video materials)
- Must provide prioritized list of research materials when scheduling

**Licensing:**
- Media assets available at rockhall.com/press-room/resources/
- Third-party licensing via Alamy (RF and RM licensing)
- [Library & Archives Homepage](https://library.rockhall.com/home)

---

### National Museum of African American History and Culture (Smithsonian, Washington D.C.)

**Collections:**
- Over 45,000 objects (more than half donated by the public)
- Photography, art, artifacts, fashion, musical instruments
- Letters, cartes de visite, books, broadsheets, political buttons, ephemera
- Collection spans approximately 1700 to 2010s

**Music-Specific Holdings:**
- Hip Hop Smithsonian collection (photographs by Bill Adler)
- Smithsonian Anthology of Hip-Hop and Rap
- Jazz photography (including Billie Holiday portraits)
- "Spirit in the Dark" exhibition on Black religious life through photography

**Access:**
- Open Access Initiative: Images available via CC0 license
- Approximately 2,500 images available on Artstor
- [NMAAHC Collection](https://nmaahc.si.edu/explore/collection)
- [NMAAHC Open Access](https://nmaahc.si.edu/explore/initiatives/nmaahc-open-access)

**Licensing:**
- CC0 (Creative Commons Zero) for open access images
- Free to use, transform, and share without permission

---

### Stax Museum of American Soul Music (Memphis, TN)

**Collections:**
- Over 3,000 items of memorabilia
- Stage costumes, photographs, documents, personal items from Stax stars
- Instruments and vintage recording equipment
- Hall of Records with 900 45-singles and 300 LPs
- Circa-1906 authentic Mississippi Delta church
- Recreation of original recording studio and control room

**Notable Holdings:**
- Isaac Hayes' gold-trimmed, fur-lined 1972 Cadillac Eldorado
- Extensive Booker T. & the M.G.'s photographic archives
- Behind-the-scenes studio photography

**Historical Significance:**
- Official site on U.S. Civil Rights Trail
- Documents integrated recording company during segregation era

**Access:**
- Location: 926 East McLemore Avenue, Memphis
- [Stax Museum](https://staxmuseum.org/)

**Library of Congress Connection:**
- Part of Carol M. Highsmith's America Project (public domain photographs)

---

### Motown Museum (Detroit, MI)

**Collections:**
- Original Hitsville U.S.A. building and Studio A
- Extensive photographs and memorabilia
- Costumes, gold records, instruments
- Restored upper flat where Berry Gordy lived

**Notable Holdings:**
- Original Steinway piano (restored by Paul McCartney)
- Michael Jackson's fedora and sequined glove (donated 1988)
- Artist photographs: The Supremes, Stevie Wonder, The Jackson 5, Smokey Robinson, Martha Reeves

**Expansion Project:**
- $75 million Hitsville NEXT expansion underway
- Will grow to nearly 50,000 square feet
- [Motown Museum](https://www.motownmuseum.org/)

**Photography Resources:**
- Getty Images: 1,877+ Motown Museum stock photos
- Alamy: Additional stock photography

---

### Grammy Museum (Los Angeles, CA)

**Collections:**
- Four floors of interactive exhibits
- Historical music artifacts, costumes, instruments
- Grammy Awards memorabilia
- Handwritten lyrics, records, audio/video recordings
- First permanent Latin music gallery

**Photography Exhibits:**
- "RetroBlakesberg: The Music Never Stopped" (2025) - 150+ photographs by Jay Blakesberg
- Grammy red carpet fashion exhibition

**Access:**
- Location: L.A. Live, downtown Los Angeles
- No photography allowed inside exhibits
- [Grammy Museum](https://grammymuseum.org/)

---

### Apollo Theater Archives (New York, NY)

**Collections:**
- Rare photographs, video recordings, artifacts from 91-year history
- Columbia Center for Oral History Research partnership (since 2008)
- Historical documents and media

**Notable Exhibitions:**
- "Got To Be There: The Apollo, Its People, and Its Stories" at Laura & Frank Baker Gallery

**Smithsonian Connection:**
- Frank Schiffman Apollo Theatre Collection at Archives Center
- Sound recordings, correspondence, photographs, business records
- Donated by Jack and Robert Schiffman (1996)
- [Smithsonian Apollo Collection](https://sova.si.edu/record/nmah.ac.0540)

**Published Resources:**
- "Ain't Nothing Like the Real Thing" book (300+ photographs)
- [Apollo Theater](https://www.apollotheater.org/)

## 2. University Special Collections

### UCLA Library for the Performing Arts

**Collections:**
- One of largest academic music collections in North America
- 80,000 books, 115,000 scores, 200,000+ sound recordings
- Extensive historical recordings: popular music, jazz, film music, Broadway
- Los Angeles region music focus (20th century)
- Papers of L.A. emigre composers
- Oral histories of L.A. jazz musicians

**Notable Holdings:**
- Horace Tapscott Jazz Collection (1960-2002)
- John Coltrane Transcriptions Collection
- Charlie Parker Transcriptions Collection
- Jimmy Van Heusen Collection (1920-1991)
- Central Avenue Sounds Oral History Project
- Arhoolie Foundation's Strachwitz Frontera Collection (40,000 recordings)

**Access:**
- [UCLA Performing Arts Special Collections](https://guides.library.ucla.edu/library-special-collections/performing-arts)
- [Music Library Collections](https://www.library.ucla.edu/collections/music-library-collections/)

---

### Howard University Moorland-Spingarn Research Center

**Collections:**
- Largest and most comprehensive repository on global Black experience
- Hundreds of thousands of pamphlets, books, periodicals, photographs
- Personal papers, manuscripts, music, artifacts
- Separate Manuscript Division with four departments: manuscripts, music, oral history, prints and photographs

**Notable Holdings:**
- Papers of Paul Robeson, Alain Locke, Mary Frances Berry
- Arthur B. Spingarn Papers (includes Negro Music collection)
- Extensive video, music, reel-to-reel tapes, VHS, cassettes, record albums

**Recent Developments:**
- $2 million Mellon Foundation grant (2021)
- New audiovisual librarian position for music collection curation
- Digitization initiative underway

**Access:**
- [Moorland-Spingarn Research Center](https://msrc.howard.edu/)

---

### Indiana University - Archives of African American Music and Culture

**Collections:**
- Post-World War II era musical idioms and cultural expressions
- Black radio history documentation
- Interviews with radio pioneers

**Notable Holdings:**
- "Golden Age of Black Radio" exhibit on Google Arts & Culture
- Jack Gibson interviews about Black radio history
- WDIA, WVON historical documentation

**Access:**
- [AAAMC Archives](https://archives.iu.edu/repositories/aaamc)

## 3. Photographer Estates and Collections

### Michael Ochs Archives (via Getty Images)

**Collection Size:**
- 3 million vintage prints, proof sheets, and negatives
- Over 250,000 images digitized and available online
- Rock music photography dating to 1950s-1960s

**Historical Significance:**
- Called "the premier source of musician photography in the world" (NY Times)
- "The dominant force in the rock image marketplace" (LA Times)

**Acquisition:**
- Sold to Getty Images in February 2007
- 400,000 images digitized by 2024

**Licensing:**
- All rights and clearances managed by Getty Images
- [Getty Images - Michael Ochs Archives](https://www.gettyimages.com/photos/michael-ochs-archives)

---

### Redferns Music Picture Library (via Getty Images)

**Collection:**
- One of most comprehensive music photo libraries available
- Spans 19th-century classical to contemporary hip hop
- Live performances, private shoots, special events

**Historical Significance:**
- Founded by photographer David Redfern in early 1960s
- Redfern photographed Ready Steady Go! TV show
- Documented UK Motown tour: Stevie Wonder, Four Tops, Marvin Gaye

**Acquisition:**
- Sold to Getty Images in 2008

**Licensing:**
- [Getty Images - Redferns Collection](https://www.gettyimages.com/collections/redferns)

---

### James J. Kriegsmann Collection (via Getty Images)

**Historical Significance:**
- Only NYC photographer in 1930s willing to photograph Black entertainers
- In-house photographer for the Cotton Club
- Studio at West 46th Street, Times Square (1936-1980s)

**Notable Subjects:**
- Bill Robinson, Count Basie, Duke Ellington, Ray Charles
- The Supremes, Smokey Robinson, Stevie Wonder
- Benny Goodman, Louis Armstrong, Frank Sinatra

**Current Status:**
- Archives sold to Michael Ochs in 1988
- Now part of Getty Images collection

---

### Other Notable Photographers

**Norman Seeff:**
- 1,600+ famous portraits, 1,000+ album covers
- Subjects: Ray Charles, Tina Turner, Michael Jackson
- Worked at Columbia Records under Bob Cato
- [Norman Seeff Official](https://normanseeff.com/)

**Chuck Stewart:**
- Legendary jazz and R&B photographer
- Shot album covers for Atlantic Records artists
- Documented Motown and Atlantic artists

## 4. Label Archives

### Motown Records (Universal Music Group)

**Current Status:**
- Owned by Universal Music Group (since PolyGram acquisition 1993, then UMG 1999)
- Relocated to Capitol Music Group, Los Angeles (2014)
- Operating from Capitol Tower

**Archives:**
- Original photography studio converted to recording space (1959)
- Archives preserved at Motown Museum
- Tom & Ethel Bradley Center Digital Photograph Collections (post-L.A. relocation)

**Photography Access:**
- Getty Images: 2,087 Motown Records stock photos
- [Motown Records](https://www.motownrecords.com/)

---

### Atlantic Records (Warner Music Group)

**Historical Significance:**
- Founded 1947 by Ahmet Ertegun and Herb Abramson
- Pioneered R&B and soul: Aretha Franklin, Ray Charles, Wilson Pickett, Sam & Dave

**Notable Archive Features:**
- First major label to hire Black photographers for white artists
- Led Zeppelin first shoot by jazz photographer Chuck Stewart
- Joel Dorn located "destroyed" John Coltrane outtakes
- Ongoing discovery of unreleased Ray Charles, Ornette Coleman material

**75th Anniversary Book (2024):**
- "75 Years of Atlantic Records" - official photographic history
- Images from extensive archives, many never published

**NYU Archives:**
- Atlantic Recording Corporation and Elektra Entertainment records
- Sales data, artist information

---

### Universal Music Group

**Archives:**
- Largest music archive housed at Iron Mountain Entertainment Services
- Post-2008 fire inventory project ongoing
- "Deep Dive" documentary series spotlights archivists

**Library of Congress Donation:**
- 200,000+ historic master recordings
- 5,000 linear feet - largest single audio-visual donation ever

**Licensing:**
- UMG Sync (umgsync.com) handles licensing

---

### Sony Music Entertainment / Columbia Records

**Historical Significance:**
- Columbia founded 1887 (oldest existing label)
- Victor (now RCA) archives also held

**Legacy Recordings Division:**
- Handles archival preservation since 2005
- Manages Columbia and Epic Records archives

**Library of Congress Partnership:**
- 2008 agreement: pre-1925 recordings streamed free via National Jukebox
- Includes Victor, Columbia, Okeh labels

## 5. Trade Publications Archives

### Jet and Ebony Archives (Johnson Publishing Company)

**Collection Size:**
- 3 million+ photo negatives and slides
- 983,000 photographs
- 166,000 contact sheets
- 9,000 audio/visual recordings

**Historical Significance:**
- Most comprehensive collection documenting Black life in 20th century
- First publications addressing lack of Black representation
- Pulitzer Prize photographer Moneta Sleet Jr. was staff photographer
- 1955 Emmett Till coverage catalyzed civil rights movement

**Acquisition (2019):**
- $30 million purchase by nonprofit consortium
- Partners: Smithsonian, J. Paul Getty Trust, Ford Foundation, Mellon Foundation, MacArthur Foundation

**Current Status:**
- Physically housed at NMAAHC, Washington D.C.
- Getty committed $30 million for processing and digitization
- Images to be made available at no charge
- [Getty JPC Archive Guide](https://libguides.getty.edu/jpcarchive/faqs)

---

### Billboard Magazine

**Historical Background:**
- Founded 1894, music-focused since early 1900s
- Photojournalism began January 1963 (coated paper)

**Archives Access:**
- Google Books: Most issues 1942-2011
- Internet Archive: Volume 1 (November 1894) onward
- American Radio History: 1942-2009 PDFs
- [Billboard Magazine Archives](https://www.worldradiohistory.com/Archive-All-Music/Billboard-Magazine.htm)

---

### SOUL Magazine / SOUL Newspaper

**Historical Background:**
- Founded 1966 in Los Angeles by Regina and Ken Jones
- First soul music-focused publication worldwide
- Published 1966-1982, circulation 125,000 in 30 markets

**Collections:**
- Issues of SOUL and SOUL Illustrated (1968-1972)
- Research files, audio cassettes of interviews/performances
- Photographs, administrative files

**Notable Subjects:**
- James Brown, Aretha Franklin, Muhammad Ali, Berry Gordy Jr.
- Stevie Wonder, Marvin Gaye, Jackson Five, Diana Ross

**Archives Locations:**
- UCLA Special Archives
- Indiana University
- Digital archives at [SOUL Publications](https://www.soulpublications.co/)

---

### Blues & Soul Magazine (UK)

**Historical Background:**
- Published since late 1967, still active
- Premier soul/R&B review magazine of 1960s-1970s

**Archives:**
- First 100 issues available at [House of Soul UK](https://house-of-soul.co.uk/archive/blues-and-soul-magazine)
- Some issues on Internet Archive

## 6. Documentary Projects

### Wattstax (1973)

**Content:**
- Documentary of 1972 Stax Records benefit concert
- Watts Summer Festival at L.A. Memorial Coliseum
- Performers: Isaac Hayes, Rufus Thomas, Staple Singers
- Interspersed with 1965 riot archive footage

**Rights Holders:**
- Current copyright: The Saul Zaentz Company
- Video rights: Warner Bros.

**Archives:**
- Added to Library of Congress National Film Registry (December 2020)
- Stax Museum holds related imagery
- Available on Criterion Channel
- [IMDB](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0070902/)

---

### Soul Train

**Archive Size:**
- 1,100+ hours of archival footage
- Over 1,000 vintage episodes

**Ownership History:**
- 2008: Don Cornelius sold to MadVision Entertainment
- 2009: YouTube channel launched, Time Life DVD licensing
- 2011: Sold to Magic Johnson/InterMedia consortium
- 2016: BET Networks acquired
- Current: Owned by Paramount (via Paramount Media Networks)

**Access:**
- Reruns on Centric and Bounce TV
- Time Life DVD sets
- Getty Images: 590+ Soul Train photos
- [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soul_Train)

---

### American Bandstand

**Archives:**
- Museum of Broadcast Communications (Chicago): 1957 preserved broadcast
- Dick Clark's podium at Smithsonian
- Enterprise Center (West Philadelphia): maintains memorabilia

**Photography:**
- Getty Images: 6,086+ American Bandstand photos
- 1957-1989 broadcast run documented

**Anniversary Specials:**
- 33 1/3rd anniversary (1985) with archival footage
- 50th anniversary special (2002)

---

### Standing in the Shadows of Motown (2002)

**Content:**
- Documentary about the Funk Brothers (Motown house band)
- Archival footage, still photos, dramatized re-enactments
- New live performances with Chaka Khan, Ben Harper, Bootsy Collins

**Awards:**
- 2003 Grammy: Best Compilation Soundtrack Album
- 2003 Grammy: Best Traditional R&B Vocal Performance

**Access:**
- Available on Amazon Prime Video
- [IMDB](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0314725/)

---

### 20 Feet from Stardom (2013)

**Content:**
- Documentary about backup singers
- Features: Darlene Love, Merry Clayton, Lisa Fischer, Judith Hill
- Academy Award winner: Best Documentary Feature (2014)

**Photography:**
- Getty Images: Sundance 2013 portrait sessions
- [IMDB](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2396566/)
