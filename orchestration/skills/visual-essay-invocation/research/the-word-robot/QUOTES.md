## The Birth (1920s)

### Quote 1: Karel Čapek on the Word's Origin
> "I did not invent the word 'robot,' my brother Josef did. It was like this: I was hesitating whether to call these artificial workers 'laborers' or 'labori,' which seemed too literary. My brother Josef suggested 'robots.'"
> — **Karel Čapek**, *Lidové noviny* column, 1933

**Status**: ⚠️ Attributed (widely cited, original Czech text)
**Use for**: Etymology chapter, crediting Josef Čapek
**Visual treatment**: Art Deco typography, Czech newspaper styling

**Use for**: Establishing robot ethics in fiction
**Visual treatment**: Pulp magazine era typography

### Quote 6: Asimov's Second Law
