# Typography Research: ROBOT — The Word That Imagined Our Future

> Generated: December 12, 2025
> Special Requirement: Era-Appropriate Thematic Typography

---

## Typography Vision

The visual essay uses **typography as narrative device**—fonts evolve across the essay to mirror the word "robot's" journey through cultural and technological eras. Each chapter's typography should reflect the dominant perception of robots in that era.

---

## Era 1: The Birth (1920s-1930s)

### Characteristics
- Art Deco geometric forms
- Czech Constructivist influence
- Industrial-inspired letterforms
- Strong verticals, angular shapes
- Machine-age aesthetics

### Recommended Typefaces

#### Display/Headers
| Typeface | Style | Use Case |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **Futura** | Geometric sans | Headers, chapter titles |
| **Broadway** | Art Deco display | Pull quotes, emphasis |
| **Bifur** | Art Deco decorative | Special moments |
| **Prisma** | Outline display | Era-specific callouts |

#### Body Text
| Typeface | Style | Use Case |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **Gill Sans** | Humanist sans | Body text |
| **Twentieth Century** | Geometric sans | Alternative body |

#### Czech/Eastern European Influence
| Typeface | Style | Use Case |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **Prague** | Constructivist display | Czech context |
| **Avant Garde Gothic** | Geometric (later, but evocative) | Modernist feel |

### Visual Treatment
- Strong geometric forms
- Industrial metaphors in letterforms
- Black/white contrast with metallic accents
- Angles, diagonals, constructivist layouts

### Sample Pairing
```
ROBOT
[Futura Bold Extended or Broadway]

The word that changed everything
[Gill Sans Light]
```

## Implementation Notes

### Font Licensing Considerations
- **Google Fonts (free)**: Inter, Roboto, Open Sans, Poppins, Space Grotesk
- **Adobe Fonts (subscription)**: Futura, Helvetica, Myriad, Proxima Nova
- **Commercial licenses needed**: Circular, Söhne, Monument Extended

### Web Font Performance
- Limit to 2-3 font families per era section
- Use variable fonts where possible for weight flexibility
- Ensure fallback stacks are appropriate

### Accessibility
- Minimum 16px body text
- Sufficient contrast ratios
- Clear hierarchy between heading levels
- Avoid decorative fonts for long-form reading

## Typography as Narrative Device

The visual essay should make typography transitions visible and meaningful:

1. **Section transitions** — Font changes mark era shifts
2. **Pull quotes** — Use era-appropriate display fonts
3. **Data visualizations** — Match era's technical aesthetic
4. **Chapter headers** — Largest expression of era typography
5. **Captions** — Consistent but era-tinted

This approach makes the *form* of the essay reinforce its *content*—the evolution of the word "robot" is visible in the evolution of how we display it.
