# Timeline: ROBOT — The Word That Imagined Our Future

> Generated: December 12, 2025
> Span: 1920–2025 (105 years)

---

## Era 1: The Birth (1920-1939)

### 1920 — The Word Is Born
**Event**: Karel Čapek completes *R.U.R. (Rossum's Universal Robots)*
- Josef Čapek suggests "robot" from Czech "robota" (forced labor)
- Karel had originally considered "labori"
- Word captures industrial anxiety and class dynamics
**Significance**: HIGH — Origin of the word
**Visual**: Czech manuscript, Art Deco styling
**Typography Era**: Constructivist, Art Deco

### January 25, 1921 — World Premiere
**Event**: *R.U.R.* premieres at National Theatre in Prague
- First audience sees "robots" on stage
- Robots depicted as biological constructs, not metal machines
- Play is immediate sensation
**Significance**: HIGH — Word enters public consciousness
**Visual**: Theater program, stage photographs

### 1922 — European Spread
**Event**: *R.U.R.* productions across Europe
- German premiere in Berlin
- London production planned
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Word spreads beyond Czech borders

### 1923 — English Translation Published
**Event**: First English translation of *R.U.R.* published
- Word "robot" enters English vocabulary
- Theatre productions begin in UK and US
**Significance**: HIGH — Word enters global lingua franca
**Visual**: First edition book cover

### 1927 — Metropolis Visualizes the Robot
**Event**: Fritz Lang's *Metropolis* premieres
- Maschinenmensch (Machine-Human) becomes first iconic cinematic robot
- Establishes visual language: metallic, feminine, dangerous
- Image of Maria robot becomes cultural touchstone
**Significance**: HIGH — Visual template for robots
**Visual**: Film stills, poster art
**Typography Era**: German Expressionist, industrial

### 1929 — Robot Enters Oxford English Dictionary
**Event**: "Robot" officially added to OED
- Definition: "A machine capable of carrying out complex actions automatically"
- Word normalized in English usage
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Linguistic legitimacy

## Era 2: The Imagination (1940-1959)

### 1940 — Asimov Enters Robot Fiction
**Event**: Isaac Asimov publishes "Strange Playfellow" (later "Robbie")
- First of his robot stories
- Begins reshaping robot as benevolent helper
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Foundation for new robot narrative

### 1941 — "Robotics" Is Coined
**Event**: Asimov uses word "robotics" in "Liar!"
- First recorded use of the term
- Establishes robotics as field of study
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Terminology expansion

### 1942 — Three Laws of Robotics
**Event**: Asimov publishes "Runaround"
- Introduces the Three Laws of Robotics
- Creates ethical framework for robot behavior
- Laws become cultural touchstone for decades
**Significance**: HIGH — Robot ethics enters discourse
**Visual**: Pulp magazine covers
**Typography Era**: Pulp fiction, mid-century

### 1950 — I, Robot Collection
**Event**: Asimov's *I, Robot* published
- Consolidates robot stories into influential collection
- Establishes "positronic brain" concept
- Counters "Frankenstein complex" narrative
**Significance**: HIGH — Shapes generation of robot perception

### 1956 — First Programmable Robot Invented
**Event**: George Devol patents Unimate robot arm
- First digitally operated programmable robot
- Industrial application, not humanoid
**Significance**: HIGH — Robots become real technology
**Visual**: Patent drawings, prototype photos

## Era 3: The Industrial Age (1960-1979)

### 1961 — First Industrial Robot Deployed
**Event**: Unimate robot arm installed at General Motors (Trenton, NJ)
- Handles hot die-cast metal pieces
- Robots enter factory floor
- Beginning of automation anxiety
**Significance**: HIGH — Robots become industrial reality
**Visual**: Factory floor photography
**Typography Era**: Industrial modernism, Helvetica era

### 1966 — Shakey the Robot
**Event**: Stanford Research Institute creates Shakey
- First general-purpose mobile robot
- Uses AI for navigation and problem-solving
**Significance**: MEDIUM — AI-robot integration begins

### 1968 — HAL 9000
**Event**: *2001: A Space Odyssey* releases
- HAL 9000 is AI, not robot, but conflates concepts
- "I'm sorry, Dave. I'm afraid I can't do that."
- Reinforces fear of machine autonomy
**Significance**: MEDIUM — AI/robot fear conflation
**Visual**: Film stills

### 1970 — Uncanny Valley Proposed
**Event**: Masahiro Mori publishes uncanny valley theory
- Explains discomfort with near-human robots
- Influences robot design for decades
- Published in *Energy* magazine (Japan)
**Significance**: HIGH — Psychology of robot acceptance
**Visual**: Original graph illustration

### 1977 — Star Wars Droids
**Event**: *Star Wars* introduces R2-D2 and C-3PO
- Friendly, comedic robots as companions
- R2-D2 and C-3PO become cultural icons
- Shifts perception toward robots as helpful friends
**Significance**: HIGH — Positive robot characters
**Visual**: Film stills, merchandise
**Typography Era**: Retro-futuristic

## Era 4: The Fear Peak (1980-1999)

### 1984 — The Terminator
**Event**: James Cameron's *The Terminator* releases
- T-800 becomes icon of robot menace
- "I'll be back" enters lexicon
- Crystallizes fears of robotic apocalypse
- Peak of robot-as-threat in popular culture
**Significance**: HIGH — Defines robot fear for generation
**Visual**: Film stills, iconic Terminator imagery
**Typography Era**: 1980s chrome, action movie bold

### 1982 — Blade Runner
**Event**: Ridley Scott's *Blade Runner* releases
- Replicants blur human/robot boundary
- Questions what makes someone human
- Influential visual aesthetic
**Significance**: HIGH — Philosophical robot questions
**Visual**: Noir cyberpunk imagery

### 1986 — Robot Arms Increase
**Event**: Industrial robot installations accelerate
- Automotive industry leads adoption
- Job displacement fears grow
- Union concerns about automation
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Labor anxiety peaks

### 1989 — SCARA Robots Spread
**Event**: SCARA and articulated robots common in manufacturing
- Electronics assembly automation
- Robots become invisible infrastructure
**Significance**: LOW — Normalization of industrial robots

### 1999 — Sony AIBO Launches
**Event**: Sony releases AIBO robotic dog
- First mainstream entertainment robot
- People form emotional attachments to robot pet
- Hints at domestic robot future
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Robot as companion begins
**Visual**: Product photography
**Typography Era**: Late 90s tech optimism

## Era 5: The Domestic Revolution (2000-2015)

### 2002 — Roomba Launches
**Event**: iRobot releases Roomba vacuum robot
- First mass-market home robot
- Millions of households adopt robot helper
- Changes perception: robot = useful appliance
**Significance**: HIGH — Robot enters everyday home
**Visual**: Product photos, lifestyle imagery
**Typography Era**: Early 2000s clean tech

### 2004 — Mars Rovers
**Event**: Spirit and Opportunity land on Mars
- Robots as explorers, not threats
- Public follows robot "personalities"
- NASA anthropomorphizes rovers
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Robots as scientific heroes

### 2005 — DARPA Grand Challenge
**Event**: First autonomous vehicle completes desert course
- Stanley (Stanford) wins $2M prize
- Foundation for self-driving cars
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Autonomous systems advance

### 2006 — Uncanny Valley Enters Mainstream
**Event**: Term becomes widely used in animation/robotics discourse
- Pixar, animation studios cite concept
- Humanoid robot design debates intensify
**Significance**: LOW — Terminology spreads

### 2011 — Siri Launches
**Event**: Apple introduces Siri voice assistant
- AI assistant without robot body
- Begins expansion of "robot" concept to software
**Significance**: MEDIUM — AI-robot boundary blurs

### 2011 — Alone Together Published
**Event**: Sherry Turkle publishes *Alone Together*
- Critical examination of robot relationships
- Warns about preferring robots to humans
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Critical perspective emerges

### 2014 — Pepper Robot
**Event**: SoftBank releases Pepper humanoid robot
- Designed to read emotions
- Deployed in retail, hospitality
- First social robot at scale
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Social robots commercialize

## Era 6: The AI Expansion (2016-2025)

### 2016 — Boston Dynamics Viral Videos
**Event**: Atlas robot videos go viral
- Backflipping, running humanoid robots
- Mix of awe and fear in public reaction
- "Robot uprising" memes proliferate
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Humanoid capability demonstrated
**Visual**: Video stills

### 2016 — Pew Research: Robot Jobs Survey
**Event**: Major survey on automation perception
- 65% believe robots will do most human work in 50 years
- But 80% don't think their job at risk
- Captures public ambivalence
**Significance**: HIGH — Quantitative perception data

### 2016 — Sophia Robot Unveiled
**Event**: Hanson Robotics reveals Sophia
- Hyper-realistic humanoid face
- Granted Saudi citizenship (controversial)
- Tests uncanny valley limits
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Humanoid robot celebrity
**Visual**: Sophia portrait images

### 2020 — COVID Accelerates Automation
**Event**: Pandemic drives robot adoption
- Delivery robots, disinfection robots deploy
- "Contactless" becomes driver
- Accelerated normalization of robot presence
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Pandemic catalyst

### 2022 — ChatGPT Launches
**Event**: OpenAI releases ChatGPT
- AI assistant becomes mainstream
- "Robot" colloquially applied to AI
- Expands word beyond physical machines
**Significance**: HIGH — "Robot" concept expands
**Typography Era**: Contemporary AI aesthetic

### 2023 — Humanoid Robot Race
**Event**: Multiple companies announce humanoid robots
- Tesla Optimus, Figure 01, Boston Dynamics Atlas
- Race to create general-purpose humanoids
- Factory and domestic applications promised
**Significance**: HIGH — Humanoid robot moment

### 2024 — AI Perception Gap Widens
**Event**: Studies show elite vs. public AI optimism gap
- Business leaders highly optimistic
- Public remains skeptical about impacts
**Significance**: MEDIUM — Ongoing sentiment divide

### 2025 — Word "Robot" Turns 105
**Event**: Centenary +5 of the word
- From Czech play to global technological reality
- Word has evolved from biological to mechanical to AI
- Public perception remains complex, evolving
**Significance**: Commemorative milestone
