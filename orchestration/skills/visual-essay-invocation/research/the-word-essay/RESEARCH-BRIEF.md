# Research Brief: The Word "Essay"

## Project Identification

- **Working Title**: The Word "Essay" — A History of Attempting to Think
- **Topic**: Etymology, history, and evolution of the word "essay"
- **Date Initiated**: December 14, 2025
- **Research Depth**: Deep (15-25 sources)

## Research Targets

### Figures (Layer 4)
- **Target**: 5-15 key figures (essayists who shaped the form)
- **Requirements per figure**:
  - [x] Verified biographical information
  - [x] At least one quotable statement about the essay form
  - [x] Available photography or portraiture
  - [x] Key contributions to essay tradition documented

### Timeline/Events (Layer 4)
- **Requirements**:
  - [x] Etymology chain documented (Latin → French → English)
  - [x] Major publication milestones mapped (1580, 1597, 1711, etc.)
  - [x] Evolution of the form chronologically mapped
  - [x] Turning points identified (periodical essay, personal essay, etc.)

### Visual Materials (Layer 4 + 5)
- **Requirements**:
  - [x] Manuscript imagery (Montaigne's originals if available)
  - [x] Historical typeface specimens for each era
  - [x] Portrait photography of key essayists
  - [x] Library/printing press imagery
  - [x] First edition pages of seminal essay collections

### Typography Research (SPECIAL)
- **Requirements**:
  - [x] Era-appropriate typefaces identified (1580-present)
  - [x] Typeface history documented (why Garamond, why Caslon)
  - [x] Manuscript hands vs. printed type transition
  - [x] Visual specimens for each major era

### Quotes (Layer 4)
- **Requirements**:
  - [x] Minimum 10 verified quotes with sources
  - [x] Focus on meta-essays (essayists on the essay form)
  - [x] Montaigne on "essais" specifically
  - [x] Attribution confirmed for each

## Scope Boundaries

### In Scope
- Etymology from Latin through French to English
- Michel de Montaigne as origin figure
- Key essayists who shaped the form (Bacon, Lamb, Woolf, Baldwin, etc.)
- Typography evolution as visual storytelling element
- The essay as philosophical stance (attempting vs. declaring)
- Modern evolution (blog, video essay)

### Out of Scope
- Detailed literary analysis of individual essays
- Academic essay writing pedagogy
- Non-Western essay traditions (future expansion opportunity)
- Comprehensive list of all essayists

## Special Requirements

1. **Typography as Primary Design Element**: Typography is not just functional but featured — period-appropriate typefaces, manuscript hands, evolution of letterforms
2. **The Word Itself as Motif**: The word "Essay" / "Essai" in various historical typefaces as recurring visual anchor
3. **No Templates**: Build from first principles, no existing visual essay patterns
4. **Meta-Irony**: Acknowledge the essay-about-essays layer with light wit
