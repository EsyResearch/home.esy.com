# Timeline: The Word "War"

> **Focus**: Attestations, spelling stabilization, semantic shifts
> **Date Compiled**: January 3, 2026

---

## Chronological Overview

```
PRE-HISTORY ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
    PIE *wers- "to confuse, mix up"
         ↓
PROTO-GERMANIC (c. 500 BCE - 200 CE) ────────────────────────
    *werz-a- / *werran- "to confuse"
         ↓
FRANKISH / OLD HIGH GERMAN (c. 500-850 CE) ──────────────────
    *werra "confusion, strife, quarrel"
         ↓
858 CE ── FIRST WRITTEN ATTESTATION ─────────────────────────
    Medieval Latin "werras" in episcopal letter
         ↓
OLD FRENCH / OLD NORTH FRENCH (c. 900-1300) ─────────────────
    guerre / werre
         ↓
1066 ──── NORMAN CONQUEST ───────────────────────────────────
    Anglo-Norman werre introduced to England
         ↓
c.1121 ── FIRST ENGLISH ATTESTATION ─────────────────────────
    "wyrre" in Peterborough Chronicle
         ↓
MIDDLE ENGLISH (c. 1150-1500) ───────────────────────────────
    werre, warre → war (spelling variants)
         ↓
MODERN ENGLISH (c. 1500-present) ────────────────────────────
    "war" standardized
```

## Detailed Timeline

### Pre-1000 CE: Origins

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Pre-history** | PIE *\*wers-* "to confuse, mix up" — reconstructed root | Watkins |
| **c. 500 BCE - 200 CE** | Proto-Germanic *\*werz-a-* develops | Etymological reconstruction |
| **c. 500-850 CE** | Frankish *\*werra* in use (reconstructed) | Romance evidence |
| **c. 800-1050** | Old High German *werra* "confusion, strife" attested | OHG texts |
| **858 CE** | **FIRST ATTESTATION**: Latin episcopal letter from NE France uses *werras* as vernacular term for "strifes or arguments or seditions" | OED |

---

### 1000-1200 CE: Entry into English

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| **c. 1000+** | Old French *guerre* / Old North French *werre* in use | OF/ANF texts |
| **1066** | **NORMAN CONQUEST**: Norman French (including *werre*) brought to England | Historical |
| **c. 1116** | Event referenced in first English attestation (King Henri's war in Normandy) | Peterborough Chronicle |
| **c. 1121-1122** | **FIRST ENGLISH ATTESTATION**: Peterborough Chronicle records *wyrre* (entry for 1116): "Eall þis gear wunode se cyng Henri on Normandig for þes cynges wyrre of France" | MED, OED |
| **c. 1154** | Form *uuerre* appears in Anglo-Saxon Chronicle (spelling "uu" = Norman influence) | MED |
| **late 12c** | Semantic extension: "state of active opposition or hostility" between persons/communities | OED |

---

### 1200-1400 CE: Middle English Consolidation

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| **c. 1200** | "War" used in reference to particular wars | OED |
| **c. 1200** | Verb form *werren* "to wage war" attested | OED |
| **c. 1300** | *SLeg.* (MS LdMisc 108): "Þe weorre was þo in Enguelonde" | MED |
| **c. 1300** | **"warrior"** first attested (< ONF *werreier*) | OED |
| **c. 1325** | "warrior" in *Chronicle of Robert of Gloucester* | OED |
| **c. 1330** | *Tristrem*: "Þe wer lasted so long / Til morgan asked pes" | MED |
| **mid-14c** | Semantic extension: "fighting as profession" (*man-of-war*) | OED |
| **c. 1387-95** | Chaucer, *Canterbury Tales* Prologue: "Ful worthy was he in his lordes werre" | MED |
| **late 14c** | Expression "in war and peace" = "at all times" | OED |
| **late 14c-15c** | **"civil war"** attested (from Latin *bellum civile*) | OED |

---

### 1400-1600 CE: Spelling Standardization

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| **late 15c** | Phrase "civil war" fully attested in English | OED |
| **c. 1400** | "warfare" (implied in *warfaringe*) — compound of war + fare | Etymonline |
| **c. 1476** | Caxton's printing press begins standardizing English spelling | Historical |
| **c. 1500+** | **SPELLING STABILIZATION**: "war" becomes standard form | Print evidence |
| **1540s** | "warfare" figurative use begins | Etymonline |

---

### 1600-1800 CE: Dictionary Codification

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| **1651** | "Civil war" used specifically for English Civil War | OED |
| **1755** | **JOHNSON'S DICTIONARY** published — codifies "war" and related words | Samuel Johnson |
| **1737** | Bynkershoek: "nations and princes... are not generally willing to wage war without a previous declaration" | Historical |

---

### 1800-1900 CE: Compound Proliferation

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| **1848** | **"world war"** first used in English: "A war among the great powers is now necessarily a world-war" (*The People's Journal*, Scotland) | OED |
| **c. 1850** | Marx and Engels use "world war" in *Class Struggles in France* | Wikipedia |
| **1889** | Rasmus B. Anderson uses "world war" (Swedish *världskrig*) | Wikipedia |
| **1895** | "war-weary" attested | Etymonline |
