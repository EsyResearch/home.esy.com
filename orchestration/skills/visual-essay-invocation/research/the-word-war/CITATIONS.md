## Tier 1 Sources (Primary Authorities)

- **Key Data**: First attestation in Peterborough Chronicle (c1122, referring to events of 1116); etymology from Anglo-Norman werre; note that "no Germanic nation in early historic times had in living use any word properly meaning 'war'"

## Tier 2 Sources (Etymological References)

### 7. Etymonline - "war"
- **Author**: Douglas Harper
- **URL**: https://www.etymonline.com/word/war
- **Key Data**: From late Old English wyrre/werre from Old North French werre from Frankish *werra from Proto-Germanic *werz-a- from PIE *wers- "to confuse, mix up"
- **Tier**: 2

### 8. Etymonline - "warrior"
- **URL**: https://www.etymonline.com/word/warrior
- **Key Data**: c.1300, from Old North French werreier
- **Tier**: 2

### 9. Etymonline - "warfare"
- **URL**: https://www.etymonline.com/word/warfare
- **Key Data**: Compound of war + fare; figurative use by 1540s
- **Tier**: 2

### 10. Etymonline - "civil war"
- **URL**: https://www.etymonline.com/word/civil%20war
- **Key Data**: From Latin bellum civile; phrase in English from late 15th century
- **Tier**: 2

### 11. Etymonline - "world war"
- **URL**: https://www.etymonline.com/word/world%20war
- **Key Data**: First used 1848; "World War I" coined 1939
- **Tier**: 2

### 12. Kluge, Friedrich - "An Etymological Dictionary of the German Language"
- **Entry**: "Krieg"
- **URL**: https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/An_Etymological_Dictionary_of_the_German_Language/Annotated/Krieg
- **Key Data**: From MHG kriec(g) meaning "exertion, endeavour"; almost unknown in OHG; shared only with Dutch krijg
- **Tier**: 2

### 13. de Vaan, Michiel - "Etymological Dictionary of Latin and the other Italic Languages" (2008)
- **Publisher**: Brill
- **Entry**: bellum
- **Key Data**: From older duellum; possible connection to bellus "beautiful" through Proto-Italic *dwenelo-
- **Tier**: 2
