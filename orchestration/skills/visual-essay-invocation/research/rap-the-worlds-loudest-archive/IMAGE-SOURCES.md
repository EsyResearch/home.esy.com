## Archive Sources

### Tier 1: Dedicated Hip-Hop Photography Archives

#### Janette Beckman
- **Website**: janettebeckman.com
- **Contact**: Via website contact form
- **Specialty**: 1980s New York hip-hop, British new wave crossover
- **Key Subjects**: Run-DMC, Salt-N-Pepa, LL Cool J, EPMD, Big Daddy Kane, Slick Rick, Queen Latifah
- **Licensing**: Direct licensing through photographer; rates vary by usage
- **Archive Strength**: Definitive 1980s portraits; often the only quality images of early artists
- **Notes**:
  - Run-DMC Hollis portraits are industry standard
  - Salt-N-Pepa images among most reproduced female hip-hop photos
  - Responsive to editorial requests; higher rates for commercial use

#### Joe Conzo Jr.
- **Contact**: Through Cornell University Hip-Hop Collection or direct
- **Specialty**: Bronx origins (1977-1985), Cold Crush Brothers, early DJ culture
- **Key Subjects**: DJ Kool Herc, Grandmaster Flash, Cold Crush Brothers, early block parties
- **Archive Strength**: ONLY photographer documenting hip-hop's true origins
- **Licensing**: Case-by-case; educational use often accommodated
- **Notes**:
  - Donated collection to Cornell (2007) but retains licensing rights
  - Images from 1520 Sedgwick Avenue parties are irreplaceable
  - Personal relationship with subjects adds context to images

#### Glen E. Friedman
- **Website**: burningflags.com
- **Contact**: Via website; known to be selective about usage
- **Specialty**: Punk/hip-hop crossover, Beastie Boys, skateboarding culture
- **Key Subjects**: Beastie Boys, Public Enemy, Run-DMC, Ice-T
- **Licensing**: Photographer-controlled; refuses certain commercial uses
- **Notes**:
  - Strong political views about image usage
  - *Fuck You Heroes* and *Fuck You Too* books document archive
  - Beastie Boys images essential but require negotiation

#### Ernie Paniccioli
- **Archive Location**: Cornell University Hip-Hop Collection
- **Specialty**: Comprehensive 1980s-2000s hip-hop portraiture
- **Key Subjects**: Nearly every major artist from 1980-2010
- **Archive Strength**: Breadth unmatched; 50,000+ images
- **Licensing**: Through Cornell University Library for academic use
- **Notes**:
  - Donated archive to Cornell
  - Native American heritage (Cree) informs cultural perspective
  - Author of *Who Shot Ya? Three Decades of Hip Hop Photography*

#### Chi Modu Archive
- **Status**: Estate-controlled (photographer died 2021)
- **Website**: chimodu.com (estate managed)
- **Specialty**: *The Source* magazine photography (1990s)
- **Key Subjects**: Tupac, Biggie, Method Man, Wu-Tang, Snoop Dogg
- **Licensing**: Through estate; editorial and commercial rates
- **Notes**:
  - Tupac bandana portraits are definitive
  - Biggie Coogi sweater images iconic
  - Estate actively licenses for appropriate projects

### Tier 2: Institutional Archives

#### Cornell University Hip-Hop Collection
- **Website**: hiphop.library.cornell.edu
- **Contact**: Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
- **Holdings**:
  - Joe Conzo Jr. photography archive
  - Ernie Paniccioli photography archive
  - Afrika Bambaataa papers
  - Record label ephemera
  - Flyers, zines, early press materials
- **Licensing**: Academic use free; commercial requires permission
- **Digital Access**: Limited; requires institutional access for most materials
- **Notes**:
  - Premier academic hip-hop archive
  - Flyers and ephemera highly useful for essay
  - Research appointments available

#### Smithsonian National Museum of African American History and Culture (NMAAHC)
- **Website**: nmaahc.si.edu
- **Contact**: Museum archives
- **Holdings**:
  - Hip-hop exhibition materials
  - Oral histories
  - Artifacts and memorabilia
  - Some photography
- **Licensing**: Public domain for many government-created images; others case-by-case
- **Notes**:
  - "Musical Crossroads" exhibition includes hip-hop
  - Growing collection with ongoing acquisitions
  - Less comprehensive for photography than Cornell

#### Rock and Roll Hall of Fame
- **Website**: rockhall.com
- **Contact**: Library and Archives
- **Holdings**: Inductee materials (Grandmaster Flash, Public Enemy, N.W.A, Beastie Boys, Run-DMC, 2Pac, Notorious B.I.G.)
- **Notes**: Focused on inductees; good for specific artists but not comprehensive

#### Grammy Museum
- **Website**: grammymuseum.org
- **Holdings**: Performance photos, award ceremony images
- **Notes**: Useful for validation/ceremony images

#### Universal Hip Hop Museum (Opening 2025)
- **Location**: Bronx, New York
- **Status**: Collection being assembled; opening 2025
- **Notes**: Future resource; may have unique materials

### Tier 3: Commercial Sources

#### Getty Images
- **Website**: gettyimages.com
- **Specialty**: Editorial and entertainment photography
- **Strengths**:
  - Massive archive of concert photography
  - Red carpet and award show coverage
  - Wire service (AFP, Getty Entertainment) archives
  - Searchable online database
- **Licensing**: Standard editorial rates; commercial higher
- **Notes**:
  - Best single source for post-1985 artists
  - Search "hip-hop" + decade for era-specific results
  - Premium pricing but reliable licensing

#### Associated Press (AP Images)
- **Website**: apimages.com
- **Specialty**: News photography, events
- **Notes**: Good for news events, court appearances, controversies

#### Shutterstock/Rex Features
- **Specialty**: Editorial entertainment
- **Notes**: UK coverage (grime, UK drill) often better than Getty

#### Alamy
- **Website**: alamy.com
- **Notes**: Good for editorial use; wide variety of contributors

### Tier 4: Label and Publicist Archives

#### Major Label Archives
- **Sony Music Archives**: Columbia, Epic records history
- **Universal Music Archives**: Def Jam, Interscope, Island
- **Warner Music Archives**: Atlantic, Elektra

#### Key Independent Labels
- **Def Jam Recordings**: Press kit archives (via Universal)
- **Tommy Boy Records**: Archived materials (label inactive but materials exist)
- **Profile Records**: Run-DMC early materials
- **Ruthless Records**: N.W.A materials
- **Cash Money/Young Money**: Via publicists
- **Quality Control Music**: Via publicists

## Licensing Categories

### Category 1: Public Domain
**Definition**: No copyright restrictions; free to use

**Applicable Materials**:
- Pre-1928 published works (limited hip-hop relevance)
- U.S. government works
- Works explicitly released to public domain
- CC0 designated works on Wikimedia Commons

**Hip-Hop Availability**: Very limited. Hip-hop emerged in 1973; essentially no public domain images exist.

### Category 2: Creative Commons
**Definition**: Copyrighted works with pre-granted permissions

**Types Encountered**:
- **CC BY**: Attribution required
- **CC BY-SA**: Attribution + share-alike
- **CC BY-NC**: Attribution + non-commercial only
- **CC0**: Public domain dedication

**Primary Sources**: Wikimedia Commons

**Hip-Hop Availability**:
- Equipment photos (turntables, drum machines): Good
- Location exteriors: Good
- Artist portraits: Rare (mostly publicity stills)
- Concert photography: Very rare

### Category 3: Editorial Use (Getty/AP)
**Definition**: Licensed for news, commentary, and educational contexts

**Terms**:
- One-time use fee
- Must be in editorial (not advertising) context
- Credit required
- Time-limited licenses available

**Pricing Tiers** (approximate):
- Small/web: $175-375
- Medium/print: $300-600
- Large/hero: $500-1500

**Best For**: Concert photos, red carpet, news events, most artist portraits

### Category 4: Permission Required (Archives)
**Definition**: Direct licensing from photographers or estates

**Key Archives Requiring Direct Permission**:

**Notes**: Often more flexible and sometimes less expensive than commercial sources for appropriate projects.

### Category 5: Fair Use
**Definition**: Limited use without permission for criticism, commentary, education, news

**Hip-Hop Applicability**:

| Material Type | Fair Use Strength | Notes |
|--------------|-------------------|-------|
| Album covers (small, in context) | Strong | Must be editorial/critical context |
| Chart graphics | Strong | Historical documentation |
| Award show clips | Moderate | Commentary context required |
| Music video stills | Moderate | Criticism/analysis context |
| Logo reproductions | Weak-Moderate | Factual/historical context |
| Full songs/lyrics | Weak | Generally requires license |

## Fair Use Guidelines

### What CAN Be Used Under Fair Use

**Album Covers**:
- Small reproduction (< 300px) in context of discussion
- Analysis or criticism of artwork
- Historical documentation of era
- Comparison with other covers

**Best Practices**:
- Never use album cover as decorative element
- Always include critical/analytical caption
- Keep reproduction small relative to page
- Use only as many covers as necessary for argument

**Charts and Documents**:
- Billboard chart positions (historical record)
- RIAA certification logos (factual documentation)
- Award ceremony screenshots (news documentation)
- FBI letter to N.W.A (historical document)

**Press Materials**:
- Press kit photos where artist/label distributed freely
- Promotional materials intended for press use
- Note: Distribution doesn't eliminate copyright; just weakens infringement claims

### What CANNOT Be Used Under Fair Use

**Full Album Artwork**:
- Large reproductions dominating layout
- Using cover as primary design element
- Reproductions without critical context

**Music**:
- Song lyrics beyond brief quotation
- Audio clips without license
- Sheet music reproduction

**Video**:
- Extended video clips from concerts/music videos
- Full music videos embedded

**Photography**:
- Professional photographs used as primary visual
- Concert photos used decoratively
- Portrait photos without editorial context

### Risk Assessment Matrix

| Use Case | Risk Level | Recommendation |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Small album cover in text about that album | Low | Proceed with proper context |
| Album cover as chapter header art | Moderate | Seek license |
| Album cover as merchandise/commercial | High | Must license |
| Chart showing Billboard positions | Low | Fair use likely |
| Press photo in article about artist | Low-Moderate | License if possible |
| Press photo as primary hero image | Moderate | License recommended |
| Concert photo for criticism/review | Moderate | Fair use possible |
| Concert photo as wallpaper/background | High | Must license |

3. **Editorial Framing**: Ensure all use is editorial to access lower-tier pricing
4. **Bundle Requests**: Request multiple images from same source simultaneously
5. **Academic Partnerships**: Leverage Cornell connection for educational rates
6. **Rights-Managed vs. Royalty-Free**: Evaluate based on use case
