## Part 1: Research Questions

### Core Questions (Must Answer)

#### Q1: Origins — What existed before "rap" had a name?
- What are the documented African diasporic oral traditions that prefigure rap?
- How did signifying, the dozens, and toasting function as social technologies?
- What role did Caribbean sound systems and Jamaican toasting play in NYC?
- Who were the radio personalities and spoken-word artists that influenced early MCs?
- What evidence exists for the continuity between these traditions and recorded rap?

#### Q2: Hip-Hop vs. Rap — How are they connected and distinct?
- What is the documented relationship between the four elements and rap specifically?
- When did "rap music" become an industry category distinct from "hip-hop culture"?
- How did the commodification of rap affect the other hip-hop elements?
- What tensions exist between cultural and commercial definitions?

#### Q3: NYC Foundation — Why was NYC the crucible?
- What specific neighborhoods, parks, and venues hosted early rap?
- What were the economic conditions (deindustrialization, housing policy) that shaped the culture?
- Who were the DJs that created the sonic platform before MCs emerged as focus?
- How did the transition from party ritual to recorded form happen?
- What label, radio, and venue infrastructure enabled professionalization?

#### Q4: Regional Revolution — Why did other regions become decisive?
- What are the specific local ecosystems (radio, clubs, distributors, labels) for each major region?
- What sound innovations came from each region that couldn't have come from NYC?
- How did regional scenes build economies "without coastal permission"?
- What specific dates and releases mark each region's national breakthrough?

#### Q5: Global Spread — How did rap become global and what did it become?
- What are the 10-14 key global rap scenes with documented local context?
- What political/censorship/resistance contexts shaped each global scene?
- What local languages, flows, and production styles distinguish each scene?
- Who are the key figures, venues, and platforms in each global scene?

#### Q6: Technology Evolution — How did sound change with technology?
- What specific gear (turntables, drum machines, samplers, DAWs) defined each era?
- Who are the key producers associated with each technological shift?
- How did Auto-Tune, vocal processing, and melody integration change rap?
- What role do streaming algorithms and social platforms play now?

#### Q7: Political Economy — How did themes evolve with economics and policy?
- How did deindustrialization shape rap's origins?
- What is the documented relationship between the War on Drugs, policing, and rap content?
- How did mass incarceration affect the industry and content?
- What role did media incentives play in shaping commercial rap narratives?

#### Q8: Myths vs. Evidence — What myths persist, and what does evidence show?
- "Rap was invented in a studio" — What is the evidence?
- "Auto-Tune ruined rap" — What is the actual sonic history?
- "Sampling is theft" — What are the competing views and legal/artistic realities?
- "Rap is finished" — What does current evidence show?
- Regional "firsts" — What claims are contested and what is verifiable?

## Part 10: Ethics Guidelines

### Content Handling

1. **Violence**: Document where essential to history; do not glamorize
2. **Misogyny**: Analyze as historical phenomenon; do not reproduce uncritically
3. **Exploitation**: Document industry practices; center affected parties
4. **Death/Tragedy**: Handle with restraint; verify all facts

### Representation Requirements

1. **Women**: Integrated throughout every era, not footnotes
2. **LGBTQ+**: Where documented, with appropriate context
3. **Regional balance**: No single region dominates narrative
4. **Behind-the-scenes**: Producers, engineers, executives, journalists visible

### Copyright Compliance

1. **No extended lyrics**: Paraphrase and analyze only
2. **Quotes limited**: 8-12 BlockQuotes maximum
3. **Images sourced**: Archive/licensed only; no fan sites
