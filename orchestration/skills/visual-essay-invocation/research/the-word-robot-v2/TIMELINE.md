# Timeline: ROBOT — Grand Machina Edition

> 105 Years of the Word "Robot" (1920–2025)

---

## Era 1: The Birth (1920–1939)

| Year | Event | Significance | Source |
|------|-------|--------------|--------|
| **1920** | Karel Čapek writes R.U.R. | Word "robot" created by Josef Čapek | Britannica, MIT Press |
| **1921** | R.U.R. premieres in Prague | First public use of "robot" | Theatre archives |
| **1922** | R.U.R. premieres in New York | Word enters English-speaking world | Theatre history |
| **1923** | "Robot" enters English language | First OED citation | Oxford English Dictionary |
| **1927** | Fritz Lang's Metropolis released | Visual template for robots established | Film archives |
| **1938** | Karel Čapek dies | Original author gone before WWII | Biographical sources |
| **1945** | Josef Čapek dies in Bergen-Belsen | True inventor of word killed by Nazis | Biographical sources |

**Era Character**: The word is born in a play about rebellion and forced labor. Fiction precedes reality.

## Era 2: The Imagination (1940–1959)

| Year | Event | Significance | Source |
|------|-------|--------------|--------|
| **1941** | "Liar!" published | Asimov coins "robotics" (first use) | Astounding SF |
| **1942** | "Runaround" published | Three Laws of Robotics introduced | Astounding SF |
| **1950** | I, Robot published | Asimov's robot stories collected | Gnome Press |
| **1954** | First programmable robot | George Devol patents Unimate concept | USPTO |

**Era Character**: Fiction programs our expectations. Asimov counters fear with ethics. "Robotics" becomes a science.

| Year | Inflection | Before | After |
|------|------------|--------|-------|
| 1920 | R.U.R. written | No word for artificial workers | "Robot" enters vocabulary |
| 1942 | Three Laws introduced | Robots = threat | Robots = ethical subjects |
| 1961 | Unimate deployed | Robots = fiction | Robots = industrial reality |
| 1984 | Terminator released | Various robot fears | Unified robot-apocalypse archetype |
| 2002 | Roomba launched | Robots = factories/labs | Robots = home appliances |
| 2022 | ChatGPT released | Robot = physical machine | Robot = any AI (embodied or not) |
