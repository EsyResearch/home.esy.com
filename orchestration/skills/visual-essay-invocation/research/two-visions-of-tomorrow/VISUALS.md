### Visual Treatment Specifications

#### Split-Screen Concept

The hero and recurring visual motif uses split-screen to show both worlds:

| Left Side (1984) | Right Side (BNW) |
|------------------|------------------|
| Grey/cold palette | Warm/saturated palette |
| Surveillance camera | Smartphone |
| Propaganda poster | Advertisement |
| Brutalist concrete | Gleaming glass |
| Stern faces | Smiling faces |
| Prison bars | Open doors |
| Telescreen | Entertainment screen |
| Fear | Pleasure |

#### Color Palettes

**1984 Palette**
- Primary: Steel grey (#6B7280)
- Secondary: Concrete (#9CA3AF)
- Accent: Warning red (#DC2626)
- Text: Cold white (#F9FAFB)
- Shadow: Deep black (#111827)

**Brave New World Palette**
- Primary: Sky blue (#0EA5E9)
- Secondary: Warm cream (#FEF3C7)
- Accent: Soma pink (#F472B6)
- Text: Soft white (#FFFBEB)
- Highlight: Electric yellow (#FCD34D)

**Synthesis Palette**

- Smartphone glow (#A78BFA)

- Alert orange (#F97316)

### Animation Concepts

#### Hero Sequence: "The Split World"
1. Screen divided vertically
2. Left: Grey world materializes (surveillance, watchers)
3. Right: Colorful world materializes (pills, screens, smiles)
4. Text emerges from center divide
5. Both sides animate toward viewer
6. Merge into single smartphone screen

#### The Letter Reveal
1. Aged paper texture background
2. Handwritten text appears letter by letter
3. Date materializes: "October 21, 1949"
4. Two portraits fade in on sides
5. Central question lands

#### Progress Bar: The Divided Line
- Left half: Grey fill (1984 progress)
- Right half: Color fill (BNW progress)
- Both advance simultaneously
- Merge at synthesis chapter
