## Pre-R&B Era (1920s-1940s)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1920 | First commercial blues recording | Mamie Smith's "Crazy Blues" launches "race records" category |
| 1928 | Regal Theater opens (Chicago) | 2,500-seat venue, six years before Apollo |
| 1931 | RCA 44 ribbon microphone introduced | Enables crooner intimacy in recording |
| 1934 Jan 26 | Apollo Theater opens for Black audiences | Sidney Cohen converts whites-only burlesque theater |
| 1939 | Dew Drop Inn opens (New Orleans) | Listed in Negro Motorist Green Book; crucial R&B venue |
| 1939 | United Sound Systems opens (Detroit) | Future site of first Motown recording |
| c. 1940 | T-Bone Walker electrifies blues guitar | First bluesman to amplify guitar as solo instrument |
| 1945 | J&M Recording Studio opens (New Orleans) | Cosimo Matassa opens at age 18 |
| 1945 | Specialty Records founded (Los Angeles) | Art Rupe starts label |
| 1946 | Imperial Records founded (Los Angeles) | Lew Chudd starts label |
| 1947 | Neumann U47 microphone released | Becomes R&B vocal recording standard |
| 1947 | Atlantic Records founded (New York) | Ahmet Ertegun, Herb Abramson start with $10,000 |
| 1947 | Ruth Brown wins Apollo Amateur Night | Discovered by Atlantic Records |

## The Naming (1949) and Early R&B Era (1949-1959)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **1949 Jun 17** | **"Rhythm and Blues" term coined** | **Jerry Wexler replaces "race records" in Billboard** |
| 1949 | Fats Domino records "The Fat Man" | Signed to Imperial; sells 1 million copies |
| 1950 | Sun Studio opens (Memphis) | Sam Phillips founds Memphis Recording Service |
| 1951 | Fender Precision Bass released | Solves bass audibility problem |
| 1951 | "Rocket 88" recorded at Sun | Often cited as first rock and roll single |
| 1952 | Big Mama Thornton records "Hound Dog" | #1 R&B for 7 weeks; earns $500 total |
| 1953 | Vee-Jay Records founded | First major Black-owned independent label (Carter/Bracken) |
| 1953 | Jerry Wexler becomes Atlantic partner | Shapes label's artistic direction |
| 1954 | LaVern Baker petitions Congress | Seeks copyright protection for arrangements |
| 1955 | Little Richard records "Tutti Frutti" | Improvised at Dew Drop Inn; recorded at J&M |
| 1955 | "Ain't That a Shame" - Fats Domino | First crossover pop hit |
| 1956 | "Why Do Fools Fall in Love" | Frankie Lymon - first Black teenage pop star |
| 1957 | Stax Records founded (Memphis) | Jim Stewart and Estelle Axton start Satellite Records |
| 1957 | Hi Records founded (Memphis) | Later home to Al Green |
| 1957 | Chess Records moves to 2120 S. Michigan | Iconic Chicago address |
| 1958 | Atlantic gets first 8-track recorder | Tom Dowd innovations begin |
| **1959** | **Motown Records founded (Detroit)** | **Berry Gordy borrows $800 from family** |

## Soul Era (1960-1969)

|------|-------|--------------|

| 1962 | "Green Onions" - Booker T. & the M.G.'s | Million-selling instrumental defines Stax sound |
| 1964 | Motown upgrades to 8-track | Studio technology advancement |
| 1964 | Rolling Stones record at Chess | "2120 South Michigan Avenue" instrumental tribute |
| 1965 | "Uptight (Everything's Alright)" | Sylvia Moy saves Stevie Wonder's career |
| 1966 | Vee-Jay Records collapses | First major Black-owned label fails |
| 1966 | SOUL Magazine founded (Los Angeles) | First soul music-focused publication |
| 1967 Jan | Aretha Franklin signs to Atlantic | Jerry Wexler produces at Muscle Shoals |
| 1967 | "Respect" - Aretha Franklin | #1 pop and R&B; feminist/civil rights anthem |
| 1967 | "Soul Man" - Sam & Dave | Peak Stax/Memphis soul |
| 1968 | Sigma Sound Studios opens (Philadelphia) | Joe Tarsia founds studio |
| 1968 Dec | Otis Redding's "(Sittin' On) The Dock of the Bay" | First posthumous #1 single |

| 1969 | Muscle Shoals Sound Studio opens | Swampers leave FAME |

## Funk/Disco Era (1970-1979)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1970 | Willie Mitchell discovers Al Green | Begins Hi Records partnership |
| 1970 | Gamble & Huff partner with Sigma Sound | Philadelphia Sound crystallizes |
| 1971 | "What's Going On" - Marvin Gaye | First Motown album to credit Funk Brothers |
| 1971 | Philadelphia International Records founded | Gamble and Huff launch PIR |
| 1971 | "There's a Riot Goin' On" - Sly Stone | Pioneering overdub/drum machine use |
| 1971 | "Let's Stay Together" - Al Green | #1 pop and R&B |
| 1972 Mar | "Music of My Mind" - Stevie Wonder | First "classic period" album with TONTO synth |
| 1972 | Motown moves to Los Angeles | Funk Brothers discover via notice on door |
| 1973 | Sea-Saint Studios opens (New Orleans) | Allen Toussaint and Marshall Sehorn |
| 1973 | Wattstax concert (Los Angeles) | Stax benefit at L.A. Memorial Coliseum |
| 1974 | "TSOP" - MFSB | First TV theme to reach #1; Soul Train theme |
| 1975 | Stax Records collapses | Distribution deal failures |
| 1975 Oct | Al Jackson Jr. murdered | Drummer for M.G.'s killed at home |
| 1976 | Tina Turner leaves Ike | Escapes with "36 cents and a gas station credit card" |
| 1978 | Sugar Hill Records founded | Sylvia Robinson creates hip-hop label |

## Drum Machine Era (1979-1989)

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1979 | "Rapper's Delight" - Sugarhill Gang | Hip-hop's first hit; 8 million copies sold |
| 1980 | Linn LM-1 drum machine released | First with digital samples |
| 1980 | Roland TR-808 released | $1,200 analog drum machine |
| 1982 | "The Message" - Grandmaster Flash | Shifts perception of rap as art form |
| **1982** | **"Sexual Healing" - Marvin Gaye** | **First US hit single to feature TR-808** |
| 1982 | *Thriller* - Michael Jackson | Uses Shure SM7 microphone; best-selling album ever |
| 1983 | Yamaha DX7 released | $1,995; best-selling synth in history |
| 1984 | Tina Turner - *Private Dancer* | Comeback at age 45; 20 million copies |
| 1986 Feb 4 | *Control* - Janet Jackson | Seizes creative independence with Jam & Lewis |
| 1986 | Uptown Records founded | Andre Harrell creates hip-hop soul |
| 1987 | E-mu SP-1200 released | $1,500 sampler enables sampling era |
| 1987 Oct 18 | "New Jack Swing" term coined | Barry Michael Cooper in Village Voice |
| 1987 | "I Want Her" - Keith Sweat | First New Jack Swing hit |
| 1988 | Motown sold to MCA | $61 million |
| 1988 Dec 8 | Akai MPC60 released | Roger Linn's pad-based sampler |
| 1988 | Ruth Brown co-founds R&B Foundation | Royalty reform advocacy |
| 1989 | LaFace Records founded (Atlanta) | L.A. Reid and Babyface |
