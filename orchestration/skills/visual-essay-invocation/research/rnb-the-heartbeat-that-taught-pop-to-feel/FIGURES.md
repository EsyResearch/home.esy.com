## Founding Era (1940s-1950s)

### Jerry Wexler (1917-2008) — "The Man Who Invented Rhythm & Blues"
- Coined term "rhythm and blues" while at Billboard (June 1949)
- Partner at Atlantic Records from 1953
- Produced Aretha Franklin at Muscle Shoals
- Inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (2017)
- **Quote**: Replaced "race records" with "rhythm and blues" in Billboard chart
- **Photograph**: Press photo, recording studio, Atlantic Records era
- **Source**: Getty Images, Rock Hall Archives

### Ruth Brown (1928-2006) — "Miss Rhythm"
- Built Atlantic Records foundation ("The House That Ruth Built")
- 149 weeks on R&B chart, 21 Top 10 hits (1949-1955)
- Co-founded Rhythm and Blues Foundation for royalty reform
- **Quote**: "Leveraged Atlantic Records into paying her back royalties - and she didn't stop there"
- **Photograph**: Glamorous performer, elegant evening wear, late 1940s-50s
- **Source**: Getty Images, NMAAHC, Atlantic Records archives

### LaVern Baker (1929-1997) — Copyright Pioneer
- Petitioned Congress to protect musical arrangements
- "Tweedle Dee" cover version controversy sparked investigation
- One of first Black singers to cross over to white teenage audience
- **Quote**: "Infuriated at the blatant rip-off" when Georgia Gibbs copied her arrangement
- **Photograph**: Elegant performer, sophisticated 1950s style
- **Source**: Getty Images, Rock Hall Archives

### Big Mama Thornton (1926-1984) — Original "Hound Dog"
- Recorded "Hound Dog" (1952), 7 weeks at #1 R&B
- Wrote "Ball and Chain" (later made famous by Janis Joplin)
- Helped usher in the dawn of rock and roll
- **Quote**: "That song sold over 2 million records. I got one check for $500 and never saw another."
- **Photograph**: Powerful, physically imposing, men's clothing, commanding presence
- **Source**: Library of Congress, Getty Images

### Fats Domino (1928-2017) — "Cornerstone of New Orleans Rock'n'Roll"
- "The Fat Man" (1949) first recording, sold over 1 million copies
- 40+ hits with Dave Bartholomew for Imperial
- Crossover success: "Ain't That a Shame" (1955), "Blueberry Hill" (1956)
- **Photograph**: Signature smile, piano, New Orleans setting
- **Source**: Getty Images, 64 Parishes archives

### Dave Bartholomew (1918-2019) — New Orleans Architect
- Imperial Records' New Orleans A&R man
- Bandleader, songwriter, producer, trumpet player
- Co-wrote 40+ hits with Fats Domino
- House band for J&M sessions
- **Photograph**: Bandleader with trumpet, studio or stage setting
- **Source**: 64 Parishes, Getty Images

### Cosimo Matassa (1926-2014) — Recording Pioneer
- Opened J&M Recording Studio at age 18 (1945)
- Engineered virtually every New Orleans R&B hit (late 1940s-early 1970s)
- Inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (2012)
- **Photograph**: Behind recording equipment, J&M studio
- **Source**: 64 Parishes, Rock Hall Archives

### Professor Longhair (1918-1980) — "Fess"
- Godfather of New Orleans piano
- Developed "rhumba-boogie funk" style
- "Bald Head" (1950) only national hit
- Rediscovered in 1970 sweeping out a record store
- Inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1992)
- **Quote**: "More than any New Orleans musician since Jelly Roll Morton, Professor Longhair epitomized the New Orleans sound."
- **Photograph**: At piano, New Orleans setting
- **Source**: 64 Parishes, Getty Images

## Soul Era (1960s)

### Aretha Franklin (1942-2018) — "Queen of Soul"
- First woman inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1987)
- Arrived at sessions with arrangements already prepared
- Transformed Otis Redding's "Respect" into feminist anthem
- **Quote**: "Jerry asked me to sit at the piano and bring in some things, where, at Columbia, they pretty much were telling me what to do."
- **Quote**: "Respect... was the need of a nation, the need of the average man and woman in the street."
- **Photograph**: At piano, regal, elegant, spiritual depth
- **Source**: Getty Images, Detroit Historical Society, Atlantic Archives

### Berry Gordy (b. 1929) — Motown Founder
- Borrowed $800 from family to start Tamla Records
- Applied assembly-line principles to music production
- Wrote/co-wrote 240 of 15,000 songs in Jobete catalog
- Inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1988)
- **Photograph**: Business setting, Hitsville U.S.A., executive portrait
- **Source**: Motown Museum, Getty Images

### James Jamerson (1936-1983) — "The Invisible Anchor"
- Motown Funk Brothers bassist
- Innovative walking bass lines defined Motown sound
- Posthumously inducted into Rock Hall (2000)
- Never publicly credited until "What's Going On" (1971)
- **Quote**: "Played on more Motown hits than the Beatles, Rolling Stones, Beach Boys, and Elvis combined"
- **Photograph**: With bass, Hitsville studio setting
- **Source**: Motown Museum, Standing in the Shadows of Motown

### Booker T. Jones (b. 1944) — Stax Musical Director
- Joined Stax as session musician at age 17
- Co-founded Booker T. & the M.G.'s (1962)
- "Green Onions" became million-selling instrumental
- De facto musical director for Stax
- **Photograph**: With Hammond B3 organ, Stax studio
- **Source**: Stax Museum, Getty Images

### Etta James (1938-2012) — Bridge Builder
- Bridged gap between R&B and rock and roll
- Added gospel elements to secular music, helping create soul
- Sampled extensively in hip-hop (Notorious B.I.G., Jay-Z, Tupac)
- **Quote**: "Sure, I can sing the blues all night long. I love the blues. I honor the form. But another part of me knows I can sing country and western just as soulfully."
- **Photograph**: Blonde signature look, expressive performance
- **Source**: Getty Images, NPR

### Sam Phillips (1923-2003) — Sun Records Founder
- Founded Sun Studio (1950), discovered Elvis Presley
- Pioneered slap-back echo technique
- Recorded Howlin' Wolf, B.B. King before rock era
- **Quote**: "If I could find a white man who had the Negro sound and the Negro feel, I could make a billion dollars"
- **Photograph**: Sun Studio control room
- **Source**: Sun Studio archives, Getty Images

## 1970s Innovators

### Stevie Wonder (b. 1950) — Synthesizer Pioneer
- *Music of My Mind* (1972) - first of "classic period"
- TONTO synthesizer collaboration with Cecil/Margouleff
- Played all synth parts himself
- **Photograph**: With TONTO synthesizer, Moog equipment
- **Source**: Motown Museum, Getty Images, Reverb

### Kenny Gamble (b. 1943) & Leon Huff (b. 1942) — The Philadelphia Sound
- Co-founded Philadelphia International Records (1971)
- Wrote over 3,000 songs together
- 170+ gold and platinum records by 1997
- **Quote**: Street renamed "Gamble Huff Walk"
- **Photograph**: Sigma Sound Studios, producer setting
- **Source**: Getty Images, Philadelphia Sound archives

### Thom Bell (1943-2022) — Classical Soul
- Born in Jamaica, classical training from childhood
- Produced Delfonics, Stylistics, Spinners
- Drew on classical influences and Ennio Morricone
- Grammy for Best Producer (1975)
- Inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (2025, posthumous)
- **Quote**: "To put it in a nutshell, he's responsible for everything that's happened to me in my career." - Russell Thompkins Jr.
- **Photograph**: Studio setting, arranger's position
- **Source**: Getty Images, Wikipedia

### Willie Mitchell (1928-2010) — Hi Records Architect
- Discovered Al Green (1968)
- Created Hi Records sound with Hodges brothers
- Seven consecutive gold singles with Al Green (1970+)
- **Quote**: Royal Studios "nothing more than a remodeled nickelodeon"
- **Photograph**: Royal Studios, with artists
- **Source**: Royal Studios, Getty Images

### Al Green (b. 1946) — Hi Records Signature Artist
- Sold 20+ million records during Hi Records career
- "Let's Stay Together" (1971), "I'm Still in Love with You" (1972)
- Used RCA 77DX ribbon microphone ("No. 9")
- **Photograph**: Performance, Royal Studios
- **Source**: Royal Studios, Getty Images

## 1980s-1990s Era

### Teddy Riley (b. 1967) — Father of New Jack Swing
- Created New Jack Swing as teenager in Harlem
- Produced Bobby Brown, Michael Jackson (*Dangerous*)
- **Quote**: "New Jack Swing is a movement... When you're doing New Jack Swing, you're doing a singer and a rapper together"
- **Photograph**: Studio with equipment, 808/DX7
- **Source**: Red Bull Music Academy, Getty Images

### Janet Jackson (b. 1966) — Control Artist
- *Control* (1986) - seized creative independence
- Moved to Minneapolis, worked with Jimmy Jam & Terry Lewis
- Fusion of R&B, rap, funk, disco established new R&B standard
- **Quote**: "I just wanted to get out of the house, get out from under my father"
- **Photograph**: *Control* era, military style, key earring
- **Source**: Getty Images, Rolling Stone

### Jimmy Jam (b. 1959) & Terry Lewis (b. 1956) — Flyte Tyme
- 100+ gold, platinum, multi-platinum, and diamond albums
- Janet Jackson's *Rhythm Nation* production
- "Squeezing every ounce of soul out of machines"
- **Photograph**: Flyte Tyme studios, with Janet Jackson
- **Source**: Getty Images, Wikipedia

### Sylvia Robinson (1935-2011) — "Mother of Hip-Hop"
- Founded Sugar Hill Records (1978)
- Conceived and produced "Rapper's Delight" (1979)
- Co-produced "The Message" (1982)
- **Quote**: "Arguably one of the most consequential producers and label owners of all time"
- **Photograph**: Sugar Hill Records setting
- **Source**: Billboard, Getty Images

### Mary J. Blige (b. 1971) — "Queen of Hip-Hop Soul"
- *What's the 411?* (1992) invented hip-hop soul genre
- Discovered via mall karaoke recording
- Founded FFAWN philanthropic organization
- **Quote**: "I started speaking about what I was dealing with through my music, and 4 million women responded and said, 'Us too, Mary.'"
- **Photograph**: Urban chic, blonde hair, large sunglasses
- **Source**: Getty Images, Grammy.com

### Missy Elliott (b. 1971) — Producer-Artist
- First female rapper inducted into Songwriters Hall of Fame (2019)
- First female rapper in Rock & Roll Hall of Fame
- Produced for Aaliyah, Mariah Carey, Whitney Houston, Janet Jackson
- **Quote**: "I didn't want to just be an artist and let someone else have all that control over me. I knew I would have to produce."
- **Photograph**: Avant-garde costume, futuristic style
- **Source**: Grammy.com, Getty Images

### Timbaland (b. 1972) — Sound Innovator
- Breakthrough: Aaliyah's *One in a Million* (1996), Missy's *Supa Dupa Fly* (1997)
- "Taking a hip-hop sound and applying it to R&B"
- Blurred distinction between hip-hop and R&B production
- **Quote** (on Missy): "She is the Aretha Franklin of hip-hop"
- **Photograph**: Studio with equipment, Akai MPC
- **Source**: Getty Images, Wikipedia

### The Neptunes (Pharrell Williams & Chad Hugo)
- 2003: 20% of British radio, 43% of American radio
- Korg Triton, Ensoniq ASR-10 production
- Minimalist approach: "Many of their biggest songs comprise only a few elements"
- **Photograph**: Studio setting, early 2000s
- **Source**: Getty Images, Time Magazine

## Label Executives & Business Figures

### Ahmet Ertegun (1923-2006) — Atlantic Records Founder
- Son of Turkish ambassador to the United States
- Started Atlantic with $10,000 from family dentist
- "The most successful record man of all time"
- Inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1987)
- **Photograph**: Atlantic Records office, business setting
- **Source**: Getty Images, Atlantic Archives

### Tom Dowd (1925-2002) — Recording Pioneer
- Built third eight-track machine ever made
- "The most revered recording engineer in rock music history"
- Pioneered stereo recording at Atlantic
- **Photograph**: Control room, behind console
- **Source**: Atlantic Archives, Getty Images

### Sylvia Rhone (b. 1952) — Pioneering Executive
- First African-American woman to lead major record company (Elektra, 1994)
- Launched careers of Missy Elliott, Busta Rhymes, Tracy Chapman
- Built ecosystems of young Black executives
- **Quote**: "There were many in the music community who questioned my ability as an African-American and a woman to run a label."
- **Photograph**: Executive portrait, professional setting
- **Source**: Billboard, Getty Images

### Andre Harrell (1960-2020) — Uptown Records Founder
- Created "hip-hop soul" sound
- Discovered Mary J. Blige, mentored Sean "Puffy" Combs
- Later CEO of Motown Records (1995)
- **Photograph**: Uptown Records era, with artists
- **Source**: Getty Images, Wikipedia

### L.A. Reid (b. 1956) & Babyface (b. 1959) — LaFace Records
- Founded LaFace Records (1989)
- 33 #1 singles, 100+ million units sold
- Discovered TLC, Usher, Pink
- **Photograph**: LaFace Records era, Atlanta
- **Source**: Getty Images, New Georgia Encyclopedia

### Jermaine Dupri (b. 1972) — So So Def Founder
- Discovered Kris Kross at age 19
- "Jump" - first rap song to stay #1 for eight consecutive weeks
- Produced for TLC, Usher, Mariah Carey, Janet Jackson
- **Photograph**: So So Def studio, with artists
- **Source**: Getty Images, Wikipedia

## Contemporary Era

### Beyoncé (b. 1981) — Business Architect
- Founded Parkwood Entertainment (2008)
- Complete creative control: music, visuals, branding
- Parkwood valued at $300+ million
- **Quote**: "I wanted to follow the footsteps of Madonna and be a powerhouse and have my own empire"
- **Photograph**: Performance, executive, *Lemonade* era
- **Source**: Getty Images, AfroTech

### Solange (b. 1986) — Saint Heron Founder
- Founded Saint Heron (2013) music and cultural hub
- Saint Records released "genreless" R&B compilation
- 2021: Expanded to studio, creative agency, library, art gallery
- **Quote**: "Our vision is to build a space that engages radical conceptual imaginings"
- **Photograph**: Artistic, avant-garde, sculptural fashion
- **Source**: Surface Magazine, Getty Images

### SZA (b. 1989) — Alternative R&B Pioneer
- First female artist at Top Dawg Entertainment (2013)
- Co-wrote for Nicki Minaj, Beyoncé, Rihanna
- Billboard's Woman of the Year (2023)
- **Quote**: "When you try to label it, you remove the option for it to be limitless"
- **Photograph**: Natural, unconventional, braids
- **Source**: Britannica, Getty Images

### H.E.R. (b. 1997) — Multi-Instrumentalist
- Plays piano, bass, drums, acoustic and electric guitar
- Four Grammys by age 23; Academy Award (2021)
- Writes socially conscious material ("I Can't Breathe")
- **Photograph**: Signature oversized sunglasses, guitar
- **Source**: Britannica, Getty Images

### The Weeknd (b. 1990) — Alternative R&B
- Credited with shaping indie R&B and electropop
- *808s & Heartbreak* influence acknowledged
- PBR&B genre pioneer alongside Frank Ocean
- **Photograph**: Performance, signature hairstyle
- **Source**: Getty Images, The Ringer

### Frank Ocean (b. 1987) — Alternative R&B
- *channel ORANGE* (2012) - Grammy for Best Urban Contemporary Album
- PBR&B pioneer
- Experimental production, introspective lyrics
- **Photograph**: Enigmatic, artistic portraits
- **Source**: Getty Images, Wikipedia

## Session Musicians & Behind-the-Scenes

### The Funk Brothers — Motown House Band
- Played on more hits than Beatles, Stones, Beach Boys, Elvis combined
- Key members: James Jamerson (bass), Benny Benjamin (drums), Earl Van Dyke (keys)
- Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award (2004)
- Not publicly credited until 1971; discovered Motown's move to L.A. from notice on door
- **Photograph**: Studio A group shot, with instruments
- **Source**: Motown Museum, Standing in the Shadows of Motown

### Booker T. & the M.G.'s — Stax House Band
- Steve Cropper, Al Jackson Jr., Duck Dunn
- "Green Onions" million-seller; de facto Stax musical directors
- Steve Cropper died December 2024
- **Photograph**: Stax studio, with instruments
- **Source**: Stax Museum, Getty Images

### MFSB — Philadelphia Sound
- 30+ studio musicians behind Philadelphia International hits
- Earl Young (drums), Ronnie Baker (bass), Norman Harris (guitar)
- "TSOP" (1974) - first TV theme to reach #1 on Hot 100
- **Photograph**: Sigma Sound group shot
- **Source**: Getty Images, Philadelphia Sound archives

### Cissy Houston (1933-2024) — Vocal Architect
- Led The Sweet Inspirations (backed Elvis, Aretha, Hendrix)
- Mother/vocal coach to Whitney Houston
- **Quote**: "When we hear those sounds in new generations of singers, we're hearing the Cissy Houston effect"
- **Photograph**: Performance, with Sweet Inspirations
- **Source**: Britannica, Getty Images
