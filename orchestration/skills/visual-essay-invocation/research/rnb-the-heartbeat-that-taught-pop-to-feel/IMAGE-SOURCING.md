## Executive Summary

With a $0 budget, this report identifies all freely available imagery for "R&B: The Heartbeat That Taught Pop to Feel."

**Reality Check**: R&B photography from 1949–present is largely copyrighted. Getty Images and Michael Ochs Archives own the most iconic shots. True public domain R&B imagery is limited to pre-1929 photographs and CC-licensed Wikimedia Commons content.

**Strategy**: Combine available CC-licensed photographs with:
- Pre-1929 historical imagery (race records era)
- Wikimedia Commons CC-licensed artist photos
- Equipment/studio photography (Unsplash/Pexels)
- Location photography (Hitsville today, etc.)
- Record label/album artwork in public domain
- U.S. Government archives

## Visual Strategy for $0 Budget

### What We CAN Show Well

| Category | Source | Coverage |
|----------|--------|----------|
| **Contemporary artists (1990s+)** | Wikimedia Commons | ✅ Good |
| **Equipment/technology** | Wikimedia + Unsplash | ✅ Excellent |
| **Studio locations (exteriors)** | Wikimedia Commons | ✅ Good |
| **Vinyl/records** | Unsplash | ✅ Excellent |
| **Studio atmospherics** | Unsplash | ✅ Excellent |
| **Pre-1929 blues** | LOC, Wikimedia | ✅ Good |

### What Will Be Challenging

| Category | Issue | Mitigation |
|----------|-------|------------|
| **1950s R&B pioneers** | Copyrighted photos | Use location photos, equipment, typography |
| **Session musicians** | Very few photos exist | Use silhouette treatment, symbolic imagery |
| **Specific studio interiors** | Copyrighted | Use generic studio shots |
| **Gamble & Huff, Teddy Riley** | No free photos | Use equipment/production imagery |
| **Album artwork** | Copyright | Reference only, no display |

### Creative Alternatives

1. **Typographic Treatment**: For eras with limited photography, use era-specific typography and color treatments to evoke the period.

2. **Location Over Person**: When artist photos unavailable, use location photos (Hitsville, Stax, Apollo) with text overlays.

3. **Equipment as Subject**: Technology chapter can be visually rich using equipment photography.

4. **Silhouette Treatment**: For session musicians chapter, use backlit studio silhouettes to represent "invisible" players.

5. **Contemporary Performers**: Most post-1990 artists have CC-licensed concert photography.
