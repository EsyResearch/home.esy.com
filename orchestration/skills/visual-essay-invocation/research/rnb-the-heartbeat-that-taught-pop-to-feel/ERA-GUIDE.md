# Era Guide: R&B — The Heartbeat That Taught Pop to Feel

> **Status**: Complete
> **Purpose**: Visual treatment specifications for each era

---

## Era 1: Race Records & Jump Blues (1920-1948)

### Time Period
1920-1948 (pre-"R&B" terminology)

### Visual Character
- **Tone**: Sepia, warm browns, cream tones
- **Grain**: Heavy film grain, period-appropriate degradation
- **Contrast**: High contrast, dramatic shadows
- **Processing**: Vintage photograph restoration aesthetic

### Color Palette
- Sepia (#704214)
- Cream (#FFFDD0)
- Dark Brown (#3E2723)
- Faded Black (#2C2C2C)

### Typography Cues
- Art Deco influences
- Bold sans-serif headlines
- Elegant script accents

### Signature Imagery
- Juke joints and roadhouses
- Traveling musicians with instruments
- Apollo Theater (pre-1934 and early era)
- 78 RPM records on Columbia, Okeh, Vocalion labels
- Cotton Club interiors
- Billboard "race records" charts

### Mood
Foundational, pre-commercial, juke joint energy, migration-era resilience

## Era 2: The Naming & Early R&B (1949-1959)

### Time Period
1949-1959 (Jerry Wexler coins "rhythm and blues" to present)

### Visual Character
- **Tone**: Warm blacks and whites with occasional hand-tinted color
- **Grain**: Medium grain, crisp for era
- **Contrast**: Classic portrait contrast
- **Processing**: 1950s publicity photo aesthetic

### Color Palette
- True Black (#000000)
- Warm White (#F5F5DC)
- Atlantic Blue (accent) (#1976D2)
- Imperial Red (accent) (#C62828)
- Specialty Yellow (accent) (#FFD600)

### Typography Cues
- 1950s display type
- Record label typography (Atlantic, Imperial, Specialty)
- Jet/Ebony magazine style

### Signature Imagery
- Ruth Brown, LaVern Baker, Big Mama Thornton glamour shots
- J&M Recording Studio (New Orleans)
- Sun Studio exterior
- 45 RPM singles with colorful labels
- Apollo Theater marquee
- Dew Drop Inn
- Fats Domino at piano
- Chess Records building

### Mood
Birth of the genre, Black glamour, independent label energy, crossover possibility

## Cross-Era Visual Themes

### Recurring Motifs
1. **Studios as sacred spaces** - J&M → Hitsville → Stax → Sigma Sound → The Dungeon
2. **Record labels as identity** - 45 rpm labels, company logos
3. **Instruments as icons** - Fender bass, Hammond B3, drum machines, MPC
4. **Fashion evolution** - Glamour → Afros → Platform → Baggy → Bling → Natural
5. **Geographic markers** - City-specific visual codes (NOLA, Memphis, Detroit, Philly, ATL)

### Era Transition Zones
- **1959**: B&W to color photography begins
- **1969**: Psychedelic influence, Afrocentric imagery
- **1979**: Disco to early 80s synthesizer aesthetic
- **1987**: New Jack Swing visual vocabulary
- **1999**: Hip-hop soul to digital age
- **2010**: Alternative/experimental aesthetics

### Technology Visualization
Each era should feature its signature technology:
- Era 1-2: Microphones, tape machines, record players
- Era 3: Multitrack consoles, studio equipment
- Era 4: Synthesizers, string sections
- Era 5-6: Drum machines, samplers
- Era 7-8: Laptops, DAWs, home studios

## Visual Processing Specifications

### Era 1-2 (B&W)
```css
filter: grayscale(100%);
sepia: 30%;
contrast: 1.2;
grain: high;
```

### Era 3 (Transition)
```css
saturation: 0.8;
warmth: 1.1;
contrast: 1.1;
vignette: subtle;
```

### Era 4-5 (70s-80s Color)
```css
saturation: 1.1;
warmth: 1.15;
contrast: 1.05;
film-stock: kodak-warm;
```

### Era 6-7 (Video Era)
```css
saturation: 1.0;
sharpness: high;
contrast: 1.1;
video-quality: true;
```

### Era 8 (Contemporary)
```css
saturation: 0.85;
grain: intentional;
contrast: variable;
tone: cool-warm-artist-specific;
```
