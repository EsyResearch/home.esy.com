### Era 1: Scientific Discovery (1767-1780s)

| Date | Event | Location | Significance | Source | Priority |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|--------|----------|
| 1767 | Joseph Priestley discovers carbonation at Leeds brewery | Leeds, England | First artificial carbonation; suspended water bowl over fermenting beer vat | Wikipedia, Science History Institute | P1 |
| 1771 | Torbern Bergman perfects carbonation process | Sweden | Developed apparatus using sulfuric acid and chalk for efficient CO2 production | Wikipedia | P2 |
| 1772 | Priestley publishes "Impregnating Water with Fixed Air" | England | First scientific documentation of carbonation process; describes using sulfuric acid on chalk | Wikipedia, ACS | P1 |
| 1773 | Priestley awarded Copley Medal by Royal Society | London | Scientific recognition for carbonation discovery | Wikipedia | P2 |

### Era 2: Commercial Pioneering (1783-1850)

| Date | Event | Location | Significance | Source | Priority |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|--------|----------|
| 1783 | Johann Jacob Schweppe founds company in Geneva | Geneva, Switzerland | First commercial carbonated water producer; patented "Geneva System" | Wikipedia | P6 |
| 1792 | Schweppe expands to London | London, England | Factory established at 141 Drury Lane; beginning of British market | Wikipedia | P2 |
| 1806 | Benjamin Silliman opens soda fountain in New Haven | New Haven, Connecticut | First American to produce soda water in bulk; marketed for medicinal properties | Connecticut History | P2 |

| 1821 | Johann Jacob Schweppe dies | Geneva, Switzerland | Founder's legacy continues; company thrives | Wikipedia | P3 |
| 1831 | King William IV grants Schweppes royal warrant | United Kingdom | First "by appointment to" royal designation for soft drink | Wikipedia | P2 |
| 1832 | John Mathews creates commercial carbonation apparatus | United States | Lead-lined chamber for large-scale soda production; 30+ minutes manual agitation | Art of Drink | P2 |

|------|-------|----------|--------------|--------|----------|

| December 24, 1902 | Pepsi-Cola Company incorporated | North Carolina | Bradham as president | NCpedia | P3 |
