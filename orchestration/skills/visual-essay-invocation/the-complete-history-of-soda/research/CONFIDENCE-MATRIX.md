> Research Orchestrator | January 1, 2026

## Purpose

This matrix maps major claims in the visual essay to their evidence base and confidence level. Writers should calibrate language based on confidence levels.

## Confidence Levels

| Level | Icon | Meaning | Language Guidance |
|-------|------|---------|-------------------|
| HIGH | ✅ | Multiple independent sources; primary sources available | State as fact |
| MEDIUM | ⚠️ | Reliable secondary sources; some primary evidence | Use with attribution |
| LOW | ❓ | Single source or contested claims | Qualify heavily or omit |
| UNVERIFIED | ❌ | Cannot verify; appears only in unreliable sources | Do not use |

## Writer Guidance

### Use Freely (HIGH Confidence)
- Major dates and events
- Biographical birth/death dates
- Published works and patents
- Well-documented business transactions
- Events with multiple independent sources

### Use with Attribution (MEDIUM Confidence)
- Specific dollar amounts and statistics
- Quoted statements (use "reportedly said" or "according to")
- Exact numbers of plants, products, employees
- Market share figures
- Corporate claims about scale

### Avoid or Qualify (If any LOW found in future research)
- Contested "first" claims
- Precise quantities from single sources
- Unverified anecdotes
