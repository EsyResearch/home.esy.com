### Scope Definition

This visual essay traces the word "slang" from its earliest attestations through its evolution as both a term and a linguistic phenomenon. The essay will explore:

1. **Etymology** — Origin theories, earliest uses, semantic shifts
2. **Social Function** — Why slang exists (belonging, rebellion, identity, speed)
3. **Historical Timeline** — U.S. focus with global spread
4. **Key Figures** — Lexicographers, documenters, champions
5. **Modern Era** — Internet as slang engine, acceleration, half-life

## Key Research Questions

1. What is the earliest attested use of the word "slang" and in what context?
2. What are the competing etymological theories and their evidence strength?
3. How did the term's connotation shift (pejorative → neutral → identity marker)?
4. Who were the key lexicographers/scholars who documented and legitimized slang?
5. How does slang differ from argot, cant, jargon, vernacular, colloquial speech?
6. What role did specific eras (Jazz Age, Hip-Hop, Internet) play in slang proliferation?
7. Why do slang words have a "half-life" — burning bright then vanishing?
