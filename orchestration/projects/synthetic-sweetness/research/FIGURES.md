## Discoverers and Scientists

### 1. Constantine Fahlberg (1850-1910)
**Role**: Discovered saccharin's sweet taste

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Born | December 22, 1850, Tambov, Russia |
| Died | August 15, 1910, Nassau, Germany |
| Education | German descent; chemistry training |
| Discovery | Saccharin sweet taste (June 1878) |
| Patents | US 326,281; 496,112; 496,113; 564,784 |

**Key Quote**: "With the name 'saccharin' I am providing a formal designation for a substance discovered by me in June 1878."

**Controversy**: Patented saccharin without crediting or informing Ira Remsen, leading to bitter dispute.

**Image Sources**:
- Wikimedia Commons: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Constantin_Fahlberg
- Limited high-resolution imagery available

### 2. Ira Remsen (1846-1927)
**Role**: Johns Hopkins professor; co-discoverer of saccharin

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Born | February 10, 1846, New York City |
| Died | March 4, 1927, Carmel-by-the-Sea, California |

### 3. Michael Sveda (1912-1999)
**Role**: Discovered cyclamate

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Born | February 3, 1912, West Ashford, Connecticut |
| Died | August 10, 1999, Stamford, Connecticut |
| Education | B.S. Toledo (1934); Ph.D. Illinois (1939) |

### 4. James M. Schlatter
**Role**: Discovered aspartame

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Position | Chemist at G.D. Searle & Company |
| Location | Skokie, Illinois |
| Discovery | Aspartame (December 1965) |
| Original Goal | Synthesizing tetrapeptide for anti-ulcer drug |

**Key Quote**: "I licked my finger and it tasted good."

**Discovery Story**: While recrystallizing aspartame from ethanol, some powder landed on his finger. He licked it while picking up papers and noted the unexpected sweetness.

**Image Sources**: Limited public imagery available; check G.D. Searle/Pfizer archives

### 5. Shashikant Phadnis
**Role**: Discovered sucralose

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Position | Postdoctoral researcher |
| Institution | Queen Elizabeth College, London |
| Discovery | Sucralose (1975/1976) |
| Supervisor | Professor Leslie Hough |
| Sponsor | Tate & Lyle |

**Key Quote**: "I thought I ought to taste it [...] so I took a little bit of it on a spatula and tasted it with the tip of my tongue. What a surprise! It was intensely and pleasantly sweet."

**Discovery Story**: Misheard "test" as "taste" due to his Indian accent and lab acoustics. When he reported the discovery, Prof. Hough said: "Are you crazy? How can you taste compounds, without knowing anything about their toxicity?"

### 7. Karl Clauss
**Role**: Discovered acesulfame-K

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Company | Hoechst AG, Frankfurt, Germany |
| Collaborator | Harald Jensen |
| Discovery | Acesulfame-K (1967) |
| Patent | 1975 US patent assigned to Hoechst |

**Discovery Story**: Accidental discovery while conducting a reaction between 2-butyne and fluorosulfonyl isocyanate. Licked his fingers to pick up paper and discovered the sweet taste.

## Regulatory and Political Figures

### 8. Harvey Washington Wiley (1844-1930)
**Role**: Father of the FDA; Pure Food advocate

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Born | October 18, 1844, Kent, Indiana |
| Died | June 30, 1930, Washington, D.C. |
| Position | Chief Chemist, USDA Bureau of Chemistry (1883-1912) |
| Achievement | Championed Pure Food and Drug Act of 1906 |

**Saccharin Controversy**: Wiley sought to ban saccharin in 1907, believing it was an adulterant. This led to his famous confrontation with President Roosevelt.

**Image Sources**:
- Library of Congress Manuscript Division
- Harvey Wiley Papers (10 boxes of prints and photographs)

### 9. Theodore Roosevelt (1858-1919)
**Role**: President who defended saccharin

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Term | 26th President (1901-1909) |
| Context | Personally used saccharin on doctor's orders |

**Famous Quote**: "Anybody who says saccharin is injurious to health is an idiot." (Said to Harvey Wiley, 1907)

**Action**: Appointed Ira Remsen to a Referee Board to reconsider food additive policies, overruling Wiley's attempts to ban saccharin.

| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
